Context

Our verdict

Sucre, constitutional capital of Bolivia (the administrative capital being La Paz, constitutional dualism unique in the world): 280,000 inhabitants at 2810m altitude. Listed UNESCO 1991 for its immaculate white colonial historic centre ("la Ciudad Blanca", urban regulation that imposes white whitewash on all houses since the 19th century, iconic view). More accessible and lower than La Paz (3650m), often recommended as the first stop in Bolivia to acclimatise to altitude. August is my favourite month (8.4/10): 6-21°C, 12% rain, this is the dry season, intense permanent blue sky. From June to October, it's the dry season: 6-22°C, 7-22% rain, stable temperatures thanks to altitude (summer in the Andes paradoxically = dry season, not hot). From December to March, it's the rainy season: 10-20°C, 49-77% rain, often overcast sky. Constant climate thanks to equatorial altitude: stable temperatures 6-22°C year-round, main variations on precipitation. Highlights: Plaza 25 de Mayo (main square, central pavilion, Government Palace of Chuquisaca), Casa de la Libertad (museum where Bolivian independence was declared on 6 August 1825, signed by Simón Bolívar), Catedral Metropolitana (mix of Renaissance, baroque and neoclassical 16th-19th century), Iglesia y Convento de San Felipe Neri (panoramic view from the roof), Museo de la Recoleta (17th-century Franciscan convent converted into museum, view from Recoleta square), Mercado Central (traditional market, fresh fruit juices, food stalls), Cementerio General (one of the most beautiful cemeteries in Latin America, luxurious mausoleums), Tarabuco 2h away on Sundays (Yampara ethnic market, one of the most colourful in South America). Excursions: Parque Cretácico 5 km away ("Cal Orck'o", the largest concentration of dinosaur tracks in the world, 12,000 tracks of 8 species over 1.5 km of vertical limestone wall revealed by erosion of a cement quarry), Potosí 3h away (UNESCO, former colonial silver centre, Cerro Rico mines poignant visit of miners in operation), Salar de Uyuni 8h away (the largest salt desert in the world, one of the iconic Bolivian destinations, classic 3-day Uyuni-San Pedro de Atacama tour). Try salteñas (sweet-spicy meat turnover), api con pastel (hot purple corn drink + turnover), chuño (Andean dehydrated potato), llajwa (traditional spicy sauce).
