Asunción, capital of Paraguay: 525,000 inhabitants (2 million in urban area) on the east bank of the Río Paraguay facing Argentina. Founded in 1537 by the Spanish, one of the oldest capitals in South America ("Madre de Ciudades" = "Mother of Cities" as other cities like Buenos Aires were founded by expeditions departed from Asunción). Very little touristic capital of one of the least visited countries in South America (but also one of the cheapest, ideal for restricted budget). September is my favourite month (8.9/10): 17-28°C, 25% rain, this is the austral spring. From April to September, it's the austral winter: 14-29°C, 18-38% rain, mild bearable climate. From November to March, it's the hot austral summer: 21-33°C with 36-40% rain: extreme heat (sometimes 42°C+ with crushing humidity typical of the Paraguayan Chaco), reduced hotel prices. Highlights: Palacio de los López (presidential palace on Plaza Constitución, 19th-century European architecture built for President Francisco Solano López who was never able to live there — died in the disastrous Triple Alliance war 1864-1870 that destroyed Paraguay), Catedral Metropolitana (19th century), Panteón Nacional de los Héroes (mausoleum of national heroes including President Solano López and Carlos Antonio López), Casa de la Independencia (where Paraguay's independence was declared on 14 May 1811), Museo del Barro (the best museum of folk and indigenous art in Paraguay, magnificent collection of ñandutí textiles, masks and contemporary Guaraní art), Manzana de la Rivera (renovated quarter around the square with colonial houses), Mercado 4 (gigantic chaotic market, local atmosphere of non-tourist South America), Costanera (promenade along the Río Paraguay, urban river beaches). Excursions: Jesuit Mission of Trinidad and Jesús de Tavarangue 4h away (UNESCO, ruins of 17th-18th century Jesuit reductions, 30 ruins in total in southern Paraguay and northern Argentina The Mission film by Roland Joffé 1986), Paraguayan Pantanal in the north. Try chipa (cassava and cheese bread, the national bread), sopa paraguaya (paradoxically "the only solid soup in the world" as it is actually a salted corn bread), tereré (cold mate instead of hot like in Argentina/Uruguay, ideal for the Paraguayan heat), asado, chipa guazú.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
