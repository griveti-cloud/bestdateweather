Context

Our verdict

Geneva, Switzerland's 2nd city and European HQ of the UN: 200,000 inhabitants in the city, 600,000 in the urban area. International capital (HQs of WTO, WHO, UNHCR, ILO, ICRC), world capital of luxury watchmaking (Patek Philippe, Vacheron Constantin, Rolex at Plan-les-Ouates), private finance capital. On the shore of Lake Geneva with views of Mont Blanc on clear days. July-August are my favourite months (8.9/10): 17-27°C, 29-33% rain, ideal temperatures, swimming in the lake from the Bains des Pâquis, a Genevan institution. From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 13-27°C. From November to March, expect 0-10°C and 36-47% rain: winter cold, possible but rare snow on the plain (Geneva is in a zone protected by the Jura to the west), bise sometimes glacial north wind, Place Bel-Air Christmas market. Highlights: Jet d'eau (Geneva's visual iconic, 140m high, 500 litres/second, visible from anywhere in the city, created in 1886 as a hydraulic factory pressure-relief valve, became a tourist icon in 1891), Old town (one of Europe's largest old towns, cobbled alleys, Saint Peter's Cathedral 12th-century Romanesque-Gothic — site of the Calvinist Reformation, seat of John Calvin 1536-1564 — panoramic towers, archaeological excavations 3rd-century basement), Maison Tavel (Geneva's oldest house from the 12th century, urban history museum), Place du Bourg-de-Four (oldest square), Reformation Wall (1909, tribute to the Reformers Calvin/Knox/Bèze/Farel sculpted on 100m), Palais des Nations (UN European HQ, guided tour 17 CHF, the world's largest intergovernmental meeting space), International Red Cross and Red Crescent Museum (opposite the Palais des Nations, moving humanitarian view), CERN at Meyrin (European Organization for Nuclear Research, where the Higgs boson was discovered in 2012, free guided tours by booking), MAMCO (modern contemporary art museum), Patek Philippe Museum (one of the world's largest watchmaking collections, free on the 1st Saturday of the month). Excursions: Mont-Salève by cable car 5 min from Veyrier (Geneva's home mountain on the French side, 360° panorama over the city and the Alps), Lausanne 35 min by train, Chamonix-Mont-Blanc 1h away (Aiguille du Midi cable car 3842m), Annecy 30 min away (Europe's purest lake), Yvoire medieval lakeside village. Try longeole (Geneva sausage with cumin), lake perch fillets, half-and-half fondue, longeole, local Geneva Beer brewery. Geneva-Cointrin airport (GVA) 7 min by train from the centre, one of Europe's most practical.
