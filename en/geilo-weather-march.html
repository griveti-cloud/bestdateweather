Context

Our verdict

Geilo, Norwegian ski resort on the Oslo-Bergen railway in Hallingdal: 2400 inhabitants at 800m altitude between Hardangervidda and Hallingskarvet national parks. Oldest tourist ski resort in Norway = developed from 1909 when the Oslo-Bergen railway was inaugurated crossing Geilo, more traditional and family atmosphere than neighbouring more festive Hemsedal. June-July are my favourite months (7.2-7.5/10): 1-17°C, 35-48% rain, ideal temperatures for Norwegian alpine hiking with long days (18-20h of day at the June solstice). Geilo never tops 7.5/10 (cold Scandinavian alpine climate). From June to August, it's the summer high season: -7-17°C, Hardangervidda hiking = the largest altitude plateau in Europe 8000 km², mountain bike, salmon fishing, observation of the last wild reindeer of Europe in their natural state. From December to April, expect -10/4°C and 32-49% precipitation often as snow: ski high season. Pistes: 39 km Geilo Skisenter (modest vs Alps), 2 sectors (Geilo + Vestlia) + 220 km of cross-country trails ("langlauf" = Norwegian national sport, more practised than alpine skiing locally). iconic pistes: Tjødnaheis (summit 1178m), Vestlia family side. No sporty off-piste at Geilo (gentler relief than at neighbouring Hemsedal), ideal for beginner families. Nordic winter activities: dog sledding (husky safari), snowmobile, cross-country skiing (the largest cross-country area in Norway), ice fishing on frozen lakes, Northern Lights safari (Geilo is at 60°N so at the southern limit of the Northern Lights observation zone, uneven conditions unlike Finnish Levi 67°N). Highlights: Hardangervidda National Park 30 min away (the largest altitude plateau in Europe, lunar landscapes of alpine tundra, last wild reindeer of Europe in herds, "most beautiful Norwegian hike" Trolltunga 27 km 4h from Geilo), Vøringfossen Waterfall (one of the most impressive in Norway 182m, accessible 1h by car), Kongsberg Mining Museum 2h away (17th-century royal silver mine). Oslo-Bergen train = the most picturesque railway in Norway, ranked among the world top 10, crosses Geilo in 4h from Oslo or 3h from Bergen. Audience: Norwegians in the majority (Oslo 3h by train, traditional family atmosphere), Swedes, Danes, few foreigners (the real international ski resorts in Norway are Lillehammer + Hemsedal + Trysil). Hotels: Vestlia Resort (4*+), Highland Hotel, prices 150-500€/night. Try reindeer ("reinsdyrkjøtt"), elk, Norwegian Atlantic salmon, brunost (iconic Norwegian caramelised brown cheese), Norwegian aquavit, local Hansa beer.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Geilo?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 8.8/10). For hiking/summer: March (8.8/10, 1.0°C).
Can you ski in Geilo in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.7/10. Cold temperatures (-4°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Geilo in summer?
In March, temperatures average 1.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Geilo?
October is the wettest month with October has the lowest climate score of the year.
