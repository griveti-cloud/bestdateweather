Context

Our verdict

Grenadines (Saint Vincent and the Grenadines), Caribbean micro-state in the south of the Lesser Antilles: 110,000 inhabitants on 32 islands of which 9 inhabited. The country is made up of the main island Saint Vincent + 32 small Grenadines islets (Bequia, Mustique, Canouan, Mayreau, Union Island, Tobago Cays, Palm Island, Petit Saint Vincent). Independence from the United Kingdom 1979, Commonwealth member. April is my favourite month (9.1/10): 24-29°C, 16% rain, sea at 27°C, ideal temperatures. January-March stay excellent (8.7-8.9/10). From January to May, it's the dry season: 23-30°C, this is the tourist high season (ultra-smart clientele), hotel prices x3-x4. From June to October, expect 24-31°C with 32-56% rain: active cyclonic season. From November to December, transition. Highlights / Main islands: Saint Vincent (main island, capital Kingstown, active volcano La Soufrière 1234m last devastating eruption April 2021, summit hike 6h round trip, Kingstown botanical gardens 1765 = the oldest in the Caribbean), Bequia 1h by ferry from Kingstown ("Pearl of the Grenadines", picturesque port with yachts, Princess Margaret Beach, relaxed atmosphere), Mustique (most exclusive private island in the Grenadines, only 100 villas, very limited access, celebrities Mick Jagger/Princess Margaret/Bryan Adams have houses, Cotton House 5*+ unique hotel, prohibitive prices), Canouan (smart island with marina), Mayreau (very small island, 200 inhabitants, Salt Whistle Bay beach = one of the most beautiful Caribbean beaches), Union Island (diving stopover), Tobago Cays (national maritime park, archipelago of deserted islets in a turquoise lagoon = the iconic view of the Grenadines, cult Caribbean yacht mooring, snorkelling with green turtles, accessible only by boat), Palm Island (private island 18 hectares, 5*+ unique hotel-resort), Petit Saint Vincent ("PSV", another even more exclusive private island, 22 scattered cottages). Activities: sailing = the n°1 attraction of the Grenadines, one of the world sailing paradises (sailboat rental with or without skipper, week 2000-10000€ depending on category, classic route Saint Vincent-Bequia-Mustique-Canouan-Mayreau-Tobago Cays-Union-PSV), diving sites Devil's Table, Anchor Reef, Mayreau Gardens, kitesurfing (Union Island = kite capital of the Grenadines), traditional sailing (Bequia preserves the tradition of sailing whaling according to IWC International Whaling Commission quotas, controversial but legal), Soufrière Volcano hike. Filming locations: Pirates of the Caribbean (Wallilabou Bay on Saint Vincent = village reconstituted as Port Royal for the film). Public: ultra-wealthy on yacht, European-American sailors, few traditional tourists, discreet VIP atmosphere. Try roti (Indian-Trinidadian heritage), callaloo soup, conch fritters, mountain chicken (actually endemic giant frog), local Sunset Captain Bligh rum, breadfruit. Logistics: Argyle International Saint Vincent airport (SVD) opened 2017, direct charter flights Miami, Toronto, London, several inter-island airports (Mustique MQS, Canouan CIW, Union UNI, Bequia BQU).
