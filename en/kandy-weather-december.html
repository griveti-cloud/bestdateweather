Context

Our verdict

Kandy, Sri Lanka's 2nd city in the heart of the central mountains: 125,000 inhabitants at 500m altitude. Last Sinhalese royal capital (1469-1815 before the British conquest, the Kandyan kingdom heroically resisting the Portuguese then Dutch for 300 years), cultural and religious capital of Sinhalese Buddhism. UNESCO 1988 for the sacred city. January-February are my favourite months (8.7/10): 20-29°C, 29% rain, this is the ideal dry season. December stays excellent (7.9/10). From January to April, it's the relative dry season: 20-31°C. From May to October, it's the south-west monsoon: 69-83% rainfall, but more bearable temperatures (22-28°C, cooler than Colombo thanks to the altitude). From October to December, it's the 2nd inter-monsoon. Permanent climate: Kandy never exceeds 31°C in average maximum and rarely drops below 20°C in average minimum, a major advantage of tropical altitude ("Eternal Spring" climate similar to Da Lat in Vietnam). Highlights: Temple of the Tooth (Sri Dalada Maligawa, Temple of the Tooth, the most sacred place in Sinhalese Buddhism, houses the relic of the Buddha's left tooth arrived in Sri Lanka in the 4th century AD, major pilgrimage site, free access for foreigners, strict dress code shoulders/legs covered, the tooth is never visible to the faithful — in a gold reliquary itself within 7 concentric reliquaries — but the outer reliquary can be seen at the 3 daily "poojas" 5:30-9:30-18:30, mystical atmosphere with drums, chants, candles, lotus offerings), Kandy Lake (artificial lake created by the last king in 1807, 4 km walk around), Royal Botanical Gardens of Peradeniya 6 km away (one of Asia's most beautiful botanical gardens, 60 hectares, founded 1843 by the British on a former royal Sinhalese garden, royal palms, orchids, cinnamon-tree avenues), Bahirawakanda Vihara Buddha Statue (giant 26m white Buddha on the hill to the north, panorama over Kandy), Kandy Cultural Show (traditional Sinhalese Kandyan dance show, fire walking, every evening 17:30-18:30 in several tourist halls 1500-2500 LKR ≈ 5-8€), Kandy Central Market (spices, tropical fruits), Esala Perahera = great annual festival of the Temple of the Tooth (July-August by lunar calendar, 10 nights of illuminated processions with 100 caparisoned elephants in gold-red, dancers, jugglers, the largest religious festival in South Asia with 1 million spectators, one of the world's most impressive cultural shows). Excursions: Sigiriya 2h30 away (UNESCO, "Lion Rock" 200m fortress-palace on a rock outcrop 5th century, one of Sri Lanka's most visited sites), Polonnaruwa 3h away (UNESCO, former 12th-century medieval capital), Anuradhapura 3h away (UNESCO, oldest Sri Lankan capital 4th century BC), Dambulla Cave Temple 2h away (UNESCO, frescoed Buddhist cave temples), Ella 6h away by spectacular train (high mountains), Nuwara Eliya tea plantations 2h away ("Little England", Ceylon tea, English climate at 1900m altitude). Try Sri Lankan rice and curry (plate with 5-7 different curries), kottu roti, hopper, fish ambul thiyal, curd and treacle (buffalo yoghurt with palm sugar syrup), local Ceylon tea. Direct Colombo-Kandy train 3h (one of Asia's most scenic).
