San Cristóbal de las Casas, colonial town of the Chiapas in southern Mexico in the Maya highlands: 220,000 inhabitants at 2200m altitude. Cultural capital of the indigenous Tzotzil and Tzeltal Maya peoples (Chiapas has the highest concentration of Maya population in Mexico, 1.2 million Mayans in the region, mother tongues Tzotzil/Tzeltal/Cho'ol/Tojolabal still majority in surrounding villages). Centre of the 1994 Zapatista uprising (EZLN, Sub-Comandante Marcos, indigenous revolutionary movement pacified since 2003 but persistent cultural influence). March is my favourite month (8.3/10): 11-22°C, 31% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From January to April, it's the dry season: 9-22°C, permanent springtime climate thanks to altitude. From May to October, it's the wet season: 47-89% rainfall (but stable temperatures 12-22°C). From November to February, expect 9-19°C: relative cold for Mexico, freezing nights 8-10°C, pleasant sunny days, sometimes need a sweater even at midday. San Cristóbal highlights: San Cristóbal Cathedral (yellow ochre 1528, one of the oldest in Mexico), Templo de Santo Domingo (16th-century baroque sculpted, craft market at the foot = one of the best in Mexico for Tzotzil textiles), Templo del Carmen (17th century with arch unique in Mexico straddling the street), Andador Eclesiástico (pedestrian street connecting Santo Domingo and the cathedral, colonial atmosphere), Andador Guadalupano (pedestrian street leading to the Guadalupe church, panoramic view 79 steps), San Cristóbal Central Market (spices, tropical fruits, astonishing for mountains), Museo de los Altos de Chiapas (Maya highlands museum in former convent), Mayan Medicine Museum (traditional Maya medicine, medicinal plants, reconstituted shamanic ritual), Café Museum (coffee museum), Centro Cultural El Carmen, Plaza 31 de Marzo (central Zócalo square with mariachi music in the evening). Essential excursions = this is why one comes to San Cristóbal: San Juan Chamula 30 min away (Tzotzil village with fascinating syncretic church = Catholicism + Maya shamanism, floor covered with pine needles, live chicken sacrifice for healing, photos absolutely forbidden under penalty of confiscation of camera/phone by the villagers — take this very seriously), Zinacantán 30 min away (neighbouring Tzotzil village, traditional Maya weaving workshop), Sumidero Canyon 1h away (1000m deep canyon, 2h boat tour on the Grijalva river, spectacular panoramas), Cascadas de Agua Azul 4h away (spectacular turquoise waterfalls in natural staircases), Cascada de Misol-Ha 4h away (unique 35m waterfall, possibility of walking behind the water curtain), Palenque 4h away (UNESCO, one of the most impressive Maya sites in Mexico with Tikal Guatemala, ruins in tropical jungle, Templo de las Inscripciones tomb Pacal the Great 683), Lacandon Jungle (virgin jungle Guatemala border, isolated preserved Lacandon Maya communities), Yaxchilan and Bonampak (Maya ruins border, difficult access by boat on the Rio Usumacinta). Try Chiapaneco coffee (one of the best in Mexico, Maya Vinic Tzotzil cooperative), cochito al horno, Chiapanec tamales, sopa de pan, posh (traditional Tzotzil cane eau-de-vie used in ritual), artisanal Mexican Kakaw chocolate (Mexico = origin of cocoa + chocolate). Logistics: Tuxtla Gutiérrez airport (TGZ) 1h by bus, San Cristóbal 12h by ADO luxury bus from Mexico City.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
