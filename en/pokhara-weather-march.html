Context

Our verdict

Pokhara, Nepal's 2nd city 200 km west of Kathmandu: 425,000 inhabitants at 800m altitude at the foot of the Annapurna massif. Nicknamed "Nepal's tourist capital", main base for Annapurna treks. Spectacular view of Machapuchare 6993m ("Fishtail Mountain", sacred peak never climbed), Annapurna I 8091m (10th highest summit in the world), Annapurna South 7219m. April is my favourite month (8.9/10): 15-27°C, 25% rain, ideal temperatures before the monsoon. March-October-November stay excellent (8.5/10). From March to May and October-November, these are the best trekking seasons: 6-27°C, clear skies. From June to September, it's the monsoon: 69-97% rainfall, summits hidden by mist. From December to February, expect 6-21°C: winter cold, high-altitude passes closed. Highlights: Phewa Lake (Phewa Tal, Pokhara's iconic alpine lake at 800m, colourful "doonga" boat rides with Annapurna views, early morning at 6am the summits reflect perfectly in the flat water), Tal Barahi Temple (small Hindu temple on an islet in the middle of Phewa lake, accessible by boat), World Peace Pagoda (Shanti Stupa, white Buddhist stupa at 1100m on Anadu hill, 1h hike from the lake, 360° panorama over Pokhara + Annapurna), Sarangkot at 1600m (classic observation point for sunrise over the Annapurna at 5am, access by taxi 1h or 3h hike, iconic view with Machapuchare lit up in gold-pink), International Mountain Museum (museum on Himalayan mountaineering and the mountain peoples Sherpa-Gurung-Magar-Tamang), Devi's Falls (waterfall plunging into an underground sinkhole), Mahendra Caves and Bat Cave, Begnas Lake and Rupa Lake (quieter alternative lakes), Gurkha Memorial Museum (museum about the Nepali Gurkha soldiers famous in the British Army since 1815). Activities: paragliding (Pokhara is one of the world's most prestigious paragliding sites, flights from Sarangkot 1600m with landing on Phewa lakeside, view of the Annapurna during the flight, October-April mainly, tandem 70-100 USD/person 30 min flight), short hikes: Poon Hill 3-4 days = the most accessible trek in Nepal (3210m), Mardi Himal 5 days, Annapurna Base Camp 10-12 days starting from Pokhara, rafting on Trisuli or Seti rivers, zipline (one of the longest and steepest in the world at 1.8 km descent 600m). Lakeside district ("Baidam", lakefront with hotels, restaurants and shops for trekkers, cosmopolitan atmosphere). Try dal bhat (national dish), momos, thukpa, gundruk (fermented leaf-vegetable), local Everest beer. Logistics: Pokhara 7h by bus from Kathmandu or 25 min by plane (panoramic flight with Himalaya view), Pokhara International airport (PKR) opened 2023.
