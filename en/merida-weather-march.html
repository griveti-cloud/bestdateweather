The month in detail

What to expect in March

March is peak dry season in Mérida. The air is warm and humidity more bearable than in the rainy season. Rain is virtually absent (9.0% of days) — ideal for planning without a backup. Not the best window, but acceptable if dates are constrained. January (8.6/10) is significantly better if possible.

Context

Our verdict

Mérida is the capital of Yucatán: 1 million inhabitants, Spanish colonial city built on the ruins of the Mayan city T'ho. January is my favourite month (8.6/10): 19-28°C, 18% rain, mild climate after the infernal summer. From March to August, expect 22-36°C: extreme heat of the Yucatán interior (sometimes 42°C), Méridans coming out after 19h, plazas waking up in the evening. From June to October, it's the rainy season: 39-57% rainfall, but relative respite from the heat. Highlights: Plaza Grande (Catedral San Idelfonso, Casa de Montejo, Palacio Municipal), Paseo de Montejo (avenue inspired by the Champs-Élysées, 19th-century mansions), Mercado Lucas de Galvez, Gran Museo del Mundo Maya. Excursions: Chichén Itzá 1h30 away (UNESCO, sunrise to avoid crowds and heat), Uxmal 1h away (UNESCO, Puuc ruins less frequented than Chichén), Izamal 1h away ("yellow city", 16th-century Franciscan convent), Cenotes of Cuzamá (3 cenotes by mule train), Celestún 2h away (pink flamingos). Friday evening: free open-air music on Plaza Grande. Try cochinita pibil (pork marinated in achiote, cooked in the earth), poc chuc, papadzules, sopa de lima, marquesitas.
