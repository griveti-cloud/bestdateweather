Raja Ampat, archipelago in the extreme east of Indonesia in West Papua province: 1500 islands, 64,000 inhabitants, considered the world epicentre of marine biodiversity. Over 1500 species of fish, 700 species of molluscs, 75% of the coral species known in the world (versus 50% for the Australian Great Barrier Reef). Preserved by its extreme remoteness and difficulty of access. August is my favourite month (6.5/10): 23-28°C, 85% rain. Raja Ampat never tops 6.5/10 (equatorial tropical climate with frequent rains year-round, but short and warm rains). From October to April, it's the wetter season: 85-95% rainfall. From May to September, it's the relatively drier season: 82-95% (Raja Ampat is one of the wettest regions in Indonesia). Optimal diving visibility October-April (calmer water), but locals prefer June-September for the climate. Highlights / Main islands: Waigeo (the largest, capital Waisai), Misool (in the south, spectacular karst landscapes, Misool Eco Resort 5*+ reference), Salawati, Batanta, Kri Island (one of the main diving bases, world record 374 fish species identified on a single site Cape Kri by Gerald Allen 2012), Wayag (in the north, iconic landscape of Raja Ampat = panorama from the summit over dozens of emerald karst peaks in turquoise sea, the postcard image of the archipelago, difficult access by boat 4-6h from Waisai), Pianemo (more accessible alternative to Wayag, similar panorama), Arborek (traditional village on stilts), Pulau Gam (birds of paradise = endemic), Birds of Paradise Wilson's and Red Bird of Paradise (early morning observation 4am in the jungle of Waigeo and Gam). Diving: Raja Ampat is top 1 worldwide for many divers and operators: cult sites Cape Kri, Sardine Reef, Manta Sandy, Blue Magic, Mike's Point, sometimes strong currents (intermediate-advanced level diving required), liveaboard 7-12 days = ideal formula (1500-5000€/person all inclusive from Sorong). Snorkelling exceptional too (visibility 20-30m, multi-coloured corals), accessible to non-divers from homestays on Kri or Mansuar. Accommodation: Misool Eco Resort 5*+ (1500-2500€/night), Papua Diving (Sorido Bay, Kri Eco Resort), Balinese homestays on Kri/Arborek (50-80€/night full board). Audience: advanced divers, underwater photographers, well-off ecotourists. Try ikan bakar, Papuan sago (local starch), sambal sagu. Logistics: Sorong airport (SOQ) at the western end of Papua, flights from Jakarta 4h via Makassar or Manado, then public ferry from Sorong port to Waisai 2h (135,000 IDR ≈ 8€) or private speedboat.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
