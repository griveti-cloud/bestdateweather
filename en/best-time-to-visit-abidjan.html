Founder · BestDateWeather

Abidjan, economic capital of Ivory Coast on the Gulf of Guinea coast: 5 million inhabitants in urban area (the 2nd most populated in francophone West Africa after Lagos). Former chief town of French West Africa (AOF) and historic capital until 1983 when Houphouët-Boigny transferred the political seat to Yamoussoukro his native village (but Abidjan remains the de facto economic capital, with 90% of the country's economic activity, nicknamed "the Pearl of the Lagoons" then "Africa's Manhattan" for its skyscraper skyline rare in sub-Saharan Africa outside Johannesburg). August is my favourite month (7.5/10): 23-27°C, 51% rain, this is the relative dry season. Abidjan never tops 7.5/10 (humid equatorial tropical climate hot year-round). From November to February, it's the dry season: 23-32°C, more bearable climate, harmattan Saharan atmosphere December-January. From May to July, it's the great rainy season: 70-80% rainfall. From October, it's the small rainy season. Abidjan highlights: Plateau (business centre with skyscrapers from the 1970s-2000s, CGRAE Tower, Postel Tower 2001 21 storeys), Saint Paul Cathedral (1985, modern architecture by Aldo Spirito spectacular, figurative stained glass representing African evangelisation, one of the most impressive cathedrals in Africa), Cocody Quarter (smart residential with embassies, presidential palace), Marcory Quarter (historic French residential), Treichville Quarter (popular, lively traditional markets), Félix Houphouët-Boigny University (Cocody campus, one of the largest in francophone Africa), Treichville Market (the most traditional in Abidjan, pagne fabrics, spices, traditional masks), Museum of Civilisations of Ivory Coast (traditional Senufo, Baoulé, Dan mask artefacts), Ébrié Lagoon (urban lagoon of Abidjan 566 km² with traditional pinasse ferries between quarters), Assinie-Mafia Beach 1h30 away (Ivorian beach resort favourite of Abidjanese on weekends, beach hotels). Ivory Coast excursions: Yamoussoukro 3h away (nominal political capital with Basilica of Our Lady of Peace 1989 = the largest Christian church in the world by ground area, copy inspired by St Peter's Vatican but 1.5x larger, 158m high, capacity 18,000 places, integrally financed by Houphouët-Boigny himself 300 million USD, controversy about appropriateness in poor country, little used as functional church), Grand-Bassam 30 min away (UNESCO 2012, former French colonial capital 1893-1900, preserved colonial architecture, beach), Taï National Park 8h west (UNESCO, one of the last primary forests in West Africa, chimpanzees, endemic pygmy hippopotamuses), Korhogo north ("Senufo capital", traditional textile and mask craftsmanship), Man (the Teeth of Man mountains, spectacular Dan stilt mask tradition). Safety: Abidjan globally safe in the Plateau/Cocody/Marcory quarters, caution Treichville/Adjamé/Yopougon. Try attiéké (cassava couscous, Ivorian national dish) with grilled fish, kedjenou (chicken in earthen pot), foutou (pounded plantain), aloko (fried plantain), bangui (palm wine), bissap. Abidjan Félix Houphouët-Boigny airport (ABJ).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
