Pantelleria, Italian volcanic island between Sicily and Tunisia: 7700 inhabitants over 83 km², closer to Tunisia (60 km) than to Sicily (110 km), considered Italy's most secret island. Nicknamed "the island of water and wind" in Arabic ("bint al-rayh" = daughter of the wind). The Italian elite has its summer residence here (Giorgio Armani, Sting, Madonna have houses here), deliberately preserved atmosphere. June is my favourite month (9.0/10): 21-25°C, 14% rain, sea at 21°C, ideal temperatures. May and September-October stay excellent (8.4-8.8/10). July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (24-30°C, sirocco Saharan wind sometimes 40°C+). From November to March, expect 9-16°C and 26-37% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, but windy (Pantelleria is one of Italy's windiest islands, Saharan scirocco and north-west mistral), many hotels close out of season. Highlights: Lago di Venere ("Lake of Venus", crater volcanic thermal lake, sulphurous hot springs 35-40°C, therapeutic mud to apply on the body, iconic of Pantelleria, free swimming), Specchio di Venere ("Mirror of Venus", another purer Venusian lake), Arco dell'Elefante ("Elephant Arch", rocky formation by the sea in the shape of an elephant drinking from the sea, iconic view of Pantelleria), Bagno Asciutto ("Dry Bath", natural volcanic sauna caves 50°C, exit and wrap in a towel), Cala Levante and Cala Tramontana (coves accessible only on foot, crystal-clear water), Faro Punta Spadillo, Cala Cinque Denti ("5 Teeth", iconic rocky formations), Sesi archaeological centre (UNESCO in progress, 7000 prehistoric megalithic burials 1800 BC in volcanic black stone unique in the world), Dammusi ("traditional houses of Pantelleria" = architecture in black volcanic stone with white round domes to collect rainwater, medieval Arab-Berber heritage, architectural iconic unique in the world, many converted into smart boutique hotels). Vineyards: Pantelleria is famous for its Passito di Pantelleria DOC (sweet liqueur wine made from Zibibbo Moscato d'Alessandria dried in the sun on basalt slabs, millennial technique, one of Italy's great sweet wines), UNESCO 2014 recognises the "vigne couchée pantesca" = viticulture technique in circular bowls dug into the rock to protect the vines from the Saharan wind, classified as intangible heritage of humanity. Pantelleria IGP Capers = among the best in the world (the other rival being Salina in the Aeolians). Activities: diving (crystal-clear water, ancient underwater archaeology), volcanic hiking (Mount Grande 836m, 360° panorama over Tunisia + Lampedusa visible on clear days), boat around the island (one of the only ways to access the wild coves). Hotels: Resort Sikelia, Tenuta Borgia, Khamma (smart boutique hotels in renovated dammusi, 300-1000€/night). Try couscous pantesca (Arab heritage), pesto pantesco (made from tomatoes and capers, different from Genoese pesto), insalata pantesca (potato tomato caper onion salad), Passito di Pantelleria DOC, Zibibbo wine. Logistics: Pantelleria airport (PNL) with flights from Palermo/Trapani/Rome (1h), ferry from Trapani Sicily in 5h.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
