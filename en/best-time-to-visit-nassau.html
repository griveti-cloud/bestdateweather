Nassau, capital of the Bahamas on New Providence Island: 280,000 inhabitants (70% of the country's population lives here). Bahamas archipelago = 700 islands in the tropical Atlantic, 80 km east of Florida. Independent since 1973 (Commonwealth realm), strong British cultural influence + Afro-Bahamian Caribbean. World's leading cruise destination. March is my favourite month (8.7/10): 22-26°C, 27% rain, sea at 25°C, end of the high season. From December to May, it's the tourist high season: 21-28°C, 27-54% rain, hotel prices x2-x3, daily liners (Atlantis Resort, Baha Mar Resort 5*+ with thousands of rooms). From June to November, it's the cyclone season: 54-66% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Dorian devastated Abacos and Grand Bahama in September 2019, 70 deaths, catastrophic damage). Nassau highlights: Bay Street (colonial commercial street, duty-free Cartier/Bulgari/Rolex), Parliament Square (pink colonial buildings), Fort Charlotte (18th century), Pirates of Nassau Museum (Nassau was the HQ of the "pirate golden age" Edward Teach "Blackbeard" early 18th century), John Watling's Distillery (rum), Fish Fry at Arawak Cay (local Bahamian street food), Cable Beach (long beach with hotels), Atlantis Paradise Island (themed resort with aquarium and waterpark). Beach excursions: Pig Beach (Big Major Cay, Exuma, ferry/private plane, beach with viral swimming wild pigs Instagram), Eleuthera and Harbour Island (pink sand unique in the world Pink Sand Beach), Exuma Cays (catamaran cruise), Andros (largest island, blue holes, diving). Try conch fritters/salad/cracked, rock lobster, johnnycake, sky juice (gin coconut cocktail), Kalik beer (Bahamian pride).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
