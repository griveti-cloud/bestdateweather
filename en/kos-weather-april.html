Context

Our verdict

Kos, Greek island in the Dodecanese off the Turkish coast: 33,000 inhabitants over 290 km², birthplace of Hippocrates (ancient doctor, creator of the oath) in the 4th century BC. Renowned for its beaches, its Greco-Roman heritage and its European charter atmosphere (British in the majority). June is my favourite month (9.8/10): 22-27°C, 4% rain, sea at 23°C. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (24-30°C, sometimes meltemi wind) and the crowds (British in the majority, hotel prices x3, congested beaches). September-October stay excellent (8.6-9.4/10). From November to March, expect 11-20°C and 28-42% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, many hotels close. Highlights: Asclepieion of Kos (sanctuary of Asclepius 4th century BC on 3 terraces, place of Hippocrates' medical teaching, ancient school of medicine founded here, 4 km from the port), Old Town of Kos (city centre, 14th-century Knights of Saint John castle, ancient agora, Hippocrates' Tree = 700-year-old plane tree said to have seen Hippocrates teaching under its shade, more legend than fact), Embros Therma (sulphurous hot springs 50°C flowing into the cold sea, free natural swimming, access by 10-min path), Beaches: Tigaki (12 km), Paradise Beach (Bubble Beach, volcanic gas bubbles emerge from the sand), Kefalos (Camel Beach), Mastichari (kitesurf), Nikolas at Plakas (one of the most beautiful beaches of Kos), Lambi (urban), Zia (altitude village for the sunset). Excursions: Bodrum (Turkey) 1h by ferry (former Halicarnassus, Mausoleum of Halicarnassus one of the 7 ancient Wonders), Nisyros (active volcano, neighbouring island), Kalymnos (climbing), Pserimos. Try krasotyri (local cheese), Greek baklava, Kos ouzo. Kos airport (KGS) with charters from Europe.
