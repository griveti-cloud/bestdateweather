Context

Our verdict

San Miguel de Allende, UNESCO-listed, has become the favourite Mexican town of expat Americans (10-15% of the population). At 1900m altitude, near-constant temperate climate year-round. April is my favourite month (9.2/10): 14-29°C, 10% rain, jacarandas in bloom, pink Parroquia silhouetted against blue sky. From June to September, it's the rainy season (40-49% showers), often in late afternoon, refreshing to 14-26°C. The town centres come alive in the evening after the showers. Día de los Muertos late October and Día de la Independencia mid-September are the two cultural peaks. Bookings 4-6 months ahead. The art scene (Fábrica La Aurora), the hot springs around Taboada, and the food tours of Guanajuato cuisine are the classics. Guanajuato 1h by bus complements the stay well. Gentrification has driven prices up: plan 80-150€/night for a boutique hotel.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit San Miguel de Allende?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: April (ski score 2.0/10). For hiking/summer: January (9.4/10, 22.0°C).
Can you ski in San Miguel de Allende in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 1.9/10. Cold temperatures (22°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in San Miguel de Allende in summer?
In January, temperatures average 22.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in San Miguel de Allende?
May is the wettest month with May has the lowest climate score of the year.
