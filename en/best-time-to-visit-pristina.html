Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Pristina, capital of Kosovo: 200,000 inhabitants, young capital (independence 2008, recognised by 100 states including France, contested by Serbia and Russia). Not the prettiest but one of the youngest in Europe (60% of the population under 25). July is my favourite month (9.1/10): 17-28°C, 26% rain, terraces. August stays excellent (9.0/10). From June to September, it's the high season: 13-29°C, dry continental climate. From February to March drop to 1.9-2.0/10 due to humidity, grey sky, low temperatures. From November to March, expect -2/13°C: continental winter cold. Highlights: Bill Clinton Statue ("hero of Kosovo" for having launched the NATO bombings 1999 that ended the conflict, Bill Clinton boulevard), National Library (1982 polarising brutalist architecture, covered with 99 white domes and metal grids, one of the ugliest in the world according to some rankings — therefore to see), Mother Teresa Cathedral (Catholic, in completion, dedicated to the saint of Albanian origin), Newborn Monument (NEWBORN letters repainted each year according to a theme, erected on the day of independence 17 February 2008), Sultan Mehmet Fatih Mosque (15th century, Ottoman), Bazaar (Old Bazaar). Excursions: Prizren 1h30 away (the "real" cultural capital of Kosovo, charming Ottoman old town), Visoki Dečani Monastery (UNESCO, Serbian Orthodox monastery protected by KFOR, 14th-century frescoes), Rugovo Canyon near Peja (canyon, skiing in winter). Try burek, ćevapi, flija (pancake cake), raki (plum eau-de-vie). Capital among the cheapest in Europe (beer 1€, meal 5-10€).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
