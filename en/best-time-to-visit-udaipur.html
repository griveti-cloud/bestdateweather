Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Udaipur is the "Venice of Rajasthan": romantic city surrounded by 7 lakes, the most love-laden and elegant of Rajasthan. February is my favourite month (9.4/10): 13-27°C, 1% rain, ideal temperatures, golden light on Lake Pichola. From November to March, expect 10-31°C: tourist high season, optimal climate. From April to June, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 22-37°C (sometimes 42°C), bearable thanks to altitude (598m) but punishing. From July to September, it's the monsoon: 47-72% rainfall, but green landscapes (rare for Rajasthan), full lakes. Highlights: City Palace (the largest palace in Rajasthan, 11-palace complex over 244m long, Mewar dynasty continuous since 728), Lake Pichola (sunset cruise), Lake Palace (white palace floating in the middle of the lake, Taj 5*+ hotel, non-guest access impossible), Jagmandir Island (another island-palace, accessible by boat), Saheliyon-ki-Bari (gardens of the maids of honour), Monsoon Palace (on the Sajjangarh hill, panoramic view at sunset). Eklingji and Nagda Temples 22 km away (millennial). Try dal baati churma, gatte ki sabzi, malpua. Octopussy from James Bond (1983) shot here (Lake Palace in the film). Direct flight from Delhi (1h15) or overnight train from Delhi/Mumbai. October-November and March-April are the real sweet spots tourism/weather.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
