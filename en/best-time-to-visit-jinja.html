Jinja, town in Uganda on the north shore of Lake Victoria 80 km east of Kampala: 75,000 inhabitants. World capital of White Water rafting on the White Nile which exits directly from Lake Victoria at Jinja, considered one of the 3 best world destinations for whitewater rafting with Zambezi Zambia + Futaleufú Chile. Nicknamed "adventure capital of East Africa". Official source of the White Nile where it exits Lake Victoria to begin its 6695 km journey to the Mediterranean Egypt = major worldwide geographic signature. February is my favourite month (8.3/10): 19-26°C, 53% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January-June-July-August-December stay excellent (7.7-8.3/10). Jinja never tops 8.3/10 (mild equatorial altitude climate). From June to August, it's the dry season: 18-25°C, temperate-tropical altitude climate thanks to altitude 1140m + Lake Victoria influence. From March to May and October-November, these are the wet seasons: 65-72% rainfall. Highlights: Source of the White Nile ("Source of the Nile", 1862 monument in a riverside park marking the place where British explorer John Hanning Speke discovered the source of the Nile 1858 = major geographic signature, historical controversy as others have contested that the "real" source is Lake Victoria vs Lake Tana Ethiopia for Blue Nile vs Burundi-Rwanda for the southernmost sources of the White Nile, boat cruise to the exact point of the "exit" of the Nile), Bujagali Falls (former signature waterfalls of Jinja, unfortunately drowned by the construction of the Bujagali Dam 2012 = controversial hydroelectric project that destroyed the signature natural site of Uganda, memorial loss), Itanda Falls (more modest replacement waterfalls), Owen Falls Dam (1954, first dam on the Nile, hydroelectric). Signature activities: Class 5 White Water rafting on the Nile = world signature of Ugandan adventure tourism (Adrift Adventure + Nile River Explorers + Nalubale Rafting main operators, 1-day expeditions 80-100 USD = considered the best world value for Class 5 rafting, signature experience among the most extreme in the world, waves 5m+ and rapids named "Surf City" + "Nile Special" + "Itanda Falls"), kayaking (Jinja hosts the annual Nile River Festival = international freestyle kayak competition), bungee jumping (44m above the Nile), jet boat, quad biking, horseback riding on the Nile, Lake Victoria boat tours. Uganda excursions: Sipi Falls 4h away (spectacular Mount Elgon waterfalls), Mount Elgon NP 5h away (dormant volcano 4321m), Murchison Falls NP 6h away (the most beautiful waterfall in Africa according to rankings, the Nile crosses a 7m wide fissure), Queen Elizabeth NP 8h away (Big Four except rhinoceros), Bwindi Impenetrable NP 12h away (mountain gorilla tracking major tourist signature of Uganda), Entebbe 2h away (already covered), Kampala 1h30 away (capital, already covered by BDW). Safety: Jinja globally safe, relaxed atmosphere of cosmopolitan adventure capital, moderate tourist crime. Try matoke (boiled mashed plantain Ugandan national dish), grilled tilapia + Nile perch, rolex (chapati + omelette signature Ugandan street food), Lake Victoria smokey fish, Ugandan Bugisu Arabica coffee top world, Nile Special Beer signature local. Logistics: Jinja 1h30 from Entebbe Airport (EBB) + 1h30 from Kampala by car, direct buses from Kampala 4 USD.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
