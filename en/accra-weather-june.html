Context

Our verdict

Accra, capital of Ghana on the Gulf of Guinea coast: 2.5 million inhabitants intra-muros, 4 million in urban area. Ghana = first British colony in sub-Saharan Africa to obtain independence (6 March 1957 under Kwame Nkrumah, influential pan-African leader). Today considered one of the most stable democracies in West Africa, rapidly growing economy (2nd world cocoa producer after Ivory Coast), West African cultural capital. August is my favourite month (8.1/10): 24-27°C, 18% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From July to February, it's the relative dry season: 23-31°C with short "little dry season" in July-August in the heart of the south-west monsoon. From March to June and September-October, expect 23-32°C with 28-79% rain: rainy season ("major rainy season" May-June, "minor rainy season" September-October). From December to February, harmattan wind = dry dusty Saharan wind that refreshes but reduces visibility. Accra highlights: Independence Square ("Black Star Square", 3rd largest public square in the world after Tiananmen and Red Square, with independence arch and pan-African iconic black star), Kwame Nkrumah Memorial Park (mausoleum of the father of Ghanaian independence, statue, museum in his former house), Jamestown (historic fishing quarter, James fort 17th century British slave trade place, popular traditional atmosphere), Osu Quarter (the liveliest in Accra, restaurants and nightlife, Oxford Street the most famous in West Africa), Makola Market (the largest traditional market in Accra, West African atmosphere, kente fabrics, spices), National Museum of Ghana (Ashanti and other Ghanaian ethnic group traditional artefacts), W.E.B. Du Bois Memorial Centre (the African-American intellectual father of Pan-Africanism lived and is buried here), Labadi Beach (the urban beach of Accra, lively at the weekend), Bojo Beach 30 min away (more peaceful beach). Essential Ghana excursions: Cape Coast and Elmina Castles 3h away (UNESCO 1979, former British and Dutch slave fortresses 15th-19th century = the "Door of No Return" through which millions of slaves were embarked towards the Americas between 1500-1850, overwhelming memorial place frequented by African-American descendants in roots pilgrimage, Barack Obama gave a memorable speech here 2009), Kakum National Park 3h away (jungle canopy accessible by Canopy Walkway = suspended walkways 350m at 30m high between the trees, one of the longest canopy walkways in the world), Mole National Park in the north (safari park with elephants observable on foot = unique in Africa), Lake Volta (the largest artificial lake in the world by surface area 8500 km², created Akosombo dam 1965). Try jollof rice (the most famous West African dish, rice in spicy tomato sauce, culinary war between Ghana/Senegal/Nigeria for paternity), banku and tilapia, fufu (kneaded cassava/yam ball), kelewele (fried spicy plantain), waakye (rice beans), bissap (hibiscus juice). Kotoka Accra airport (ACC).
