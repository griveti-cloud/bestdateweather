Founder · BestDateWeather

The Galápagos is the Ecuadorian archipelago 1000 km from the mainland: 13 main islands, 200 endemic species (Darwin drew his theory of evolution here in 1835). January is my favourite month (7.8/10): 22-26°C, 68% rain in brief showers, warmer sea (24°C). From December to May, it's the hot/wet season: often sunny sky between showers, calm and warm sea (26°C), ideal for diving. From June to November, it's the dry/cold season: Humboldt Current bringing the cool (18-23°C), frequent "garúa" mist, rough cold sea (18-22°C, useful wetsuit for diving). Penguin and albatross season (Española). Logistics: flight Quito or Guayaquil → Baltra or San Cristóbal (2h, 350-500$ return). National park entrance fee 200$ + transit card 20$. Cruises 4-8 days (1500-5000$ per person) or land-based stay (Santa Cruz/Puerto Ayora or San Cristóbal/Baquerizo Moreno) with day trips (200-400$/person). Wildlife: marine iguanas, giant tortoises (Charles Darwin Centre), sea lions, blue-footed boobies, manta rays, hammerhead sharks. Sites: Bartolomé (Pinnacle Rock), Floreana (Post Office Bay correspondence), Española (albatrosses April-December), Genovesa (birds). Controlled tourism: 240,000 visitors/year max.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
