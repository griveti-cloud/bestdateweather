G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Etosha is the reference safari park in Namibia: 22,000 km² around an immense dried-up salt lake that becomes blinding white. May is my favourite month (9.4/10): 15-29°C, 1% rain, animals concentrated around the permanent watering holes (Okaukuejo, Halali, Namutoni). The whole dry season from April to August is excellent for safari: maximum visibility, animals predictable at the waterholes. The nights at Okaukuejo waterhole (illuminated until midnight) remain a rare experience: elephants, black rhinos, lions parading at 30m. From September to December, expect 18-36°C: the heat becomes crushing in midday (3 safari outings: dawn, end of day, night). From January to March (rainy season), animals dispersed, some tracks impassable, but green photogenic landscapes and baby season. Accommodation: 3 NWR camps inside the park (to book 6 months ahead in high season) or private lodges on the periphery.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
