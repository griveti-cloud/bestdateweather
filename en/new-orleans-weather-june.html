Context

Our verdict

New Orleans is one of the most singular American cities: French/Spanish/African/Creole heritage, jazz born here (Louis Armstrong), Cajun cuisine, Mardi Gras. October is my favourite month (8.6/10): 20-27°C, 28% rain, end of the stifling heat, festivals (Voodoo Festival late October). From June to September, expect 24-31°C: hot, humid, real hurricane risk (Katrina 2005, Ida 2021). From December to February, expect 8-20°C: mild, ideal for visiting without crowds. Mardi Gras (variable date February-March, 2 weeks of festivities, peak on the Tuesday before Lent) = absolute tourist peak, prices x4-x5, massive crowds on Bourbon Street. Highlights: French Quarter (Vieux Carré), Jackson Square and Saint Louis Cathedral, Bourbon Street (touristy), Frenchmen Street (local jazz, frequented by residents), Garden District (antebellum houses, Anne Rice's tomb), St Louis Cemetery No. 1 (Marie Laveau's tomb, voodoo). Try beignets at Café du Monde, gumbo, jambalaya, po'boy at Domilise's, étouffée. Plantation tours 1h away (Oak Alley, Whitney Plantation for the enslaved-people perspective).
