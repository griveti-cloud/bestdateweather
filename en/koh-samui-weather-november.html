Context

Our verdict

Koh Samui, Thailand's 2nd biggest island (228 km²) in the Gulf of Thailand: 60,000 inhabitants, more upmarket than Phuket, family beaches, base for Koh Phangan (Full Moon Party) and Koh Tao (diving). From January to August (6.6-6.9/10): 24-31°C, 35-67% rain. Koh Samui never tops 7/10 (constant equatorial climate). From October to December, it's the rainy season: 63-88% rainfall (Koh Samui has its own rainy season inverted from Phuket thanks to its position in the Gulf of Thailand). From February to May, it's the driest season: 35-49% rainfall, sea at 28°C. Beaches: Chaweng (the most famous, lively, hotels, nightlife), Lamai (2nd liveliest, quieter alternative), Bophut (charming Fisherman's Village on Friday evenings), Maenam (family-friendly), Choeng Mon (quiet, upmarket). Highlights: Big Buddha (12m statue on a small island linked to the north), Wat Plai Laem (colourful Chinese temple), Na Muang Waterfalls, Hin Ta Hin Yai (suggestive "grandfather/grandmother" rocks). Excursions: Ang Thong Marine Park (42 islands, snorkelling, kayak), Koh Phangan 30 min by ferry (Full Moon Party at Haad Rin Beach on full-moon nights, more paradise beaches than Samui), Koh Tao 1h30 (the world's cheapest diving, over 50 sites). Try tom kha gai, seafood.
