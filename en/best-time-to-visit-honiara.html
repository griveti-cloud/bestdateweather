Honiara, capital of the Solomon Islands on the island of Guadalcanal in the south-west Pacific: 85,000 inhabitants. Melanesian archipelagic country of 992 islands, 700,000 inhabitants in total, independent of the United Kingdom since 1978, Commonwealth member. Site of the bloody Battle of Guadalcanal August 1942-February 1943 = first major US ground offensive against the Japanese in the Pacific = turning point of the Pacific War with 7000 US deaths + 31,000 Japanese in 6 months of jungle-sea fighting, US code name "Operation Watchtower". July-August are my favourite months (7.8/10): 24-29°C, 54-59% rain, this is the ideal dry season. May-October stay excellent (7.6-7.8/10). From May to October, it's the relative dry season "Tradewinds" south-east trade winds: 24-29°C, sea at 28°C, this is the tourist high season. From November to April, it's the wet season: 80-88% rainfall, sea sometimes rough. Highlights: Battle of Guadalcanal Memorials (American and Japanese memorials, Mt Austen, Henderson Field = current airport built by Japanese and captured by marines in August 1942 = central stake of the battle, Bloody Ridge = hill where one of the decisive battles of September 1942 took place), Vilu War Museum 30 min from Honiara (private open-air museum with Japanese and US plane wrecks, rusty cannons, overwhelming memorial atmosphere), Iron Bottom Sound ("iron bottom" = sea arm between Guadalcanal and Florida Islands where 50+ American and Japanese ships were sunk in 1942-43 = top world for WWII wreck diving, frequent reef sharks, technical diving), Honiara Central Market (traditional Melanesian atmosphere, fruits-vegetables-fish, blond-redhead naturally curly-haired people endemic to the Solomon Islands = unique world genetic phenomenon of the south-west Pacific Melanesians), National Museum of Solomon Islands, Centrale Hill (panorama over Honiara). Excursions: Florida Islands 1h by boat (Iron Bottom Sound diving), Marovo Lagoon in Western Province (UNESCO Tentative, the largest insular lagoon in the world 700 km² protected by double coral reef, kayaking between islets, preserved traditional Melanesian communities), Rennell Island (UNESCO 1998 = the largest raised coral island in the world, unique endemic biodiversity, Lake Tegano = the largest insular lake in the south Pacific), Tetepare Island (the largest uninhabited island in the south Pacific, community ecotourism), Tikopia and Anuta (very traditional Polynesian outlier islands at the extreme east of the country, populations preserving ancestral Polynesian culture). Safety: Honiara has experienced ethnic riots in 2006 (Chinese quarter looted) and November 2021 (Chinese quarter looted again, anti-Chinese and anti-Prime Minister Sogavare), political tensions with pro-China diplomatic rapprochement 2019 = Western (USA-Australia) concerns about Chinese militarisation of the south-west Pacific. Try kokoda (raw fish in coconut milk iconic Melanesian), poi (fermented taro), grilled fish, ceremonial Melanesian kava, local SolBrew beer. Logistics: Honiara airport (HIR), flights from Brisbane Australia 3h, Port Moresby Papua, Nadi Fiji, basic tourist infrastructure.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
