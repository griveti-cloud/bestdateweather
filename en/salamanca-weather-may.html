Context

Our verdict

Salamanca, in north-west Spain: 145,000 inhabitants, university city (the oldest in Europe, 1218, Europe's 3rd oldest university after Bologna and Oxford), UNESCO historic centre in golden sandstone ("piedra de Villamayor" that takes a coppery colour at sunset). July is my favourite month (9.5/10): 16-30°C, 6% rain, ideal temperatures thanks to the altitude (800m). August stays excellent (9.3/10). From June to September, it's the high season: 12-30°C, dry and hot continental climate. From October to May, expect 0-20°C: long cool spring (university term lively September-June), snow possible December-February. From December to February, expect 0-13°C and 23-26% rain: dry cold, fireplace atmosphere in the bars, terraces on Plaza Mayor with blankets and heaters. Highlights: Plaza Mayor (18th century, Churrigueresque baroque, one of Spain's most beautiful squares, the social epicentre of Salamantines in the evening), University of Salamanca (Plateresque façade 1529, game: find the "frog on the skull" = lucky charm of the students), Old and New Cathedrals (adjacent, 12th-century Romanesque + 16th-century Gothic, spot the astronaut and the faun with ice cream sculpted in the 20th century during a restoration), Casa de las Conchas (façade decorated with 300 carved 15th-century Saint Jacques shells), Ieronimites, Ponte Romano (1st-century Roman bridge, 26 arches), Convent of San Esteban, Convento de las Dueñas (Plateresque cloister). Try jamón ibérico de bellota (neighbouring Guijuelo region = the most prestigious AOC in Spain), hornazo (savoury ham-chorizo cake), farinato (bread sausage), neighbouring Rueda and Toro wines. 2h30 from Madrid by AVE train.
