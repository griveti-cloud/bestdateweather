Context

Our verdict

Addis Ababa, capital of Ethiopia: 5 million inhabitants at 2355m altitude, one of the highest capitals in the world. Cultural and political capital of Africa (seat of the African Union since its creation in 2002, heir of the OAU founded in Addis in 1963). Ethiopia = only country in Africa never colonised (except briefly by Italy 1936-1941), preserves an Orthodox calendar with 13 months and is in the year 2018 compared to the Gregorian calendar (8-year offset). January is my favourite month (8.7/10): 8-22°C, 5% rain, end of the dry season, clear sky. From November to February, it's the tourist high season: 8-22°C, 2-19% rain, mild and dry climate ideal for visiting the country (notably northern historic route). From July-August drop to 4.6/10 due to the rainy season ("kremt"): 98-100% rainfall, tropical deluges, cut tracks. From October, expect 8-20°C with 19% rain: excellent period for the green highlands post-rains. Highlights: National Museum of Ethiopia ("Lucy" alias Dinkenesh = Australopithecus afarensis skeleton 3.2 million years discovered in 1974 by Donald Johanson in the Awash valley, one of the most complete ever discovered), Holy Trinity Cathedral (Holy Trinity, Ethiopian neo-baroque, tomb of Emperor Haile Selassie died in 1975), Mount Entoto (3200m, former site of Menelik II, panorama, monasteries), Mercato (one of the largest open-air markets in Africa), Red Terror Martyrs Memorial (memorial of the Derg communist regime 1974-1991, about 500,000 deaths), Shola Market, Fendika Cultural Center (traditional azmari music-dance). Excursions: Lalibela (UNESCO, northern historic route, see dedicated review), Aksum (UNESCO, ancient kingdoms), Gondar (UNESCO, see dedicated review), Rift Valley and Lake Tana source of the Blue Nile, Omo Valley (Mursi tribes, Hamer = last "first" peoples of Africa). Try injera (sour spongy flatbread based on teff, base of all meals), wat (spicy sauces), tibs (sautéed meat), kitfo (local steak tartare), Ethiopian coffee (origin of coffee in the world, traditional ceremony mandatory).
