Context

Our verdict

Ouarzazate, town in southern Morocco: 70,000 inhabitants at 1160m altitude at the foot of the High Atlas, nicknamed "the gateway to the desert" and "Moroccan Hollywood" (Atlas Studios = the largest film studios in the world by surface area, 30+ international films shot here including Lawrence of Arabia, Gladiator, Babel, Game of Thrones, Mission Impossible). April is my favourite month (9.6/10): 11-27°C, 4% rain, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. From March to May and October-November, these are the best seasons: 7-28°C, 4-9% rain, dry and sunny climate. From July-August drop to 3.9/10 due to the extreme heat (22-38°C). From December to February, expect 2-18°C: pleasant days, cold nights, snow possible on the Atlas (skiing possible at Oukaïmeden near Marrakech). Highlights: Taourirt Kasbah (18th century, former residence of the Glaoui Berber pasha who ruled southern Morocco late 19th-early 20th with the approval of the French protectorate, partially restored), Atlas Studios (visit of the permanent sets: Tibet, Ancient Egypt, ancient Jerusalem, etc., in partnership with CLA Studios), Cinema Museum, Place Al Mouahidine. Excursions: Aït Benhaddou 30 km away (UNESCO 1987, spectacular fortified ksar 1000 years in red pisé, one of the most famous in Morocco and filming location of hundreds of films, to see at sunset), Drâa Valley in the south ("palm valley" 200 km of palm groves bordering the Drâa river, largest oasis in Morocco), Roses Valley at Kalaat M'Gouna (May rose festival = Moroccan perfumery), Dadès Valley with its spectacular gorges, Todgha Valley (300m high gorges, climbing), Tinghir, Telouet (Glaoui kasbah), Erg Chebbi/Merzouga 4-5h away (desert, see dedicated review). Logistics: Ouarzazate 4h from Marrakech by the panoramic road of the Tichka pass 2260m (one of the most beautiful roads in Morocco), regional airport. Try Berber tagine, couscous, mehrouss (local pastry).
