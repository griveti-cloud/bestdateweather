Context

Our verdict

Djerba, Tunisian island in the Gulf of Gabès (south-east): 165,000 inhabitants over 514 km², legendary island of the "land of the Lotus-eaters" mentioned in Homer's Odyssey (Odysseus reportedly stopped here, his sailors having tasted the lotus forgot their mission). Old Sephardic Jewish community (1st Ghriba synagogue mentioned 6th century BC, one of the world's oldest still-active synagogues). May is my favourite month (9.3/10): 18-26°C, 7% rain, sea at 22°C, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. From April to June and September-October, these are the best seasons: 13-28°C, 7-11% rain, ideal dry climate. From July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to heat (24-33°C sometimes 40°C+), and this is the tourist high season. From November to March, expect 10-22°C: mild, European snowbirds wintering (Djerba receives many Belgians, Germans, Italians in winter). Highlights: Houmt Souk ("neighbourhood market", capital and port of the island, picturesque medina, Bordj el-Kebir fort 16th century), Ghriba Synagogue (at Erriadh, legend has it that its founding stone comes from Solomon's destroyed temple in Jerusalem; pilgrimage of Lag Ba'omer 33 days after Pesach by the Sephardic Tunisian Jewish diaspora), Djerbahood (at Erriadh, 2014 street art project = 250 international murals on the white walls of the Berber village, become a major tourist attraction), Traditional Heritage Museum (Guellala), Lalla Hadria Museum (Islamic art collections). Beaches: Sidi Mahres Beach (northern tourist zone with hotels), Pink Flamingos Beach, Seguia Beach (wilder). Activities: camel ride on the beach at sunset, thalassotherapy (Djerba has a major balneo-thermal industry), pink flamingos island. Try Djerban fish couscous, brik, ojja, harissa, Djerban goat cheese.
