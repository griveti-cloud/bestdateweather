Context

Our verdict

Dresden, capital of Saxony in Germany: 560,000 inhabitants on the edge of the Elbe. Nicknamed the "Florence of the Elbe" ("Elbflorenz") for its exceptional baroque architecture, former capital of the Kingdom of Saxony (Wettin dynasty 13th century-1918). Massively bombed by the Allies from 13 to 15 February 1945 at the end of the war (35,000 deaths, 90% of the old town razed, controversy over the necessity of the bombing still debated today), patient reconstruction over 50 years, Frauenkirche rebuilt last in 2005. July is my favourite month (8.6/10): 15-25°C, 36% rain, ideal temperatures. From June to August are the tourist high season: 14-25°C. From November to March, expect -1/9°C and 36-46% rain: continental cold, possible snow, Striezelmarkt Christmas market (the oldest in Germany since 1434). Highlights: Frauenkirche ("Notre-Dame de Dresden", baroque 18th century, 96m stone dome considered one of the most beautiful in Europe, destroyed 1945 and left in ruins for 50 years as a memorial, rebuilt identically 1994-2005 thanks to international donations, some original blackened stones are incorporated into the rebuilt façade), Zwinger (baroque palace 1709-1728, one of the baroque masterpieces of Germany, houses the Gemäldegalerie Alte Meister with the Sistine Madonna by Raphael 1512 = one of the best-known works of the Renaissance), Semperoper (neo-Renaissance opera house 1841-1878 by Gottfried Semper, one of the most prestigious in Germany), Residenzschloss (royal palace of the Wettin, houses the Grünes Gewölbe = "Green Vaults" = one of the largest collections of court treasures in Europe, 4000 precious objects), Brühlsche Terrasse ("balcony of Europe" = elevated promenade by the Elbe), Fürstenzug ("Procession of Princes", 102m fresco in Meissen porcelain 1907 showing 35 Saxon rulers, miraculously spared by the 1945 bombings), Cathedral of the Holy Trinity (Hofkirche, former Catholic church of the Saxon court), Neustadt ("new town" on the north bank, alternative quarter Kunsthofpassage, bohemian atmosphere). Excursions: Saxon Switzerland 30 min away (Sächsische Schweiz, national park with the famous Bastei Bridge rock formations, spectacular hikes), Meissen 30 min away (porcelain town, first European manufactory 1710), Berlin 2h by train, Prague 2h by bus. Try Sauerbraten (marinated roast), Kartoffelsuppe (potato soup), Eierschecke (three-storey Saxon cake), Christstollen (PGI Christmas cake from Dresden since the 15th century).
