Context

Our verdict

Bishkek, capital of Kyrgyzstan: 1 million inhabitants at 800m altitude at the foot of the Tian Shan Mountains (5000m visible from the city), former Soviet capital of the Kirghiz Republic (Frunze 1926-1991, return to the historic name Bishkek after the independence from the USSR). Most accessible and bohemian city in Central Asia (free visa for Europeans 60 days, relaxed atmosphere). June-August are my favourite months (8.9-9.3/10): 16-32°C, 15-23% rain, this is the continental summer. From May to September, it's the tourist high season: 12-32°C, dry and sunny ideal climate. From December to February, expect -5/3°C and 23-28% rain: cold continental winter, but skiing possible at neighbouring Karakol and Chimgan. Highlights (Bishkek is above all a base for exploring the country, few monuments in themselves): Ala-Too Square (centre, statue of Manas national epic hero who replaced the statue of Lenin), Osh Bazaar (huge market, Central Asian specialties, dried fruits), History Museum ("Lenin Museum" historically, massive Soviet heritage), Holy Resurrection Cathedral, Panfilov Park, White House (Parliament). Major Kyrgyz excursions: Lake Issyk-Kul 4h away (2nd largest alpine lake in the world after Titicaca, 6200 km², 700m deep, never frozen despite altitude 1600m thanks to its salinity, beaches on the north side at Cholpon-Ata, hiking on the south side at Karakol), Karakol Valley and Lenin Peak (7134m, one of the only "easily" accessible 7000m summits, 3-week ascent with acclimatisation), Khan Tengri Peak (7010m), Kyrgyz nomad yurts (homestay in high jailoo pasture, milking of mares, kymyz fermented milk), Song-Kul (altitude lake 3016m, summer nomadic yurts). Try beshbarmak (national dish based on horse or mutton), laghman (sautéed thick noodles), manty (steamed dumplings), plov (pilaf rice), shashlik (skewers), kymyz, Kyrgyz vodka.
