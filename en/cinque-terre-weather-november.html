Context

Our verdict

Cinque Terre is 5 UNESCO fishing villages clinging to the cliffs of the Ligurian Riviera: Monterosso, Vernazza, Corniglia, Manarola, Riomaggiore. July is my favourite month (9.1/10): 18-26°C, 24% rain, sea at 22°C. August excellent (8.8/10) but overtourism: 3 million visitors/year across 5 villages totalling 4000 inhabitants, the famous Sentiero Azzurro Manarola-Riomaggiore aka "Via dell'Amore" closed since 2012 (reopening underway), many villages limit access. From November to March, expect 3-13°C and 39-48% rain: many hotels and restaurants close, trails sometimes closed for landslides (Sept 2012, Oct 2018 were devastating). May-June and September-October are the real sweet spots. Cinque Terre Express train serving the 5 villages = the most practical solution (5€ per trip or 18.50€ day pass). Hikes: Sentiero Azzurro (50€ Cinque Terre Card permit), higher Sentiero Verde-Azzurro. Try pesto genovese, focaccia, Monterosso anchovies, sciacchetrà (straw wine). La Spezia 10 min by train for cheaper, quieter hotels.
