Context

Our verdict

Alcúdia, town-resort in the north-east of Mallorca in the Spanish Balearics: 21,000 inhabitants 50 km from Palma. Unique combination of a Roman and medieval fortified old town with its 1.4 km of preserved medieval ramparts, and a 12 km beach at one of the longest beaches in Europe at Port d'Alcúdia. June is my favourite month (9.7/10): 20-28°C, 14% rain, sea at 21°C, ideal temperatures before the summer heatwave. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (22-31°C) and the extreme crowds (Germans in the majority, hotel prices x3-x4, 12 km beach congested but remains workable as very long). September-October stay excellent (8.4-8.6/10). From November to March, expect 8-19°C and 21-30% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, ideal for visiting without crowds (golf and beach resort hotels often closed). Highlights: Old town of Alcúdia ("Casc Antic" = Old City, fortified by 26 towers and 1.4 km of largely intact 14th-century medieval ramparts, accessible on foot on the ramparts, preserved Mediterranean atmosphere, maze of paved alleys with cafés-restaurants), Pollentia (vestiges of the Roman city founded 123 BC, one of the oldest towns in Mallorca still inhabited, 1st-century Roman theatre, archaeological excavations south of the ramparts), Port d'Alcúdia (modern marina with 750 mooring places, marina + ferry to Ciutadella Menorca), Alcúdia Beach (12 km of fine white sand facing north-east of Mallorca, protected dunes, European Blue Flag since 1996), Muro Beach (south-west extension of Alcúdia beach, wider white sand, naturist on some sections), Cap Pinar (to the north, wild peninsula, hikes towards Mirador de la Victòria panorama over Alcúdia bay, Alcanada beach 5*+ golf), Coves de Sant Martí (natural cave where a chapel was dug 14th century), Sant Jaume Church (13th-14th century Gothic in the centre of the Old Town). Excursions: Cap de Formentor 30 min away (extreme north of Mallorca, spectacular 300m cliffs, hairpin road, 1892 lighthouse, Formentor beach), Pollença 15 min away (neighbouring medieval town-village with 365 steps of the Calvari, viewpoint), Sa Calobra 1h away (legendary hairpin road in the Tramuntana mountains), Caves del Drach 1h30 away (at Porto Cristo, one of the largest underground lakes in the world), Soller and Valldemossa 1h30 away (UNESCO Tramuntana). Activities: beach, water sports, cycling (the island is a paradise for cycling tourism with 1200 km of cycle paths and many pros training in Mallorca March-May), Hidropark Aqua Park, golf Alcanada Golf Club 5*+ Robert Trent Jones Jr 2003. Try ensaimada de Mallorca, sobrasada (spicy black Mallorcan pork charcuterie), pa amb oli, tumbet, Binissalem DO wines, Pla i Llevant DO wines. Palma de Mallorca airport (PMI) 50 min by car.
