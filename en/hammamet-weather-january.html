Context

Our verdict

Hammamet, beach resort in Tunisia on Cap Bon: 100,000 inhabitants south-east of Tunis. First modern beach resort in Tunisia (developed in the 1930s by European artistic circles, then 60s-90s tourist explosion). May is my favourite month (9.0/10): 16-25°C, 18% rain, sea at 21°C, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. From April to June and September-October, these are the best seasons: 13-30°C, 17-25% rain. From July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (23-33°C), but it's full European tourist high season (French, Germans, Italians). From November to March, expect 9-21°C and 17-26% rain: mild, half-asleep resort, golf and thalassotherapy open. Highlights: Hammamet Medina (15th century, fortified by the sea, kasbah, maze of white and blue alleys, preserved atmosphere), Hammamet Beach (long fine-sand beach), Yasmine Hammamet (modern hotel zone in the south with reconstituted tourist Medina-village and marina), Carthage Land (theme park on Tunisian history). Excursions: Nabeul 15 min away (Tunisian pottery capital, Friday pottery market), Cap Bon and Kelibia to the north (Byzantine fortress, beach), Korbous (seaside hot springs), Tunis 1h away, Carthage and Sidi Bou Saïd 1h away, Kairouan 1h30 away (UNESCO, 4th holy city of Islam). Resort profile: Hammamet is the destination of cheap all-inclusive packages (350-700€ all-inclusive week flight+hotel depending on 3*-5* category), with pleasant climate May-June and September-October. Try couscous, ojja, brik, harissa, makroudh el louz (date-almond paste cake), mint tea with pine nuts. Direct flight from several European cities to Enfidha (30 min away) or Tunis-Carthage (1h away).
