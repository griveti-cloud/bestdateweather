Context

Our verdict

La Clusaz, in Haute-Savoie: 1900 inhabitants at 1100m, traditional village-resort in the Aravis range (Aiguille de Borderan, Mont Charvin), 30 min from Annecy. Often called "the resort that kept its soul" (wood-and-slate chalet architecture, year-round village life, AOP reblochon dairy). July-August are my favourite months (8.2/10): 12-23°C, 43-47% rain in storms, hiking the Aravis, Lake Annecy 30 min away, leisure base at Les Confins. From December to March, expect -4/6°C and 40-46% precipitation often as snow: prime ski season. Pistes: 132 km La Clusaz + neighbouring Le Grand-Bornand (single ski pass), 84 runs, 1500m vertical drop, altitude 1100-2600m, 4 sectors (Beauregard, L'Etale, L'Aiguille, La Balme — the highest). Off-piste: Combe du Fernuy, Combe de Borderan. La Clusaz is cheaper than Megève and less technical than Tignes/Val d'Isère, ideal for families and beginner-intermediates. Crowd: Lyon and Annecy locals on weekends, French families, few Brits (opposite of Morzine). Hotels: Au Cœur du Village (5*), Beauregard (4*), 150-600€/night. Reblochon AOP). Try tartiflette made with reblochon (its birthplace), reblochon fondue, diots sausages, Savoie wines. Logistics: La Clusaz 30 min from Annecy, 1h from Geneva, accessible by bus from Annecy TGV station.

When is the best time to visit La Clusaz?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: January (ski score 8.7/10). For hiking/summer: January (8.7/10, 4.0°C).
Can you ski in La Clusaz in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.4/10. Cold temperatures (4°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.

In January, temperatures average 4.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
