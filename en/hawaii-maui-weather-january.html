Context

Our verdict

Big Island (officially Hawaiʻi) is the largest and youngest of the archipelago: active volcanoes (Kilauea, Mauna Loa), 11 climate zones on a single island, observatories on Mauna Kea (4205m). July is my favourite month (8.8/10): 20-27°C, 32% rain on the dry west side (Kona/Kohala), much wetter at Hilo on the east (4500mm/year, one of the rainiest cities in the USA). Volcanoes National Park remains the essential stop: unpredictable eruptions, Kilauea has erupted several times since 2020. Mauna Kea at sunset (4205m, plan a down jacket even in July) with public stargazing twice a week. From December to May, expect 15-24°C with 38-56% rain on the east side: humpback whale season offshore. The high tourist season remains December-April (dry season): prices x2-x3. For the diversity (snorkelling at Kealakekua, black/green beaches, national park), plan 5-7 days minimum.
