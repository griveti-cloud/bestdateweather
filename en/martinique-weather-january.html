Context

Our verdict

Martinique, French overseas department in the Lesser Antilles: 1100 km², 360,000 inhabitants, the "island of flowers" (in Creole "Madinina"). January is my favourite month (8.1/10): 23-28°C, 58% rain, this is the carême (dry season), sea at 27°C. From December to May, it's the tourist high season: 22-30°C, hotel prices x2-x3, From July to October, it's the active hurricane season ("hivernage"): 70-81% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Maria 2017 hit). From June and November are the best shoulder seasons. Highlights: Fort-de-France (capital, spice market), Saint-Pierre (former "Little Paris of the Antilles", destroyed by the eruption of Mount Pelée on 8 May 1902 = 30,000 deaths in 1 minute, only 1 survivor Cyparis in his cell, ruins visitable), Mount Pelée (1397m, 6h round-trip hike, still active volcano), Trois-Îlets (birthplace of Empress Josephine), Diamond Rock (Le Diamant, iconic rock to the south), Pointe du Bout (hotel zone), Anses-d'Arlet (iconic beachside church), Plage des Salines (the most famous, south), Tartane (Caravelle, wild coast), Habitations Clément (rum, former plantation), Saint-James (rum). Activities: diving (Diamond Rock, Saint-Pierre), Canal des Esclaves hike, Grenadines cruises south (Saint Lucia 30 min by helicopter). Try cod accras, chicken colombo, Creole boudin, ti-punch, planteur, AOC Martinique rum (the world's only AOC for rum).
