Context

Our verdict

Tarawa, capital of Kiribati on the South Tarawa atoll in the central Pacific: 65,000 inhabitants. Archipelagic micro-state country of 33 atolls scattered over 3.5 million km² of ocean ("largest archipelagic country by marine surface area in the world"), 119,000 inhabitants in total, independent of the United Kingdom since 1979. Country split between 3 different time zones thanks to its dispersion = only country in the world in all hemispheres 24h/24 (equator crosses Kiribati, international meridian 180° also). Very little touristic (3000 visitors/year, among the least visited countries in the world). Country at the highest world risk with Tuvalu of disappearance by sea level rise. March is my favourite month (8.7/10): 27-28°C, 29% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January-February stay excellent (8.3/10). From January to April, it's the dry season "Aumeang": 26-29°C, sea at 28°C. From May to December, it's the wet season "Aumaiaki": 60-94% rainfall. Highlights: Battle of Tarawa November 1943 Memorials (Tarawa = theatre of one of the most violent battles of the Pacific in 76 hours 20-23 November 1943, 1000 US Marines killed + 3000 Japanese out of 4500 defenders, the deadliest amphibious operation of the Pacific War as the marines had underestimated tidal currents and Japanese fortifications, modest memorials scattered on the atoll), Bairiki Beach (capital beach, white sand), South Tarawa and North Tarawa (2 distinct V-shaped parts of the atoll, boat access), Japanese military vestiges (rusty cannons, concrete bunkers still visible in the jungle, ghostly resemblance to Iwo Jima), Maneaba ("community house" traditional Kiribati = large pandanus-covered building for village meetings, iconic Gilbert islands cultural, to see in the outer village of Tarawa). Activities: diving and snorkelling (spectacular living reefs, manta rays, reef sharks, PADI training possible but limited infrastructure), traditional fishing with local fishermen, tour of neighbouring atolls by boat (Abaiang 30 min by lancha), traditional Kiribati dances ("te bino", village performances during ceremonies, legendary Kiribati hospitality). Cultural particularities: Christmas Island (Kiritimati in Kiribati, one of the largest coral islands in the world 388 km², eastern part of Kiribati, world-renowned bonefish fishing, marine bird watching), Banaba (Ocean Island) (phosphate island exploited until exhaustion by British 1900-1979 then abandoned, population transferred to Rabi Fiji, become almost uninhabited island). Safety: peaceful Kiribati, near non-existent crime but great poverty, very basic tourist infrastructure, few accommodations (3-4 hotels-guesthouses on Tarawa). Try tuna sashimi (fresh fish, the main industry of the country = tuna fishing licences sold to foreign fishermen), te kabubu (traditional pandanus porridge), te bero (coconut pancake), grilled fish, coconut milk, te kamaimai (fermented coconut palm syrup). Logistics: Tarawa Bonriki airport (TRW), Fiji Airways flights from Suva 3h or Air Niugini from Brisbane via Honiara, among the most difficult to access in the world, high prices.
