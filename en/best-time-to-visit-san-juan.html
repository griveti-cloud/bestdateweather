Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

San Juan, capital of Puerto Rico on the north-east coast: 320,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 2.3 million in urban area. Puerto Rico = unincorporated US territory since 1898 (passage from Spain to the USA after the Spanish-American war), unique political status: US citizens with US passports but without presidential voting rights or representation in Congress, 4 referendums on independence or statehood since 1967 without change. Unique Caribbean Hispanic atmosphere mixed with American influence. Hard hit by Hurricane Maria in September 2017 (3000 deaths, devastated infrastructure, slow reconstruction). January is my favourite month (8.3/10): 22-28°C, 41% rain, this is the ideal dry season. December-February stay excellent (7.9-8.3/10). From December to April, it's the tourist high season: 22-30°C, sea at 27°C. From June to October, it's the active cyclone season: 51-72% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Puerto Rico is on the Caribbean "Hurricane Alley"). From September, it's the cyclone peak. San Juan highlights: Old San Juan (Old San Juan, Viejo San Juan, UNESCO 1983 Spanish colonial old town, one of the oldest European cities in the New World founded 1521, paved alleys in blue adoquines = cobalt-blue cobblestones imported from Spain 16th-17th, colonial houses with pastel pink/yellow/blue/green façades), Castillo San Felipe del Morro ("El Morro", 16th-18th century Spanish fortress on the promontory defending the entrance to the bay of San Juan, one of the most imposing Spanish fortifications in the Americas, 6 levels, 360° panorama), Castillo San Cristóbal (other 18th-century fortress, larger than El Morro, defended the city on the land side, secret tunnels), Cathedral of San Juan (1521, contains the tomb of the conquistador Juan Ponce de León founder of Puerto Rico), Plaza de Armas and Plaza Colón (lively colonial squares), Paseo de la Princesa (seaside promenade in the ramparts), Condado Quarter (modern seafront with hotels, urban beach, restaurants), Isla Verde Quarter (longer urban beach, tropical atmosphere), Santurce Quarter (street art, local nightlife). Puerto Rico excursions: El Yunque National Forest 1h away (the only tropical forest in the American National Park system, waterfall hiking), Bioluminescent Bay (Mosquito Bay on the island of Vieques = one of the brightest bioluminescent bays in the world, night kayaking with Dinoflagellate micro-organisms that light up at movement), Vieques and Culebra neighbouring islands (beaches among the most beautiful in the Caribbean, Flamenco Beach Culebra = top 5 worldwide, accessible by 1h ferry from Fajardo), Cabo Rojo south-west (spectacular cliffs, pink salt pans), Camuy Caves (3rd largest cave system in the world), Ponce in the south ("Pearl of the South", colonial + Art Deco architecture). Try mofongo (plantain mash, Puerto Rican national dish), arroz con gandules (rice with pigeon peas), lechón asado (spit-roasted pork), pasteles, alcapurrias, tostones, piña colada (cocktail invented in San Juan in 1954), Medalla light beer (national beer), Puerto Rican coffee. Luis Muñoz Marín San Juan airport (SJU), the largest in the Caribbean after the Dominican Republic.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
