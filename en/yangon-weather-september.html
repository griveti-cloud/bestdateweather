Context

Our verdict

Yangon (formerly Rangoon), former capital of Myanmar (Burma): 5 million inhabitants, downgraded from capital status in 2005 when Naypyidaw was created artificially (administrative capital 320 km to the north). Yangon remains the country's economic and cultural capital. Marked by the military coup of February 2021 (dark era, international sanctions, travel advice unfavourable). From November to February are my favourite months (6.9/10): 20-32°C, 5-30% rain, the ideal dry season. Myanmar never tops 6.9/10 (hot tropical climate). From March to May, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 23-38°C (sometimes 42°C), impossible to visit in the middle of the day. From June to October, it's the monsoon: 68-99% rainfall, flooding possible, oppressive humidity. Highlights: Shwedagon Pagoda (Myanmar's spiritual iconic, 99m gilded stupa covered in 60 tonnes of gold and 4500 diamonds at the summit, glittering at sunset from afar, foreign access 5$ + obligation to remove shoes and to cover bare legs/shoulders), Sule Pagoda (in the heart of downtown, cardinal landmark), Bogyoke Aung San Market (Scott Market, British colonial heritage 1926), Colonial quarter (19th-20th century British architecture in shabby-chic decay), Lake Inya and Lake Kandawgyi (public parks), Botataung Pagoda (riverside). Major Myanmar excursions: Bagan 1h flight (3000 stupas and temples on the plain, hot air balloon at sunrise), Mandalay 1h flight, Inle Lake (one-legged Intha fishermen). Important: unstable political situation since the military coup of February 2021. Many countries advise against travel.
