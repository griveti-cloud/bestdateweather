Context

Our verdict

Tofo (Praia do Tofo), small fishing village on the south coast of Mozambique: 5000 inhabitants 22 km east of the town of Inhambane. Diving destination world-renowned for the giant manta rays and whale sharks visible year-round in local waters. November is my favourite month (9.0/10): 23-28°C, 28% rain, sea at 27°C, end of the dry season, ideal temperatures. From June to November, it's the dry season: 18-28°C, 17-30% rainfall, blue sky and refreshing trade winds, this is the best period. From December to April, it's the rainy season: 34-61% rainfall, but stable temperatures (22-29°C), real cyclonic risk (Cyclone Idai 2019 killed 1300 people in Mozambique, Freddy 2023 directly hit Tofo). Major activities: Whale shark diving (Rhincodon typus, the largest fish in the world 12-18m, present year-round at Tofo, one of the only places in the world allowing such frequent encounters - 40-50% of outings), Manta ray diving (Manta alfredi coastal + Manta birostris giant oceanic 5-7m, largest world population of Manta alfredi at cleaning station at Manta Reef and Giant's Castle), Surfing (consistent waves Tofinho Point for advanced surfers, beach break Tofo for intermediates), Big game fishing (sailfish, marlin), traditional dhow boutre excursion at sunset. Beaches: Praia do Tofo (the liveliest, 7 km of white sand), Praia da Rocha, Praia da Barra (7 km north, smarter and privatised by resorts), Closeau. Hotels and lodges: Tofo Mar Sea View Hotel, Mozambeat Motel, Praia da Barra resorts (Barra Lodge, Flamingo Bay), 50-300€/night. Public: international divers, surfers, young backpackers, South Africans on weekends (Tofo 8h by car from Johannesburg). Try Mozambique prawns LM (giant grilled prawns with local piri-piri), peri-peri chicken (Portuguese heritage), matapa (cassoulet cassava-peanut-coconut leaves), 2M beer ("Dois M" national pride), Tipo Tinto rum.
