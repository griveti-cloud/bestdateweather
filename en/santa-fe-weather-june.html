Context

Our verdict

Santa Fe, capital of New Mexico (USA): 85,000 inhabitants at 2200m altitude (the 2nd most elevated state capital in the USA, after Cheyenne Wyoming), founded by the Spanish in 1610 ("La Villa Real de la Santa Fe de San Francisco de Asís"), the oldest state capital in the USA. Uniformly adobe (raw earth) Pueblo Revival architecture imposed by urban regulation since 1957, unique cocktail of Hispanic-Indian-Pueblo influences. June is my favourite month (9.4/10): 14-28°C, 14% rain, ideal temperatures before the summer storms. From May to October, it's the tourist high season: 8-29°C, dry altitude climate. From July to August, expect 15-29°C with 23-28% rain: characteristic afternoon storms ("monsoon season" typical of the US south-west, dangerous lightning). From November to March, expect -6/12°C: dry altitude cold, snow possible (Santa Fe Ski Basin 30 min away, among the highest-altitude ski resorts in the USA, 4000m), Christmas in Santa Fe very celebrated (farolitos paper lanterns on the adobe roofs, iconic view of the town). Highlights: Plaza de Santa Fe (Spanish central square 1610, market with Native American sellers every day under the Palace of the Governors), Palace of the Governors (1610, the oldest continuously used public building in the USA), Saint Francis of Assisi Cathedral (1869, French Romanesque in a sea of adobe), Loretto Chapel ("miraculous" spiral staircase without central support, 1878), Canyon Road (1 km of art galleries, 100+ galleries, one of the highest concentrations of art in the USA), Santa Fe Opera (July-August, open-air hall design Polshek 1998, one of the most prestigious operas in the USA), Georgia O'Keeffe Museum (iconic painter of the American South-West who lived and painted in Abiquiu). Excursions: Taos 1h30 away (UNESCO Taos Pueblo, livable for 1000 years, Taos ski), Bandelier National Monument 1h away (12th-15th century Pueblo cliff dwellings), Carlsbad Caverns 5h away (UNESCO, caves), White Sands 4h away (white gypsum dunes). Santa Fe Indian Market (3rd weekend of August, the largest Native American art market in the world, 1000 artists). Try green chile (iconic New Mexican sauce, on everything — red or green according to preference, Christmas = both), enchiladas, posole, sopaipillas, New Mexico wines.
