Context

Our verdict

Mandalay, Myanmar's 2nd city (Burma): 1.2 million inhabitants, the last royal Burmese capital (1857-1885 before the British conquest). Cultural, religious and artistic centre of the country (60% of Myanmar's Buddhist monks are concentrated here). January is my favourite month (9.3/10): 17-28°C, 6% rain, end of the dry season, ideal temperatures. December stays excellent (9.3/10). From November to February, it's the ideal dry season: 17-30°C, 2-19% rain. From April to September drop to 3.8-5.2/10 due to extreme heatwave (26-38°C, sometimes 45°C in the central dry plain, one of the hottest regions in South-East Asia). From May to October, it's the monsoon: 47-82% rainfall, but Mandalay receives relatively little rain (in a rain-shadow zone). Highlights: Mandalay Royal Palace (vast 4 km² fortified enclosure, teak palace rebuilt after WWII destruction, moats), Mandalay Hill (240m, 1729 covered steps, iconic panorama at sunset over the city and the Irrawaddy river), Kuthodaw Pagoda ("the world's largest book": 729 white stupas each enclosing a slab engraved with the Tipitaka Buddhist canon), Mahamuni Pagoda (Buddha statue covered in gold leaves by the faithful for 200 years, becoming shapeless), Shwenandaw Monastery (entirely in carved teak, the only vestige of the original palace saved from the 1945 bombing), U Bein Bridge at Amarapura (1.2 km, the world's longest teak bridge, enchanting at sunset), Sagaing Hill (600 stupas and monasteries on the hills opposite). Excursions to the former capitals: Inwa (Ava), Sagaing, Mingun (gigantic unfinished pagoda 1790), Bagan 4h by boat or 5h by bus (3000 stupas, hot air balloon). Try mohinga (fish noodle soup), tea leaf salad. Important: politically unstable situation since the military coup of February 2021.
