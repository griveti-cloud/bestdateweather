Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Aruba is part of the ABC islands (Aruba-Bonaire-Curaçao): 32 km long, 110,000 inhabitants, autonomous Dutch dependency, outside the hurricane zone (southern Caribbean). February-March are my favourite months (8.6/10): 25-29°C, 34-38% rain, steady trade winds, sea at 27°C. From September to December, expect 26-32°C with 28-65% rainfall: only period when showers are frequent (but often brief). From December to April, it's the American tourist high season: hotel prices x2-x3. The whole year is viable since it's outside the hurricane zone: a major advantage vs other Caribbean islands. Highlights: Eagle Beach (regularly ranked Caribbean beach #2-3 after Grace Bay), Palm Beach (livelier, hotels along the shore), Arikok National Park (40% of the island, desert landscapes with cacti, rock paintings, Natural Pool, Conchi), Oranjestad (colourful Dutch capital), Baby Beach (calm lagoon in the south), California Lighthouse, Alto Vista Chapel. Activities: kitesurf and windsurf at Fisherman's Hut (steady trade winds), diving (SS Antilla wreck), 4×4 in Arikok. Currency: AWG (florin) but USD accepted everywhere, papiamento (local creole) + Dutch + English + Spanish spoken. Try keshi yena, pastechi, balashi (local beer). Direct flight from Amsterdam (8h) or via Curaçao.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)

Aruba averages 11.6 sunshine hours per day in February. It is the sunniest month with a climate score of 8.9/10.
