When to visit Toronto?

Verdict: In Toronto, July and August clearly stand out. December concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Toronto is Canada's largest city: 6.5 million in the urban area, one of the world's most diverse (50% foreign-born). July-August are my favourite months (8.7-8.8/10): 18-26°C, 32-36% rain, terraces, festivals (Caribana late July, TIFF early September), Toronto islands by ferry for swimming. From December to March, expect -6/3°C and 31-41% precipitation (often snow): cold with lake-effect snow, but the PATH (29 km of underground tunnels) connects all downtown buildings. September-October are my real sweet spots: 8-22°C, Ontario foliage, Niagara 1h30 away. Highlights: CN Tower (553m, EdgeWalk for the brave), Royal Ontario Museum, Distillery District (Victorian brick), St. Lawrence Market, ethnic neighbourhoods: Chinatown, Little Italy, Greektown, Koreatown. Sport: Maple Leafs (NHL), Raptors (NBA), Blue Jays (MLB) at Rogers Centre. Niagara Falls 1h30 away (the Canadian side more impressive), Niagara-on-the-Lake for ice wines, Algonquin Park 3h away for nature.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
