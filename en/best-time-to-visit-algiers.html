Algiers ("El Djazaïr"), capital of Algeria: 3.5 million inhabitants in urban area on the Bay of Algiers (Mediterranean). Nicknamed "Algiers the White" for its lime-whitewashed Haussmannian buildings and casbah, perched in tiers between sea and hills. Limited tourist visit (few Western tourists in Algeria, 2 million/year total against 11 million in neighbouring Morocco). June is my favourite month (9.6/10): 18-27°C, 7% rain, sea at 22°C, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. From May and September-October, these are the best seasons: 15-29°C, 15-22% rain. From July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (22-31°C, occasional sirocco at 40°C+). From November to April, expect 7-20°C and 22-38% rain: mild, more humid. Highlights: Casbah of Algiers (UNESCO 1992, fortified old town 16th-century Ottoman, 3 km², maze of steep tier alleys, 70m vertical drop in 1 km, Dar Aziza and Dar Mustapha Pacha palaces, Ketchaoua Mosque, Casbah suffers from the slow degradation of century-old buildings not renovated since 1962), Notre-Dame d'Afrique (basilica 1872 perched 124m above the sea, iconic panorama, atypical as Catholic in Muslim country and still in operation), Maqam Echahid (Martyrs' Memorial, monument 92m high 1982 commemorating the war of independence 1954-1962), Hamma Botanical Garden (1832 botanical garden, one of the most beautiful in Africa with giant ficus, Tarzan scenes filmed here), Bardo Museum (prehistory, ethnography), MAMA - Museum of Modern and Contemporary Art of Algiers (in former Galerie de France department store 1909). Excursions: Tipaza 1h away (UNESCO, Roman ruins on the seaside, much better preserved than the more famous ones in Morocco/Tunisia), Cherchell, Constantine 4h away ("city of bridges" suspended above the Rhumel gorges). Safety: Algiers north is globally safe, the south of the country (Sahara, Mali-Niger-Libya borders) remains inadvisable for Europeans. Try Algerian couscous (different from Moroccan and Tunisian), chakhchoukha, makroudh, mhajeb, mint tea.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
