When to visit San Francisco?

The decision rests on several criteria. Climate score, tourist crowds and feasible activities.

Verdict: In San Francisco, June and August clearly stand out. December concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

San Francisco plays on the cool of its microclimate: California Current + Pacific = 12-22°C all year. June-September are my favourite months (8.3-8.4/10): 12-19°C, 0-4% rain. Karl the Fog (the characteristic coastal fog) rolls into the bay at the end of the day even in midsummer (Mark Twain: "the coldest winter I ever spent was a summer in San Francisco"). From December to March, expect 8-14°C and 29-36% rain: this is the wet season, often grey skies. Highlights: Golden Gate Bridge (cycle from Sausalito late afternoon), Alcatraz (book 2-3 months ahead in peak season), cable cars on Powell-Hyde, Lombard Street (the most twisting street), Coit Tower, Painted Ladies at Alamo Square, Mission District for the murals. Districts: Mission (Latino), Castro (historic LGBT, parade late June), Haight-Ashbury (60s hippie), North Beach (Italian). Try sourdough at Boudin (since 1849), Mission burrito at La Taqueria, Dungeness crab. Napa and Sonoma Valley 1h30 away for the vineyards.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
