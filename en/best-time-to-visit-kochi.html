Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Kochi (Cochin), port city of the State of Kerala in south-west India on the Malabar coast: 600,000 inhabitants. 1500-year port history trading with Romans, Arabs, Chinese, then successively colonised by Portuguese (1500-1663, Vasco da Gama died here in 1524 and was buried here until transfer to Portugal), Dutch (1663-1795), British (1795-1947). 2000-year Jewish community (Cochin Jews). January-February are my favourite months (6.9/10): 24-31°C, 18-23% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Kochi never tops 6.9/10 (very humid equatorial tropical climate year-round). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 24-31°C, sea at 28°C. From June to October, it's the south-west monsoon: 85-100% rainfall (Kerala is the 1st region to receive the monsoon in India, June = nearly permanent torrential monsoon), many hotels stay open but limited excursions. Highlights: Fort Kochi (historic peninsula, preserved colonial atmosphere blend Portuguese-Dutch-British, ideal for walking/cycling), Chinese fishing nets ("Chinese Fishing Nets" Cheena Vala, cantilever wooden structures brought by Zheng He in the 14th century, still active today, iconic view of Kochi at sunset over the bay), St Francis Church (1503, the oldest European church in India, original burial place of Vasco da Gama 1524), Santa Cruz Basilica (16th-19th century Catholic cathedral), Mattancherry Palace ("Dutch Palace" 16th-century Portuguese expanded by the Dutch, 17th-century Hindu mural frescoes), Paradesi Synagogue at Mattancherry (1568, the oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth, Cochin Jews community today reduced to 5 people after emigration to Israel in 1950), Jew Town (antiques street unique atmosphere), Kashi Art Café (essential café-gallery Princess Street, art-literature scene of Kochi). Essential excursions: Kerala Backwaters at Alleppey 1h30 by train ("Venice of India", 900 km of navigable canals, 1-2 day cruise on a traditional houseboat "kettuvallam" converted, 100-300€/night for 2 people all included), Munnar 4h by bus (Western Ghats mountains, 1500-2700m tea plantations, unique hill landscapes), Periyar National Park (elephant reserve, rare tigers), Marari Beach (quiet beach 1h away). Major festival: Kochi-Muziris Biennale (the largest contemporary art biennial in Asia since 2012, December-March, multiple sites). Try meen moilee (coconut fish curry), karimeen pollichathu (banana-leaf grilled fish), appam (fermented rice pancake), puttu, masala dosa, kerala thali, local Robusta-Arabica coffee. Logistics: Cochin International airport (COK) 30 min away, direct flights from Dubai/Doha/Singapore.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
