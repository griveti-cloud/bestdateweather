Context

Our verdict

Åre, main ski resort of Sweden in Jämtland province in central Scandinavia: 1400 permanent inhabitants (15,000 in tourist season). Largest ski resort in the Nordic countries with 89 pistes and 41 lifts (in competition with Trysil/Hemsedal Norway). Host of several FIS World Cups and the 2007 and 2019 Alpine Ski World Championships. June-July are my favourite months (7.3/10): 8-17°C, 50-54% rain, fresh temperatures ideal for Scandinavian hiking and midnight sun (Åre is far enough north to benefit from long summer days 21h of day at the June solstice, but not the complete midnight sun unlike Finnish Levi). From June to August, it's the summer high season: 6-17°C, fjälls Scandinavian mountain hiking, mountain bike, fishing, kayaking. From December to April, expect -8/4°C and 36-53% precipitation often as snow: ski high season. Pistes: 89 km Åre Skistar (modest vs Alps 600 km, but one of the largest Scandinavian areas), altitude 380-1420m (Mt Åreskutan 1420m), vertical drop 890m. iconic pistes: Åre Olympia (World Cup descent), Susabacken, Hummelliften (highest lift at 1274m). Cultural particularity: Åre is one of the smartest alpine resorts in Scandinavia, more festive atmosphere than Hemsedal Norway or Levi Finland, lively après-ski. Snow Park excellent for freestyle. Off-piste: Åre offers accessible back-country with short skin hiking, wild descents in the fjälls, possibility of heli-ski exceptionally practised in Sweden (rare authorisation in Europe, contrasting with Alps where heli-ski is forbidden in France-Germany but authorised in Italy-Switzerland). Nordic winter activities: dog sledding (husky safari, more accessible than Finnish Lapland from European airports), snowmobile, cross-country skiing (Sweden = national country of langlauf, iconic Swedish Olympic sport), snowshoeing, Northern Lights safari (Åre is at 63°N so right at the southern limit of the Northern Lights observation zone, less reliable conditions than Finnish Lapland 2h further north, observation possible November-March in good conditions only, about 30-40% chance per stay of 5 days), reindeer and moose safari ("elk safari", the moose = Swedish animal iconic, observation early morning by bus). Swedish sauna = cultural institution practised but less traditional than in Finland, more contemporary. Highlights: Tännforsen 30 km away (the largest waterfall in Sweden 32m high 60m wide, magnificent in winter frozen as ice cathedral), Mt Åreskutan (1420m summit accessible by Åre cable car, 360° panorama over the Scandinavian fjälls), Åre Old Church (Åre Gamla Kyrka, 12th century, one of the oldest stone churches in Sweden), Åreälven (Åre river, salmon fishing in summer). Hotels: Copperhill Mountain Lodge (5*+ Swedish design Bjørklid architects 2008), Holiday Club Åre, Hotel Diplomat Åre, 200-1200€/night in high season. Audience: Swedes in the majority (70%), Norwegians, Danes, Finns in the minority, few non-Scandinavian tourists (Germans, British) unlike the Alps. Try renskav (sliced reindeer), gravlax (Swedish marinated salmon), köttbullar (meatballs IKEA-style but artisanal), Swedish moussaka (kålrullar = stuffed cabbage), surströmming (ultra-smelly fermented herring iconic Swedish = extreme culinary experience reserved for the brave), kanelbullar (cinnamon buns), aquavit (Swedish caraway eau-de-vie), Swedish craft beers Mariestad/Norrlands. Logistics: Åre 1h by train from Östersund airport (OSD) or 6h by train from Stockholm-Arlanda, direct European charter flights in season.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Åre?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: April (ski score 9.3/10). For hiking/summer: April (9.3/10, 4.0°C).
Can you ski in Åre in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.1/10. Cold temperatures (-3°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Åre in summer?
In April, temperatures average 4.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Åre?
October is the wettest month with October has the lowest climate score of the year.
