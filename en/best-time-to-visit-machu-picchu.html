G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Machu Picchu, at 2400m altitude, is the world's most famous Inca archaeological site: 6000 visitors/day capped via timed tickets. July is my favourite month (7.9/10): 11-21°C, 34% rain, Peruvian dry season, generally clear skies. From June to August, it's the tourist peak: tickets to book 3-4 months ahead, prices around 50-200$ depending on the route (3 different routes since 2024). From December to March, it's the rainy season: 83-98% rainfall, frequent mist (can block the view, but also makes the site mysterious), Inca Trail closed all February. April-May and September-October are the real sweet spots: reduced crowds, still acceptable weather, prices slightly off-peak. Logistics: Lima-Cusco flight, Cusco-Aguas Calientes train (3h30 by PeruRail Vistadome or Inca Rail Premium, 60-200$ one way), Aguas Calientes-Machu Picchu bus (12$ return, 25 min). Plan a minimum 1 night in Aguas Calientes (Machupicchu Pueblo) for 6am entry. Huayna Picchu or Montaña Machu Picchu = additional climb (separate booking, limited capacity). Local guide mandatory since 2017 (50-150$/group).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
