Rosario, 3rd city of Argentina: 1.3 million inhabitants on the Río Paraná in the province of Santa Fe 300 km north-west of Buenos Aires. Industrial port city often neglected by tourists but rich in history: birthplace of Che Guevara (born 14 June 1928, house transformed into discreet memorial) and Lionel Messi (born 24 June 1987, childhood club Newell's Old Boys = his first team). Subtropical humid climate of the Argentine Pampa. November is my favourite month (9.1/10): 16-27°C, 23% rain, this is the austral spring, everything blooms. From December to March, it's the austral summer: 18-31°C, sometimes heatwave (37°C+), reduced hotel prices as Argentinians leave for the coast (Mar del Plata). From April to May and September-October are the best seasons: 10-27°C. From June to August, it's the austral winter: 7-18°C, 14-20% rain, mild for the southern hemisphere. Highlights: Monumento Histórico Nacional a la Bandera (National Monument to the Flag, 1957, the Argentine flag was created here in 1812 by Manuel Belgrano facing the Paraná, lift to the top for panorama), Costanera (promenade along the Paraná, La Florida river beaches in summer), Parque de la Independencia (the largest urban park in Argentina), Catedral Basílica, Plaza 25 de Mayo (main square), Pasaje Juramento (19th-century pedestrian passage), Museo Castagnino + Macro (modern art in colourful reconverted grain silo), Estadio Marcelo Bielsa (Newell's Old Boys, Messi's childhood club). Excursions: Paraná Islands by boat (delta with fishing villages), Buenos Aires 4h by bus, Córdoba 5h away. Try asado (Argentine grill), choripán, mate, fernet con coca (national drink), helado (Argentine Italian ice cream), milanesa.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
