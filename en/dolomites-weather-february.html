Context

Our verdict

The Dolomites (UNESCO 2009) are the pale limestone of the eastern Italian Alps: high-altitude refuges, well-known via ferrate. July-August are my favourite months (4.8/10, misleading climate score because our data reflects the altitude zones where summer stays cool 12°C). In the valley (Bolzano, Cortina, Selva di Val Gardena), expect 18-25°C in midsummer. From June to September, it's hiking and via ferrata season: Tre Cime di Lavaredo (9 km loop, classic), Lagazuoi (cable car + war tunnels), Seceda (famous panorama). The CAI refuges open June to September, booking required in high season. From December to April, it's ski season: Sella Ronda (26 km ski loop around the Sella massif, 4 valleys), Cortina d'Ampezzo (2026 Olympics), Val Gardena (World Cup). May and October-November are off-seasons: refuges closed, lifts stopped, capricious weather. To avoid unless you have a specific objective. Access from Venice (2h30) or Innsbruck (1h30).
