Context

Our verdict

Zanzibar, Tanzanian archipelago in the Indian Ocean: 2 main islands (Unguja the larger, called by confusion "Zanzibar", and Pemba), 1.3 million inhabitants, Afro-Arab cultural mix (Sultanate of Oman 1698-1856), historic capital of the slave and spice trade. July-August are my favourite months (8.3/10): 22-28°C, 50-51% rain, this is the "dry season" (kusi south-east wind). From June to October, it's the tourist high season: 22-30°C, 50-59% rainfall in brief showers, sea at 26°C. From March to May, it's the long rainy season ("masika"): 67-92% rainfall, many hotels close, rough sea. From November to February, it's the "hot season" (kaskazi north-east wind): 24-32°C, 41-75% rainfall in brief afternoon showers, sea at 28°C, warmer for swimming. Highlights: Stone Town (UNESCO, capital, maze of alleys, characteristic carved wooden doors with 800 doors recorded, House of Wonders, former slave market turned Anglican cathedral memorial Slave Market), Forodhani Gardens (night market every evening, skewers, soups), Spice Tour (30 min away, clove/cardamom/vanilla/cinnamon plantations, Zanzibar remains the world's largest clove exporter). East coast beaches (the most beautiful, but big tides ±3m exposing 1 km of sand at low tide): Paje (kitesurf), Jambiani (quiet), Pingwe. North coast beaches (no tide effect, ideal for swimming): Nungwi (lively), Kendwa (Full Moon Party). Activities: swimming with turtles at Mnemba Atoll, diving on Mnemba Reef, dolphin watching at Kizimkazi. Try Zanzibar pizza (local street food), spiced pilau, octopus curry. Logistics: Zanzibar 1h30 from Dar es Salaam by ferry + 30 min by plane (direct flight Paris or via Doha/Istanbul).
