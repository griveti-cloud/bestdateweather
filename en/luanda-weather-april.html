Context

Our verdict

Luanda, capital of Angola on the Atlantic coast of southern Africa: 8 million inhabitants in urban area. Former Portuguese colony (1575-1975), independence by colonial war then civil war MPLA-UNITA-FNLA for 27 years (1975-2002, one of the longest and deadliest African conflicts of the 20th century). Today Angola = 2nd oil producer in sub-Saharan Africa (after Nigeria), Luanda is paradoxically one of the most expensive cities in the world for expatriates (top 5 worldwide according to Mercer, due to chronic inflation and rare imported goods). June is my favourite month (9.5/10): 18-24°C, 3% rain, this is the ideal cool dry season. From June to September, it's the dry season: 16-27°C, this is the relative tourist high season (but Angola = still little touristic). From January to May, it's the wet season: 21-30°C with 9-26% rain, hotter and more humid. From October to December, expect 21-30°C: transition to wet season. Paradoxical temperate climate: Luanda is on the geographic equator but the cold Benguela current rising from Antarctica along the West African coast cools the climate = Luanda benefits from a unique freshness in tropical zone, "cold desert" hyper-arid and cool type climate. Highlights: San Miguel Fortress (1576, Portuguese colonial architecture, houses the Armed Forces Museum + panorama over Luanda Bay), Luanda Cathedral (16th-century Portuguese Jesuit), N.S. de Nazaré Church (16th century), National Museum of Anthropology (one of the best in Africa on Bantu cultures), National Slavery Museum (30 km south, in the former Portuguese chapel where slaves were baptised before transatlantic deportation to Brazil = Angola = main source of slaves to Brazil 1500-1850, 4 million Angolans deported), Marginal of Luanda (renovated 2012 seaside promenade, luxurious atmosphere contrasting with neighbouring musseques shantytowns), Ilha do Cabo (urban peninsula with beaches, smart seafood restaurants), Roque Santeiro Market (one of the largest informal markets in Africa, chaotic atmosphere, partially dismantled 2010 but persisting). Excursions: Mussulo (peninsula in the south with preserved beaches), Kissama National Park 70 km away (reserve with elephants, antelopes, restocking after civil war), Quedas de Kalandula 4h away (105m, one of the largest waterfalls in Africa, little known), Lubango in the south (Serra da Leba mountains, temperate climate, Cristo Rei statue copy of Rio in large), Benguela (south coast, beaches, former colonial capital). Safety: high urban crime in Luanda (frequent armed robberies, inadvisable to go out alone at night), northern and eastern provinces residual civil war anti-personnel mines. Try moamba de galinha (chicken in palm oil peanut sauce, national dish), funje (cassava flour mash), calulu (dried fish dish), jindungo (spicy sauce), Angolan coffee (one of the best in Africa but little known, Robusta plantations), cerveja Cuca (national beer). Logistics: Luanda Quatro de Fevereiro airport (LAD), mandatory visa for most nationalities, e-visa since 2018.
