Da Lat, altitude city in the central highlands of Vietnam: 230,000 inhabitants at 1500m altitude in Lam Dong province, nicknamed "Little Paris" or "City of Eternal Spring". Founded in 1893 by Alexandre Yersin (Franco-Swiss doctor, discoverer of the plague bacillus) as a hill resort for French colonials fleeing the heat of Saigon. Remarkable French colonial architecture, temperate climate year-round thanks to altitude. February-March are my favourite months (8.7/10): 14-25°C, 18-28% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From November to April, it's the dry season: 14-26°C, permanent spring climate. From May to October, it's the monsoon: 49-95% rainfall, frequent mists (but the landscapes stay spectacular under mist). Permanent climate: Da Lat never exceeds 26°C in average maximum and rarely drops below 14°C in average minimum, this is the major advantage of tropical altitude. Highlights: Hang Nga Crazy House ("Crazy House", organic architecture inspired by Gaudí and Tolkien created by architect Dang Viet Nga since 1990, hotel + tourist attraction), Xuan Huong Lake (central artificial lake, 5 km walk around), Dalat Cathedral (Notre-Dame de Dalat, neo-Gothic 1942 French heritage), Chua Linh Phuoc ("Glass Pagoda", entirely decorated in coloured broken-glass mosaics), Da Lat Market (central market, flowers and vegetables of the highlands: artichokes, strawberries, berries, temperate-climate leafy vegetables unique in Vietnam), Coffee plantations (Da Lat = Vietnamese capital of Robusta coffee and rarer arabica, Vietnam is the 2nd world producer after Brazil), Flower plantations (Da Lat = Vietnam's floral capital, exports to the whole country), Datanla Falls (waterfalls with alpine slide opened 2007), Pongour Falls 50 km away (Vietnam's largest waterfall in the rainy season). Activities: canyoning (Da Lat is the centre of canyoning in Vietnam), Mount Lang Biang hike 2167m (the highest point in the highlands), mountain bike, horse riding. Excursions: Mui Ne 4h by bus (coast), Saigon 6h by bus, Nha Trang 3h away (coast). Try banh trang nuong ("Vietnamese pizza" = grilled rice flatbread topped), banh can, weasel coffee (Vietnamese kopi luwak), Da Lat wines (one of the few Vietnamese vineyards), artichokes in broth. Lien Khuong airport (DLI) 30 min away.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
