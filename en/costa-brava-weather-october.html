Context

Our verdict

Costa Brava, rocky coast in north-eastern Spain in Catalonia: 200 km of coast from Blanes (35 km north of Barcelona) to the French border at Portbou. The "Wild Coast" in Catalan ("brava" = wild), rocky coves between pine forests and traditional white villages. July is my favourite month (9.6/10): 21-28°C, 12% rain, sea at 23°C. August stays excellent (9.3/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 18-28°C, sea at 21-25°C, hotel prices x3, crowds in popular resorts (Lloret de Mar, Blanes, Tossa de Mar = European charter clientele). From November to March, expect 6-16°C and 18-30% rain: mild for northern Spain, many hotels close on the coast (except big cities Roses, Palamós, Begur). Costa Brava highlights: Cadaqués (preserved white fishing village on the Cap de Creus peninsula, former refuge of Salvador Dalí who had his house here at Port Lligat today a museum, bohemian atmosphere, the bay of Cadaqués features in several Dalí paintings), Cap de Creus (extreme east of peninsular Spain, nature park, lunar landscapes), Tossa de Mar (12th-century fortified Vila Vella old town on a promontory, one of the last remaining medieval fortifications on the Spanish coast), Begur (white altitude village, 17th-century castle, modern smart), Calella de Palafrugell and Llafranc (photogenic fishing villages with colourful "barques de mitjana" boats), Aiguablava ("blue water" in Catalan, one of the most beautiful coves on the coast), Sa Tuna, Tamariu (preserved coves), Pals (medieval inland village), Empordà (DO vineyards), Empúries (Roman and Greek remains on the coast), Camí de Ronda (200 km coastal path from Blanes to the French border, spectacular Begur-Sa Riera sections). Dalí sites: Theatre-Museum Dalí at Figueres (UNESCO, retrospective designed by the artist 1974, iconic geodesic dome), Salvador Dalí House at Port Lligat (visit by booking), Castle Gala Dalí at Púbol (offered to his wife Gala). Try suquet de peix (fish casserole), mar i muntanya (chicken with langoustines), butifarra, DO Empordà wines, ratafia Catalan liqueur. Girona airport (GRO) the most practical for Costa Brava.
