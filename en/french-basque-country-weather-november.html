Context

Our verdict

French Basque Country, region in south-west France on the Spanish border on the Bay of Biscay: 300,000 inhabitants concentrated on the coast (Biarritz, Saint-Jean-de-Luz, Hendaye, Bayonne). The Basque Country is divided between 3 provinces on the French side (Labourd, Basse-Navarre, Soule) and 4 provinces on the Spanish side (Biscay, Guipuzcoa, Alava, Navarre). Strong cultural identity (Basque language euskara, dances, cuisine, red-green-white architecture of traditional etxe houses). August is my favourite month (8.5/10): 16-25°C, 35% rain, ideal temperatures. July stays excellent (8.4/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 14-25°C, big crowds (French and Spanish tourists, hotel prices x2-x3). From November to March, expect 4-13°C and 43-53% rain: mild but very rainy (Basque Country = 1500 mm/year, one of the wettest regions of France), unique atmosphere under mist on the Basque cliffs. Highlights coast: Biarritz (smart beach resort since Napoleon III who had Villa Eugénie built here in 1854 for his wife Eugénie, Grande Plage, European capital of surfing, Biarritz Surf Festival, 5* Hôtel du Palais), Saint-Jean-de-Luz (tuna fishing port, old town with Basque half-timbered houses, Place Louis XIV where the king married Marie-Thérèse 1660), Hendaye (extreme south-west of France, 3 km beach, Spanish border via the Bidassoa), Bayonne (Basque cultural capital, fortified Vauban old town, 13th-16th century Gothic cathedral, famous for its chocolate introduced to France by Sephardic Jews fleeing Spain in the 17th century and its Bayonne ham IGP), Anglet between Biarritz and Bayonne. Highlights interior: Espelette (red-white-green village, capital of Espelette pepper AOC since 2000, façades covered in dried peppers in September), Sare and Saint-Jean-Pied-de-Port (stage on the Camino de Santiago, starting point of the Camino Francés), Iraty Forest (the largest beech forest in Western Europe 17,000 hectares, hikes), Rhune mountain (905m, 1924 cog railway from Espelette, panorama over the Atlantic and the Pyrenees). Activities: surf (Biarritz, Anglet, Hossegor 30 min away on the Landes side — European surf capital), Basque pelota (national sport, jai alai at Cesta Punta), Pyrenees hiking, gastronomy. Festivals: Fêtes de Bayonne in July-August (the largest festival in France after the Nice Carnival, 1 million people dressed in white-red in the streets, since 1932 inspired by the Pamplona festivities), Biarritz Festival of surf, Quiksilver Pro France. Try piperade, axoa de veau, ttoro, IGP Bayonne ham, Ossau-Iraty AOC cheese, gâteau basque, Irouléguy AOC red wine, Patxaran (Basque sloe liqueur), txakoli on the Spanish side. Biarritz-Anglet-Bayonne airport (BIQ).
