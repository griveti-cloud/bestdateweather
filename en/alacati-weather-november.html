Context

Our verdict

Alaçatı, smart beach resort of the Çeşme peninsula in south-western Turkey: 9000 inhabitants. Former Greek town of Καλά Ακτή ("beautiful beaches"), deserted after the 1923 population exchange, rediscovered in the 2000s by upper-middle Istanbul classes. Preserved Greek-Ottoman architecture, bohemian-smart atmosphere, capital of windsurfing and kitesurfing in the eastern Mediterranean (constant thermal northerly winds May-September, the bay is sheltered by a peninsula creating an ideal water plan). June is my favourite month (9.7/10): 21-29°C, 7% rain, sea at 22°C, ideal temperatures. September stays excellent (9.6/10). July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (23-32°C, but refreshing thermal winds). From November to March, expect 7-19°C and 14-41% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, many establishments close off-season. Highlights: Old centre (cobblestone streets between Greek-Ottoman stone houses with blue shutters and iconic bougainvillea, unique Mediterranean atmosphere in Turkey, smart restaurants-bars-boutiques, Kemalpaşa Caddesi street is the main shopping street), Hacımemiş Mosque and Pazaryeri Mosque (former Greek churches converted into mosques 1923), Saturday market ("Cumartesi Pazarı", one of the most beautiful fresh markets in Turkey, Aegean fruits and vegetables, ot kınık herbs local specialties), Alaçatı beach (5 km from the old town, shallow bay 1m on 100m = ideal for windsurf and kite beginners, restaurants-cafés on the seafront, nightlife), Iliada Beach, Çark Plajı, Delikli Koy (wild coves to the south of the peninsula). Activities: windsurfing and kitesurfing (the main attraction, international schools: ASPC, Kalipso, Myga; constant thermal winds 18-25 knots daily in season, optimal sessions 11am-7pm, equipment rental 50-80€/day, beginner courses), SUP (paddle), diving (modest). Excursions: Çeşme 8 km away (other beach resort, ferry to Chios Greece 1h), Izmir 1h away, Ephesus 1h30 away. Audience: Istanbulites 30-45 years in the majority, Turkish expats, few Western foreigners (essentially Israelis). Restaurants: Asma Yaprağı (iconic vine décor, refined Mediterranean cuisine), Köfteci Halil (traditional köfte balls), Imren Lokantası. Try kumru sandwich Çeşme, local mastic (neighbouring Chios = world capital of mastic), enginar dolması (stuffed artichokes), Aegean figs, Turkish raki. Izmir airport (ADB) 1h30 away.
