The month in detail

What to expect in May

May is peak dry season in Maputo. The air is warm and humidity more bearable than in the rainy season. Rain risk remains low (14.0% of days), leaving good room for outdoor activities. A good balance between weather and crowds, even if August (9.0/10) remains theoretically better.

Context

Our verdict

Maputo keeps its Portuguese Art Deco character and its tropical Mediterranean side. Humid climate, scorching summer. August-September are my favourite months (9.0/10): 16-27°C, 9-19% rain, constant sunshine. Macaneta beach 30 km away is accessible year-round. From December to February (austral summer), expect 22-30°C with 43-54% rainfall: heavy humidity, cyclone risks (Mozambique in moderate risk zone). Inhambane and the Bazaruto archipelago 6-8h by road are the real cards of Mozambique. The Janeta markets and the Portuguese-Mozambican cuisine (chamuças, peri-peri prawns) remain alive. Safety has improved but remains to be checked at night outside the Polana and Sommerschield quarters. Direct flights from Lisbon facilitate access from Europe.
