Context

Our verdict

El Nido, municipality at the far north of Palawan in the Philippines: 40,000 inhabitants facing the Bacuit archipelago (45 karst islands), considered one of the most beautiful natural settings in the world. Emerald waters, vertical limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, deserted beaches. January is my favourite month (6.9/10): 25-29°C, 47% rain, this is the ideal dry season. El Nido never tops 6.9/10 (hot tropical climate, 8 months of monsoon a year). From November to May, it's the tourist high season: 25-32°C, sea at 28°C. From June to October, it's the south-west monsoon: 73-89% rainfall, rough sea, some island-hopping tours cancelled, many hotels close. Highlights / Island-hopping tours: Tour A = grand classic (Big Lagoon, Small Lagoon, Secret Lagoon, Shimizu Island, Seven Commando Beach), Tour B (Snake Island with serpentine sandbar, Pinagbuyutan Island, Cathedral Cave, Cudugnon Cave, Entalula Beach), Tour C (Hidden Beach, Matinloc Shrine, Secret Beach accessible by crawling through an underwater cave at low tide, Helicopter Island, Star Beach), Tour D (Cadlao Island, Natnat Beach, Bukal Beach, Ipil Beach). Plan 1500-1800 PHP/person (25-30€) with BBQ lunch on the beach, 8h on the water in a bangka. Big Lagoon, turquoise lagoon between 2 cliffs 200m, paddleboard or kayak (200 PHP rental), daily visitor numbers limited. Nacpan Beach 30 min by tricycle (4 km of white sand, one of the world's most beautiful beaches according to Condé Nast), Las Cabanas Beach (the closest to El Nido Town, ideal for sunset), Marimegmeg Beach, Duli Beach (surfers Nov-March). Activities: diving (PADI Open Water around 17,000 PHP ≈ 280€), kayak, paddleboard, climbing on the cliffs, ATV to Nacpan. Nightlife: Trattoria Altrove (pizza), Republika Sunset Bar, fire shows at Las Cabanas. Audience: backpackers in the majority, young 20-35, Asian tourists (Koreans, Chinese) and Europeans (French in particular). Logistics: El Nido airport (ENI) with direct flights from Manila (1h15 by AirSWIFT 250€ return), alternative van from Puerto Princesa (5-6h, 600 PHP) or overnight bus (8h).
