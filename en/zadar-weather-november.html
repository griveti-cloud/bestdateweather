Context

Our verdict

Zadar, town in Croatia in northern Dalmatia: 75,000 inhabitants on the Adriatic coast. Often overlooked in favour of Split or Dubrovnik, but both more traditional and strategically placed to visit Plitvice National Park (1h30) and the Kornati Islands (140-island rocky archipelago). June is my favourite month (9.4/10): 19-27°C, 18% rain, sea at 22°C, golden light on the Riva. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (21-30°C) and the summer crowds (Italians and Germans in the majority, hotel prices x3). From November to March, expect 3-13°C and 30-45% rain: mild for Croatia, many establishments close off-season. Highlights: Old town on peninsula, rich Roman heritage (1st-century Roman Forum, the largest in the Balkans) and Venetian (Clock Tower, Land Gate), Saint Anastasia Cathedral (13th century), Saint Donatus Church (9th-century pre-Romanesque, one of the oldest in Croatia), Sea Organ ("Sea Organ", 2005 acoustic installation by architect Nikola Bašić: 35 tubes under the promenade emit sounds under the effect of the waves, to listen to at sunset), Greeting to the Sun (next to the Sea Organ, 22m disc of solar panels that lights up at night), Riva (seaside promenade, one of the most beautiful sunsets in the world according to Alfred Hitchcock who stayed here in the 60s). Excursions: Plitvice National Park 1h30 away, Krka National Park 1h away (waterfalls, swimming permitted), Kornati Islands by boat (140 islands in a marine national park). Try pasticada (Croatian-style beef), peka, Skradin wines. Direct airport from Paris (Ryanair).
