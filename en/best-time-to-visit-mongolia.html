Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Mongolia, landlocked country between Russia to the north and China to the south: 3.4 million inhabitants over 1,564,116 km² (density among the lowest in the world, 2 inhabitants/km²). Capital Ulaanbaatar, one of the coldest in the world. 30% of the population remains nomadic in yurts (ger), practises extensive livestock farming (sheep, goats, horses, yaks, Bactrian camels). Cradle of the Mongol empire of Genghis Khan (13th century, largest continental empire in history). June-August are my favourite months (8.1-8.5/10): 9-25°C, 31-40% rain, this is the short continental summer. From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 4-25°C, mild climate, green landscapes ("flower season"), Naadam Festival mid-July (3 days of archery + traditional bökh wrestling + 30 km children's horse races, national festival UNESCO intangible heritage 2010). From December to February, expect -23/-9°C and 2-7% rain often as snow: extreme continental winter (sometimes -40°C, -50°C in remote areas, blizzard, dzud climate catastrophes killing millions of livestock). Regions to know: Gobi Desert in the south (1.3 million km², 2-humped Bactrian camels, Khongor sand dunes 100m, Yolyn Am icy gorge oasis even in summer, dinosaur fossils discovered by Roy Chapman Andrews in the 1920s inspired Indiana Jones), Orkhon Valley (UNESCO 2004, historic valley of the Mongol emperors, former capital Karakorum 13th century today ruined), Lake Khövsgöl ("Mother Mongolia" or "Blue Pearl", 2nd largest lake in Mongolia, 2% of world freshwater, pure waters, Tsaatan reindeer-breeding people in the north), Central Mongolian steppes (infinite landscapes, stay with nomadic locals in yurt), Terelj National Park 70 km from Ulaanbaatar ("Easy Mongolia", spectacular landscapes Turtle Rock, giant equestrian statue of Genghis Khan 40m the largest equestrian statue in the world 2008), Erdene Zuu Monastery at Karakorum (the oldest Buddhist monastery in Mongolia 1585), Ulaanbaatar (capital, already covered). Try khorkhog (mutton cooked with hot stones in metal container, traditional nomadic dish), buuz (steam-cooked dumplings, Mongol equivalent of dim sum), khuushuur (fried turnover), aïrag (fermented mare's milk, alcoholic), suutei tsai (salted milk tea), Mongol vodka Chinggis (named after Genghis Khan).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
