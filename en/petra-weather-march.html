What to expect in March

Spring marks the start of the good season in Petra. Temperatures rise (18.0°C) and tourists have not yet arrived en masse. Rain risk remains low (19.0% of days), leaving good room for outdoor activities. A good balance between weather and crowds, even if May (9.4/10) remains theoretically better.

Context

Our verdict

Petra demands you choose your moment carefully: 2200m altitude in the heart of a desert, so cold in winter, scorching in summer. May is my favourite month (9.8/10): 17-28°C, 4% rain, golden light on the pink sandstone. The hike up to the Monastery (Ad Deir, 40 minutes of stair climbing) stays viable. From June to August, the mercury hits 22-32°C from 9h. It becomes essential to leave before 6h for the Treasury (Khazneh) in morning light, and return before noon. Bedouins serve tea everywhere on the site. From December to February, nights drop to 6°C, rare but possible snowfalls can close the site for a few hours. Wadi Rum 2h by road complements Petra well (1-2 nights bivouacking). The "Petra by Night" 3 times a week is touristy but stays a singular moment.
