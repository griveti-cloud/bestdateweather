Context

Our verdict

Chefchaouen (the "blue pearl" of the Moroccan Rif): 40,000 inhabitants at 600m altitude in the northern mountains, medina entirely painted blue (Jewish tradition of the 15th century, popularised by Andalusian refugees). Has become an Instagram phenomenon since 2015. June is my favourite month (9.6/10): 16-28°C, 12% rain, ideal temperatures at altitude (10°C cooler than Marrakech). From May to September, it's the high season: 12-34°C, mild climate thanks to altitude. From July to August, warmer temperatures (19-34°C) but bearable. From December to March, expect 5-16°C and 27-32% rain: cold for Morocco, unheated riads, but medina without crowds (August = tourist peak with 80% domestic tourists). Highlights: Plaza Uta el-Hammam (main square with 15th-century Kasbah and its ethnography museum, starting point for photographing the blue alleys), Plaza el-Makhzen, Grand Mosque (exterior visit only), Souika Quarter (main souk), Petit Tanguer and Bab Onsar (water sources to understand the city's origin), Spanish Mosque on the hill (panoramic view at sunset, 30 min walk). Excursions: Akchour 30 min away (waterfalls, hike to the Bridge of God), Talassemtane Park (cedars and Barbary macaques). Logistics: Chefchaouen accessible by bus from Tangier (3h), Fes (4h), no airport (the closest: Tangier or Tetouan). Try chicken tagines with preserved lemon, local goat cheeses, harira. Cannabis grown in the Rif region (origin of "Chefchaouen hashish", illegal sale but omnipresent).
