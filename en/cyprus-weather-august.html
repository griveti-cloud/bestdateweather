Context

Our verdict

Cyprus, island-country in the north-east of the Eastern Mediterranean between Greece, Turkey, Lebanon, Israel: 1.2 million inhabitants on 9251 km², 3rd largest Mediterranean island after Sicily and Sardinia. Island divided since 1974: 60% in the south = Republic of Cyprus (EU and eurozone member), 35% in the north = Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus (recognised only by Turkey), Green Line crosses the capital Nicosia (the last divided capital in the world after the fall of the Berlin Wall 1989). June is my favourite month (9.7/10): 16-30°C, 4% rain, sea at 22°C, ideal temperatures. May and October stay excellent (8.7-9.0/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 16-32°C. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (24-35°C). From November to March, expect 6-16°C and 18-43% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, snow possible in the Troodos mountains (1952m, skiing possible Mt Olympus December-March), ideal for visiting without crowds. Cyprus South highlights: Nicosia (Lefkosia, divided capital, crossing of the Green Line at the Ledra Street point on foot with passport, 16th-century fortified Venetian old town with 11 bastions, Selimiye Mosque = former Saint Sophia Cathedral 13th-century Gothic on Turkish side), Paphos (see dedicated review), Larnaca (see dedicated review), Limassol (Limasol, dynamic beach resort with smart marina, Carnival), Ayia Napa (festive youth resort), Akamas Peninsula (wild peninsula at the extreme west, Lara beach protected for the nesting of green sea turtles Caretta caretta), Petra tou Romiou ("Aphrodite's Rock", beach with rocks where according to Greek mythology the goddess Aphrodite was born from the foam of the sea, iconic view of Cyprus), Choirokoitia (UNESCO, Neolithic site 7th millennium BC, one of the oldest human dwellings in the Mediterranean), Painted Churches of the Troodos UNESCO 1985 (10 12th-16th century Byzantine churches in the Troodos mountains with exceptional frescoes), Kykkos Monastery (richest monastery in Cyprus 11th century, where a young Tassos Papadopoulos = future president — was a pupil). Cyprus North sites (with Green Line passage): Famagusta (Gazimagusa, fortified Venetian old town, Othello tower where Shakespeare set his play), Salamis (spectacular Greco-Roman vestiges), Karpaz Peninsula (wild extreme east peninsula, wild donkeys, preserved beaches). Try Cypriot meze (assortment of 20-30 traditional small dishes local institution), souvla (skewers), halloumi (Cypriot IGP cheese, to grill), sheftalies (sausages), kleftiko (oven-baked lamb), commandaria (medieval sweet wine, one of the oldest wines still produced in the world, mentioned by Richard the Lionheart 12th century). Larnaca (LCA) and Paphos (PFO) airports.
