Context

Our verdict

Catania, 2nd city of Sicily: 310,000 inhabitants at the foot of the Etna (3357m, the highest active volcano in Europe, in near-permanent eruption). Town rebuilt after the 1693 earthquake (60,000 deaths, 17 towns destroyed in eastern Sicily) in black Etna lava stone and white limestone, giving its characteristic baroque chequered black-white appearance. UNESCO 2002 (Val di Noto). June is my favourite month (9.7/10): 21-29°C, 7% rain, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (24-33°C, frequent sirocco sometimes reaches 45°C in the open air with sand wind). September stays excellent (8.9/10). From November to March, expect 8-20°C and 21-31% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, snow on Etna in winter (skiing possible December-March on 2 stations Etna south and Etna north). Catania highlights: Piazza del Duomo (central baroque square, Fontana dell'Elefante = lava elephant Liotru = symbol of the city, Sant'Agata Cathedral dedicated to the patron saint), Via Etnea (main 3 km street leading towards the Etna, perspectives on the volcano), Roman Theatre (1st century BC in black lava, surprising conservation), Pescheria Quarter (the liveliest, daily fish market in the morning), Castello Ursino (13th century, castle of Frederick II), Bellini Theatre (opera). Mount Etna (30 min by car, access Rifugio Sapienza 1900m south side, cable car towards 2500m, 4x4 jeep towards 2900m south crater, mandatory accompaniment to go to the 3357m summit, possibility of snowshoeing excursion in winter, spectacular view in eruption with night lava flows depending on the activity of the moment). Excursions: Taormina 1h away (already covered by BDW), Syracuse 1h away (UNESCO), Valley of the Temples Agrigento 2h30 away (UNESCO), Aeolian Islands by boat from Milazzo. Try pasta alla Norma (aubergine pasta with salted ricotta, iconic dish of Catania), arancini (fried rice balls, in Catania they are conical against round in Palermo = internal Sicilian war), Sicilian granita (typical breakfast), cannoli, Etna Rosso wines (Nerello Mascalese grape on volcanic soils). Catania-Fontanarossa airport (CTA).
