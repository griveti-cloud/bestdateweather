Mont-Tremblant, Quebec ski resort in the Laurentides: 9000 inhabitants at 200m altitude (Mont-Tremblant summit reaches 875m), 130 km north of Montreal. One of the most famous resorts in eastern Canada and North America, renowned for its pedestrian village rebuilt in 1991 (Intrawest) with very successful pseudo-European architecture. July is my favourite month (8.2/10): 16-25°C, 49% rain, but Mont-Tremblant is above all a winter destination. From June to September, expect 5-25°C: active summer season (36-hole golf Le Géant and Le Diable, hiking, mountain bike, swimming Tremblant lake). From December to March, expect -15/2°C and 41-47% precipitation often as snow (4-5m/season): this is ski high season. Pistes: 102 pistes, 14 lifts, vertical drop 645m (modest compared to the Alps but excellent for North America), 4 sides (South main, North, Soleil, Edge). Characteristic conditions: extreme dry cold (-25°C in January-February, frozen breath on the face within minutes), fresh snow nearly guaranteed (severe continental climate), icy patches typical. Audience: Quebecers in the majority, Northeast Americans (Boston, NY), Europeans seeking a North American experience. Tremblant Pedestrian Village: 50+ restaurants and shops, lively nightlife, resurrected European village atmosphere. Hotels: Fairmont Tremblant (5*), Hôtel Quintessence (5*+ on the lakeside), Westin, 150-800 CAD/night. Try poutine, maple syrup, tourtière, Quebec microbrewery beer (La Diable). Logistics: Mont-Tremblant 1h30 from Montreal by car (highway 15 north then 117), Mont-Tremblant International airport (YTM, seasonal flights).

How we calculate the score

For mountain destinations, we calculate two scores per month — skiing and hiking — and display the higher of the two.

⛷️Ski score DEC → MAY
  • 40% snow cover (slope T° corrected by lapse rate -6.5°C/1000m)
  • 40% winter thermal comfort (-5 to 5°C optimal)
  • 20% sunshine
  • Off-season penalty: ×0.70 adjacent months · ×0.35 outside season
🥾Hiking score JUN → SEP
  • 40% temperature (10–22°C optimal)
  • 40% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 20% sunshine

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Mont-Tremblant?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 8.9/10). For hiking/summer: March (8.9/10, 2.0°C).
Can you ski in Mont-Tremblant in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.8/10. Cold temperatures (-5°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Mont-Tremblant in summer?
In March, temperatures average 2.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Mont-Tremblant?
April is the wettest month with April has the lowest climate score of the year.
