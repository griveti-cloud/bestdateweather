Context

Our verdict

Iguazu, the world's most impressive falls (UNESCO): 275 cascades over 2.7 km, 80m high, Argentina-Brazil border. September is my favourite month (8.8/10): 18-28°C, 28% rain, powerful flow after southern rains, mild temperatures. From June to August is the tourist high season: 15-28°C, 19-31% rain, ideal to visit but massive crowds. From December to February (austral summer), expect 23-32°C and 47-54% rain: stifling heat (tropical humidity), but maximum flow after the rains. Argentine side (Iguazú National Park, 80% of the falls): 3 circuits (Lower 1300m, Upper 1750m, Garganta del Diablo 1100m, ecological train included). Plan a full day. Pueblo Puerto Iguazú as base. Brazilian side (Foz do Iguaçu): a single 1200m panoramic walkway, more spectacular overall view but a briefer experience (3h). Seeing both sides is ideal (plan 3 days minimum). Excursions: Macuco Safari on the Brazilian side (boat that goes under the falls), Gran Aventura on the Argentine side (equivalent), helicopter flight from Brazil (160$, banned on the Argentine side to preserve wildlife). Triple frontier Argentina-Brazil-Paraguay (Ciudad del Este for duty-free shopping). Airports: IGR (Argentina) and IGU (Brazil), 30 min by bus to the parks. Flight from Buenos Aires in 1h30.
