Context

Our verdict

Doha, capital of Qatar on the Persian Gulf: 2.4 million inhabitants (90% expatriates mostly Asian for construction and services, only 10% Qataris). Ultra-rich emirate thanks to oil and especially natural gas (3rd world reserve after Russia and Iran). Host of the 2022 Football World Cup (1st Arab country to organise the event, controversy over working conditions of Asian workers and LGBTQ+ rights). March is my favourite month (8.9/10): 16-26°C, 7% rain, this is the ideal dry season. November-December stay excellent (8.6/10). From November to April, it's the tourist high season: 13-31°C, pleasant dry and warm climate. From May to October drop to 2.2-6.4/10 due to extreme heatwave (25-42°C, sometimes 50°C+, oppressive coastal humidity). Highlights: Souq Waqif (traditional souk rebuilt in 2006 in its 19th-century style, traditional oriental atmosphere with merchants of spices/perfumes/fabrics/falcons — falconry = traditional Qatari sport, Arab restaurants, terraces, Doha's #1 attraction for tourists), Museum of Islamic Art ("MIA", I.M. Pei architecture 2008, one of the architectural masterpieces of the 21st century, exceptional global Islamic art collection, free), National Museum of Qatar (spectacular architecture by Jean Nouvel 2019 inspired by desert rose crystals, panorama of Qatari history), The Pearl-Qatar (4 km² artificial island European Riviera-style with marinas, smart restaurants, luxury boutiques), Katara Cultural Village (cultural centre opened 2010, amphitheatre, mosques, exhibitions), Doha Corniche (7 km seaside promenade with panoramic view over the West Bay skyscraper skyline, best photos at sunset), Doha Skyline (West Bay, iconic skyscrapers by international architects Norman Foster + Jean Nouvel + I.M. Pei + Zaha Hadid), Lusail (futuristic new city created 30 km north of Doha for the 2022 World Cup, Lusail Stadium 80,000 seats site of the Argentina-France final 18 December 2022), Aspire Park and Khalifa International Stadium (World Cup legacy), Inland Sea Khor Al Adaid 1h away (UNESCO under listing, inland sea unique in the world where the sea meets the desert dunes), Qatari desert ("dune bashing" = 4×4 expedition on the dunes iconic of Gulf stays), Camel Race Track (camel races on Friday mornings November-March, camels ridden by robot-jockeys since 2005 after scandal of underpaid Asian child jockeys). Excursions: Bahrain 30 min by plane (other neighbouring island emirate), Dubai 1h by plane. Try machboos (spiced rice Qatari national dish), majboos kebab, harees (spiced porridge for ramadan), shawarma, kunafa, Arabic coffee with mabsali dates, fresh fruit juices (alcohol is strictly controlled in Qatar, sold only in certain 5*+ licensed hotels at prohibitive prices). Safety: Qatar is one of the safest countries in the world, zero tolerance for drugs/alcohol/indecent dress. Hamad International airport (DOH) Qatar Airways hub, one of the world's best airports.
