Perugia, capital of Umbria in central Italy: 165,000 inhabitants at 500m altitude on a hill. Former Etruscan capital then medieval town, rich in Gothic churches and palaces. Made worldwide famous by Perugina chocolate (creator of Baci Perugina 1922 = hazelnut-filled chocolates wrapped in a blue star with a small love quote). University town (Università per Stranieri di Perugia since 1925 = the oldest university for foreigners in Italy). July is my favourite month (9.2/10): 18-31°C, 15% rain, ideal temperatures. June-August stay excellent (9.0/10). From April to October, it's the tourist high season: 6-31°C. From November to March, expect 0-13°C and 30-38% rain: mild for the altitude, few tourists (except festivals like Eurochocolate in October = the most famous chocolate festival in Italy since 1993, 1 million visitors over 9 days). Highlights: Piazza IV Novembre (central medieval square, iconic Fontana Maggiore = Gothic fountain 1278 by Nicola and Giovanni Pisano), Perugia Cathedral (San Lorenzo, 14th-15th century Gothic, houses the "Sacred Ring" = relic of Mary and Joseph's marriage), Palazzo dei Priori (Gothic town hall 13th-14th century, houses the Galleria Nazionale dell'Umbria with collection of Umbrian art: Pietro Vannucci called "Il Perugino" master of Raphael — born in Città della Pieve near Perugia), Collegio del Cambio (15th-century exchange chamber with frescoes by Il Perugino 1496-1500), Rocca Paolina (papal fortress 1540 buried and accessible by escalators — underground quarter unique in the world, razed Baglioni palaces buried), Etruscan Well (3rd century BC Etruscan well 37m deep), Etruscan Arch (Arco Etrusco, 3rd century BC, one of the only Etruscan arches entirely preserved), Augustus Arch. Umbria excursions: Assisi 30 min away (UNESCO, birthplace of Saint Francis and Saint Clare, basilicas with Giotto frescoes), Spoleto 1h away (lyric festival), Orvieto 1h away (magnificent Gothic cathedral on volcanic tuff), Lake Trasimene 30 min away (4th largest lake in Italy), Marmore Falls 1h away (165m Roman artificial waterfall, the highest in Europe), Norcia (Italian capital of charcuterie, ham and black truffle), Civita di Bagnoregio ("dying town", perched eroding village). Major festival: Umbria Jazz in July (one of the largest jazz festivals in Europe since 1973). Try Norcia black truffles, prosciutto di Norcia IGP, Baci Perugina, torta al testo (Umbrian flatbread), Sagrantino di Montefalco DOCG wines, Orvieto Classico white wines.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
