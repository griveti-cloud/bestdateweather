Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Jodhpur, the "Blue City": 1.1 million inhabitants, capital of the Thar desert, dominated by the Mehrangarh fortress perched on a 122m rocky outcrop. February is my favourite month (9.5/10): 14-28°C, 1% rain, ideal temperatures, golden light on the blue Brahmin walls. From November to March, it's the high season: 10-30°C, ideal dry climate. From April to June, it's extreme heatwave: 25-41°C (sometimes 49°C, one of the hottest cities in India), impossible. From July to September, it's the monsoon: 21-48% rainfall (low for India), more bearable temperatures (25-35°C). Highlights: Mehrangarh Fort (UNESCO World Monuments Watch, one of the largest forts in India, 121m on the cliff, 7 gates, extraordinary interior palace with Phool Mahal and Sheesh Mahal, museum), Jaswant Thada (19th-century white marble mausoleum), Umaid Bhawan Palace (Art Deco palace 1929-1943, the last Indian palace built, partially converted into Taj 5*+ hotel and current Maharaja's residence), Clock Tower / Sardar Market (old bazaar, spices and omelettes at Omelette Shop), Brahmpuri (blue old town, labyrinthine alleys). Bishnoi Villages 30 km away (excursion to ecologist rural communities). Try mawa kachori, mirchi vada, makhaniya lassi. October-November and March are the best tourism/weather compromises.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
