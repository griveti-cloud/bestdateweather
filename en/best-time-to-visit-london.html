When to visit London?

The decision rests on several criteria. Climate score, tourist crowds and feasible activities.

Verdict: In London, August and July clearly stand out. December concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

London works almost all year round despite its reputation: July-August are my favourite months (8.2-8.3/10), 14-22°C, 28-34% rain, parks in full swing (Hyde Park, Hampstead Heath), Wimbledon early July, BBC Proms. From December to February, expect 2-10°C and 37-40% rain, days closing at 16h. Paradoxically the best museum season (free, half the visitors): British Museum, Tate Modern, V&A, National Gallery. Christmas markets at Hyde Park Winter Wonderland. May-June and September are the real sweet spots: weather often mild, tourist crowds less dense than in midsummer. The Soho pubs, Borough Market, Albert Hall concerts, West End musicals stay viable all year round. Brexit reminder: passport now required, no ID card.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)

Source : ERA5 · Open-Meteo · 10 years (2016–2025) · Full methodology →
