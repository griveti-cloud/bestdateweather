Context

Our verdict

Laos (Lao People's Democratic Republic), landlocked country in South-East Asia between Vietnam, Cambodia, Thailand, Myanmar, China: 7.5 million inhabitants (the least populated in continental South-East Asia). 65% Theravada Buddhist country, ruled by the Lao Communist Party since 1975. Nicknamed "Vietnam before mass tourism" for its preserved atmosphere and quiet pace. Laos received more bombs during the Vietnam War than the entire Second World War combined (Secret War 1964-1973 = 2 million tonnes of US bombs dropped or 1 air sortie every 8 min for 9 years, 80 million unexploded "bombies" cluster bombs still present today = main cause of accidental mortality in some rural regions). February is my favourite month (9.1/10): 17-32°C, 1% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January-March stay excellent (8.5-9.0/10). From November to February, it's the cool dry season: 14-32°C. From March to April, expect 21-37°C: pre-monsoon heatwave + agricultural slash-and-burn polluting the atmosphere of the north ("Burning Season"). From May to October, it's the monsoon: 39-86% rainfall, the greenest landscapes. Highlights: Luang Prabang (UNESCO 1995, one of the most beautiful towns in South-East Asia, former royal capital of Laos until 1975, preserved Franco-colonial atmosphere mixed with Buddhist architecture, 33 wat temples, morning alms ceremony 5:30am with saffron monks parading in single file to receive offerings from the faithful, already covered by BDW), Vang Vieng 4h south of Luang Prabang (spectacular karst landscapes similar to terrestrial Halong Bay, Nam Song river, tubing = inflatable rubber-ring descent iconic of backpackers since 2000, sometimes excessive and dangerous prohibition of bars on the river since 2012, today wiser and family-friendly), Vientiane (calm and bohemian capital, already covered by BDW), Bolaven Plateau in the south (multiple waterfalls, Bolaven coffee plantations highlands 1200m), 4000 Islands (Si Phan Don, Mekong archipelago Cambodian border, Irrawaddy dolphins, extremely tranquil atmosphere), Plain of Jars at Phonsavan (UNESCO 2019, archaeological plain with 2000 megalithic stone jars 2500-3000 years of unknown origin, still debated theory: probable funeral ossuaries), Wat Phou (UNESCO 2001, pre-Angkorian Khmer sanctuary 5th-8th in the south of the country). Try laap (chopped meat salad, Lao national dish), tam mak hoong (spicy papaya salad), khao niaw (typical Lao hand-eaten sticky rice), or-lam, sai oua sausage, Beer Lao (the national pride, 90% of the national market).
