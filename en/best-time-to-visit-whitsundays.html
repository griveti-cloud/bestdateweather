The Whitsundays are 74 tropical Queensland islands at the heart of the Great Barrier Reef: Whitehaven Beach (98% silica white sand, among the most beautiful beaches in the world), Heart Reef (heart-shaped reef). October is my favourite month (8.8/10): 22-25°C, 23% rain, end of the dry season, calm sea, maximum visibility. From November to April, it's the wet season: 40-66% rainfall, possible cyclones (historic category 5), irukandji jellyfish (stinger suit or netted beach). From May to October, it's the dry season: 23-40% rain, ideal. Major activities: Whitehaven Beach (boat excursion from Airlie Beach, 7 km of white sand, Hill Inlet for the iconic aerial photo of the swirls), Heart Reef (only seen by plane or helicopter, panoramic flights 200-400$), diving and snorkelling (day outings or 3-7 day liveaboard), Hamilton Island and Hayman Island (luxury resorts), Daydream Island. Airlie Beach = base on the continent (animation, artificial lagoon as no swimmable urban beach due to crocs/jellyfish). Catamaran cruises 2-3 days (300-700$) very popular. Try coral trout, mud crab, prawns. Flight from Brisbane to Hamilton Island or Whitsunday Coast (Proserpine).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
