Port-au-Prince, capital of Haiti: 1 million inhabitants (3 million in urban area) on the coast of the Gulf of Gonâve. French-speaking and Creole-speaking country (the most famous Creole country in the world), first independent black country (1804 after slave revolt). Marked by the catastrophic earthquake of 12 January 2010 (200,000 deaths, city destroyed at 80%, slow and incomplete reconstruction) and chronic political instability (assassination of President Moïse in July 2021, gangs controlling 80% of the capital in 2024-2026). Travel advisers: most Western countries formally advise against travel to Haiti since 2022 (disastrous security situation, embassies in partial exit). January is my favourite month (8.7/10): 22-31°C, 14% rain, end of the dry season. From December to March, it's the dry season: 22-32°C, 13-32% rain. From June to August drop to 6.3-6.9/10 due to heat (26-35°C) and the cyclonic season. From July to October, it's the cyclonic season: 23-42% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Matthew 2016, Hurricane Tropical Storm Laura 2020). Highlights (to know theoretically, very restricted access in 2026): Champ de Mars (main square, ruins of the Presidential Palace destroyed in 2010 never rebuilt), Notre-Dame Cathedral (destroyed in 2010, in slow reconstruction), Iron Market (Marché Hyppolite, metallic structure 1891, restored after 2010), National Pantheon Museum (MUPANAH), Pétion-Ville Quarter (on the heights to the south-east, safer and residential, international hotels). Haiti highlights outside the capital: Citadelle Laferrière (UNESCO, Cap-Haïtien in the north, largest fortress in the Americas, built by Henri Christophe king of Haiti after independence 1805-1820), Sans-Souci Palace (UNESCO), Bassin Bleu (waterfalls at Jacmel), Jacmel (French colonial town in the south, carnival festival). Try griot (fried pork), djon-djon rice (black mushroom), pikliz (spicy pickles), Barbancourt rum, kremas (coconut liqueur).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
