Context

Our verdict

Matsuyama, capital of Shikoku (the smallest of the 4 main islands of Japan) on the Seto Inland Sea: 510,000 inhabitants. Largest city in Shikoku, cultural capital of the island 4 times on the list of the most beautiful castles in Japan. Place of the most famous hot spring in Japan = Dōgo Onsen = the oldest in operation in the country (3000 years according to tradition, mentioned in the "Nihon Shoki" imperial chronicle 720 AD). Direct inspiration of the Studio Ghibli animated film "Spirited Away" 2001 (Hayao Miyazaki was inspired by Dōgo Onsen for the spirit baths in the film). May is my favourite month (7.7/10): 14-25°C, 18% rain, ideal temperatures with wisteria + rose blooming. March-April-October-November stay excellent (7.4-7.6/10). From March to May and October-November, these are the best seasons (spring + autumn iconic of Japan, sakura cherry blossom in April, momiji red maple in November). From June to September, it's the wet + hot season: 19-31°C with 67-78% precipitation ("tsuyu" Japanese monsoon season June-July), potential typhoons August-October. From December to February, expect 0-13°C and 13-31% rain: relatively mild Japanese cold (Shikoku warmer than northern Honshu). Matsuyama highlights: Matsuyama Castle (Matsuyama-jō, 1602-1603, one of the 12 original castles of Japan not rebuilt = ensemble of castles surviving fire and WWII bombings = national treasures, perched on Mount Katsuyama 132m in the heart of the city, panorama over Matsuyama + Seto Inland Sea, access by cable car-chairlift or 30-min hike), Dōgo Onsen Honkan ("main bath", historic 1894 wooden building on 3 floors, the postcard image of Matsuyama and Studio Ghibli inspiration, thermal bathing in 2 baths "Bath of the Gods" + "Bath of the Spirits" alkaline water 42°C, iconic traditional Japanese experience, Tama-no-yu imperial salon where Emperor Hirohito stayed, open to the public on cultural visit), Bocchan Karakuri Clock (in front of Dōgo Onsen, mechanical clock opening every hour with characters from "Botchan" 1906 novel by Natsume Sōseki iconic of the city), Ishiteji Temple (one of the 88 temples of the Shikoku pilgrimage, iconic Japanese cultural = pilgrim circuit of 1200 km traversed on foot 40-60 days by the henro in traditional white garb), Bansuiso Villa (French neo-renaissance villa 1922 inspired by French châteaux), Shiki Memorial Museum (museum of the haiku poet Masaoka Shiki born in Matsuyama 1867 = father of modern haiku). Shikoku excursions: Naoshima Art Island 2h by train + ferry ("contemporary art island" UNESCO Tentative, Tadao Ando architecture iconic museums, Yayoi Kusama Pumpkin giant pumpkin = viral Instagram image of Japan), Imabari (capital of Japanese shipbuilding, Shimanami Kaido bridges connecting Shikoku-Honshu = famous cycle path 70 km on 6 spectacular bridges), Kotohira (Konpira-san Shinto shrine), Kochi (other original Shikoku castle), Iya Valley (isolated valley with traditional liana bridges). Try tai-meshi (rice with real sea bream iconic of Matsuyama), Iyokan (local Ehime orange), gokoku barley shochu, Yamanashi Japanese wines rare in Shikoku. Matsuyama airport (MYJ).
