Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Cotonou, economic capital of Benin on the Atlantic coast of the Gulf of Guinea: 800,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 2.4 million in urban area. Official capital = Porto-Novo (30 km east) but 90% of economic activity is concentrated in Cotonou. Benin = cradle of voodoo (vodun, traditional West African religion practised by 50% of the population, exported to Brazil/Haiti/Cuba via the transatlantic slave trade 15th-19th century). January is my favourite month (6.7/10): 26-30°C, 18% rain, this is the ideal "little dry season" + harmattan = dry dusty Saharan wind that refreshes. Cotonou never tops 6.9/10 (humid hot equatorial climate year-round). From December to February, it's the dry season: 26-31°C. From March to June, it's the great rainy season: 43-82% rainfall. From July to August, it's the relative "little dry hivernage season": 51-57% rain. From September to November, it's the small rainy season. Cotonou highlights: Dantokpa Market (the largest open-air market in West Africa 20 hectares, extraordinary chaotic West African atmosphere, voodoo fetishes, pagne fabrics, spices, meat, dried fish, to see in the company of a local guide), Place de l'Etoile Rouge (communist-Marxist memorial heritage of the People's Republic of Benin 1975-1990 under Mathieu Kérékou), Cathedral of Notre-Dame des Apôtres, Heritage and Tourism House, Cotonou Beach (urban, strong currents dangerous for swimming, more for the walk at sunset). Essential Benin excursions = this is why one comes to Benin: Ouidah 40 km away (historic capital of the slave trade on the "Slave Coast", embarkation port of millions of slaves to Brazil 1670-1850, Slave Route 4 km from the holding pen to the Gate of No-Return = sculpted seaside memorial, overwhelming memorial place, Ouidah History Museum, Kpassè Sacred Forest = sacred voodoo wood, Temple of Pythons = vodun institution where royal pythons are sacred and free), Ganvié 30 min away ("African Venice", lake village 30,000 inhabitants on stilts in Lake Nokoué, founded 18th century by the Tofinu fleeing the Fon slave hunters who could not approach the water for ritual reasons, still active and inhabited, accessible by pirogue from Abomey-Calavi), Abomey 130 km away (UNESCO 1985, former capital of the 17th-19th century Kingdom of Dahomey, royal palaces of the 12 successive kings with spectacular bas-reliefs telling the military history including the famous Dahomey Amazons = elite corps of female warriors), Pendjari National Park (safari park north of Benin, lions, elephants, hippopotamuses, one of the last places in West Africa with wildlife). Safety: Benin globally stable, but northern borders with Burkina Faso and Niger affected by jihadist terrorism since 2018, inadvisable France Diplomacy for the north. Try moyo (tomato-chilli-fish sauce), wagasi (Fulani cheese), akassa (fermented corn ball), cassava paste, fufu, plantain cream, sodabi (palm eau-de-vie), bissap. Cotonou Cardinal Bernardin Gantin airport (COO).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
