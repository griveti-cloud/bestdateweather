Baghdad, capital of Iraq on the Tigris River: 7 million inhabitants. Former capital of the Abbasid Caliphate 762-1258 = one of the largest cities in the medieval world with 1.2 million inhabitants in the 9th century, cultural and scientific capital of classical Islam ("House of Wisdom" Bayt al-Hikma translating Greek texts into Arabic and preserving ancient knowledge for later European Renaissance). Destroyed by the Mongols 1258, never regained its historical rank. Major Mesopotamian archaeological sites: Babylon, Ur, Uruk, Nineveh, Nippur = cradle of writing (3500 BC), mathematics, law (Hammurabi Code 1750 BC), astronomy. April is my favourite month (9.0/10): 16-30°C, 7% rain, this is the ideal dry season. March-October-November stay excellent (7.6-8.5/10). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 4-23°C, dry and tempered climate. From May to September drop to 3.9-6.0/10 due to extreme heatwave (24-46°C, sometimes 53°C+ = among the highest temperatures in the world, extreme desert climate). Highlights: Mausoleum of Imam Kadhim ("Al-Kadhimiya Mosque", one of the 4 largest Shia mosques in the world, signature golden dome and minarets, sacred place for Shias), Tahrir Square ("Liberation Square", historic central square), National Museum of Iraq (one of the most important archaeological museums in the world, Sumerian-Akkadian-Babylonian-Assyrian civilisations, partially looted in April 2003 during the US invasion, some pieces recovered later), Mutanabbi Street ("booksellers' street", traditional book market, Baghdad cultural signature, every Friday). Major archaeological sites = this is why one visits Iraq: Babylon 90 km south (UNESCO 2019, former capital of the Babylonian empire of Hammurabi 1792-1750 BC + Nebuchadnezzar 605-562 BC who built the Hanging Gardens = one of the 7 Wonders of the ancient world today disappeared, partially rebuilt by Saddam Hussein 1980 controversially with bricks marked "Saddam Reconstructor of Babylon"), Ur in the south (former Sumerian city 3rd millennium BC, preserved signature ziggurat, considered the birthplace of Abraham according to biblical tradition), Najaf and Karbala (the 2 most sacred Shia cities in the world, sanctuaries of Imam Ali and Imam Hussein = places of pilgrimage for 25 million Shias annually during Arbaeen which rivals the Hajj of Mecca by its massive attendance). Safety: inadvisable France Diplomacy for the entirety of Iraq except autonomous Iraqi Kurdistan (Erbil, Sulaymaniyah, Dohuk), residual ISIS terrorism + Shia/Sunni sectarian tensions + post-2003 US invasion political violence. Very inadvisable travel except professional accompaniment and expert organisation. Try masgouf (grilled Tigris carp signature of Baghdad), Iraqi kebab, dolma (stuffed vegetables), kubba (meatballs), tea with cardamom, tamarind juice, Iraqi dates (the best historical world producer). Baghdad International airport (BGW).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
