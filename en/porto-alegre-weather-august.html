Context

Our verdict

Porto Alegre, capital of Rio Grande do Sul in southern Brazil: 1.4 million inhabitants at the confluence of 5 rivers forming Lago Guaíba. Subtropical climate as opposed to tropical Brazil, "gaúcho" atmosphere (South American cowboy) recalling neighbouring Argentina or Uruguay (mate is consumed daily as in Buenos Aires, equestrian traditions). December is my favourite month (8.7/10): 19-29°C, 34% rain, this is the start of the austral summer. From November to March, it's the austral summer: 17-30°C, 32-41% rain, sometimes scorching (38°C+ with oppressive humidity). From April to May and September-October are the best seasons: 14-24°C. From June to August, it's the austral winter: 10-21°C, mild but sky often grey. Highlights: Mercado Público (1869, central 19th-century market in Portuguese architecture), Casa de Cultura Mario Quintana (cultural centre former Hotel Majestic Art Nouveau), Praça da Matriz (historic square with Catedral Metropolitana), Parque Farroupilha ("Parque da Redenção", 37-hectare central park, craft market on Sundays), Usina do Gasômetro (former gas plant converted into a cultural centre, iconic view of the sunset over Lago Guaíba — Gaúchos say Porto Alegre has "the most beautiful sunset in the world"), Memorial of Rio Grande do Sul, Jardim Botânico. Excursions: Gramado 2h away (Bavarian town in the Serra Gaúcha, year-round Christmas atmosphere with the Natal Luz market, massive Brazilian domestic tourism), Canela (Cascata do Caracol), Bento Gonçalves and Vale dos Vinhedos (Brazilian wine region, 19th-century Italian immigration), Atlantic Coast 2h away (Torres, Capão da Canoa). Try churrasco (the real gaúcho churrasco, not tropical Brazilian: meat with salt on a sword, cowboy tradition), chimarrão (gaúcho mate), arroz carreteiro, dulce de leche, Vale dos Vinhedos wines.
