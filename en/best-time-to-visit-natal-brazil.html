Natal, capital of Rio Grande do Norte in the Nordeste of Brazil: 900,000 inhabitants on the Atlantic coast at the closest point in the Americas to Africa. The sunniest city in Brazil (300+ days per year), nicknamed "Cidade do Sol". Famed for its spectacular shifting dunes (Genipabu) and its iconic urban beach Ponta Negra. September-October are my favourite months (8.2/10): 23-29°C, 34-36% rain, this is the driest season. From September to February, it's the tourist high season: 23-30°C, 32-49% rain, steady Atlantic breezes. From March to June, it's the rainy season ("inverno do Nordeste" ironically called winter despite stable temperatures 23-29°C): 65-75% rainfall, but typical brief afternoon showers. Highlights: Praia de Ponta Negra (iconic urban beach with Morro do Careca = 120m sand dune-cliff falling directly onto the beach, iconic view of Natal), Forte dos Reis Magos (16th-century Portuguese star-shaped fort with 5 points, at the mouth of the Rio Potengi, official founding of Natal on 25 December 1599), Centro Histórico (Cidade Alta, 17th-18th century Portuguese colonial architecture: Catedral Velha, Igreja do Bom Jesus das Dores, Palácio Felipe Camarão), Parque das Dunas (1170 hectares of shifting dunes within the city itself, protected trails). Beach and dune excursions (Natal's major attraction): Genipabu 30 min to the north (the famous shifting dunes of Brazil, buggy ride with local bugueiros, sandboarding, Pititinga freshwater lake, dromedaries by the sea brought from the Canaries), Praia da Pipa 1h30 to the south (one of the most beautiful beaches in Brazil according to many rankings, red cliffs, dolphins, bohemian atmosphere), São Miguel do Gostoso, Galinhos. Try moqueca de camarão, carne de sol, tapioca with coconut/banana/coalho cheese, água de coco. Direct flight from Lisbon, or via São Paulo/Recife.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)

Natal averages 11.4 sunshine hours per day in September. It is the sunniest month with a climate score of 8.9/10.

Is Natal good in winter?
