Context

Our verdict

Suzhou, 100 km west of Shanghai in Jiangsu province: 6 million inhabitants, nicknamed the "Venice of the East" for its canals dating from the Tang-Song dynasties (6th-10th centuries). UNESCO 1997 for its classical Chinese gardens (Suzhou is the cradle of the art of Chinese gardens). September is my favourite month (8.0/10): 22-28°C, 44% rain, ideal temperatures after the summer heatwave. From September to November, it's the tourist high season: 10-28°C, garden maple foliage in November. From April to May, expect 12-26°C: spring with cherry blossoms. From June to August, expect 21-34°C with 46-54% rain: stifling heat typical of the Yangtze delta, oppressive humidity. From December to February, expect 1-11°C and 20-30% rain: humid cold, few tourists. Highlights: Classical Gardens of Suzhou (UNESCO, 9 listed gardens, to see in priority: Humble Administrator's Garden Zhuozheng Yuan the largest 5 hectares 16th century, Lingering Garden Liu Yuan calligraphies, Master of the Nets Garden Wangshi Yuan the most intimate to see illuminated in the evening, Lion Grove Garden Shizilin with its limestone rocks in lion shapes), Pingjiang Road (historic pedestrian street along a canal, ancient atmosphere, tearooms, traditional shops), Tiger Hill (Huqiu, hill with 11th-century leaning pagoda that leans even more than the Tower of Pisa), Hanshan Temple (made famous by the Tang poem "Mooring at night at Maple Bridge" by Zhang Ji, ancestral bell struck 108 times for the New Year), Suzhou Museum (architecture I.M. Pei born in China 2006), Suzhou Silk Museum (Suzhou is the historic capital of Chinese silk for 4000 years). Excursions: Tongli, Zhouzhuang, Wuzhen (water towns 1-2h away, ancestral water villages). Try Suzhou-style noodles, songshu yu (squirrel fish sweet-sour), Jiangsu cuisine. 30 min by fast train from Shanghai, ideal day trip.
