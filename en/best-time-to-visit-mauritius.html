Mauritius, Indian Ocean island state: 2000 km², 1.3 million inhabitants (Creoles, Indians, Chinese, Europeans), French-speaking (colonial heritage), volcanic island ringed by turquoise lagoons and coral reefs. October is my favourite month (7.7/10): 17-23°C, 63% rain. Mauritius never tops 7.7/10 (tropical climate with frequent rain). From May to November, it's the austral winter: 16-25°C, sea at 22-26°C, drier period (58-69% rainfall), ideal for surfing at Le Morne, humpback whales mid-July to mid-November. From December to April, it's the austral summer: 20-26°C, sea at 26-28°C, but also cyclone season (5-7 cyclones/season, 1-2 majors hit the island). Highlights: Le Morne Brabant (UNESCO, south-west peninsula, 556m mountain, iconic Mauritius beach with the underwater waterfall optical illusion), Chamarel (seven coloured earths, unique geology), Black River Gorges National Park (macaques, hiking), Île aux Cerfs (boat trip, paradise beach but crowded), Belle Mare (east coast, wild beaches), Trou aux Biches (north coast, beach resort), Port Louis (capital, Caudan Waterfront, central market, Pamplemousses garden). Activities: kitesurfing at Le Morne (steady wind), diving (Cathedral at Flic en Flac), golf (50% of luxury hotels have their own course), swimming with dolphins at Tamarin. Try rougail saucisse, vindaye, dholl puri, sweet-potato cake. Eureka House at Moka for colonial architecture.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
