Context

Our verdict

Vanuatu is an archipelago of 83 islands in the South Pacific: 320,000 inhabitants, capital Port Vila on Efate, former Franco-British condominium of the New Hebrides (independence 1980). Active volcanoes accessible to tourists. June-July are my favourite months (8.3/10): 22-26°C, 37-39% rain, austral dry season. From May to October, it's the relative dry season: 36-55% rainfall, temperatures 21-27°C. From November to April, it's the wet season: 55-85% rainfall, heavy heat, very high cyclone risk (Pam 2015 category 5 devastating, Harold 2020 too). Highlights: Mount Yasur on Tanna island (active volcano accessible up to the crater, an experience unique in the world, eruptions visible every minute, leave at sunset for the incandescent lava), Champagne Beach on Espiritu Santo (beach ranked among the world's most beautiful), Million Dollar Point (US WWII military equipment wrecks dumped into the sea in 1945, diving), SS President Coolidge (one of the largest wrecks accessible to recreational diving in the world). Melanesian cultural villages. Land diving at Pentecost (May-June, ancestor of bungee jumping, men dive from a wooden tower with vines tied to their ankles). Try kava (traditional drink), raw fish, lap lap (traditional pudding). Flight from Brisbane/Sydney in 3h, compulsory Auckland stopover from Europe.
