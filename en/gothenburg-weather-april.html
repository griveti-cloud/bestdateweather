Context

Our verdict

Gothenburg, 2nd city of Sweden: 600,000 inhabitants on the west coast, port city with 21 km of canals (planned by the Dutch in the 17th century). More accessible and cheaper than Stockholm, more relaxed and creative atmosphere (Volvo has its headquarters here). June is my favourite month (7.9/10): 12-19°C, 43% rain, 18h days, terraces on the canals. From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 12-20°C, 43-55% rain. November to March, expect -1/7°C and 46-57% precipitation often as snow: Christmas market at Liseberg mid-November to late December (one of the largest in Scandinavia, amusement park transformed into enchanting market with 5 million lights). Highlights: Liseberg (the largest amusement park in Scandinavia, founded 1923, Helix roller coaster, wooden Balder leading the world rankings), Haga (old pedestrian working-class quarter, wooden houses, kanelbullar = giant cinnamon buns at Café Husaren), Skansen Kronan (17th-century fortress on hill, panoramic view), Feskekôrka Fish Market ("Fish Church", astonishing 1874 neo-Gothic architecture), Götaplatsen (square with statue of Poseidon by Carl Milles), Universeum (science centre with animals, popular with families), Avenyn (main avenue Kungsportsavenyn). Excursions: Gothenburg Archipelago (20 islands accessible by ferry, car-free for the most numerous, ideal in summer), Marstrand 1h away (fortress island, sailing), Smögen (colourful fishing village). Try västkustsallad (seafood salad), kanelbullar, gravlax, surströmming (fermented herring = shock olfactory experience), Swedish craft beer. More accessible than Stockholm in price terms (-15-20%).
