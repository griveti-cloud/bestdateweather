Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Myanmar (Burma), country in South-East Asia between India, China, Bangladesh, Thailand: 54 million inhabitants. Mysterious and closed country for decades under military junta (1962-2011), brief democratic opening 2011-2021 with Aung San Suu Kyi 1991 Nobel Peace Prize, then 1 February 2021 military coup which overthrew Suu Kyi (still imprisoned), civil war since. Tourism now inadvisable by France Diplomacy for security reasons (ongoing fighting in some regions, international sanctions, heavy military checks). From January to February are my favourite months (6.9/10): 14-32°C, 7-9% rain. Myanmar never tops 6.9/10 (hot tropical climate + pre-monsoon heatwave). From November to February, it's the ideal dry season: 14-33°C. From March to May, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 18-39°C (sometimes 45°C in the central dry plain), impossible to visit at noon. From June to October, it's the monsoon: 60-86% rainfall. Highlights: Bagan (one of the most impressive archaeological sites in the world, 3000 Buddhist stupas and temples 9th-13th centuries scattered over 100 km², overflown by hot air balloon at sunrise, landscapes with morning mist + golden temples emerging unique in the world), Lake Inle (altitude lake 880m, Intha people = traditional fishermen who paddle on a single leg surrounded by the oar = unique technique in the world, floating villages on stilts, floating gardens, signature visual), Mandalay (already covered by BDW, former royal capital), Shwedagon Pagoda in Yangon (already covered, the most sacred golden stupa in Myanmar), Mount Kyaiktiyo ("Golden Rock", miraculously balanced rock on cliff covered with gold leaves, major pilgrimage place), Pindaya Caves (limestone caves with 8000 Buddha statues accumulated over 200 years), Mrauk U (former 15th-century royal capital in the western Rakhine State = Rohingya conflict zone, inadvisable), Hpa-an (karst landscapes in the south-east, terrestrial alternative to Halong Bay similar to Ninh Binh Vietnam). Rohingya crisis: since 2017, systematic genocide against the Muslim Rohingya minority in Rakhine State, 1 million refugees in neighbouring Bangladesh, one of the worst humanitarian dramas of the 21st century. Important: extremely unstable political situation since February 2021, inadvisable France Diplomacy. If travel maintained: avoid central Yangon, Kachin/Karen/Shan/Rakhine fighting zones, strictly respect local instructions.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
