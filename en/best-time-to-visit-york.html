York, historic city in North Yorkshire in England: 200,000 inhabitants. 2000 years of continuous history: Roman capital of Britannia Inferior founded 71 AD (Eboracum), Anglo-Saxon capital (Eoforwic 7th century), Viking capital of Jorvik (9th-10th centuries), kingdom of Northern England (10th-13th centuries), 2nd city of England in the Middle Ages, royal stage on the York-Edinburgh road. Nicknamed the "capital of the North". UNESCO universal value not yet listed but 1 of the 10 most-visited English heritage sites. August is my favourite month (7.8/10): 13-20°C, 39% rain, ideal temperatures. From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 11-20°C, long days (17h of daylight at the solstice). From November to March, expect 2-9°C and 35-46% rain: damp cold, rare snow, St Nicholas Fair Christmas market (one of the oldest in England since the 13th century). Highlights: York Minster (the gothic cathedral: the largest gothic cathedral in England, construction 13th-15th century, one of the largest in the world, Great East Window stained glass 15th century = the largest medieval stained-glass surface in the world 23m × 9m, 275-step ascent of Central Tower panorama, 12th-century Norman crypt with ancient remains), The Shambles (medieval narrow street with 15th-century timber-framed houses with overhanging upper storeys nearly touching above the street, former butchers' street "Shambles" = butchery in old English, the inspiration for Diagon Alley in Harry Potter with witch shops today), City Walls (medieval ramparts 4.5 km, one of the only English cities with complete walls accessible on foot, panorama above the city), Jorvik Viking Centre (immersive museum with reconstruction of the 10th-century York Viking quarter discovered during excavations 1976-1981), York Castle (Clifford's Tower, 1245 keep), National Railway Museum ("national railway museum", the world's largest railway museum, free, Mallard locomotive holder of the steam speed record 203 km/h in 1938, replica of Stephenson's Rocket 1829), Yorkshire Museum (Roman and Viking artefacts including the 15th-century Middleham Jewel), Treasurer's House (haunted house according to legend, Roman ghosts spotted in the cellar which was on the former Roman road), Stonegate quarter (historic shopping street). Yorkshire excursions: Yorkshire Dales and North York Moors National Parks (hill landscapes, ruined abbeys), Whitby 1h away (Viking port, ruined abbey that inspired Bram Stoker for Dracula 1897), Castle Howard 30 min away (18th-century baroque palace filming location of Brideshead Revisited), Harrogate 30 min away (Victorian spa town), Edinburgh 2h30 by train. Try Yorkshire pudding (the traditional version), Wensleydale cheese (PGI Yorkshire cheese), parkin (traditional ginger oat cake), fish and chips, Yorkshire ales (Black Sheep, Theakston). Direct train London King's Cross 2h.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
