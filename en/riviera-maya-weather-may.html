Context

Our verdict

Riviera Maya, Caribbean coast of Mexico in the state of Quintana Roo in the south-east of the country: 130 km of coastline from Cancún to Tulum, 1.5 million inhabitants. Tourist capital of Mexico with more than 13 million visitors/year (Cancún alone = the busiest airport in Mexico, 27 million passengers/year). Powdery white-sand beaches + turquoise water + cenotes (natural freshwater wells sacred to the Mayans). March is my favourite month (8.8/10): 24-28°C, 27% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From January to April, it's the dry season: 22-29°C, sea at 27-28°C, this is the tourist high season (American/Canadian snowbirds in the majority, Europeans, hotel prices x3). From May to June, expect 26-30°C with 46-67% rain: start of the wet season. From July to October, it's the active cyclone season: 54-72% rainfall, real hurricane risk (the Riviera Maya was devastated by Wilma in 2005, Dean in 2007, Delta in 2020). Sargassum season = a major problem since 2014: brown algae invading the beaches mainly May-October, especially in June-August, the coast from Tulum to Playa del Carmen more affected than Cancún or Holbox, hotels invest in daily cleaning but the beach experience is sometimes spoiled. Highlights / Main resorts: Cancún (the best known, Hotel Zone 23 km of beaches with all-inclusive resorts, dynamic Coco Bongo nightlife, public beach Playa Delfines with iconic "CANCUN" Insta-photo), Playa del Carmen ("Playa" to regulars, smarter and more modern cosmopolitan town than Cancún, 5th Avenue 30-block pedestrian street with restaurants-bars-shops, ferry departures to Cozumel 30 min), Tulum (the smartest and most bohemian, Mayan ruins perched on a cliff above turquoise sea = cult iconic view, preserved wild beaches, eco-chic boutique hotels, hippie-yoga atmosphere), Akumal ("turtle bay" in Mayan, snorkelling with green turtles guaranteed), Puerto Morelos (preserved fishing village between Cancún-Playa del Carmen). Mayan archaeological sites: Chichén Itzá 2h away (UNESCO, one of the 7 New Wonders of the World 2007, iconic El Castillo pyramid, ball court, observatory), Tulum Ruins (the only seaside Mayan ruins in the world, exceptional setting on a turquoise cliff, access at 8am to avoid crowds + heat), Cobá 1h away (Nohoch Mul pyramid 42m still climbable until 2020, jungle trails), Ek Balam 2h away (little touristy, preserved stucco friezes). Cenotes, more than 6000 cenotes recorded, crystal-clear water 22-25°C constant year-round, sacred Mayan place for rituals): Cenote Dos Ojos at Tulum (the most famous, diving), Gran Cenote at Tulum (the most touristy but splendid), Cenote Ik Kil at Chichén Itzá (the most photogenic with hanging vines), Cenote Suytun (circular platform with sun ray filtering at noon). Excursions: Isla Mujeres 25 min by ferry from Cancún (quieter island, turquoise beaches, snorkelling whale sharks June-September), Cozumel 30 min by ferry from Playa del Carmen (world-renowned diving island, top 5 diving globally according to Cousteau), Bacalar Lagoon 4h away ("lake of 7 colours", extraordinary turquoise lagoon), Sian Ka'an Biosphere Reserve 1h from Tulum (UNESCO, preserved mangrove biosphere reserve). Try ceviche, cochinita pibil (Mayan pork roasted in a banana leaf), tacos al pastor, mole, tamales, mezcal, tequila, Sol/Corona/Pacifico beer. Cancún airport (CUN).
