Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Tehran, capital of Iran: 9 million inhabitants (15 million in urban area) at 1200m altitude at the foot of the Alborz Mountains (Damavand summit 5610m, highest volcano in Asia). Capital since 1796 under the Qajar dynasty. Modern megacity with striking contradictions: theocratic conservative regime since 1979 (Islamic Revolution) facing an educated urban youth (60% of the population under 35, regular protests). Limited tourism since international sanctions (accessible visa but Western feeling of going to "forbidden" country). May is my favourite month (9.2/10): 17-30°C, 13% rain, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. From April-May and September-October, these are the best seasons: 12-32°C, 1-22% rain. From June to August, it's the extreme heatwave: 23-38°C (sometimes 42°C), suffocating atmospheric pollution (Tehran is one of the most polluted cities in the world, persistent smog also in winter). From December to February, expect 0-11°C and 18-26% rain: dry cold, snow possible, skiing on the neighbouring heights at Dizin and Shemshak (50 min from Tehran, 3600m altitude, among the best ski resorts in the Middle East). Highlights: Golestan Palace (UNESCO 2013, former 19th-century Qajar residence, 17 monuments in a Persian garden, iconic mosaics), Saadabad Palace (in the north on the heights, former summer residence of the shah), Niavaran Palace (last residence of Shah Mohammad Reza Pahlavi before exile 1979), National Jewels Treasury (under the Central Bank, one of the most impressive royal jewellery collections in the world: peacock throne in gold 26000 precious stones, Darya-ye Noor diamond 182 carats one of the largest pink diamonds in the world), Tehran Bazaar (the largest covered bazaar in the world 10 km of alleys), National Archaeological Museum (ancient Persian and Achaemenid collections), Azadi Tower ("Liberty", 1971 commemorating 2500 years of Persian empire), Milad Tower (435m, 6th tallest tower in the world 2008), Tajrish Quarter in the north (smart and modern, restaurants and cafés). Excursions: Mount Damavand (5610m, 3-day ascent), Caspian 3h away, Qom Shia holy city 2h away, Isfahan 5h by bus (UNESCO, see dedicated review). Try chelo kabab (saffron rice + skewers), ghormeh sabzi, fessenjan (chicken in walnut-pomegranate sauce), sangak (bread), Persian tea tchaï, doogh (salted sparkling yoghurt).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
