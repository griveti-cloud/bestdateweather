Context

Our verdict

Gdansk (Danzig), large port city of northern Poland on the Baltic Sea: 470,000 inhabitants. Former free Hanseatic city, unique atmosphere mixing German-Polish-Jewish heritage. Birthplace of the Solidarność movement (Lech Wałęsa, electrician shipyard striker, who precipitated the fall of Polish communism 1989, prelude to the fall of the Berlin Wall). 90% destroyed by the Soviets in March 1945 in street fighting, rebuilt identically 1950-1960 according to 18th-century plans. August is my favourite month (8.3/10): 15-22°C, 36% rain, ideal temperatures. From June to August are the tourist high season: 13-22°C, Baltic swimming 18-20°C cool but possible (Brzeźno beaches, neighbouring Sopot). From November to March, expect 0-8°C and 35-43% rain: continental winter cold, snow possible, Christmas market (the most famous in Poland). Highlights: Old town (Główne Miasto, rebuilt identically after 1945 but with preserved urban planning authenticity), Długi Targ ("Long Market", main pedestrian artery between the Golden Gate and the Green Gate with colourful Hanseatic houses, 1633 Neptune fountain iconic), Town Hall (Gothic-Renaissance, panorama 81m tower), Saint Mary's Church (Bazylika Mariacka, the largest brick church in the world, capacity 25,000 people, Gothic 14th-16th century), Medieval crane (Żuraw, iconic view of Gdansk, largest port crane of the Middle Ages 15th century in Europe), Mariacka Street (typical pedestrian street with characteristic Gdansk outdoor terraces "przedproża", sellers of Baltic amber = world capital of fossilised amber), European Solidarity Centre (modern museum 2014 on the history of Solidarność and the fall of communism, to see, one of the best museums in Poland), Stocznia Gdańska Shipyard (place where Lech Wałęsa and 17,000 workers went on strike 1980, memorial with 3 monumental crosses 42m erected 1980 for the workers killed 1970), Westerplatte (place where the Second World War began on 1 September 1939 at 4:45 am when the German battleship Schleswig-Holstein fired on the Polish garrison, memorial). Excursions: Sopot 25 min by train (smart Baltic beach resort, longest wooden pier in Europe 511m), Hel Peninsula (35 km arc peninsula, beaches, grey seals), Malbork 30 min away (UNESCO, the largest brick castle in the world 13th-14th century, former capital of the Teutonic Knights). Try pierogi, zurek (sour fermented rye soup), bigos (cabbage-sauerkraut meat), kaszanka (groats blood pudding), Zubrówka vodka (bison grass vodka), Sopot beer.
