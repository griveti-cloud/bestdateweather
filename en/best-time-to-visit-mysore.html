Mysore (Mysuru), 2nd city of Karnataka in southern India: 1 million inhabitants at 770m altitude at the foot of the Western Ghats. Former capital of the kingdom of Mysore under the Wodeyar dynasty (1399-1947, one of the longest uninterrupted reigns in history), second tourist destination of Karnataka after Bangalore. World capital of Ashtanga Vinyasa yoga (Pattabhi Jois school founded 1948, one of the most influential yoga schools in the world, students: Madonna, Sting, Gwyneth Paltrow). January is my favourite month (9.3/10): 17-29°C, 6% rain, ideal temperatures. December stays excellent (9.0/10). From November to February, it's the tourist high season: 17-32°C, dry and cool climate thanks to altitude. From March to May, expect 20-35°C: hot but bearable thanks to altitude (Mysore is cooler than the plain cities). From June to October, it's the south-west monsoon: 61-79% rainfall, but milder temperatures (20-29°C). Highlights: Mysore Palace ("Amba Vilas Palace", one of the largest palaces in India, neo-Saracenic 1912 rebuilt after the wood palace fire of 1897, mix of Hindu Rajput Mughal Gothic architectures, 7 royal gates, 12 temples in the enclosure, richly decorated interior with glass mosaics and painted ceilings, illuminated every Sunday evening and every evening during the Dussehra festival with 100,000 golden bulbs, the postcard image of South India), Chamundi Hills (1062m 13 km away, 1000 steps on foot or car road, Nandi the bull of Shiva 5m statue carved in a single block, Chamundeshwari temple dedicated to the patron goddess of Mysore), Devaraja Market (colourful spicy market of flowers and fruits since 1880, one of the most photogenic in South India), Brindavan Gardens 25 km away (Mughal-style gardens on the Krishnaraja Sagar dam 1932, illuminated musical fountains in the evening), Maharaja's Palace Jaganmohan Palace (Sri Jayachamarajendra art gallery), Railway Museum (one of the only railway museums in India), Mysore Zoo (one of the oldest in India 1892, captive elephant breeding programme). Major festival: Dussehra (10 days September-October according to the Hindu calendar, one of the largest religious festivals in South India, monumental royal processions of caparisoned elephants gold and illuminated palace), Yoga World in June (International Yoga Day). Excursions: Bandipur and Nagarhole National Parks 1-2h away (tigers, elephants), Hassan and Halebidu/Belur 3h away (UNESCO 12th-century Hoysala temples), Sravanabelagola (Bahubali statue 17m monolithic 9th century), Coorg/Kodagu 4h away ("Scotland of India", coffee plantations). Try Mysore masala dosa (the crispiest and spiciest version), Mysore pak (chickpea flour cake with ghee), bisi bele bath, Coorg coffee, Mysore silk sari (renowned). Logistics: Mysore airport (MYQ) with flights to Bangalore, direct Bangalore-Mysore train 3h.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
