Chișinău (Kishinev in Russian), capital of Moldova: 530,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 800,000 in urban area. Country among the poorest in Europe (lowest GDP per capita in Europe), former Soviet republic 1940-1991. Population shared between Romanians-Moldovans (75%) and Russian-Ukrainian-Gagauz minorities. Frozen conflict in Transnistria (pro-Russian separatist territory in the east, 500,000 inhabitants, internationally unrecognised). Very little touristic. August is my favourite month (9.2/10): 18-29°C, 15% rain, ideal temperatures. July stays excellent (9.0/10). From May to September, it's the tourist high season: 11-29°C. From November to March, expect -4/9°C and 21-24% rain: continental cold, little snow (drier climate than Belarus), unique atmosphere. Highlights: Great National Assembly Square (Piaţa Marii Adunări Naţionale, main square with triumphal arch Arcul de Triumf 1840 + Orthodox Cathedral Naşterea Domnului 19th-century neo-classical Russian), Ștefan cel Mare Avenue (main avenue 4 km named after the great 15th-century Moldovan prince Ștefan cel Mare = heroic defender against Ottomans, national hero), Ștefan cel Mare Park (central park with statue of the prince), National Museum of History of Moldova, Cathedral of the Transfiguration (Schimbarea la Faţă), Pre-war Jewish Quarter (memory of the 1903 and 1905 pogroms + Holocaust 1941 where 90% of the Moldovan Jewish community was exterminated), Doina Cemetery (where Maria Cebotari famous Moldovan soprano rests). Excursions = this is why one comes to Moldova: Orhei Vechi Caves 1h away ("Old Orhei", 13th-century troglodyte Orthodox monastery dug in the limestone cliff above the Răut river, mystical-bucolic atmosphere uniquely, iconic view of Moldova), Mileștii Mici Caves 30 min away (the largest wine cellar in the world 200 km of underground galleries with 1.5 million bottles, Guinness Book record, mandatory visit by car as distances large), Cricova Caves 30 min away (other large cellar 120 km of galleries, fine tasting), Purcari Caves 2h away (vineyards Negru de Purcari = legendary Moldovan wine served at the court of the Russian tsars 19th century + Queen Elizabeth II), Tipova 1h30 away (other spectacular troglodyte monastery), Soroca in the north (15th-century medieval fortress + Roma Tzigane capital of Moldova with kitsch palace-houses built by enriched Roma). Moldovan wines: Moldova produces more wine per capita than any other country in the world (3.8% of GDP, 25% of exports). Try mămăligă (polenta), sarmale (stuffed cabbage), plăcinte (cheese turnover), brânză (fermented sheep cheese), Moldovan wines Negru de Purcari/Feteasca Neagră/Riesling, Divin (Moldovan cognac), Kvas. Chișinău airport (KIV).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
