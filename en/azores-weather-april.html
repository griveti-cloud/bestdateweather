Context

Our verdict

The Azores is the Portuguese archipelago of the Atlantic: 9 volcanic islands, spectacular green landscapes (hills, crater lakes, hot springs). July is my favourite month (8.0/10): 18-23°C, 46% rain, mild temperatures for hiking. Oceanic climate = rain possible all year (very changeable). From June to September is the relatively drier season (45-50% rain). From November to March, expect 13-17°C and 52-56% rain: Atlantic storms, rough sea, inter-island ferries sometimes cancelled. 9 main islands: São Miguel (the largest, see dedicated review), Pico (the volcano 2351m, highest summit in Portugal, 5h round-trip ascent), Faial (Horta marina with its Peter Café Sport for sailors from around the world), Terceira (Angra do Heroísmo UNESCO, bulls in the streets), São Jorge (cheese), Flores (waterfalls, one of the most beautiful), Corvo (the smallest, 400 inhabitants), Graciosa, Santa Maria (the warmest). Whale watching April-October (25 different species possible). Diving in the Azores among the best spots in the Atlantic (manta rays at Princess Alice, sharks). Logistics: Azores Airlines mandatory inter-island flights, 1 single ferry (Atlanticoline). Plan 10-15 days minimum for 3-4 islands. Direct flight Paris-Ponta Delgada (4h).
