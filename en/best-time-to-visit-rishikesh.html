Rishikesh, sacred Hindu town at the foot of the Himalayas in the state of Uttarakhand in northern India: 100,000 inhabitants on the Ganges. "World capital of yoga" since the Beatles spent 6 weeks here in February-April 1968 with Maharishi Mahesh Yogi, creating the White Album with 48 songs written during the stay. Today a global hub for yoga, meditation, ayurveda and rafting on the Ganges. March is my favourite month (9.2/10): 13-28°C, 15% rain, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. October stays excellent (9.2/10). From February to March and October-November, these are the best seasons: 8-29°C. From July to August, it's the monsoon: 83-85% rainfall, Ganges in flood swollen with mud, rafting closed by the authorities for danger. From April to June, expect 17-35°C: hot but dry, yoga still practicable. From November to February, expect 6-22°C: cold relative to north India, ideal for intensive practice. Highlights: Lakshman Jhula (former 1929 suspension bridge over the Ganges — closed for renovation since 2019, replaced temporarily by Bajrang Setu), Ram Jhula (another suspension bridge), Triveni Ghat (the main ghat for the ritual bath in the Ganges, Ganga Aarti = fire ceremony at sunset every evening with chants and floating lamps released on the water, spiritual experience), Parmarth Niketan Ashram (one of the world's largest ashrams, 1000 rooms, welcome for yoga classes, spectacular evening Aarti with 1000 faithful), Beatles Ashram (Chaurasi Kutia, ashram of Maharishi Mahesh Yogi where the Beatles stayed in 1968, abandoned since 1997, open to visits since 2015 with psychedelic graffiti and Beatles murals), Bharat Mandir (Rishikesh's oldest temple, founded by Adi Shankaracharya 8th century), Neelkanth Mahadev Temple 30 km away (Shiva temple on a hill 1675m, place where Shiva is said to have swallowed the poison of the sea to save the world according to Hindu mythology). Activities: yoga (hundreds of schools, daily classes 5-15€, TTC Teacher Training Course 200h or 500h = international Yoga Alliance certificate, prices 1500-3000€ for 1 month residential intensive), meditation Vipassana, ayurveda (massages, panchakarma cures), rafting (the Ganges at Rishikesh = one of Asia's best rivers for rafting, 16 km on grade III-IV, 800-1500 INR ≈ 10-18€/person September-June), bungee jumping (Mohan Chatti 83m, India's first bungee jump). Important: Rishikesh is vegetarian and alcohol-free by strict religious decree (absolute ban on meat, fish, egg, alcohol throughout the town). Try sattvic food (pure yogic cuisine: no onion, no garlic, no strong spice), vegetarian thali, lassi, masala chai, fresh juices. Logistics: Dehradun airport (DED) 30 min away, alternative Delhi 6h by bus.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
