Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Mayotte (Maore in Mahorais), French overseas department in the Mozambique Channel north-west of Madagascar: 320,000 inhabitants over 374 km² (huge density 855 hab/km²), 101st French department since 2011 (the youngest department). Made up of Grande-Terre and Petite-Terre linked by a ferry. Administrative status unique in France: 95% Muslim population, polygamy tolerated until 2010, mother tongue Shimaoré for the majority. Chronic migration and security crisis for the past 20 years (50% of the population are undocumented Comorians, high crime rate). Cyclone Chido in December 2024 devastated the island. July is my favourite month (9.0/10): 22-27°C, 21% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From June to September are the tourist high season: 22-28°C, lagoon water at 25°C, also humpback whale season (June-October, observation of the Madagascar-Antarctica crossing). From November to April, it's the rainy + cyclonic season: 70-89% rainfall, real cyclone risk but usually low (Chido 2024 = major exception since 1934). Highlights: Lagoon of Mayotte (1500 km², one of the world's largest closed lagoons, turquoise waters over white-sand bottom, rare double coral barrier = the outer barrier + an inner barrier, exceptional diving and snorkelling), Mayotte islands: Petite-Terre (atolls Dzaoudzi, Pamandzi with airport, no direct link with Mayotte), Mayotte/Grande-Terre (main island, capital Mamoudzou), Ilot M'Bouzi (reserve, lemurs), Mtsamboro Island, Turtle Island, Bandrélé and Saziley (green turtle laying Nov-March), Mount Choungui (594m, the highest point accessible by 1h walk, 360° panorama), Lake Karihani (sacred lake in a volcanic crater), Saziley (extreme southern peninsula, difficult access, preserved beaches), Soulou Waterfall. Activities: diving in the lagoon (Manta Point, S Pass, Wreck of Sébastien), snorkelling, humpback whale watching (June-October, more accessible and cheaper than in neighbouring Réunion or Tahiti), kayaking in the mangrove at Bouéni, excursion to Moutsoumboutsou ("rooster crests" = sandy limestone islets, iconic tropical landscapes). Safety: Mayotte has significant security problems (burglaries, social tensions), solo travel inadvisable, frequent thefts even in broad daylight, official France Diplomatie advisory of heightened vigilance. Try ailes (rice with coconut accompanied by fish), grilled lagoon fish, brèdes mafane (a particular leaf vegetable), fruit pickles, forest honey. Logistics: Dzaoudzi-Pamandzi airport (DZA) on Petite-Terre, flights from Paris-CDG in 11h via Réunion or Nairobi, high prices (1500-2500€ return).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
