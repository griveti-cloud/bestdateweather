Context

Our verdict

Krakow is the former Polish royal capital, spared by the war, one of Central Europe's most beautiful cities. June-August are my favourite months (8.2-8.5/10): 14-25°C, 37-48% rain, terraces packed on Rynek Główny (Europe's largest medieval square), Jewish festivals in July (Festival of Jewish Culture in Kazimierz). From December to February, expect -2/6°C and 38-43% rain or snow: Christmas markets on the market square, magnificent city under the snow. Auschwitz-Birkenau 1h30 by bus (booking required with guide in peak season, do not do it as a same-day round trip), Wieliczka salt mines 30 min away (UNESCO), Wawel Castle, Kazimierz (former Jewish quarter now trendy). Try a pierogi at Pierogarnia Krakowiacy, a żurek (Polish sour soup), a vodka at Wódka Café Bar. Krakow stays very cheap: traditional meals 5-10€, beer 1.50-2€. May and September-October are the best tourism/weather compromises.
