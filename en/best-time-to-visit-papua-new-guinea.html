Papua New Guinea (PNG, "Papua New Guinea"), archipelago country of Melanesia north of Australia: 11 million inhabitants on 462,840 km². Country unique in the world for its ethno-cultural diversity = 850 different languages spoken (the most linguistically diverse country in the world, 12% of world languages), 1000+ distinct tribal ethnic groups, Highlanders peoples who had no contact with the outside world before the 1930s-1950s ("first contact" of the Highlands in 1933 by the Leahy brothers = extraordinary documentary expeditions). Country shared between independent 1975 (formerly Australian colony 1949-1975) east of the island of New Guinea + West Papua in the west = territory occupied/annexed by Indonesia 1969 (international controversy, OPM separatist movement repressed). June-July are my favourite months (4.9-5.0/10): 7-14°C, 85-92% rain, cool altitude temperatures for Highlands. PNG never tops 5.0/10 in its Highlands due to humid alpine tropical climate. From June to October, it's the relative dry season "tradewinds" south-east trade winds: 7-15°C Highlands, 24-32°C coasts. From November to May, it's the tropical wet season: 89-96% rainfall, stable temperatures 8-14°C Highlands. Highlights / Regions: Port Moresby capital (on the south coast, modest capital 400,000 inhabitants, high crime, little touristic in itself), Mount Hagen central Highlands ("cultural capital" of the Highlands, access base for iconic tribal festivals), Goroka Show (annual cultural festival September at Goroka Highlands, 100+ tribes present their sing-sings = ceremonial tribal dances in spectacular traditional costumes "bilas" feathers-shells-body paintings + ritual masks, one of the largest tribal festivals in the world), Mount Hagen Show (other major festival August), Trobriand Islands (preserved Melanesian archipelago, peoples famous for matrilineal culture and permissive sexual customs documented by anthropologist Bronisław Malinowski 1922 = pioneer modern anthropology), Sepik River (the longest river in PNG 1126 km, cruise between preserved tribal villages with stilt architecture + ritual masks of the spirit house spirit house iconic cultural), Kokoda Track (96 km WWII trail through the Highlands, place of the Battle of Kokoda 1942 between Australians and Japanese = major Australian military memory, 8-10 day trek for WWII enthusiasts and experienced adventurers), Rabaul and Tavurvur Volcano on East New Britain Island (active volcano that destroyed Rabaul 1994, post-apocalyptic atmosphere, top world diving on Japanese WWII wrecks in Simpson Harbour). iconic activities: Tufi diving and Madang (top world for fjords + reefs + WWII wrecks), tribal sing-sings (cultural festivals), birds of paradise birds of paradise endemic 39 species unique in the world with spectacular feathers (one of the last great world sites for ornithology), Kokoda Track trekking. Safety: increased France Diplomacy vigilance for PNG = very high urban crime in Port Moresby (rascals = armed gangs), possible tribal tensions in the Highlands, organised travel with experienced local guides mandatory, never travel alone. Try mumu (pork cooked in stone oven-pit iconic Melanesian heritage of ceremonial sing-sings), kau kau (sweet potato Highlands food base), saksak (sago palm), grilled fish, betel nut ("buai" traditional chewed stimulant ubiquitous in PNG). Port Moresby Jacksons airport (POM), flights from Brisbane Australia + Cairns + Singapore.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
