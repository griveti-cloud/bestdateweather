Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

The Lofoten Islands, Norwegian archipelago beyond the Arctic Circle: 7 main islands connected by bridges, 24,000 inhabitants, the most spectacular landscapes of Norway (1000m mountains plunging into the sea, colourful fishing villages). July is my favourite month (7.5/10): 11-16°C, 44% rain, this is the "midnight sun" (from 28 May to 14 July, no sunset for 7 weeks), hikes, kayaking, fishing. From June to August, expect 8-16°C: summer high season (few tourists outside cruises), Reine and Hamnøy are the most photographed villages (packed in July). From September to March, expect -5/7°C and 47-61% precipitation often as snow: this is the Northern Lights season (Lofoten = one of the best spots in the world, visible as soon as it's dark AND sky clear, which is rare in winter Lofoten), cod fishing (skrei) in March (the gold of the Norwegian sea, traditional fishing continued for 1000 years). From December to January, it's the polar night (no sun above the horizon for 4 weeks, but magnificent blue light). Highlights: Reine (the most famous village, iconic photo from Reinebringen 448m, 1500 wooden steps renovated 2018), Hamnøy and its red rorbuer (fishermen's cabins on stilts), Henningsvær ("Venice of the Lofoten", bridge since 1981), Kvalvika Beach (1h hike, white sand), Ramberg (the most accessible beach). Activities: kayaking between the islands, climbing, fishing, Munkebu/Reinebringen hike. Try bacalao, fish soup, brunost (brown goat cheese). Logistics: Oslo-Bodø flight + Bodø-Moskenes ferry 4h or direct Oslo-Leknes/Svolvær flight.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
