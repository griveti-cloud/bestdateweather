Context

Our verdict

Macau, former Portuguese colony (1557-1999), Special Administrative Region of China since 1999: 690,000 inhabitants over 33 km², 4th highest population density in the world. World capital of gambling (casino revenues 7x higher than Las Vegas pre-Covid). November is my favourite month (8.5/10): 20-25°C, 23% rain, ideal temperatures before winter. From October to December, it's the high season: 15-28°C, mild climate. From June to September, it's the hot/humid season: 65-88% rainfall, oppressive humidity (90%), frequent typhoons (Macau is hit by 5-6 per season on average). From January to March, expect 15-23°C: mild but sky often grey. UNESCO heritage highlights (Historic Centre 30 monuments): Ruins of Saint Paul's (17th-century façade, iconic of Macau), Largo do Senado (Portuguese paved square), A-Ma Temple (origin of the name "Macau" = Mât de A-Ma, Ma'Gao in Chinese), Na Tcha Temple, Fortaleza do Monte, Mandarin's House. Modern casinos: Cotai Strip (the "Asian Las Vegas" on island reclaimed from the sea linking Taipa and Coloane) with Venetian Macao (the largest casino in the world, indoor gondolas), City of Dreams, Wynn Palace, Grand Lisboa, MGM. Try Macanese fusion cuisine: galinha à portuguesa (curry chicken), pork with shrimp paste, pastéis de nata (Portuguese egg tarts, origin here then Lisbon), almond cookies. Logistics: Macau 1h by ferry from Hong Kong (TurboJet), direct flight from Bangkok/Tokyo/Manila. Visa issued on arrival for Europeans (90 days). Pataca (MOP) pegged to HKD.
