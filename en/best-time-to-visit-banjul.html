Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Banjul, capital of The Gambia on the Atlantic coast of West Africa: 31,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 400,000 in urban area with Serekunda. Smallest country in continental Africa = 11,300 km², 2.5 million inhabitants, micro-state surrounded by Senegal on 3 sides + 80 km Atlantic frontage. English-speaking country (British colonial heritage 1888-1965) at the heart of francophone West Africa. More peaceful and accessible atmosphere for Western visitors ("The Smiling Coast of Africa" official nickname, one of the most welcoming African destinations for tourism). March-April-May are my favourite months (9.3/10): 21-30°C, 0-1% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January stays excellent (9.0/10). From November to May, it's the tourist high season: 21-32°C, pleasant dry and hot climate, refreshing Atlantic trade winds, harmattan Saharan wind December-February = relative cold and dusty. From June to October, it's the rainy season (West African monsoon "hivernage"): 11-77% rainfall. Highlights: Arch 22 (Arch 22, 35m high monument 1996 commemorating the coup of 22 July 1994 of President Yahya Jammeh = controversial memorial atmosphere as Jammeh became authoritarian), Albert Market (traditional West African central market, wax pagne fabrics, iconic Gambian sculpted wood, batik), National Museum of The Gambia, Notre-Dame Cathedral of the Assumption, Historic Banjul Quarter (preserved British colonial old town), Atlantic beaches = the main Gambian tourist attraction: Senegambia Beach + Kotu Beach + Cape Point 15-30 km from Banjul, golden sand, cool Atlantic water 22-25°C thanks to the cold Canary current, moderate surf. Essential Gambia excursions: Abuko Reserve 1h away (the most accessible of the Gambian wildlife reserves, observation vervet monkeys-crocodiles-iguanas, simplified African experience for families), James Island/Kunta Kinteh Island (UNESCO 2003, former 16th-19th century transatlantic slave embarkation port, popularised worldwide by the novel "Roots" by Alex Haley 1976 telling the story of his ancestor Kunta Kinteh kidnapped in The Gambia 1767, place of African-American Roots Heritage Tour pilgrimage, island eroded by the sea in progressive submersion), Juffureh (Kunta Kinteh's village of origin according to Roots, memorial atmosphere), Makasutu Cultural Forest (private forest reserve, ecotourism), River Gambia National Park (river cruise on the Gambia river crossing the whole country = "Gambia Bird Trail" one of the best bird-watching spots in West Africa, 600 bird species including endemic Abyssinian hornbill). Tourist particularity: The Gambia has become famous for "bumster" tourism = improvised local guides soliciting Western tourists on the beaches, sometimes male prostitution towards Northern European women (mainly British), significant social phenomenon since the 1990s. Try domoda (chicken peanut sauce, Gambian national dish), benachin (spiced fish rice), yassa (chicken with onion lemon, neighbouring Senegalese heritage), thieboudienne (West African iconic fish rice), bissap (hibiscus juice), JulBrew Beer (Gambian national beer). Banjul Yundum airport (BJL).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
