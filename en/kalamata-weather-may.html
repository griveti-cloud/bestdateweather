Context

Our verdict

Kalamata, capital of western Peloponnese in Greece: 70,000 inhabitants on the Messenian Gulf. World capital of Kalamata olives (variety of black olive in the shape of an almond, with fleshy flesh, AOC since 1996 = the first olive AOC in Greece, exported worldwide) and Greek olive oil. Rebuilt after devastating 1986 earthquake (20 deaths, 80% of old buildings destroyed). June is my favourite month (9.1/10): 19-29°C, 16% rain, sea at 21°C, ideal temperatures. September stays excellent (8.9/10). July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (22-33°C). From April to October, it's the tourist high season: 10-33°C. From November to March, expect 6-20°C and 33-40% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, ideal for visiting without crowds. Kalamata highlights: Old town (Kastro, on hill, 13th-15th century Venetian-Frankish castle, panorama), Kalamata Cathedral (Agios Apostoloi), Kalamata urban beach (3 km of pebbles), Central market (local institution to taste the Kalamata olives directly from the producers and IGP Kalamata dried figs), Military museum, Traditional costume museum. Essential Peloponnese excursions: Mystras 1h away (UNESCO, former Byzantine capital of the despotate of Morea 13th-15th, one of the most beautiful Byzantine medieval cities, impressive ruins on hill, 30 monasteries and palaces), Sparta 1h away (legendary ancient city-state, few visible vestiges), Olympia 2h away (UNESCO, place of the ancient Olympic Games 776 BC to 393 AD, palaestra, gymnasium, temple of Zeus, exceptional museum), Mycenae 2h away (UNESCO, capital of Agamemnon in the Iliad, Lion Gate, Treasury of Atreus), Epidaurus 3h away (UNESCO, best preserved ancient theatre in the world, exceptional acoustics still used for theatre in summer), Gythio and Mani peninsula 1h away ("Mani", the wildest region of Greece, unique stone Maniote towers, preserved beaches, austere atmosphere), Monemvasia 2h30 away ("Eastern Gibraltar", medieval fortified town on islet connected by sand strip, unique atmosphere in the Mediterranean, one of the postcard images of the Peloponnese), Pylos and Methoni 1h away (Venetian fortresses, Bay of Navarino where the Ottoman fleet was destroyed in 1827 = the decisive event of the Greek war of independence). Beaches: Voidokilia 1h away near Pylos (beach in regular arc circle in a semi-circular bay, one of the most beautiful beaches in Greece and Europe according to many rankings). Try IGP Kalamata olives (the famous black almond-shaped olives, to taste at the local producers), IGP Kalamata olive oil (the most prestigious in Greece with that of Crete), IGP Kalamata dried figs, thyme honey, lalagia (sweet beignets in olive oil), Mantineia AOC moscofilero white wine. Kalamata airport (KLX) with charter flights from Europe in season.
