Maseru, capital of Lesotho in southern Africa: 330,000 inhabitants at 1600m altitude on the Caledon river bordering South Africa. Only country in the world entirely above 1000m altitude ("Kingdom of the Sky", highest point Thabana Ntlenyana 3482m), royal enclave entirely surrounded by South Africa. Constitutional kingdom under King Letsie III since 1996, majority Basotho people = historic warriors with mokorotlo hat = national cultural iconic + traditional Basotho blankets. September-October are my favourite months (8.8/10): 7-26°C, 11-20% rain, this is the ideal austral spring. January-November-December stay excellent (8.4-8.5/10). From September to March, it's the hot summer season: 12-27°C with altitude storms. From May to August, it's the cool winter season: 0-21°C with snow at altitude (Lesotho has the only ski resort in sub-Saharan Africa = Afriski at 3050m, season June-August). Unique permanent climate: Maseru never tops 27°C in maximum average and descends to 0°C in minimum average in winter, exceptional cold-temperate altitude climate for Africa. Highlights: Maseru Centre (modest capital, relaxed Basotho atmosphere), Central market (Basotho craftsmanship, iconic pointed mokorotlo hats, colourful Basotho blankets), Royal Palace (royal palace, no visit access but exterior photo), Notre-Dame des Victoires Cathedral, Cathedral of the Holy Trinity, Khotso House Quarter (Father of the Nation memorial), Lephole Pony Trekking (tours on Basuto ponies = endemic breed of robust ponies adapted to Lesothan mountains, iconic experience). Essential Lesotho excursions = this is why one comes to Lesotho: Sani Pass ("highest drivable road in sub-Saharan Africa" 2876m, southern border with South Africa, vertiginous switchback road in the Drakensberg, accessible by mandatory 4x4, 360° panorama over Lesothan mountains at the summit, Sani Mountain Lodge "highest pub in Africa"), Maletsunyane Falls (192m, one of the highest single-drop waterfalls in Africa, accessible by 6h hike or heliport, abseil rappel descent), Thaba-Bosiu ("Mountain of the Night", birthplace of the Lesotho kingdom with King Moshoeshoe I 19th century, considered the spiritual cradle of the Basotho nation), Lesothan Drakensberg (UNESCO 2000 Maloti-Drakensberg Park cross-border with South Africa, San rock paintings = prehistoric Bushmen art 4000 years, hiking), Katse Dam (the largest dam in sub-Saharan Africa 185m, hydroelectric project that exports water to Johannesburg), Afriski Mountain Resort (3050m, only sub-Saharan ski resort in Africa with Tiffindell South Africa, modest 3 pistes but unique experience). Try papa (iconic Sotho corn porridge), moroho (green vegetables), nyama (grilled meat), seswaa (sliced neighbouring Botswanan beef), mokoenya (Sotho beignet), local Maluti Beer. Logistics: Moshoeshoe I International Maseru airport (MSU), also accessible by car from Johannesburg South Africa 5h via Maseru Bridge border.

How we calculate the score

For mountain destinations, we calculate two scores per month — skiing and hiking — and display the higher of the two.

⛷️Ski score DEC → MAY
  • 40% snow cover (slope T° corrected by lapse rate -6.5°C/1000m)
  • 40% winter thermal comfort (-5 to 5°C optimal)
  • 20% sunshine
  • Off-season penalty: ×0.70 adjacent months · ×0.35 outside season
🥾Hiking score JUN → SEP
  • 40% temperature (10–22°C optimal)
  • 40% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 20% sunshine

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Maseru?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: July (ski score 2.4/10). For hiking/summer: August (9.2/10, 19.0°C).
Can you ski in Maseru in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 2.0/10. Cold temperatures (27°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Maseru in summer?
In August, temperatures average 19.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Maseru?
January is the wettest month with January has the lowest climate score of the year.
