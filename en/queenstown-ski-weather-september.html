Context

Our verdict

Queenstown Ski ("Coronet Peak" and "The Remarkables"), ski areas near Queenstown on the South Island of New Zealand in the Otago region: no permanent population (Queenstown base-town 35,000 inhabitants 30 min from the resorts). One of the main ski destinations in the southern hemisphere with Thredbo-Perisher Australia, spectacular alpine-lake landscapes around Lake Wakatipu. Southern hemisphere ski season = June-October. February is my favourite month (7.7/10): 6-22°C, 18% rain, ideal temperatures in austral summer. January-March stay excellent (7.6-7.7/10). From December to April, it's the summer season: 1-22°C, Routeburn-Milford-Greenstone-Caples hiking, mountain bike, jet boat on the Shotover River = Queenstown iconic, bungee jumping (Kawarau Bridge bungee = birthplace of world tourist bungee 1988 by AJ Hackett), parachute jump. From June to October, it's ski season: -1/9°C and 23-43% precipitation often as snow. Pistes: 280 ha combined (Coronet Peak 280 ha + The Remarkables 220 ha + Cardrona 1h away + Treble Cone 1h away, one of the largest southern hemisphere ski areas), altitude 1187-1957m (The Remarkables summit 2381m but ski to 1957m), vertical drop 437m. Coronet Peak (the closest to Queenstown 25 min, iconic night skiing Thursdays-Saturdays 4pm-9pm under floodlights), The Remarkables (40 min, more difficult and higher in altitude, spectacular panoramas over Queenstown + Lake Wakatipu). iconic pistes: Greengates ("The Big One" Coronet Peak black descent), Sugar Bowl The Remarkables off-piste accessible. Off-piste: Queenstown is one of the most respected southern hemisphere heli-ski bases, operators Harris Mountains Heliski + Methven Heliski offering virgin descents in the New Zealand Southern Alps backcountry (July-September seasons), high prices 800-1500 NZD/day. Queenstown town: nicknamed "world capital of adventure", young-festive cosmopolitan atmosphere (international clientele 70% foreign, especially British and Australians), dynamic restaurants, nightlife. Activities: Shotover jet boat on the rapids at 80 km/h in narrow canyons, Kawarau Bridge bungee jumping (43m, the 1st commercial in the world 1988), Queenstown skydive, TSS Earnslaw cruise (1912 steamer on Lake Wakatipu to Walter Peak Sheep Station = visit to typical New Zealand sheep farm), Lord of the Rings tours (Glenorchy filming locations Isengard-Lothlorien-Argonath). Essential excursions: Milford Sound 4h away (UNESCO Te Wahipounamu, spectacular fjord nicknamed "8th wonder of the world" by Rudyard Kipling, Milford Sound cruise + iconic Stirling Falls waterfall), Wanaka 1h away (peaceful neighbouring lake, more traditional atmosphere), Mt Cook National Park 3h away (highest point Aoraki 3724m), Fox Glacier and Franz Josef Glacier 5h away (spectacular west coast). Try New Zealand lamb (the best in the world), New Zealand king salmon, lamb pie, Pavlova (national dessert), L&P (kiwi lemonade), craft beers Speights/Mac's, New Zealand wines Pinot Noir Central Otago + Sauvignon Blanc Marlborough. Queenstown airport (ZQN), domestic flights + direct Australia Sydney-Brisbane-Melbourne.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Queenstown Ski?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: August (ski score 8.6/10). For hiking/summer: August (8.6/10, 7.0°C).
Can you ski in Queenstown Ski in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 1.2/10. Cold temperatures (21°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Queenstown Ski in summer?
In August, temperatures average 7.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Queenstown Ski?
May is the wettest month with May has the lowest climate score of the year.
