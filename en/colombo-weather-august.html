Context

Our verdict

Colombo, economic capital of Sri Lanka on the west coast: 750,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 5.6 million in the urban area (Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte = official administrative capital next door). Sri Lanka emerged from a bloody civil war in 2009 (26 years between the Sinhalese majority and the Tamil LTTE minority), reconstruction since with massive Chinese investment (artificial Port City designed in the sea, 2014-2030). January-December are my favourite months (6.9/10): 23-30°C, 43-58% rain, this is the relative dry season. Colombo never tops 6.9/10 (humid equatorial tropical climate year-round). From December to March, it's the tourist high season: 23-31°C, sea at 28°C. From May to September, it's the south-west monsoon: 88-97% rainfall in Colombo (but the eastern monsoon = dry season for the east coast Trincomalee/Arugam Bay), stable temperature 25-29°C. From October to November, it's the inter-monsoon: 82-92% rainfall. Highlights: Galle Face Green (long seaside promenade, local atmosphere at sunset, street vendors, kites, many Sinhalese families, Indian Ocean view), Galle Face Hotel (one of East Asia's oldest hotels 1864, British colonial heritage, Veranda bar an institution), Pettah (chaotic central bazaar, spice merchants, vegetable market, Eastern atmosphere), Jami Ul-Alfar Mosque in Pettah ("Red Mosque" 1908, iconic red-and-white Islamic brickwork façade), Wolvendaal Cathedral (1749, Dutch colonial heritage), Independence Memorial Hall (independence memorial 1948), National Museum of Colombo (national museum, traditional masks, Buddhist statues), Lotus Tower (350m TV tower inaugurated 2019 financed by China, the tallest construction in South Asia, tourist panorama), Gangaramaya Temple (eclectic Buddhist temple with various Asian dependencies), Cinnamon Gardens (chic residential district of the Colombo elite). Excursions: Kandy 3h away by spectacular train (UNESCO, last royal Sinhalese capital), Galle 2h by train (UNESCO, fortified Dutch town 17th century), Negombo 1h away (urban beach + airport), Mount Lavinia 30 min away (Colombo suburb, accessible urban beach), Yala National Park 5h away (leopard park, one of the highest densities in the world). Try kottu roti (national dish, chopped paratha with vegetables-meat-spices), egg hopper ("appa" fermented rice pancake with fried egg), fish curry in coconut milk, string hoppers (vermicelli), pol sambol (coconut chutney), local Ceylon tea (the country is one of the world's biggest tea producers), local Lion Lager beer. Colombo airport (CMB).
