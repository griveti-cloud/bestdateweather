Context

Our verdict

Perisher, largest ski resort in Australia and the southern hemisphere: no significant permanent population, 100% tourist resort in Kosciuszko National Park (NSW). Largest skiable area in Australia (in competition with Treble Cone New Zealand for the southern hemisphere crown), 4 connected villages: Perisher Valley, Smiggin Holes, Blue Cow, Guthega. Short ski season June-September. January-February are my favourite months (7.6/10): 8-19°C, 43-50% rain, ideal temperatures in austral summer. Perisher never tops 7.6/10 (austral alpine climate with narrow season). From December to April, it's the summer season: 4-19°C, Snowy Mountains hiking, mountain bike (developed network of mountain bike trails in summer), fishing in alpine lakes. From June to September, it's ski season: -3/3°C and 42-46% precipitation often as snow. Pistes: 100 km Perisher Resort (the largest area in Australia, 5x larger than neighbouring Thredbo), altitude 1605-2034m, vertical drop 429m (more modest than Thredbo). iconic pistes: Front Valley (family, ideal for beginners), Mount Perisher (summit 2034m), Olympic (red), Pretty Valley. Strengths: 4 villages connected by Skitube = unique Australian underground tunnel train 6 km long to connect the base of the mountain (Bullocks Flat) to the heart of the area (Perisher Valley) avoiding icy roads in winter, unique experience in a ski resort (similar to Funivia del Ghiacciaio at Cervinia but underground). No off-piste officially authorised in Australia (protected national park), but local skiers occasionally practise back-country with skins. Audience: Australians in the majority (Sydney 6h by car, Melbourne 6h, Canberra 2h30), families with children attracted by the Perisher Snowsports ski school (one of the largest ski schools in the world with 600 instructors), few Western foreigners (prefer Aspen Vail Park City Niseko Hokkaido for flights from Asia or Pacific). Cost: high prices ($170-250 AUD/day pass + $400-700 AUD/night hotel), often considered "expensive for what one has" compared to northern hemisphere. Hotels: no in-resort hotels (the NSW Government refused construction of hotels in the national park), all accommodation is in the neighbouring village Jindabyne 30 min away or Bullocks Flat at the foot of the Skitube, which is a major drawback of Perisher vs Thredbo which has hotels at the foot of the slopes. Excursions: Mt Kosciuszko 2228m highest point of continental Australia, Thredbo 40 min away, Jindabyne Lake (kayaking in summer), Canberra 2h30 away, Sydney 6h away. Try meat pie, Pavlova, Australian fries ("chips" in Australia), Aussie beers. Logistics: Canberra airport (CBR) 2h30 away, Cooma-Snowy Mountains airport (COA) 1h30 away the closest, access from Sydney 6h by car, from Melbourne 6h.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Perisher?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: July (ski score 8.2/10). For hiking/summer: July (8.2/10, 2.0°C).
Can you ski in Perisher in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 0.9/10. Cold temperatures (19°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Perisher in summer?
In July, temperatures average 2.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Perisher?
May is the wettest month with May has the lowest climate score of the year.
