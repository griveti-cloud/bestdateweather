Context

Our verdict

Jeju (Jeju-do), volcanic island of South Korea 100 km south of the mainland: 670,000 inhabitants over 1849 km² (Korea's largest island), UNESCO-listed 2007 (lava-tube volcanoes and unique ecosystem), reputed as "the honeymoon island" for Korean couples. May is my favourite month (7.9/10): 15-22°C, 36% rain, ideal temperatures, cherry blossoms (sakura, southern Korea blooms late March-early April, Jeju the first ones in early March). From May to September, it's the tourist high season: 15-30°C. From July to August, expect 25-30°C with 57-59% rain: heavy and humid heat, but also Korean tourist peak (school holidays). From December to March, expect 2-13°C and 40-46% rain: mild for Korea (Jeju is the warmest island in Korea), snow possible on Hallasan in winter. Highlights: Mount Hallasan (1947m, the highest peak in South Korea, dormant volcano with crater lake, 9 km ascent via Seongpanak or Gwaneumsa trail, 4-5h up, sometimes closed in winter for snow), Manjanggul (UNESCO, one of the world's longest lava tubes at 7.4 km, 1 km accessible to visitors with lanterns, cool 11°C constant year-round), Seongsan Ilchulbong ("Sunrise Peak", UNESCO, 5000-year-old volcanic crater on the extreme east, 182m, 30-min ascent, spectacular sunrise view), Jusangjeolli Cliff (basalt cliffs in geological hexagonal columns), Cheonjeyeon Falls and Jeongbang Falls (waterfalls), Cheonjiyeon Waterfall, Olle Trails (network of 425 km of coastal trails in 27 segments, popularised since 2007 on the model of the Camino de Santiago), Beaches: Hyeopjae (white sand, turquoise water), Hamdeok, Jungmun. Cultural haenyeo phenomenon ("women of the sea", apnea divers without equipment who fish abalone, sea urchins, abalone up to 70-80 years old, millennia-old matriarchal tradition UNESCO 2016, in the process of disappearing — 4500 in the 1960s, fewer than 4000 today of whom 60% are over 70). Try heuk dwaeji (Jeju black pork), abalone porridge, hallabong (Jeju mandarin), omija tea. Direct Seoul-Jeju flight in 1h (one of the busiest air routes in the world, 80,000 flights/year).
