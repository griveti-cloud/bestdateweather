Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Hoi An is the UNESCO former merchant trading post of central Vietnam: yellow Japanese and Chinese houses, colourful lanterns, beach 5 km away. February is my favourite month (7.9/10): 20-25°C, 49% rain, end of the dry season before the heat. From March to May, expect 22-32°C and 25-44% rain: mild, ideal for An Bang beach. From September to December, it's the rainy/typhoon season: 65-85% rainfall, flooding possible in the old town (the Thu Bon river overflows, the city becomes navigable by sampan, photogenic but disruptive). From April to August, often clear sky but heat (26-34°C). Highlights: Japanese Covered Bridge, Tan Ky House, Cantonese Assembly Hall, Central Market. Tailors everywhere (made-to-measure in 24-48h, suit 80-150$, variable quality). Cooking classes very popular (at Madame Vy, Red Bridge). My Son 1h away (Hindu Cham ruins). An Bang beach or Cua Dai (severe erosion since 2010). Try cao lau (specialty), white rose (dumplings), banh mi Phuong (the best in Vietnam according to Bourdain).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
