Tripoli (Tarabulus), capital of Libya on the Mediterranean coast: 1.2 million inhabitants. Founded by the Phoenicians 7th century BC under the name Oea, one of the three "Tripolis" cities ("3 cities" in Greek) with Sabratha and Leptis Magna forming the Roman province of Tripolitania. Capital of Libya Ottoman then Italian 1911-1943 (Italian colonial heritage still visible) then independent 1951 under King Idris then under Muammar Gaddafi 1969-2011 = 42-year authoritarian reign ended by 2011 revolution under NATO bombings, since = chronic civil war between rival factions. June is my favourite month (9.5/10): 19-29°C, 1% rain, this is the ideal Mediterranean spring. May-October stay excellent (8.7-9.0/10). From April to June and September-October, these are the best tourist seasons: 13-31°C, sea at 22-26°C, ideal Mediterranean climate. From July to August, expect 22-32°C with possible heatwave ghibli = hot dry Saharan wind that can raise temperatures to 45°C+. From November to March, expect 8-19°C and 17-32% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, rare cold. Highlights: Medina of Tripoli (preserved Ottoman-Italian atmosphere, one of the most beautiful medinas in North Africa, labyrinthine alleys, traditional souks), Arch of Marcus Aurelius (165 AD, Roman vestige in the heart of the medina), Gurgi Mosque (18th-century Ottoman), Karamanli Mosque (18th century), Tripoli Cathedral (1923 Italian heritage transformed into Gamal Abdel Nasser Mosque 1970), Red Castle / As-Saraya Al-Hamra (Ottoman palace and seat of government, one of the best archaeological museums in Libya before the war, Roman-Punic masterpieces), Martyrs' Square (former Gaddafi Green Square renamed 2011), Arch of Septimius Severus (Roman vestige), Tripoli Beach (urban Mediterranean). Major Roman archaeological sites Libya = this is why Libya was visited before 2011: Leptis Magna 130 km to the east (UNESCO 1982, one of the most beautiful Roman cities in the world, birthplace of Emperor Septimius Severus 145-211 AD who transformed it into a monumental imperial city, theatre + amphitheatre + arch of Septimius Severus + baths + forum in exceptional state of preservation thanks to burial by Saharan sand until the 19th century = one of the largest Roman archaeological sites in the world rivalling Pompeii-Ephesus), Sabratha 70 km to the west (UNESCO 1982, other Roman-Punic city with exceptional 3-level theatre and temples), Cyrene 1000 km to the east (UNESCO 1982, Greek then Roman city in eastern Libya, in the Cyrenaica region), Ghadamès in the south-west (UNESCO 1986, Saharan oasis with unique-in-the-world troglodyte Berber architecture, abandoned and in danger). Safety: inadvisable France Diplomacy for the entirety of Libya = civil war since 2011, 2 rival governments (Tripoli vs Benghazi), armed militias, Western kidnappings, Islamist terrorism, extremely inadvisable travel. Try Libyan couscous, bazin (iconic barley flour paste with spicy sauce), shorba arabia (barley soup), Tunisian-Libyan brik, baklava, mint tea. Tripoli Mitiga airport (MJI).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
