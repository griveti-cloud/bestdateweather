Context

Our verdict

Bari, capital of Italian Puglia and Italy's 9th city: 320,000 inhabitants on the Adriatic coast. Major port for crossings to Greece and Albania, strategic stopover for visiting Puglia (Alberobello UNESCO trulli, Lecce baroque, Matera). June is my favourite month (9.4/10): 20-29°C, 18% rain, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to heat (22-31°C, sometimes 38°C with sirocco). September stays excellent (9.0/10). From November to March, expect 6-18°C and 28-36% rain: mild for southern Italy, ideal for visiting without crowds. Highlights: Bari Vecchia (labyrinthine fortified old town, narrow alleys, atmosphere more Sicilian than Italian, women making orecchiette by hand on boards in front of their houses on Via Arco Basso), Basilica of Saint Nicholas (Basilica di San Nicola, 11th-century Romanesque, houses the relics of Saint Nicholas of Myra brought back by 47 sailors of Bari in 1087 from present-day Turkey, place of Orthodox and Catholic pilgrimage as Saint Nicholas is the origin of Father Christmas), Saint Sabin Cathedral (12th-century Romanesque), Castello Normanno-Svevo (Frederick II 13th century), Lungomare (one of Italy's longest seaside promenades, 13 km), Petruzzelli Theatre (Italy's 4th largest theatre). Essential Puglia excursions: Alberobello 1h away (UNESCO, village of trulli = conical dry-stone houses iconic of Puglia), Matera 1h away (UNESCO, troglodyte city of the sassi inhabited continuously for 9000 years, European Capital of Culture 2019), Polignano a Mare 30 min away (perched village on cliffs with iconic coves), Ostuni (white city), Lecce 1h30 away (baroque), Castel del Monte 1h away (UNESCO, octagonal Frederick II castle), Trani 30 min away (iconic Romanesque cathedral by the sea), Gargano (peninsula to the north, Vieste). Try orecchiette (ear-shaped pasta), focaccia barese, sgagliozze (polenta fries), panzerotti (stuffed turnover), burrata (originating in Puglia), Primitivo and Negroamaro wines. Bari airport (BRI).
