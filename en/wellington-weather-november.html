What to expect in November

Autumn in Wellington is often underrated: 17.0°C, golden light and declining crowds. An interesting window. Expect rain on 39.0% of days — a light raincoat in the bag is recommended. A good balance between weather and crowds, even if January (8.3/10) remains theoretically better.

Context

Our verdict

Wellington, the New Zealand capital: 215,000 inhabitants, the windiest city in the world ("Windy Welly", average annual wind 27 km/h), intense café culture, cultural scene. January-February are my favourite months (8.1-8.3/10): 15-20°C, 26% rain, austral summer. From June to August (austral winter), expect 9-13°C and 41-43% rain: mild but humid and windy, days at 9h of light. Highlights: Te Papa (National Museum, free, one of the best museums in Oceania), Mount Victoria (view over the bay), Cable Car up to Kelburn and the Botanic Garden, Cuba Street (bohemian pedestrian street, cafés), Weta Workshop (special effects from Lord of the Rings, The Hobbit, Avatar: 30-min visit). Wellington Movie Tour on the Lord of the Rings sets. Zealandia (eco-restored sanctuary, NZ wildlife). Marlborough vineyards 3h away (by ferry to South Island, Sauvignon blanc capital), Kapiti Coast 1h away, Martinborough wineries 1h30 away (Pinot Noir). Inter-Islander Ferry Wellington-Picton (3h30, Cook Strait, one of the most beautiful crossings in the world). Try flat white, fish and chips, hokey pokey. Wellington has the highest density of cafés/restaurants per inhabitant in the world.
