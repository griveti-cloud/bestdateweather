Context

Our verdict

Khiva, 3rd museum-city of Uzbekistan on the Silk Road: 90,000 inhabitants in the heart of the Kyzyl Kum desert, 35 km from the Turkmen border. UNESCO 1990 for its Itchan Kala (fortified old town), considered the most intact museum-city in Central Asia (old town entirely preserved intra-muros, almost like a frozen 19th-century film set). September is my favourite month (9.6/10): 15-28°C, 2% rain, ideal temperatures after the heatwave. From May and September-October, these are the best seasons: 8-30°C, 2-13% rain. From June to August, it's the extreme heatwave: 22-38°C (July 38°C+), avoid July (3.9/10). From November to March, expect -3/11°C and 6-13% rain: dry continental cold. Highlights (almost all concentrated in the fortified Itchan Kala 26 hectares, 2 km of mud-brick ramparts): Kalta Minor ("short minaret", 1851, was meant to be 70m but stayed at 26m because Khan Mohammad Amin died before its completion, iconic of Khiva with its turquoise ceramic), Mohammed Amin Khan Madrasa (1855, next to the Kalta Minor, converted into a hotel), Kuhna Ark Citadel (residence of the khans 17th century), Djouma Mosque (18th century, frame on 213 sculpted black-elm wood columns, atmosphere out of time), Islom-Khoja Madrasa and its Minaret (1910, 56m, the tallest in Khiva, 118 narrow steps ascent, 360° panorama), Tash Hauli Palace (1830-1838, khan's harem, 163 rooms, sculpted wood and ceramic works), Pakhlavan Mahmud Mausoleum (poet and wrestler 1247-1326, revered as a local saint), Toza Bog Palace. Logistics: Khiva 11h by train from Tashkent (overnight train), 1h by plane to neighbouring Urgench + 30-min taxi. Classic "Silk Road" combination: Tashkent + Samarkand + Bukhara + Khiva = 8-10 days. Try shivit oshi (Khiva iconic green dill pasta), tukhum-barak (egg dumplings), plov, samsa, green tea.
