Monteverde (Cloud Forest Reserve), nature reserve in the centre of Costa Rica on the Tilarán Cordillera: 7000 inhabitants at 1300-1850m altitude. Tropical cloud forest = unique ecosystem where the clouds remain constantly hooked to the canopy creating a permanently humid microclimate that allows exceptional epiphytes and orchids. Reserve founded in 1972 by an American Quaker community come seeking a peaceful life away from US military service (Costa Rica has no army), today one of the most famous natural destinations in the world. March is my favourite month (8.0/10): 13-21°C, 27% rain, this is the ideal dry season at altitude. From December to April, it's the dry season "verano": 12-22°C with 21-46% rain. From May to November, it's the green season: 67-89% rainfall, but constant fogs and mists typical of the cloud forest, unique atmosphere. Permanent climate: Monteverde never tops 22°C in maximum average and rarely descends below 12°C in minimum average thanks to altitude 1300-1850m, ideal tropical temperate climate, cooler than the Pacific coast 30 min by car downhill (15°C difference in 30 min). Highlights: Monteverde Cloud Forest Biological Reserve (main reserve 10,500 hectares, accessible by paths, signature wildlife observation: resplendent quetzal = sacred bird of the Mayas-Aztecs with long green-red-blue feathers, signature of Monteverde, observable especially February-June during nesting, limited geographic zone Central America, considered one of the most beautiful birds in the world, presence guaranteed 30-50% of morning outings with guide), Sky Walk or Selvatura Park (suspension bridges through the canopy at 60m height, signature canopy experience of Monteverde), Zipline ("zipline", Monteverde is the cradle of the tourist canopy zipline concept invented here in 1979 by the American biologist Donald Perry to observe the canopy, today dozens of operators in Monteverde Selvatura/100% Aventura/Sky Trek, zip lines among the longest in the world 1500m+ with "Tarzan swing" descent), Curi-Cancha Reserve (more peaceful alternative with fewer tourists), Bat Jungle (interactive centre on bats), Frog Pond and Butterfly Garden (centres with local species), Selvatura Hummingbird Garden (gardens with hummingbirds in large numbers, several species including brilliant hummingbirds, facilitated photography). Sister forest: Santa Elena Cloud Forest Reserve (smaller municipal reserve but less frequented), Children's Eternal Rainforest (the largest private reserve in Costa Rica 23,000 hectares, founded 1988 by Swedish children to save the Costa Rican jungle, one of the most beautiful conservation initiatives in the world). History of the Quakers: the American pacifist Quaker community founded in Monteverde in 1951 (44 people initially) founded the dairy culture on the plateau (Monteverde Cheese factory institution since 1953, gouda and other cheeses = visit + tasting). Try Monteverde cheese, café Monteverde Coffee (signature Don Juan Coffee Tour plantation visit with demonstration of production stages), gallo pinto, casado, batidos. Logistics: Monteverde 4h from San José by car (partially rough road, ends in gravel track for the last 30 km = voluntary experience to preserve authenticity), no airport on site.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
