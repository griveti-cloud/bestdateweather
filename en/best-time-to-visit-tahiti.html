Tahiti, main island of French Polynesia: 200,000 inhabitants, capital Papeete, arrival base for Bora Bora, Moorea and other islands. August is my favourite month (7.8/10): 23-27°C, 65% rain, relative dry season. From May to October, it's the dry season: 65-78% rainfall (often in brief showers), temperature 23-28°C, sea at 26°C, refreshing trade winds. From November to April, it's the wet season: 78-94% rainfall, heavy heat (24-29°C), real cyclone risk (Olaf 2005, Oli 2010). Tahiti never tops 8/10: constant humid tropical-oceanic climate. Tahiti highlights: Papeete (central market, cultural scene), Lagonarium and Gauguin Museum, Trou du Souffleur at Arahoho, Faaruumai waterfalls, Teahupoo (world-renowned surf wave in the south, World Surf League stop, venue for the surfing events at the Paris 2024 Olympics held in Tahiti), lava tubes. Tahiti mostly serves as transit (1-2 days are enough) before reaching the real postcard destinations: Bora Bora, Moorea, Rangiroa, Fakarava, Marquesas. Try raw fish in coconut milk (national dish), poke, grilled mahi-mahi, fei (local banana). Paris-Papeete flight via Los Angeles (24h) or Vancouver (22h). Currency: Pacific franc (XPF, 119.33 XPF = 1 EUR fixed).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
