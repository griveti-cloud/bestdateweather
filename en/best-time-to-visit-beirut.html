Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Beirut, capital of Lebanon on the Mediterranean coast: 360,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 2.4 million in the urban area (50% of Lebanon's total population). Historically nicknamed the "Paris of the Middle East" for its cosmopolitanism and chic before the 1975-1990 civil war that destroyed the city. Reconstruction since 1990 but slowed by successive crises (Hariri assassination 2005, Israel-Hezbollah war 2006, catastrophic economic crisis 2019 with 95% devaluation of the Lebanese pound, port explosion on 4 August 2020 = 218 deaths, 7000 injured, one of the largest non-nuclear explosions in history, following 2750 tonnes of ammonium nitrate stored illegally). May-June are my favourite months (9.1-9.7/10): 18-29°C, 3-13% rain, ideal temperatures. September-October stay excellent (8.7-9.0/10). From March to November, it's the tourist high season: 11-30°C. From July to August drop to 6.9/10 due to heat (24-31°C, oppressive humidity). From December to February, expect 9-18°C and 39-46% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, snow possible in nearby mountains (1600m, skiing possible at Faraya Mzaar). Highlights: Corniche (4.8 km seaside promenade, lively atmosphere, joggers, fishermen, families, one of the symbolic places of recovered Beirut), Pigeon Rocks (Raouché Rocks, iconic rock formations in the sea facing the Corniche, photo stop), Reconstructed downtown (Beirut Central District = BCD, rebuilt in the 1990s-2000s by Solidere, private company of billionaire Rafic Hariri assassinated 2005, half-pastiche half-traditional atmosphere, Place de l'Étoile with parliament, Mohammad Al-Amin Mosque opened 2007 with iconic blue dome, Saint-Georges Greek-Orthodox Cathedral, Saint-Georges Maronite Church side by side = symbol of interfaith coexistence), Gemmayze district (rue Gouraud bohemian-trendy, bars-restaurants-art galleries, lively nightlife), Mar Mikhael (extension of Gemmayze, trendy since 2010-2015), Hamra (historic university district, AUB American University of Beirut campus among the most beautiful in the Middle East), Gouraud quarter (preserved 19th-20th century French colonial architecture), National Museum of Beirut (one of the most important archaeological museums in the Middle East, Phoenician sarcophagi), Sursock Museum (19th-century Arab Renaissance palace with contemporary Lebanese art collection, damaged by 2020 explosion but reopened 2023), Beirut archaeological site (Phoenician, Roman, Byzantine remains in the city centre). Lebanon excursions: Byblos 40 min away (UNESCO, one of the world's longest continuously inhabited cities, 7000 years), Baalbek 2h away (UNESCO, the world's best-preserved Roman sanctuary of Jupiter), Qadisha Valley 2h away (UNESCO, holy valley of Maronite monks, millennial cedars), Anjar 1h30 away (UNESCO, 8th-century Umayyad ruins), Tyre and Sidon in the south (Phoenician-Roman remains). Try Lebanese mezze (hummus, baba ghanouj, tabbouleh, fattoush, kibbeh), shawarma, knafeh, Arabic coffee with cardamom, Lebanese arak (anisette), Bekaa wines (legendary Château Musar). Safety: fragile political situation, sometimes violent protests, southern border with Israel a potential conflict zone (Hezbollah-Israel clashes).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
