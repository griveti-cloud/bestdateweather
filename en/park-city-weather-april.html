Context

Our verdict

Park City, Utah, is the former mining town reconverted into a premium ski resort, 35 minutes from Salt Lake City. July is my favourite month (9.8/10): 12-28°C, 12% rain, dry altitude climate (2100m), constantly blue sky. Ideal for hikes and mountain bike (50+ trails at Park City Mountain Bike Park). From December to March, expect -11/4°C: this is ski season. "The Greatest Snow on Earth" is the official slogan of Utah: the snow is dry and light here ("champagne powder") thanks to the dry continental climate and the Great Salt Lake effect. Park City Mountain (the largest US area, 7300 acres) + Deer Valley (ski only, no snowboard) + Canyons form a remarkable trio. Sundance Film Festival every January (former Robert Redford) fills the town for 10 days: prices x3-x4, bookings 1 year ahead. From April to May and October-November, it's the shoulder season: skiing closing, mountain biking not yet open. Direct flights from Europe via Salt Lake City.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Park City?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: February (ski score 8.8/10). For hiking/summer: September (9.2/10, 22.0°C).
Can you ski in Park City in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.6/10. Cold temperatures (-1°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Park City in summer?
In September, temperatures average 22.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Park City?
April is the wettest month with April has the lowest climate score of the year.
