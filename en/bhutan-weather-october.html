Context

Our verdict

Bhutan, small Himalayan kingdom between India and China in the foothills of the eastern Himalayas: 770,000 inhabitants. Last Himalayan Buddhist kingdom (Sikkim Tibet was annexed by India 1975, Tibet by China 1950). Country unique in the world measuring Gross National Happiness (GNH) instead of GDP since 1972 (philosophy of King Jigme Singye Wangchuck). Tourism strictly regulated: permit mandatory + minimum tariff 200-250 USD/day all inclusive (accommodation-meals-transport-guide-permit), commitment to "low volume - high value tourism" to preserve culture and environment (unique policy in the world). From November to December are my favourite months (5.0-5.3/10): 0-19°C, 6-9% rain, clear sky, ideal temperatures. Bhutan never tops 5.3/10 (Himalayan climate with monsoon and cold winter). From October to November and March-May, it's the tourist high season: -3/22°C, the clearest sky to see the Himalayan summits. From June to September, it's the monsoon: 49-72% rainfall, constant mists, greener landscapes. From December to February, expect -7/16°C: winter cold, snow possible at higher altitudes, many passes closed but high season of the Tiger of the Snows = trekking in the snow-covered Himalayas. Highlights: Tiger's Nest Monastery (Paro Taktsang, "Tiger's Nest Monastery", the most sacred Buddhist site in Bhutan, monastery perched at 3120m on vertical cliff 900m above the Paro valley, founded 1692 on the place where the Indian sage Padmasambhava allegedly meditated 3 years riding a tigress, 4-5h round-trip hike from 2600m, one of the most famous postcard images of Asia), Punakha Dzong ("fortress-monastery" of Punakha 17th century at the confluence of the Mo Chhu and Pho Chhu rivers, one of the most beautiful dzongs in Bhutan, former capital of the kingdom), Thimphu (capital, 100,000 inhabitants, one of the only capitals in the world without traffic lights — the white-gloved traffic agents have become iconic, Buddha Dordenma = gilded Buddha statue 51m on panoramic hill, Tashichho Dzong = royal palace and seat of government), Paro Valley (main tourist valley with unique airport, preserved bucolic atmosphere), Bumthang Valley (4 sacred valleys of central Bhutan, ancient monasteries), Trongsa Dzong (in the centre of the country, the largest dzong in Bhutan, monumental). Trekking: Snowman Trek = one of the most difficult treks in the world (24-25 days, 5 passes 5000m+, spectacular Himalayan landscapes). No McDonald's, Starbucks or KFC sign in Bhutan (strict cultural policy = no international chain accepted). Paro Airport: one of the most difficult airports to land in the world (only 8 pilots authorised, landing in turn in the middle of Himalayan mountains), direct flights from Delhi, Bangkok, Kathmandu only. Try ema datshi (national dish, chillies and local cheese), red rice (Bhutanese red rice), suja (Tibetan butter tea), local Druk 11000 beer.
