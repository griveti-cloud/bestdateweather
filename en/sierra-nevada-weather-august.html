Context

Our verdict

Sierra Nevada is Europe's southernmost ski resort and Spain's highest: Veleta summit 3398m, 30 km from Granada. July is my favourite month (9.3/10): 13-25°C, 13% rain, ideal sky for hiking to Mulhacén (3479m, the highest peak in mainland Spain). From December to March, expect -2/6°C and 26-45% precipitation often as snow: this is ski season, 100 km of pistes, modern resort (Pradollano at 2100m), reliable snow thanks to the altitude (north-facing). Particularity: you can ski in the morning and swim on the Costa del Sol in the afternoon (Granada-Malaga 1h30 by road). April to June and October-November are the off-seasons: skiing winding down (sometimes until late April), hiking not yet in shape. The Alpine Skiing World Cup returns regularly. Outside skiing: 3-5 day trek through the Alpujarras (south slope, Moorish villages of Pampaneira, Bubión, Capileira), Mulhacén ascent (15 km return from Hoya del Portillo, June-October). Try jamón de Trevélez (cured at altitude). Granada and Malaga are the logistics bases. Direct flight Granada or Malaga from Paris.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Sierra Nevada?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 8.6/10). For hiking/summer: July (8.9/10, 25.0°C).
Can you ski in Sierra Nevada in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.4/10. Cold temperatures (5°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Sierra Nevada in summer?
In July, temperatures average 25.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Sierra Nevada?
April is the wettest month with April has the lowest climate score of the year.
