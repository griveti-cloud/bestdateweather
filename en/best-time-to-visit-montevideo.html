Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Montevideo, capital of Uruguay: 1.3 million inhabitants (40% of the country's population, which has 3.4 million on 176,000 km² — one of the least populated countries in South America), on the Río de la Plata facing Buenos Aires (200 km by hydrofoil 2h15). Uruguay = the "Switzerland of Latin America" (politically stable, developed middle classes, security, progressive legalisations: the 1st country to legalise marijuana 2013, gay marriage 2013, divorce 1907). January is my favourite month (9.0/10): 20-26°C, 27% rain, this is the austral summer, ideal temperatures thanks to Atlantic breezes. From November to March, it's the high season: 16-26°C. From April to May and September-October are the transitions: 11-22°C. From June to August, it's the austral winter: 9-15°C, 25-29% rain, mild but windy. Highlights: Ciudad Vieja (18th-19th century old town, maze of alleys, Puerta de la Ciudadela former fortified gate, Mercado del Puerto = central market converted into asado parrillas restaurants), Plaza Independencia (main square that separates the colonial upper town and the modern lower town, statue of national hero José Artigas + his underground mausoleum, Palacio Salvo 1928 = former tallest building in South America 100m), Teatro Solís (1856, historic opera hall), Catedral Metropolitana, Rambla de Montevideo (22 km of promenade along the Río de la Plata, one of the longest urban seaside promenades in the world), Estadio Centenario (1930, place of the 1st Football World Cup won by Uruguay on its lawn, football museum), Pocitos and Carrasco (smart residential quarters with river urban beaches). Excursions: Punta del Este 2h away (the great smart Latin American beach resort, see dedicated review), Colonia del Sacramento 2h away (UNESCO, 17th-century Portuguese colonial town), Cabo Polonio 4h away (off-grid village by the ocean, sea lions). Try asado (real Uruguayan asado, slow long hours), chivito (Uruguayan iconic sandwich with steak/ham/egg/bacon/lettuce/tomato/mayonnaise), dulce de leche (controversial origin Uruguay/Argentina), mate (national Uruguayan mate more than anywhere, Uruguayans walk everywhere with their thermos of hot water and calabaza), Tannat wines (iconic Uruguayan grape).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
