Context

Our verdict

Uzbekistan, country in Central Asia on the ancient Silk Road: 35 million inhabitants. Former Soviet republic 1924-1991, massive tourist opening since 2017 under President Mirziyoyev (e-visa, removal of mandatory tourist registration). Country-treasure of the Silk Road myth-cities: Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva. September is my favourite month (9.4/10): 15-29°C, 5% rain, this is the ideal dry season after the strong summer heat. May stays excellent (9.2/10). From April to October, it's the tourist high season: 8-37°C. June-August drop to 6.9/10 due to extreme heatwave (20-37°C, sometimes 45°C+ Bukhara/Khiva). From November to March, expect -2/13°C and 22-32% rain: continental cold, snow possible Tashkent. Highlights: Samarkand (UNESCO 2001, one of the most beautiful cities in the world, capital of Tamerlane's empire 14th-15th, Registan Square = ensemble of 3 madrasas Tilla-Kari/Sher-Dor/Ulugh Beg on 3 sides iconic view, Gur-i Amir mausoleum tomb of Tamerlane, Ulugh Beg observatory 15th century one of the most advanced in the medieval world), Bukhara (UNESCO 1993, former capital of the emirate of Bukhara, 7 mosques-madrasas-minarets including Kalan Minaret 47m 12th century, Bolo Khauz Mosque, Ark fortress, Lyabi-Hauz ensemble, Po-i-Kalan), Khiva (UNESCO 1990, fortified open-air museum-city, one of the most completely preserved Silk Road cities, Itchan Kala = old town encircled by 2 km ramparts with 4 gates, unique atmosphere), Tashkent capital (3 million inhabitants, metro with Soviet decorated stations iconic, Chorsu market, Hazrat Imam mausoleum housing the oldest preserved Quran in the world 7th century), Fergana Valley (fertile agricultural region, Rishtan ceramic workshops, Margilan silks), Kyzylkum Desert (yurts in desert), Aral Sea (major ecological catastrophe, 90% disappeared since 1960 by Soviet diversion of the rivers, ghost ports Moynaq with boat carcasses in the sand). Try Uzbek plov (iconic carrot-mutton rice, national dish, UNESCO 2016), shashlik, samsa (oven turnover), lagman, manti, green or black tea, kazi (horse sausage). Tashkent (TAS) and Samarkand (SKD) airports.
