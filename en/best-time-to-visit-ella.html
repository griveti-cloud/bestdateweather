Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Ella, small mountain village in the heart of Sri Lanka in the Hill Country: 1500 inhabitants at 1041m altitude in the heart of the tea plantations. Has become one of the most popular stages of the Sri Lanka circuit for its picturesque train, its temperate climate and its accessible hikes. February-July are my favourite months (8.1-8.2/10): 16-28°C, 50-77% rain, ideal temperatures at altitude. From December to August, it's the tourist high season: 17-28°C, pleasant temperate climate despite occasional rain. From September to November, it's the 2nd inter-monsoon season: 65-89% rainfall, but stable temperatures (18-27°C, fresh in the morning/evening). Unique altitude climate: Ella never tops 28°C in maximum average and rarely descends below 16°C in minimum average, ideal tropical "Eternal Spring" climate that contrasts strongly with the stifling heat of Colombo and the coast. Highlights: Nine Arches Bridge ("Bridge of the Nine Arches", British colonial railway bridge 1921 in stone and concrete without iron, 91m long and 24m high, crossed by the cog train 4-5 times/day, the most viral image of Sri Lanka on Instagram, free access via 30-min path from Ella, panoramic view above the bridge), Little Adam's Peak (1141m, easy 30-min hike from Ella, panorama over iconic Ella Gap, sunset observation point), Ella Rock (1041m, moderate 4h round-trip hike through tea plantations, the summit is not marked so local guide recommended 1500-3000 LKR ≈ 5-10€, panorama over Hill Country more impressive than Little Adam's Peak), Ravana Falls (waterfall on the Ella-Wellawaya road, 25m, glacial swimming possible), Demodara Loop (loop railway unique in the world where the railway crosses on 3 levels in the same mountain, designed by the British 1921 to gain 30m of vertical drop without tunnels), Halpewatte tea plantations (free visit with tasting, purchase of local Ceylon tea Ceylon Tea), Lipton's Seat 1h away (viewpoint 1970m where Sir Thomas Lipton himself came to sit and contemplate his tea empire created 1890, hairpin road access, 6 km uphill hike from Dambatenne tea factory). Kandy-Ella or Nuwara Eliya-Ella train = the iconic experience (3-7h depending on departure, one of the most picturesque trains in the world, view of terraced tea plantations, viaducts, waterfalls, derisory price 1000-3000 LKR for 1st class Observation Car, ticket to buy in advance as full), Adam's Peak 4h away (Sri Pada, 2243m, sacred mountain for 4 religions — Buddhists/Hindus/Muslims/Christians — footprint of Buddha/Shiva/Adam depending on the religion, 5500 steps night ascent for sunrise at the summit December-May, marking spiritual experience). Try local Ceylon tea (Pekoe or Orange Pekoe), kottu roti, rice and curry, hoppers, fresh seafood (fish brought from the coast), Lion Lager beers. Logistics: Ella 7h from Colombo by train (via Kandy + Nuwara Eliya), 2h by bus from Wellawaya south-east side.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
