Context

Our verdict

Guilin, city in Guangxi province in China: 760,000 inhabitants, unique karst landscapes (limestone peaks rising from the Li river), considered among the most beautiful regions of China by the ancients ("Guilin shanshui jia tianxia" = "Guilin landscapes are the most beautiful in the world"). October is my favourite month (8.6/10): 18-26°C, 25% rain, ideal temperatures before winter. From September to November, it's the tourist high season: 13-31°C, ideal dry climate, orange grove harvests. From June to August, expect 24-32°C with 62-84% rain: Chinese summer high season (maximum crowds, prices x2-x3, mountain trails stormed), but also the greenest and mistiest landscapes. From December to February, expect 6-16°C and 28-55% rain: humid cold, few tourists but mystical landscapes with low mists. Highlights: Li River cruise (Lijiang, from Guilin to Yangshuo 1h by bus then 4-5h cruise, 80 km of iconic karst landscapes of traditional Chinese landscape — these are the landscapes that feature on the 20-yuan banknotes), Yangshuo (tourist village to the south, cycling in the rice paddies, climbing on the karst peaks, light show on the Li river with 600 figurants choreographed by Zhang Yimou), Longji Rice Terraces 2h away ("Dragon's Backbone", 5-level terraced rice paddies in 1000m mountains, spectacular landscapes in May-June green growth or October golden harvest, Yao/Zhuang minority villages), Reed Flute Cave (limestone cave neon-coloured in Guilin), Elephant Trunk Hill (iconic collised hill on the riverbank in Guilin). Try Guilin rice noodles (mi fen), beer fish (river fish in beer, Yangshuo specialty), osmanthus tea/cake (osmanthus flowers = symbol of Guilin).
