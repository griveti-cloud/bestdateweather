Context

Our verdict

Siargao, island in the Mindanao region in the south-east of the Philippines: 100,000 inhabitants on 437 km². Nicknamed the "surf capital of the Philippines", paradise of surfers since the discovery of the cult spot Cloud 9 in 1992 (one of the best waves in Asia according to WSL rankings). Bohemian surfer atmosphere, much less developed than Boracay or Bali. February is my favourite month (7.4/10): 24-27°C, 84% rain. From September to November, it's the surf high season (the most powerful waves, north Pacific swell), peak in September-October. From January to May, it's the relatively drier season (in reality Siargao receives a lot of rain year-round, it's one of the wettest islands in the Philippines with 3500 mm/year), 24-29°C. From June to October, it's the cyclone season (one of the most exposed in the Philippines, typhoon Odette/Rai in December 2021 devastated 90% of the island, reconstruction still in progress). Highlights: Cloud 9 (the iconic wave, extremely powerful right-hander reef break, wave for experts only, international WSL Siargao Cloud 9 contests every year September, characteristic wooden observation jetty of the surf magazine photos), General Luna ("GL" = main tourist village, restaurants-bars-hotels, white sand beach), Magpupungko Rock Pools (natural pools in the limestone rock, accessible at low tide 2-3h, crystalline water), Sugba Lagoon (turquoise lagoon between limestone cliffs, paddle or kayak, 20m natural diving board for cliff jumping), 3 Islands Tour (Naked Island without vegetation sandbank, Daku Island larger BBQ lunch, Guyam Island), Maasin River (river in the mangrove, cult Instagram liana-swing for jumping-into-water photos), Coconut Road (road bordered by coconut trees, palm grove of 3 million palm trees, to see at sunset), Pacifico Beach to the north (wild, intermediate surf). Activities: surf (Cloud 9 for experts, more accessible alternatives: Quicksilver, Stimpys, Tuason Point, Jacking Horse for intermediates, schools at GL), diving (modest but possible), island hopping, motorbike tour of the island. Audience: international surfers in the majority, bohemian backpackers, Filipino celebrities (the island has become very fashionable post-2018). Try kinilaw (Filipino raw fish ceviche with coconut milk), fresh seafood, coconut everything (water, milk, meat), San Miguel beer. Logistics: Siargao airport (IAO) with direct flights Manila (2h15) and Cebu (1h).
