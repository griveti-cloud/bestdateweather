Context

Our verdict

Astana (briefly renamed Nur-Sultan 2019-2022 in honour of Nazarbayev then re-renamed Astana in 2022 after his fall), capital of Kazakhstan since 1997: 1.3 million inhabitants in the heart of the Kazakh steppes at 350m altitude. Capital integrally planned created from scratch (transferred from Almaty for seismic and geopolitical reasons), futuristic architecture signed Norman Foster and other archistars — showcase city of the Kazakh petrodollar. July-August are my favourite months (8.9/10): 14-26°C, 26-34% rain, this is the short continental summer. From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 12-26°C, dry and hot climate. From December to February, expect -16/-7°C: extreme continental cold with glacial steppe winds (sometimes -40°C in January-February, one of the coldest capitals in the world after Ulaanbaatar), little tourism. From September-May, temperatures fluctuating enormously: -16/+21°C depending on the season. Highlights (essentially spectacular contemporary architecture): Bayterek Tower (97m, iconic of the city built in 1997 year of capital transfer, observation platform 105m with gilded mould of Nazarbayev's hand for iconic photo), Khan Shatyr ("Khan's tent", Norman Foster 2010, largest tent in the world 150m high, mall+gardens+indoor tropical beach to escape the continental climate), Pyramid of Peace and Reconciliation (Norman Foster 2006, 77m, concert hall for interfaith gatherings), Hazret Sultan Mosque (the largest in Central Asia 2012, 17,000 seats), Russian Orthodox Cathedral, Ultra-modern left bank quarter. Excursions: Lake Borovoye/Burabay 250 km north ("Kazakh Switzerland", lake landscapes and rocky formations, Soviet thermal station). Logistics: Astana 3h by plane from Moscow, direct flight Astana-Paris seasonal Air Astana. Try beshbarmak (national dish: horse or mutton meat boiled on flat pasta), kazy (horse sausage), kymyz (fermented mare's milk), shubat (fermented camel's milk), black tea with milk, vodka.
