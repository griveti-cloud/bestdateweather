Context

Our verdict

Bariloche is the "little Argentine Switzerland": northern Patagonia, lakes and araucaria forests, chalet architecture. January is my favourite month (9.3/10): 10-24°C, 11% rain, 14h days, austral summer ideal for the Circuito Chico (65 km panoramic road), Cerro Catedral (summer lift, view over Nahuel Huapi), Refugio Frey (hike to the glacial lake). From June to August (austral winter), it's the ski season on Cerro Catedral (the largest resort in South America, 120 km of pistes): temperatures at 1-6°C at the bottom, snow guaranteed from July to mid-September. From April to November, expect 1-14°C and 26-53% rain: humid Patagonian climate, but the autumn-coloured forests (May) stay photogenic. Bariloche is famous for its chocolate: Calle Mitre concentrates the chocolatiers (Mamuschka, Rapanui, Fenoglio). El Bolsón 2h away for the Argentine hippies. Cerro Tronador (active volcano) for a day hike.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Bariloche?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: July (ski score 8.7/10). For hiking/summer: December (9.3/10, 20.0°C).
Can you ski in Bariloche in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 1.0/10. Cold temperatures (24°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Bariloche in summer?
In December, temperatures average 20.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Bariloche?
May is the wettest month with May has the lowest climate score of the year.
