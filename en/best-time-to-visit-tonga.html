Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Tonga is the only Polynesian kingdom still monarchical: 100,000 inhabitants, capital Nuku'alofa on Tongatapu. 169 islands of which 36 inhabited. June-July are my favourite months (8.2/10): 21-25°C, 34-36% rain, austral dry season, sea at 25°C. From May to October, it's the dry season: 34-53% rainfall, temperatures 21-27°C. From November to April, it's the wet season: 53-86% rainfall, heavy heat, cyclonic risk (Harold 2020, Gita 2018, Yasa 2020). Tonga is world-known for swimming with humpback whales: July-October, humpback whales migrate from Antarctica to the protected lagoon to give birth and reproduce. It's one of the very rare places in the world where swimming with whales is legal and regulated (group of 4 max + guide, permit required, distance respected). Expect 200-400$/day for this experience (Vava'u, the main archipelago for this activity). Highlights: Tongatapu (capital, Talamahu market, royal palace exterior view), Vava'u (most picturesque archipelago, 50 islands, whales, sailing, diving), Ha'apai (very quiet), 'Eua (hiking). Mapu'a 'a Vaca (spectacular blowholes over 5 km of coast), Ha'amonga 'a Maui Trilithon (Polynesian Stonehenge). Try 'umu (cooking in the earth), lu sipi (mutton in taro leaves). Flight from Auckland (3h) or Fiji.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
