Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Khartoum (Al-Khartoum), capital of Sudan at the confluence of the White Nile and the Blue Nile forming the Nile which then flows towards Egypt: 5.3 million inhabitants in metropolitan area "Triple Capital" Khartoum + Omdurman + North Khartoum. Unique geographical place in the world = the confluence of the White Nile coming from Lake Victoria + Blue Nile coming from Lake Tana in Ethiopia, the 2 rivers visibly meet in Khartoum without immediately mixing (waters of different colours for several km), forming the Nile = the longest river in the world 6695 km. Country among the poorest in Africa, marked by 30 years of Omar al-Bashir Islamist dictatorship 1989-2019 (International Criminal Court indicted him for genocide in Darfur 300,000 deaths 2003-2019), then democratic transition aborted by successive military coups, civil war since April 2023 between the regular SAF army (Burhan) and the RSF paramilitary forces (Hemedti) = major humanitarian catastrophe 12 million displaced, mass famine Darfur-Khartoum, capital partially destroyed. January is my favourite month (9.2/10): 16-32°C, 0% rain, this is the ideal dry season. December-February stay excellent (8.6-8.8/10). From December to February, it's the cool dry season: 14-32°C. From March to May, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave (haboubs = spectacular Saharan sandstorms): 20-42°C. From June to October drop to 3.9-6.7/10 due to heatwave + short Sahelian rainy season. Highlights (before the war): Confluence of the Nile ("Mogran", iconic geographical point where White Nile + Blue Nile meet, observation from boat), Omdurman Central Market (one of the largest open-air markets in sub-Saharan Africa), Tomb of the Mahdi (Omdurman, mausoleum of the Mahdi Muhammad Ahmad who overthrew the Anglo-Egyptians 1885 then defeated by Kitchener 1898), National Museum of Sudan (exceptional Nubian collection, Karima statues + temples remounted), Soufi Hamed al-Nil Mosque (Omdurman, Friday Sufi whirling dervish ceremony). Major Nubian archaeological sites = Sudanese Nubia houses more pyramids than Egypt (250 vs 130 in Egypt = but they are smaller and less famous): Meroe 200 km north (UNESCO 2011, former capital of the Nubian Kingdom of Kush 3rd century BC-4th century AD, pyramid necropolis with 200 pyramids smaller but more pointed than the Egyptian ones, mystical Saharan landscape iconic view), Naga and Musawwarat es-Sufra (UNESCO 2011, other Kushite sites). Safety: inadvisable France Diplomacy for the entirety of Sudan = active civil war since 2023, capital partially destroyed, extremely inadvisable travel except experienced humanitarian missions. Try ful medames (fava beans, popular dish at breakfast shared with Egypt), kisra (iconic sorghum flatbread), aseeda (sorghum porridge), grilled Nile fish, Sudanese coffee with iconic ginger, hibiscus juice. Khartoum International airport (KRT) damaged by the 2023 war.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
