Context

Our verdict

Quito, Ecuadorian capital: 2850m altitude, the best-preserved colonial old town in South America (UNESCO 1978). July-August are my favourite months (7.7/10): 7-19°C, 32-36% rain, equatorial dry season, clear skies. From January to May, it's the rainy season: 79-93% rainfall in afternoon showers (mornings often sunny). The altitude is felt: climb the steep slopes of Old Quito gently, hydration. Highlights: Historic centre (Plaza Grande, Cathedral, La Compañía de Jesús covered in gold, San Francisco), El Panecillo (41m aluminium Virgin statue, valley view), TelefériQo (cable car to Cruz Loma at 4100m), Mitad del Mundo (geographic equator monument, 30 min away, two versions: official monument and the INRG site of the "real" equator). Otavalo market 2h away on Saturdays (the largest indigenous market in South America). Cotopaxi (5897m volcano) 1h30 away for the climb (1 night at the José Rivas refuge). Safety: Old Quito demands vigilance at night (touristy Mariscal/La Mariscal with evening atmosphere). Quito often serves as transit before/after the Galápagos. Try ceviche de chochos, llapingachos, locro de papa, hornado.
