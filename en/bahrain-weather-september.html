Context

Our verdict

Bahrain (Kingdom of Bahrain), insular micro-state of the Persian Gulf between Saudi Arabia and Qatar: 1.5 million inhabitants on an archipelago of 33 islands of which the main island (760 km² in total). Connected to Saudi Arabia by the King Fahd Causeway (25 km of bridge-causeway opened 1986). Country among the most liberal in the Arabian peninsula (alcohol authorised in hotels, flexible dress code), Al Khalifa constitutional monarchy since 1783. November is my favourite month (9.1/10): 22-28°C, 6% rain, this is the best month. March-April stay excellent (8.5-8.7/10). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 15-23°C, dry and tempered ideal climate. From May to October drop to 2.7-6.7/10 due to extreme heatwave (27-39°C, sometimes 47°C+ with oppressive coastal humidity). Highlights: Manama (capital, 270,000 inhabitants, Arab-cosmopolitan atmosphere with modern skyscrapers mixed with traditional old town), Bahrain Fort (Qal'at al-Bahrain, UNESCO 2005, vestiges over 4000 years of continuous occupation from Bronze Age to Portuguese era 16th century = one of the most complete archaeological sites in the Middle East), Bab Al-Bahrain ("Gate of Bahrain", British colonial architecture 1949 = entrance gate of the traditional souk), Manama Souk (traditional Arab souk with 22-carat gold Bahrain Gold, Bahraini pearls historically the most prestigious in the world before the invention of Japanese cultured pearls Mikimoto 1916 which ruined the Bahraini pearl industry), Al Fateh Mosque (one of the largest mosques in the world, capacity 7000 worshippers, fibreglass dome 60 tonnes the largest of its kind, open to non-Muslims), Bahrain National Museum, Bahrain World Trade Center (iconic 240m skyscraper with 3 wind turbines integrated between the 2 towers, first skyscraper in the world to integrate renewable energy production 2008), Tree of Life ("Tree of Life", 400-year-old solitary tree in the desert with no apparent water source, botanical mystery attracting many visitors, iconic of Bahrain), Riffa Fort (19th century), Pearling Trail UNESCO 2012 (testimony of the historic pearling industry of the Gulf). Excursions: Hawar Islands (south-west archipelago, preserved beaches, 1h boat access), Royal Camel Farm (royal camels), F1 Sakhir circuits (Bahrain International Circuit host of the Bahrain Grand Prix in March since 2004 = 1st F1 race of the Middle East). Try machboos (Bahraini spicy rice, Arab national dish), muhammar (sweet rice with dates, Persian heritage), harees (spicy porridge for Ramadan), Arabic coffee with dates, fresh fruit juices, alcohol available in international hotels. Bahrain airport (BAH).
