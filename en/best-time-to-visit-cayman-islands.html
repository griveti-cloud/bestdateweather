Founder · BestDateWeather

The Cayman Islands are a British overseas territory: 3 islands (Grand Cayman, Cayman Brac, Little Cayman), 65,000 inhabitants, tax haven (one of the largest offshore banking centres in the world). January-February are my favourite months (8.0/10): 25-28°C, 37-47% rain, sea at 26°C, steady trade winds. From June to October, it's the cyclone season: 53-68% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Ivan 2004 category 4 devastated). From December to March, expect 25-28°C: American high season, very high hotel prices (tax haven = wealthy clientele). Highlights: Seven Mile Beach (iconic beach of Grand Cayman, ranked among the most beautiful in the Caribbean), Stingray City (sandbank where one swims with semi-domesticated stingrays, boat excursion), Bioluminescent Bay at Rum Point (bioluminescent plankton at night), Hell (black limestone rocky formation with Hell Town post), George Town (capital, cruise port, duty-free shopping). Cayman Brac = cliffs, diving. Little Cayman = the calmest (170 inhabitants), Bloody Bay Wall for diving. Diving: among the best spots in the world (Bloody Bay Wall, Kittiwake wreck). Try conch fritters, turtle stew (Cayman Turtle Centre breeding). Currency: Cayman Dollar (KYD = 1.20 USD). Direct flight from London or Miami. Very expensive (tax haven).
