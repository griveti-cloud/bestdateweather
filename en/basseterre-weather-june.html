Context

Our verdict

Basseterre, capital of Saint Kitts and Nevis in the Lesser Antilles: 13,000 inhabitants. Smallest independent country in the Americas = 261 km², 53,000 inhabitants in total, independent of the United Kingdom 1983, Commonwealth member, parliamentary constitutional monarchy. First English colony in the Caribbean 1623 = "Mother Colony" of the British West Indies. Bilingual country (English creole + standard English official). March-April are my favourite months (8.3/10): 24-27°C, 42-44% rain, this is the ideal dry season. May-June stay excellent (8.2/10). From December to April, it's the tourist high season: 24-29°C, sea at 26-27°C, hotel prices x2-x3. From June to November, it's the active cyclonic season: 48-64% rainfall, moderate hurricane risk. Highlights: Brimstone Hill Fortress (UNESCO 1999, "Gibraltar of the West Indies", British 17th-18th century fortress perched at 240m altitude on Mount Brimstone, one of the best-preserved military forts in the Caribbean, panoramic view over Saint Eustatius + Dutch Saba + Nevis to the south, defended Saint Kitts against the French = the largest English fortification in the 18th-century Caribbean, taken by French Comte de Grasse 1782 then returned to England by Treaty of Paris 1783), Independence Square in Basseterre (main square, former 17th-19th century slave market, renamed 1983 at independence), Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception, National Museum at the former Treasury Building, Romney Manor (17th-century colonial manor transformed into Caribelle Batik batik workshop iconic local, preserved plantation atmosphere), Wingfield Estate (17th-century sugar cane plantation ruins, Caribbean slavery memorial atmosphere), Black Rocks (black volcanic formations on the Atlantic side seafront), St Kitts Scenic Railway (former 1912 sugar cane train reconverted into 30 km panoramic tourist train around the island, unique iconic Caribbean tourist), Mount Liamuiga (dormant volcano 1156m, highest point, demanding 4-5h round trip hike to the crater). Nevis (neighbouring frigate-half island, accessible 30 min ferry from Saint Kitts, calmer atmosphere with preserved British colonial architecture, Mount Nevis volcano 985m, top regional Pinney's Beach). Activities: diving and snorkelling (sites in the south of Saint Kitts), catamaran sailing between Saint Kitts and Nevis, Brimstone Hill tour, Caribelle Batik workshops, Marriott resort casino. Public: East Coast Americans in the majority (charter flights from New York-Miami), Canadian snowbirds, British. Citizenship by Investment: Saint Kitts has the oldest "Citizenship by Investment" programme in the world (since 1984) = obtaining Saint Kittian passport against 150,000 USD real estate investment or donation to the National Development Fund, attracts emerging fortunes from Russia/China/Middle East seeking 2nd passport, international controversy. Try goat water (iconic spicy goat stew, national dish unique in the Caribbean), salt fish + johnnycakes, conch chowder, Caribbean ratatouille, fried plantain, Carib beer, Brinley Gold Shipwreck Rum (local rum). Robert L. Bradshaw Saint Kitts airport (SKB).
