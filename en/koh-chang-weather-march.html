Context

Our verdict

Koh Chang, Thailand's 2nd largest island after Phuket: 217 km², 5500 inhabitants in the far east of Trat province near the Cambodian border. Nicknamed "Elephant Island" (its shape suggests an elephant). 70% jungle protected by the national park. Much wilder and less touristy than Phuket or Koh Samui. December-January are my favourite months (8.8-9.1/10): 22-28°C, 16-29% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 22-28°C, sea at 28°C, hotel prices x2-x3. From April to October, it's the south-west monsoon: 59-98% rainfall (one of Thailand's wettest islands during the monsoon), many hotels close, beaches turn into mud streams during tropical storms. Highlights: White Sand Beach (Hat Sai Khao, the longest 3 km on the west coast, nightlife), Klong Prao (the longest 4 km, quieter), Kai Bae Beach (central point, islet view), Lonely Beach (Hat Tha Nam, backpacker-bohemian atmosphere), Bang Bao Village (stilt fishing village at the far south-west, jetty restaurants, departure point for diving and island-hopping excursions), Khlong Plu Waterfall (waterfall in the jungle, 5-min access, swimming), Salakphet Bay (wild east coast), Mu Koh Chang National Park (47 islands in the archipelago: Koh Wai, Koh Mak = quiet backpacker atmosphere, Koh Kood = the smartest and most preserved, also accessible by speedboat from Trat). Activities: diving (Hin Luk Bat, Hin Rap, HTMS Chang wreck), jungle trekking, kayaking in the mangrove at Salakphet, ethical elephant ride at Ban Kwan Chang Elephant Camp (sanctuary). Snorkelling: less spectacular than Phi Phi as the corals are less developed, but Koh Rang 1h30 by speedboat is very good. Nightlife: less developed than Phuket/Phi Phi, fire shows at Lonely Beach, beach bars at White Sand Beach. Try fresh seafood (grilled fish with salt and lime), nam prik kapi (fermented prawn sauce), spicy Trat curry. Logistics: Koh Chang 5h from Bangkok (4h bus to Trat + 30 min ferry + island transfer), no airport on the island, Trat airport (TDX) the closest.
