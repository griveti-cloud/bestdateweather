Context

Our verdict

Palm Springs, in California: 45,000 inhabitants 200 km east of Los Angeles, thermal-resort station in the Sonoran desert at the foot of the San Jacinto mountains (3300m). Historic haunt of Hollywood stars of the 50s-60s (Sinatra, Presley, Marilyn). World capital of Mid-Century Modern architecture. April is my favourite month (9.3/10): 16-30°C, 5% rain, ideal temperatures, Coachella festival (40 min away, 2 weekends mid-April, one of the largest music festivals in the world, 250,000 people, hotel prices x10). From October to May, it's the high "snowbird" season: 9-31°C, 3-12% rain, ideal dry climate, golf in full activity (130 courses in the Coachella Valley = world record golf density). From June to August drop to 3.9/10 due to extreme heat (24-41°C, sometimes 49°C in July, one of the hottest cities in the USA), hotel prices divided by 2-3. Highlights: Aerial Tramway (rotating cable car, one of the longest in the world, 800 → 2596m on Mount San Jacinto in 10 min, 30°C difference between bottom and top, hiking and snow in winter), Joshua Tree National Park 45 min away (martian landscapes, iconic yucca trees, U2 album cover), Sunnylands (Annenberg residence former West Coast Camp David), Mid-Century Architecture (Modernism Week in February = the reference event, Sinatra house tours, Kaufmann House by Richard Neutra). Sonny Bono Memorial. Coachella Valley Preserve, Indian Canyons (palm oases in the heart of the desert). Try local dates (Coachella Valley = US date capital), date shake (local iconic), fish tacos.
