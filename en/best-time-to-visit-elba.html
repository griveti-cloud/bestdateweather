Elba (Elba), the largest island of the Tuscan archipelago in Italy: 32,000 inhabitants over 224 km², 3rd largest Italian island after Sicily and Sardinia. World-famous as the place of exile of Napoleon I (from 4 May 1814 to 26 February 1815, that is 9 months and 22 days, after his forced abdication at Fontainebleau), where he reigned as "emperor of the island of Elba" over 12,000 inhabitants with a personal guard of 1000 soldiers before his spectacular return to France for the Hundred Days. July is my favourite month (9.6/10): 22-29°C, 8% rain, sea at 24°C, ideal temperatures. June and September stay excellent (8.6-9.4/10). August drops to 6.9/10 due to the heat (23-30°C) and the extreme crowds (Italians on school holidays in the majority, hotel prices x4-x5, congested Piombino-Portoferraio ferries, packed beaches). From November to March, expect 8-17°C and 25-38% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, many hotels close, island almost deserted. Highlights: Portoferraio (historic capital, fortified old town Cosmopoli created by Cosimo I de Medici 1548, picturesque port), Villa dei Mulini (Napoleon's official residence in Portoferraio, modest but preserved with period furniture, open for visits), Villa San Martino (Napoleon's summer residence 5 km from Portoferraio in the countryside, with art gallery added by Anatoli Demidoff in the 19th century), Medicean Fortresses Forte Falcone and Forte Stella (panorama over Portoferraio), Mount Capanne (1019m, highest point, "cabinovie" cable car in unusual open individual basket-type cabins, 360° panorama over the Tuscan archipelago + Corsica visible 50 km to the west on clear days), Capoliveri (south-east altitude village, medieval atmosphere), Marciana and Marciana Marina (west coast), Porto Azzurro (picturesque east-coast port with Spanish fortress), Rio Marina (east coast, former capital of iron extraction plundered by Etruscans then Romans then Medici). Beaches: Elba has 150 varied beaches (sand, pebbles, rocks, volcanic black sand) depending on the coast: Cavoli and Fetovaia (sand, turquoise waters south-west coast), Sansone (white pebbles, crystal-clear water north coast), Capo Bianco (unique white pebbles), Biodola (long beach, smart hotels), Lacona (the longest), Innamorata ("lovers' beach"), Padulella, Topinetti. Excursions: Pianosa and Montecristo (preserved islands of the Tuscan archipelago, limited access by booking), Capraia (wild island), Aeolian Islands Sicily 8h by ferry. Try sburrita (Elban cod soup), totani con patate (squid potato), schiaccia briaca (Aleatico Elba DOC wine cake), Aleatico dell'Elba DOCG (iconic sweet wine of the island), Ansonica (local white wine). Logistics: Elba accessible only by ferry from Piombino in 1h (Toremar, Moby, Blunavy, 30-50€/person vehicle), no airport for commercial flights (small airstrip for private planes Marina di Campo).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
