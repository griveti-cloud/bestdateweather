Context

Our verdict

Libreville, capital of Gabon on the Atlantic coast of Central Africa: 800,000 inhabitants in urban area = 50% of the total Gabonese population. Former French colony "Gabon" 1885-1960 (the name "Libreville" 1849 refers to the liberation of slaves bought back by the French from the Brazilian slave ship Elizia = abolitionist symbol), country rich in offshore oil (4th African producer), precious wood (okoumé), manganese, iron. Modest population 2.3 million on 267,668 km² = one of the least densely populated countries in Africa (8 inhab/km²) thanks to 88% of preserved primary equatorial forest. July is my favourite month (8.2/10): 21-27°C, 32% rain, this is the ideal dry season. June-August stay excellent (7.7-8.0/10). From June to August, it's the great dry season "little dry season" ironically named as it is the longest: 21-28°C, sea at 25°C, the most pleasant dry and cool tropical climate of the year. From September to May, it's the equatorial humid season: 84-99% rainfall (Gabon is one of the most rained countries in the world with 3000-4000 mm/year). Libreville highlights: Saint Mary Cathedral of Libreville (1958 French colonial heritage), Hassan II Mosque ("Grand Mosque", financed by King Mohammed VI Morocco), Mont-Bouët Market (the largest market in Libreville, West African atmosphere), Libreville Beach / Sablière (urban Atlantic beach), Pointe Denis 30 min by boat (peninsula of preserved beaches with high-end resorts), Presidential Palace (presidential residence visible from outside), National Museum of Arts and Traditions of Gabon (Fang-Punu-Kota iconic ritual masks of African art), Petit-Paris quarter, Akanda smart suburb. Essential Gabon excursions = this is why one visits Gabon: Lopé National Park 5h away (UNESCO 2007, mosaic forest savannah unique in Central Africa, lowland gorillas + chimpanzees + mandrills + forest elephants + 19 traditional ethnic communities), Loango National Park 8h away ("Africa's Last Eden" nicknamed by National Geographic 2002, the only place in the world where one can see elephants and hippopotamuses on the Atlantic beach + gorillas + buffaloes + iconic unique world leatherback turtles in nesting), Ivindo National Park (UNESCO 2021, spectacular Kongou and Mingouli waterfalls, preserved jungle, baïs = marshy clearings where wildlife gathers), Minkébé National Park (one of the last refuges of forest elephants threatened by poaching), Lambaréné 4h away (Albert Schweitzer Hospital 1913 = medical mission of the 1952 Nobel Peace Prize Albert Schweitzer, become a humanitarian pilgrimage place), Pointe Denis near Libreville. Safety: Libreville globally safe by African standards, moderate urban crime. Try nyembwe (chicken in red palm nut sauce iconic Gabonese), saka saka (cassava leaves), grilled captain fish, chicken in moambé, atanga (Gabonese fruit), Beaufort lager + local Régab beer. Libreville Léon-Mba airport (LBV).
