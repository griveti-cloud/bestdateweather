Valparaíso, port city of Chile: 300,000 inhabitants 110 km west of Santiago on the Pacific, UNESCO-listed 2003 for its historic ensemble unique in Latin America. City built on 42 hills ("cerros") with colourful wooden and corrugated-iron houses, accessible by 15 historic funiculars ("ascensores") dating from the 19th-20th century (8 still in service, 7 in restoration). Cultural capital of Chile (poets Pablo Neruda and Vicente Huidobro lived here). January is my favourite month (8.8/10): 14-20°C, 2% rain, this is the austral summer, constant clear sky. From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 11-20°C, ideal Mediterranean climate. From June to August, it's the austral winter: 9-14°C, 16-22% rain, mild but sky often grey. Highlights: Cerro Concepción and Cerro Alegre (the 2 most touristy hills, cobbled alleys, exceptional street art — Valparaíso is one of the world capitals of street art, with murals everywhere), La Sebastiana (Pablo Neruda's house-museum, panoramic view over the bay, one of the poet's 3 houses after Isla Negra and La Chascona in Santiago), Ascensor Concepción (1883, the oldest funicular still in service in Chile), Plaza Sotomayor (main square at the port edge with the Monumento a los Héroes de Iquique), Mercado Cardonal (picturesque central market), Museo de Bellas Artes (Palacio Baburizza), Plaza Aníbal Pinto, Reloj Turri (English clock tower), Iglesia Anglicana San Pablo. Festivals: Año Nuevo (31 December, one of the world's largest fireworks displays over the bay, 1 million spectators on the hills). Viña del Mar 10 min away by metro ("la Ciudad Jardín", the "real" smart Chilean seaside resort, beaches, casino, song festival in February that brings together all of Latin America). Excursions: Isla Negra 1h away (Pablo Neruda's house on the Pacific), Santiago 1h30 away. Try chorrillana (chips with meat/onion/egg), pichanga (street food), pastel de jaiba (crab pie), Maipo and Casablanca wines.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
