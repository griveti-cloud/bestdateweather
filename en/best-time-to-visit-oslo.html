When to visit Oslo?

The decision rests on several criteria. Climate score, tourist crowds and feasible activities.

Verdict: In Oslo, June and July clearly stand out. December concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Oslo is one of Europe's most natural capitals: forests 15 minutes away (Nordmarka), 40 islands in the Oslofjord. June-July are my favourite months (7.6-7.7/10): 12-21°C, 40-42% rain, 18h days, swimming at the fjord islands (Hovedøya by ferry from Aker Brygge), festivals (Øyafestivalen early August). From December to February, expect -5/2°C and 35-41% precipitation (often snow): cross-country skiing direct from the centre (Holmenkollen), aurora borealis possible some winters. Vigeland Park (sculptures), Munch Museum (relocated in 2021), Opera House whose roof you walk up, Bygdøy for the museums (Viking, Fram, Kon-Tiki) are the classics. Oslo stays very expensive: restaurant meal 30-50€, beer 10-12€. May-June and September are the real sweet spots price/weather. Bergen 6h by train (one of Europe's most beautiful rail journeys).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
