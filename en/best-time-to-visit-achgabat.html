Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Ashgabat, capital of Turkmenistan: 1.2 million inhabitants at the foot of the Kopet Dag Mountains on the Iranian border. Exceptionally strange capital (ultra-authoritarian post-Soviet regime, personality cult of Saparmurat Niyazov "Türkmenbaşy" 1990-2006 then Berdimuhamedov, nicknamed "white marble capital": 543 white marble buildings 4.5 million m², Guinness world record 2013). Country among the least open to tourism (very difficult visa, mandatory guide, limited access). September is my favourite month (9.2/10): 20-31°C, 1% rain, ideal temperatures after the heatwave. From May and September-October, these are the best seasons: 12-31°C, 1-9% rain, ideal dry climate. From June to August, it's the extreme heatwave: 24-38°C (July 38°C+), totally inadvisable. From December to February, expect 1-11°C: mild. Highlights (essentially grandiloquent regime architecture): Neutrality Monument (75m, golden statue of President Türkmenbaşy at the top which pivoted to follow the sun until 2010 when it was moved by his successor), Türkmenbaşy Ruhy Mosque (15 km south, the largest in Central Asia, 10,000 places, entirely marble interior, the Quran and the words of "Ruhnama" book of President Niyazov engraved together on the walls — which shocked the Islamic world), Presidential Palace, Yntalanan Yyly (bizarre Wedding Palace, giant 70m cube topped with a terrestrial globe), Independence Park (massive statues of national heroes), White marble quarter. Excursions: Darvaza gas crater 270 km away in the Karakum desert ("Gates of Hell", 70m diameter, burns continuously since 1971 when Soviet geologists set fire to burn toxic gases after collapse of a drilling hole — bizarrely still active 50 years later; fantastic night spectacle, organised 4x4 visit with guide from Ashgabat 5h), Konye-Urgench (UNESCO, medieval ruins), Nisa (UNESCO, Parthian ruins), Avaza (Caspian coast, beach resort), Mary and Merv (UNESCO, former Seljuk capital). Try Turkmen plov, shashlik, dograma (bread-mutton-onion soup), gutab (pancakes), green tea.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
