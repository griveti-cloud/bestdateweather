G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Moscow, Russian capital: 12 million inhabitants, Eastern European megacity, extreme continental climate. August is my favourite month (8.3/10): 14-23°C, 25% rain, parks in full life (Gorky Park, VDNKh), terraces, 16h days. From June to August, it's the summer high season: 12-24°C, ideal temperatures. From December to February, expect -9/-2°C with 30-43% precipitation often as snow: real Russian winter, days at 7h of light, but Moscow under snow is magnificent (colourful cathedrals, Red Square ice rinks, Christmas markets), heated metro, hotels at half-price. From March-April and October-November are difficult transitions (mud, grey sky, variable temperatures). Highlights: Red Square (UNESCO, Saint Basil's Cathedral with colourful domes, Lenin Mausoleum still visitable, GUM historic department store), Kremlin (UNESCO, presidential palace, Orthodox cathedrals, armoury with Fabergé egg collection), Moscow Metro (one of the most beautiful in the world, underground palaces marble/mosaics/chandeliers, stations Komsomolskaya, Mayakovskaya, Novoslobodskaya). Bolshoi (opera and ballet, booking 3 months ahead, tickets 50-300€). Tretyakov Gallery (Russian art), Pushkin Museum (Western art). Quarters: Arbat (touristy pedestrian street), Patriarshie Prudy (smart, trendy), Tverskaya (luxury). Try borscht, pelmeni, blini with caviar, vodka. Important: since the invasion of Ukraine (February 2022), Russian visa difficult to obtain for Europeans, direct Paris-Moscow flights suspended (mandatory stopover via Istanbul or Dubai), financial restrictions (Visa/Mastercard not accepted in Russia).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
