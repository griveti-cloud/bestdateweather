Toulouse, France's 4th city: 500,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 1.5 million in the urban area, capital of Occitanie. Nicknamed the "Pink City" for its characteristic foraine pink bricks. European capital of aeronautics (Airbus world headquarters in Blagnac, ATR, Thales, the aerospace industry employs 130,000 people in the region). Student city (130,000 students out of 1.5 million inhabitants). July is my favourite month (9.3/10): 18-29°C, 23% rain, ideal temperatures. August stays excellent (9.1/10). From April to October, it's the tourist high season: 8-29°C. From November to March, expect 3-15°C and 30-45% rain: mild for France, more humid climate than Montpellier but milder than Paris. Highlights: Place du Capitole (heart of the city, 18th-century neo-classical Capitole de Toulouse town hall with its iconic pink-white-blue façade 128m long, paintings by Henri Martin), Saint-Sernin Basilica (UNESCO 1998, 11th-12th century Romanesque, one of the largest Romanesque churches in the world, major stage on the Camino de Santiago, iconic octagonal bell tower over 5 levels), Convent of the Jacobins (13th-14th century southern Gothic, houses the tomb of Saint Thomas Aquinas died 1274, iconic palm-tree vaults), Pont Neuf over the Garonne (16th-17th century, paradoxically the oldest current bridge in Toulouse), Hôtel d'Assézat (16th-century Renaissance, one of the most beautiful private mansions in France, fortune from pastel = blue dye that made the Toulouse riches in the 16th century), Cité de l'Espace 6 km away (space theme park with real-size Ariane 5 53m, Mir, Soyuz, 100m² planetarium, since 1997), Aéroscopia at Blagnac (aeronautical museum, Concorde, Airbus A300, Caravelle, Super Guppy), Airbus visit (A380 then now A350 assembly chain, by Airbus Visit booking), Musée des Augustins (Fine Arts museum in former 14th-century convent), Saint-Cyprien quarter (former working-class quarter on the left bank, today trendy). Excursions: Carcassonne 1h away (UNESCO, fortified medieval city), Albi 1h away (UNESCO, pink-brick Gothic cathedral, Musée Toulouse-Lautrec birthplace), Cordes-sur-Ciel 1h30 away ("most beautiful village in France" 2014), Pyrenees 1h30 away (Cauterets, Saint-Lary), Andorra 3h away, Lourdes 2h away, Auch Gers (capital of Armagnac foie gras). Major festival: Marathon des Mots in June, Toulouse d'Été in July-August. Try cassoulet (THE Toulouse dish, strict recipe with Tarbais beans, duck confit, Toulouse sausage, and depending on the school added lamb), Toulouse sausage (IGP in progress), Sud-Ouest foie gras, magret de canard, AOC Gaillac, AOC Madiran, AOC Fronton wines, Armagnac (eau-de-vie). Toulouse-Blagnac airport (TLS).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
