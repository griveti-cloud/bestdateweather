Context

Our verdict

Thredbo, ski resort of the Snowy Mountains of Australia in Kosciuszko National Park (NSW): 50 permanent inhabitants (3000 in ski season). One of the main ski resorts in Australia with neighbouring Perisher (the other large area, 40 min from Thredbo), accessible to Sydney and Melbourne skiers on weekends. Australian short ski season = June-October (austral winter). January is my favourite month (8.0/10): 11-21°C, 43% rain, ideal temperatures (austral summer). December-February stay excellent (7.7-7.9/10). From December to April, it's the summer season: 5-21°C, Snowy Mountains hiking, mountain bike, paragliding, ascent of Mount Kosciuszko 2228m = highest point of continental Australia (one of the Seven Summits = continental summits according to Bass classification but not according to Messner who includes Carstensz Papua 4884m), 6.5 km hike from Thredbo Top Station accessible by Kosciuszko Express chairlift = the easiest ascent of the Seven Summits (feasible in half a day, accessible to families). From June to August, it's ski season: -2/4°C and 42-52% precipitation often as snow, austral cold period. Pistes: 50 km Thredbo Resort (modest vs Alps: 600 km 3 Valleys, but one of the largest Australian areas with Perisher 100 km), altitude 1365-2037m, vertical drop 672m = the largest vertical drop in Australia. iconic pistes: Funnel Web (iconic black descent), Crackenback Supertrail (the longest piste 5.9 km), Dream Run (family). Particularities: the resort hosts the FIS World Cup Aerials every year = international aerial freestyle ski competition, Australian athletes dominate this sport (Jacqui Cooper, Lydia Lassila Olympic medallists). Audience: Australians in the majority (Sydney 6h by car, Melbourne 6h by car, Canberra 2h30), New Zealand Kiwis, few foreigners (Europeans-Americans prefer Aspen or Chamonix which have 5x more snow and 10x more pistes for the same price). Cost: high prices ($200-300 AUD/day pass, $400-800 AUD/night hotel) as Australian skiing = elite sport. Hotels: Thredbo Alpine Hotel, Lake Crackenback Resort, several lodges. Excursions: Mt Kosciuszko in summer, Perisher 40 min away (other large resort), Jindabyne 30 min away (alpine lake and Snowy Mountains hub), Canberra 2h30 away (Australian capital already covered by BDW), Sydney 6h away. Try meat pie (Australian après-ski tradition), Pavlova (iconic Australian-New Zealand dessert), Vegemite (iconic Australian fermented spread), Tim Tam (iconic chocolate biscuits), local Carlton/James Boag/Coopers beers, Australian Shiraz/Chardonnay wines. Logistics: Thredbo accessible only by car, Canberra airport (CBR) 2h30 away, Sydney airport (SYD) 6h away, Cooma airport (COA) the closest 1h30 away but few flights.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Thredbo?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: July (ski score 8.2/10). For hiking/summer: February (8.6/10, 19.0°C).
Can you ski in Thredbo in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 0.9/10. Cold temperatures (21°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Thredbo in summer?
In February, temperatures average 19.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Thredbo?
May is the wettest month with May has the lowest climate score of the year.
