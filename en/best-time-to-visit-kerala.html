Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Kerala, in south-west India: 35 million inhabitants, ranked "Top 10 destinations to see in a lifetime" by National Geographic Traveler. Beaches, backwaters, tea/cardamom plantations, ayurveda. January is my favourite month (6.8/10): 23-34°C, 11% rain. Kerala never tops 7/10 (constant humid equatorial climate). From June to September, it's the south-west monsoon: 85-98% rainfall, intensely green landscapes, ayurvedic practitioners recommend this season for cures (humid climate opens the pores). From October to November, second monsoon (north-east): 55-86% rainfall. From December to March, it's the tourist high season: 23-32°C, relatively dry climate. From April to May, expect 25-37°C: stifling heat before the monsoon. Highlights: Backwaters of Alleppey/Kumarakom (network of canals, 1-2 night cruise on a "kettuvallam" houseboat 100-300€), Kochi/Cochin (Fort Kochi, Chinese fishing nets, Paradesi synagogue, Jewish quarter), Munnar (tea plantations at altitude 1600m, cool, hikes), Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary (elephants, rare tigers), Varkala and Kovalam (cliff beaches). Theyyam (north Kerala dance-trance rituals, Oct-May). Try sadya (traditional meal on a banana leaf), Kerala fish curry, appam, puttu. Ayurvedic cure 7-21 days in a specialised resort. Main airport Kochi/Trivandrum. Equatorial climate = outside the main cyclone season.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
