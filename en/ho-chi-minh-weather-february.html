The month in detail

What to expect in February

February is peak dry season in Ho Chi Minh City. The air is warm and humidity more bearable than in the rainy season. Rain risk remains low (14.0% of days), leaving good room for outdoor activities. Not the best window, but acceptable if dates are constrained. January (6.5/10) is significantly better if possible.

Context

Our verdict

Ho Chi Minh City (former Saigon) is Vietnam's economic capital: 9 million inhabitants, tropical dynamism, French heritage. January-February are my favourite months (6.9/10): 23-34°C, 13-14% rain, this is the dry season, but HCMC never tops 7/10 (constant hot tropical climate). From May to November, it's the monsoon: 64-98% rainfall in afternoon tropical showers (often 30 min then clear sky). March-April = peak heat (25-35°C). Highlights: Cu Chi Tunnels 2h away (Viet Cong underground network, very narrow), War Remnants Museum (Museum of War Crimes), Notre-Dame Cathedral (bricks from Toulouse), Central Post Office (Eiffel), Reunification Palace. District 1 for the business centre, District 4 for the food streets, Cholon for Chinatown and the Binh Tay market. Try pho (different from Hanoi), banh xeo (crispy pancakes), com tam (broken rice), coffee with condensed milk. Mekong delta 2h away for the floating markets (Cai Be more local than Cai Rang).
