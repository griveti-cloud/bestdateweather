Context

Our verdict

The Halong Bay (UNESCO 1994) is the postcard image of Vietnam: 1969 emerging karst limestone islands over 1500 km² in the Gulf of Tonkin. November is my favourite month (8.6/10): 20-26°C, 25% rain, clear skies after the monsoon, mild temperatures. From October to March, it's the high season: 16-28°C, 15-38% rain, some days can be misty (reduced visibility, especially January-February, but also atmospheric for photography). From May to August, it's typhoon and monsoon season: 63-87% rainfall, hot temperatures (25-31°C), real risk of storms (cruises cancelled for several days). From April to May, expect 22-29°C: humid transition. Cruises: 1 night (2 days) = standard "classic" circuit, you see the essentials but it's short (50-150€/person), 2 nights (3 days) = circuit recommended to truly enjoy it (less-frequented Bai Tu Long Bay, kayak, caves, night fishing, 200-500€), 3 nights = for enthusiasts. Cat Ba Island to explore for an extra 2-3 days (national park, hikes). Highlights: Sung Sot Cave ("cave of surprises"), Ti Top Island (panorama), Lan Ha Bay (less-frequented alternative to the east, near Cat Ba), Bai Tu Long Bay (north-east, even quieter, premium cruises). Logistics: 3h30 by bus from Hanoi (motorway), boarding at Tuan Chau Marina or Halong City. Try local seafood, cha muc (squid rolls). April and September are the best crowd/weather compromises.
