When to visit Naples?

The decision rests on several criteria. Climate score, tourist crowds and feasible activities.

Verdict: In Naples, June and September clearly stand out. January concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Naples is Italy's densest, most chaotic, most alive city. June is my favourite month (9.4/10): 18-27°C, 18% rain, terraces on the Lungomare, ice cream at Gelateria della Scimmia. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to humid heatwave (22-31°C): Neapolitans fleeing to Capri or Ischia. From December to February, expect 6-15°C and 34-39% rain: nativity scenes in Spaccanapoli (San Gregorio Armeno = the year-round nativity street), pizzas at L'Antica Pizzeria da Michele (the most famous, huge queues) or Sorbillo. Pompeii 30 min by Circumvesuviana train (allow a full day), Herculaneum less crowded but more impressive, Vesuvius by bus from Pompeii to the summit (1h walk). Capri 50 min by hydrofoil, Procida and Ischia more local alternatives. Safety: the Spanish Quarters are lively in the day but warrant caution at night. May and September-October are the real sweet spots tourism/weather.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
