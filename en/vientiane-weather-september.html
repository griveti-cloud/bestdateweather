Context

Our verdict

Vientiane, capital of Laos: 950,000 inhabitants on the Mekong on the border of Thailand (the Friendship Bridge connects Vientiane to Nong Khai on the Thai side in 20 min). One of the calmest and most bohemian capitals in South-East Asia, very present French colonial heritage (architecture, daily baguette, café au lait). January is my favourite month (9.3/10): 18-28°C, 7% rain, end of the dry season, ideal temperatures. December stays excellent (9.3/10). From November to February, it's the dry season: 18-32°C, dry and cool climate in the morning (sometimes 12°C in December, surprisingly cool for tropical Asia). From March to May, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 25-34°C (sometimes 40°C), mists from agricultural slash-and-burn pollute the atmosphere of northern Laos. From June to October, it's the monsoon: 47-89% rainfall, but more bearable temperatures (23-31°C). Highlights: Pha That Luang (national iconic, golden 16th-century stupa, the most sacred monument in Laos), Patuxai ("Laotian Arc de Triomphe", resembles the one in Paris built with American cement intended for an airport in 1968), Wat Si Saket (19th century, the oldest temple remaining after the Siamese sacking of 1828, 6800 Buddha statues), Wat Sisaket and Wat Ho Phra Kaew (historic places), COPE Visitor Center (poignant memorial on the UXO cluster bombs still present 50 years after the war, Laos received more US bombs than the entire Second World War combined). Vientiane Night Market (on the banks of the Mekong, sunset, street food, crafts). Excursions: Buddha Park (Xieng Khuan) 25 km away (200 eclectic Buddhist and Hindu statues 1958), Vang Vieng 4h by bus (karst landscapes, tubing). Try laap (chopped meat salad), sticky rice, papaya salad (tam mak hoong), Beer Lao (the national pride, 90% of the beer market in Laos).
