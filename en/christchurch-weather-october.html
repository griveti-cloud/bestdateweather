Context

Our verdict

Christchurch, capital of Canterbury in South Island: 380,000 inhabitants, the most "English" city in New Zealand, tragically marked by the September 2010 and February 2011 earthquakes (185 deaths, 50% of the centre destroyed). Reconstruction still ongoing. January-February are my favourite months (8.3/10): 13-21°C, 25-28% rain, austral summer. From June to August (austral winter), expect 4-12°C: dry cold, snow possible on the Port Hills. Highlights: Cathedral Square (Cathedral under restoration since the earthquake, 10-year project), Cardboard Cathedral (Shigeru Ban, temporary cathedral 2013), 185 Empty Chairs Memorial (memorial to the earthquake victims), Botanic Gardens, Christchurch Tram. Quake City (museum about earthquakes). Excursions: Akaroa 1h30 away (Banks Peninsula, former French colony, Hector dolphins), Arthur's Pass 2h30 away (panoramic road towards the west coast), Kaikoura 2h30 away (whale watching, whales year-round), Mount Hutt and other ski resorts in winter. Christchurch is the classic starting point of South Island road trips (towards Queenstown via Wanaka, or via the west coast and Franz Josef Glacier). Direct flight from Sydney/Melbourne.
