Context

Our verdict

Gstaad, ultra-chic ski resort and retreat in the Bernese Alps in Switzerland: 7000 inhabitants at 1050m altitude. The most exclusive historic ski resort in Switzerland (with St Moritz and Zermatt), known since 1900 as a refuge of the European elite (Roger Moore lived here for 35 years, Bernie Ecclestone-Roman Polanski-Madonna own chalets here). Atmosphere of a preserved traditional alpine village thanks to a strict ban on new construction (anti-Klosters policy), no skyscrapers, renovated 17th-19th century wooden chalets. July is my favourite month (7.4/10): 11-21°C, 56% rain, ideal temperatures for alpine hiking. Gstaad never tops 7.4/10 (alpine climate with a narrow season). From June to September, it's the summer high season: 3-21°C, Bernese Oberland hiking, mountain bike, 9-hole golf, polo ("Snow Polo World Cup Gstaad" January on the frozen lake, major social event for the European elite). From December to March, expect -6/5°C and 38-49% precipitation often as snow: this is ski high season. Pistes: 220 km Gstaad Mountain Rides (modest vs 600 km of the 3 Vallées but elite quality), 6 linked sectors (Rinderberg, Wispile, Wasserngrat, Eggli, Glacier 3000, Saanenmöser), altitude 950-3000m, 2000m vertical drop. Glacier 3000, Peak Walk by Tissot = panoramic suspension bridge between 2 peaks 107m long unique in the world 2014, panoramic Botta restaurant. iconic pistes: Tiger Run black descent with 1500m vertical drop, Glacier 3000 piste 7 km. Audience: ultra-wealthy (European royal families, celebrities, oligarchs), deliberately discreet atmosphere (paparazzi rare), highest prices in the Swiss Alps (chalet rental 30,000-100,000 CHF/week). Hotels: Park Gstaad (5*+ luxury palace 1910), Le Grand Bellevue (5*+), Gstaad Palace (the institution since 1913, legendarily hosting Hollywood Roger Moore-Audrey Hepburn-Liz Taylor), prices 800-5000+ CHF/night in high season. Excursions: Lake Geneva 1h away (Montreux-Lausanne already covered), Bern 1h30 away, Interlaken-Jungfrau 2h away, Zermatt-Matterhorn 4h away. Festival: Menuhin Festival July-September (created 1957 by violinist Yehudi Menuhin who had settled in Gstaad, iconic classical festival). Try half-and-half fondue, raclette, rösti, Älplermagronen, Berner Platte, Schnaps Etter, Swiss Lavaux wines. Logistics: Gstaad accessible by panoramic Golden Pass Line train from Montreux 2h, from Geneva airport 2h30 by car.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Gstaad?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 8.9/10). For hiking/summer: March (8.9/10, 7.0°C).
Can you ski in Gstaad in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.2/10. Cold temperatures (2°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Gstaad in summer?
In March, temperatures average 7.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Gstaad?
May is the wettest month with May has the lowest climate score of the year.
