Lamu, island in the eponymous archipelago in northern Kenya: 25,000 inhabitants on the main island, UNESCO 1981 for its city centre Lamu Old Town (the oldest and best-preserved Swahili town in East Africa, founded in the 7th century, atmosphere frozen in the 18th-19th century). No cars on the island (apart from 2 administrative vehicles), only donkey and boats for transport. September is my favourite month (8.4/10): 24-28°C, 50% rain, end of the cool season. From June to October, it's the tourist high season: 24-29°C, sea at 27°C, SW monsoon, blue sky. From April to May, it's the long rainy season: 76-77% rainfall, many hotels close. From November-December, it's the short rainy season: 42-59% rainfall. Highlights: Lamu Old Town (UNESCO, maze of narrow alleys with characteristic carved wooden Swahili doors — 200 doors recorded, each with its own name, some 300 years old), Central Market (Donkey Market), Lamu Fort (19th century, built by the Sultan of Oman), Lamu Museum (siwa — crescent-shaped ceremonial ivory horns unique in the world), House of Swahili Culture (traditional house museum). Beaches: Shela Beach (12 km of white-sand beach 30 min by dhow from Lamu Town, smarter and quieter atmosphere, Peponi hotels), Manda Bay (on a neighbouring island). Activities: dhow ride (traditional sailing boat, half-day rental 25-50€/person with onboard fish barbecue lunch), sport fishing (marlin, sailfish), snorkelling at Manda Toto. Safety: Lamu is far from the Somali border (130 km to the north), an at-risk zone for Europeans since 2011 (tourist kidnappings), Kenyan military presence on the island, situation stabilised but travel advisories still demand vigilance for rural areas in northern Kenya. Try Lamu biryani (the most famous on the East African coast), samosa, kashata (coconut sweet), mishikaki meatballs. Lamu Cultural Festival in November (dhow racing, traditional dances).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
