When to visit Paris?

The decision rests on several criteria. Climate score, tourist crowds and feasible activities.

Verdict: In Paris, July and August clearly stand out. January concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Paris shows its best face from May to September. Terraces overflow, the sun stretches past 12h a day. June stays my favourite month (8.3/10): 14-23°C, 13h of light, and the city hasn't yet been emptied by holidays. Roland-Garros, the Fête de la Musique, evenings stretching until 22h on the quays — this is Paris when everything is happening before the summer exodus. July-August are hot and heavy (heatwaves arrive more and more often), but Parisians flee and the city empties in a strange and beautiful way. From December to February, the sky is grey and leaden, just 3h of sun, days closing at 17h. This is the season for Christmas markets, museums without crowds, and Marais cafés. To each their own Paris.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
