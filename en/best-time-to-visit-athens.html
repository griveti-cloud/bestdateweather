When to visit Athens?

The decision rests on several criteria. Climate score, tourist crowds and feasible activities.

Verdict: In Athens, September and May clearly stand out. December concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Athens has one of Europe's most extreme Mediterranean climates. September is my favourite month (9.2/10): 20-29°C, 10% rain, sea still at 24°C at the Apollo Coast beaches (45 min by tram), Acropolis without the heatwave. July-August drop to 6.9-8.6/10 due to the heatwave (23-34°C, sometimes 42°C): Athenians fleeing to the islands, Acropolis scorching from 11h to 17h. Visit before 9h or after 18h. From December to February, expect 6-15°C and 25-29% rain: mild, ideal for visiting the museums (Acropolis Museum, National Archaeological Museum) without crowds. Plaka district stays touristy but charming, Psyrri for alternative nightlife, Anafiotika for Cycladic calm in the city centre. Try a souvlaki at Kostas (takeaway, 5€). May and October are the best crowd/weather compromises. The Piraeus port for ferries to the islands (1-12h depending on destination).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)

Athens averages 11.2 sunshine hours per day in September. It is the sunniest month with a climate score of 9.4/10.
