Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Rarotonga is the main island of the Cook Islands: 67 km², 14,000 inhabitants, central mountain (Te Manga 653m), 32 km loop road around the island. November is my favourite month (8.2/10): 21-22°C, 38% rain, dry season. From May to October is the relative dry season (36-56% rainfall). From November to April, it's the wet season (51-66%). Highlights: Beaches Muri Beach (the most famous, calm lagoon, kayak to the motus), Aro'a Beach (snorkelling), Black Rock Beach (sunset), Cross-Island Track (6h hike across the island, Te Rua Manga "the Needle" as the highest point), Punanga Nui Market in Avarua (Saturday morning, food/crafts market), Avana Lagoon (departure for Aitutaki by boat). Activities: island bus tour (circular bus system "clockwise/anticlockwise", 1 every 30 min), scooter rental (60 NZ$/day) or car, diving and snorkelling. Cultural Village (Cook Maori culture show). Marae (sacred ancestral sites). Accommodation: no big all-inclusive Maldives-style resort, rather bungalows and family hotels at 100-300 NZ$/night. Try ika mata, taro, local pineapple. Auckland-Rarotonga flight 4h, affordable prices. Rarotonga = main low-cost alternative to Tahiti.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
