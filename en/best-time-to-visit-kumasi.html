Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Kumasi, 2nd city of Ghana and cultural capital of the Ashanti region: 2.5 million inhabitants in urban area. Historic capital of the Ashanti / Asante Kingdom 1670-1957 = one of the most powerful West African kingdoms 17th-19th century, famous for its gold (Gold Coast = colonial name of Ghana) and its prolonged armed resistance against the British (5 Anglo-Ashanti wars 1823-1900 until final defeat). Today the Asantehene = king of the Ashantis Otumfuo Osei Tutu II still reigns symbolically over 5 million Ashantis with his Golden Throne = major Ghanaian cultural iconic that was never taken by the British unlike the other African thrones. July is my favourite month (7.4/10): 22-29°C, 50% rain, this is the relative "little dry season". Kumasi never tops 7.4/10 (humid equatorial climate year-round). From June to August, it's the "little dry season": 22-30°C. From December to February, it's the relative dry season with Saharan harmattan: 22-33°C. From March to May and September-November, these are the rainy seasons: 39-78% rainfall. Kumasi highlights: Manhyia Palace (royal palace of the Asantehene king, Manhyia museum with Ashanti royal regalia + historical photos + iconic ceremonial objects), Kumasi Central Market ("Kejetia Market", one of the largest open-air markets in West Africa with 11,000 stalls, extraordinary atmosphere), Ashanti Cultural Centre and National Cultural Centre (traditional Ashanti craftsmanship: iconic colourful kente fabrics worldwide recognised Ghanaian cultural + Akan wood sculpture + gold jewellery), Saint Peter Cathedral, Tomb of Yaa Asantewaa (tomb of the Queen Mother Yaa Asantewaa 1840-1921 Ghanaian national heroine who led the last Ashanti rebellion against the British 1900 = pan-African feminist symbol), Lake Bosomtwe 30 km away (sacred Ashanti meteorite crater lake, one of the only natural lakes in Ghana, traditional ritual swimming), Owabi Wildlife Sanctuary. Essential Ghana excursions: Cape Coast Castle and Elmina Castle 4h south (UNESCO 1979, former Portuguese-Dutch-British slave forts 15th-19th century on the Gold Coast, embarkation places of millions of West African slaves to the Americas, iconic memorial of the transatlantic slave trade overwhelming = African-American Roots Heritage Tour pilgrimage place), Kakum National Park 4h south (equatorial forest park, Canopy Walkway = canopy walkways at 30m high iconic Ghana tourist), Mole National Park in the north (the largest national park in Ghana, elephants + lions + buffaloes), Accra 4h by bus (capital, already covered by BDW). Safety: Kumasi and Ghana in general globally among the most stable and safe African countries ("Africa's Gold Star"), moderate urban crime. Try fufu (iconic Ghanaian culinary hand-pounded cassava-plantain ball), banku (fermented corn balls), kelewele (fried spicy plantain), jollof rice (eternal culinary dispute Nigeria vs Ghana vs Senegal for the best version), waakye (rice beans), red red, palm wine, Star + Club + Guinness Foreign Extra Stout (the African version of Guinness brewed locally). Kumasi airport (KMS).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)

August is the best month with 28.0°C highs, 22.0°C lows, 7.7h of sunshine and 73.0% rainy days. The months of August & July offer comparable conditions.

Kumasi averages 7.7 sunshine hours per day in August. It is the sunniest month with a climate score of 8.0/10.

In August, overnight lows reach 22.0°C, while afternoon highs average 28.0°C.
Is Kumasi good in winter?
In winter (December-February), Kumasi scores 5.8/10 on average — Decent period conditions for cultural visits.. The best month overall remains August with a score of 8.0/10.
