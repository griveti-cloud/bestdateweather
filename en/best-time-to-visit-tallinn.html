Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Tallinn, Estonian capital: 450,000 inhabitants on the Gulf of Finland, the best-preserved medieval old town in northern Europe (UNESCO, 13th-16th century). Hyper-digital Estonia (e-residency, e-government global model since 1991). June is my favourite month (8.2/10): 12-20°C, 37% rain, 18h days in June (no full night at the summer solstice = "valged ööd" white nights), terraces. From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 14-21°C, very many Baltic cruises (Helsinki 2h by ferry, Stockholm 16h by overnight ferry). From November to March, expect -3/4°C and 34-50% precipitation often as snow: Tallinn under the snow is spectacular (snow-covered roofs, Christmas market on Raekoja plats considered one of Europe's most charming), days down to 6h of light end of December. Highlights: Old Town (UNESCO, divided into Toompea upper town and Vanalinn lower town): Raekoja plats (Town Hall Square, Christmas market since 1441), Alexander Nevsky Cathedral (Russian Orthodox on Toompea), Kiek in de Kök Tower, Kohtuotsa platform (iconic panorama over the red roofs and the sea), St Olaf's Tower (Oleviste, 124m, wooden lift, view), Pikk Hermann (Toompea fortress, Estonian flag raised every morning). Kalamaja district (former fishermen, now trendy, old wooden houses, Telliskivi creative city), Patarei (sinister former Soviet prison). Excursions: Helsinki 2h by ferry (popular day trip), Lahemaa National Park 1h away (forests, Baltic manor houses). Try verivorst (Christmas blood sausage), kohuke (cottage cheese bar), Vana Tallinn liqueur.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
