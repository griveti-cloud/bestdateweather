Decide

When to visit Copenhagen?

The decision rests on several criteria. Climate score, tourist crowds and feasible activities.

Verdict: In Copenhagen, June and July clearly stand out. December concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Copenhagen lives its best moments in summer: bicycle city, urban beaches (Amager Strandpark), harbour baths (Islands Brygge, Sluseholmen). June-August are my favourite months (7.9-8.1/10): 13-20°C, 33-42% rain, days stretching to 22h. From December to February, expect 1-6°C and 38-40% rain: grey skies, days down to 7h of light, hygge (candles, coffee, blankets in cafés). Tivoli opens in November for the Christmas season (lights, rides under the snow). Nyhavn stays the must-do (expensive), Christiania for the hippie utopia in the middle of the city, Nørrebro district for modern Nordic cuisine. Try a smørrebrød at Aamanns or Schønnemann, cinnamon swirl at Hart Bakery. Copenhagen stays very expensive: restaurant meal 25-40€, beer 7-10€. May-June and September are the best price/weather compromises.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
