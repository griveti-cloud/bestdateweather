Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Saint Thomas, main island of the US Virgin Islands (USVI) in the Lesser Antilles: 50,000 inhabitants on 81 km². Unincorporated US territory since 1917 (bought from Denmark for 25 million USD to prevent their seizure by Germany during the First World War), American citizens with US passports but without presidential voting rights. Capital Charlotte Amalie = former Danish capital, Caribbean atmosphere mixed with preserved Danish heritage. Main Caribbean cruise destination (15+ ships/day in high season). March is my favourite month (8.4/10): 24-26°C, 25% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January-April stay excellent (8.2-8.4/10). From December to April, it's the tourist high season: 24-27°C, sea at 26-27°C, hotel prices x2-x3. From June to November, it's the active cyclonic season: 35-56% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Saint Thomas and Saint Croix devastated by Hurricane Irma + Hurricane Maria September 2017 2 weeks apart = historic double catastrophe, multi-year reconstruction). Highlights: Magens Bay (regularly ranked among the most beautiful beaches in the world by National Geographic, regular semi-circle bay with powdery white sand and turquoise water, paying 5 USD/person but equipped), Charlotte Amalie (capital, 19th-century preserved Danish old town with Danish colonial architecture unique in the Caribbean, port-marina with cruise ships, luxury duty-free boutiques), Fort Christian (1672, the oldest building in the Virgin Islands, former Danish red-brick fort iconic), Mountain Top (722m, highest point, 360° panorama over Saint Thomas + Saint John + British Virgin Islands BVI to the east, iconic view with banana daiquiri "invented here in 1958" according to local tradition), 99 Steps (18th-century Danish historic staircase, actually 103 steps despite the name), Blackbeard's Castle (17th-century Danish tower, legend of the pirate Edward Teach "Blackbeard" who allegedly observed ships from the tower, in reality doubtful but effective marketing), Magens Bay Arboretum, Coki Beach (family snorkelling), St Thomas Skyride (panoramic cable car). Essential excursions: Saint John 30 min by ferry (the other USVI island, 60% of the island classified as Virgin Islands National Park = US federal national park, preserved beaches Trunk Bay top world according to Condé Nast, snorkelling Underwater Trail), Saint Croix 30 min by plane (the largest of the USVI, more traditional and less cruise atmosphere), British Virgin Islands (BVI, Tortola/Virgin Gorda, ferries from Saint Thomas 30 min, more peaceful and less tourist atmosphere, top world for sailing), Anegada BVI (flat coral island with turquoise lagoon, iconic lobsters), Jost Van Dyke (bohemian atmosphere, legendary Soggy Dollar Bar with iconic painkiller cocktail BVI). Try conch fritters, callaloo, johnnycakes, fungi (Caribbean polenta), salt fish, painkiller cocktail (rum-coconut-orange-nutmeg iconic BVI), Cruzan rum (local Saint Croix rum), Pusser's Rum BVI. Logistics: Cyril E. King Saint Thomas airport (STT), direct flights from Miami/New York/Atlanta, accessible to American citizens without passport (US territory), passport required for foreigners.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
