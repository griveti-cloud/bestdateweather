Context

Our verdict

Roatán, the largest of the Honduras Bay Islands in the Caribbean Sea: 80,000 inhabitants over 65 km long and 5 km wide. Former British colony 1742-1859 then integrated into Spanish-speaking Honduras while keeping English as the main language (Caribbean English creole unique to the Bay Islands), British Caribbean atmosphere distinct from the rest of Hispanic Honduras. Renowned for diving on the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef (2nd largest barrier reef in the world after the Australian, 1000 km offshore from Mexico-Belize-Guatemala-Honduras). February is my favourite month (8.2/10): 22-29°C, 26% rain, this is the ideal dry season. March-April stay excellent (8.0-8.1/10). From January to April, it's the dry season: 22-31°C, sea at 26-27°C, this is the tourist high season (Americans mainly), hotel prices x2-x3. From May to October, expect 24-31°C with 29-49% rain. From November to December, expect 22-29°C with 50-67% rain: secondary cyclone season. Highlights / Beaches: West Bay (the iconic beach of Roatán, powdery white sand 2 km, unique turquoise water, snorkelling from the beach with coral reef at 50m, one of the most beautiful beaches in the Caribbean according to many rankings), West End (seaside tourist village, restaurants-bars, smart backpacker atmosphere), Half Moon Bay (between West End and West Bay, sandy half-moon bay), Coxen Hole (capital of Roatán, ferries and cruisers), French Harbour (fishing port), Sandy Bay (peaceful residential area with diving institutes), Camp Bay Beach (east side, wild less touristy). Diving and snorkelling: Roatán is one of the world-renowned diving sites (top 10 worldwide according to many international diver rankings), 175 diving sites recorded around the island, PADI Open Water training 250-350 USD among the cheapest in the world, Mary's Place, Hole in the Wall, Spooky Channel, diving with reef sharks, green turtles, eagle rays, sometimes whale sharks March-May. Activities: Roatán Institute for Marine Sciences (dolphin research centre with framed ethical interactions, contested by some NGOs), Gumbalimba Park (exotic animals + zipline), Mahogany Bay (Carnival cruise port, day access only). Audience: Americans in the majority (60% of tourists via direct flights from Miami, Atlanta, Houston), Canadian snowbirds, international divers, Carnival/Royal Caribbean cruisers. Safety: Roatán is one of the safest destinations in Honduras, island sheltered from the crime problems of the continent (gangs MS-13 and Mara Salvatrucha which makes Honduras one of the countries with the highest homicide rates in the world, but the Bay Islands are protected by their insularity). Try baleadas (tortilla stuffed with beans-cheese, Honduran national dish), conch ceviche (lambi), grilled barracuda and grouper, fried plantain, sopa de caracol (lambi-coconut soup). Juan Manuel Galvez Roatán airport (RTB).
