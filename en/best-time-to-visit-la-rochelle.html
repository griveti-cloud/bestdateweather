La Rochelle, sub-prefecture of Charente-Maritime on the Atlantic coast: 75,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 200,000 in the urban area. Thousand-year-old port city, former major commerce port with the West Indies, Protestant stronghold in the 16th century (Siege of La Rochelle 1627-1628 by Richelieu, 23,000 deaths of which 2/3 from famine, royal victory). Capital of the Francofolies Festival (French-language song, since 1985). July is my favourite month (8.9/10): 17-23°C, 24% rain, ideal temperatures. August stays excellent (8.7/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 15-23°C, Atlantic swimming 19-21°C bracing, 2700 sunshine hours/year = one of France's sunniest cities above the Loire. From November to March, expect 5-13°C and 39-45% rain: mild climatically, little rain. Highlights: Old Port (historic heart, marina with 4500 berths, luxury moorings), 3 Towers of the Old Port (La Rochelle's visual iconic protecting the harbour entrance since the 14th century: Tour Saint-Nicolas 14th century the tallest, Tour de la Chaîne = port entrance with closing chain, Tour de la Lanterne 15th century = former lighthouse-prison with English and French prisoners' graffiti), City Hall (gothic-Renaissance 15th-17th century, splendid inner courtyard), Saint-Louis Cathedral (classical 18th century), Old town arcades (characteristic arcaded pedestrian streets, legacy of the Rochelle merchants, atmosphere unique in France), La Rochelle Aquarium (one of Europe's largest private aquariums, 12,000 marine animals, opened 1988), Maritime Museum (3 preserved vessels including the weather France II, Manta frigate), New World Museum (history of La Rochelle-Americas relations, slavery, colonialism), Fine Arts Museum, Saint-Nicolas district (former sailors' quarter). Essential excursions: Île de Ré via 2.9 km bridge (10 preserved villages with characteristic white houses with green shutters, magnificent Atlantic beaches, bicycle the main mode of transport with 100 km of cycle paths), Île d'Oléron 1h away (the 2nd largest French metropolitan island after Corsica, oyster farming), Fort Boyard (visible from the sea, fort famous from the TV game show), Marais Poitevin 1h away ("Green Venice", 100,000 hectares of marshes drained 7th century, traditional flat-bottomed boat trips), Cognac 1h30 away (world capital of cognac, Hennessy/Martell/Rémy Martin/Courvoisier/Camus visits). Major festival: Francofolies mid-July (5 days of French-language song concerts, since 1985, Indochine, Patrick Bruel, Jean-Jacques Goldman, MC Solaar have played there). Try moules-frites (with Pineau des Charentes in marinière), Marennes-Oléron oysters IGP (the only French oysters grown in claires — refining basins), Pineau des Charentes IGP (local aperitif blending grape juice and cognac), La Rochelle tinned sardines, mojettes (Vendée white beans). La Rochelle-Île de Ré airport (LRH).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
