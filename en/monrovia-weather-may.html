Context

Our verdict

Monrovia, capital of Liberia on the Atlantic coast of West Africa: 1.1 million inhabitants. Country unique in sub-Saharan Africa = colony of former American freed slaves sent from the USA by the American Colonization Society 1822 ("Liberia" = "Land of Freedom"), independence 1847 = the oldest republic in sub-Saharan Africa. Capital named after the 5th American president James Monroe 1816-1825. English-speaking country, flag identical to USA + 1 star instead of 50. Marked by the bloody civil war 1989-2003 under Charles Taylor (250,000 deaths, child soldiers Kalashnikov-armed kids horrific iconic of the conflict, ritual cannibalism of the "generals" Butt-Naked + Mosquito fighters), reconstruction since. February is my favourite month (7.9/10): 24-29°C, 56% rain, this is the relative ideal dry season. January-December stay excellent (7.8/10). Monrovia never tops 7.9/10 (very humid equatorial climate year-round). From December to March, it's the dry season: 23-29°C, refreshing Saharan harmattan in January-February. From April to October, it's the West African humid season: 86-99% rainfall (Monrovia is one of the most watered African capitals with 5000 mm/year, one of the world records for a capital). Monrovia highlights: National Capitol (American architecture inspired by the Washington Capitol), Executive House (presidential residence), National Museum of Liberia (modest ethnographic collection, americo-liberian heritage), Waterside Market (traditional West African central market), Trinity Cathedral (Episcopalian, americo-liberian heritage), Central Mosque of Monrovia, Monrovia Beach / ELWA Beach (urban Atlantic beach, strong currents dangerous for swimming), Ducor Hotel (former 5* colonial hotel abandoned since the civil war, preserved post-apocalyptic atmosphere iconic view of Monrovia, ascent of the roof for panorama), Providence Island (historic island of the Mesurado river, landing place of the first americo-liberian settlers 1822 = founding memorial place). Liberia excursions: Sapo National Park (the largest national park in Liberia, equatorial jungle with chimpanzees + pygmy hippopotamuses + forest elephants + jaguars, very difficult access), Robertsport in the north (one of the most respected surf spots in West Africa, clean Atlantic waves, bohemian atmosphere international surfers). Safety: political situation stabilised since 2003 under presidents Ellen Johnson Sirleaf 2006-2018 (1st African woman president elected, Nobel Peace Prize 2011) then George Weah 2018-2024 (former footballer Ballon d'Or 1995), moderate urban crime in Monrovia, increased France Diplomacy vigilance. Try jollof rice (West African iconic), pepper soup, palm butter (iconic red palm nut sauce), cassava leaf, fufu, fried plantain, palm wine (local fermented palm wine), local Club beer. Monrovia Roberts International airport (ROB).
