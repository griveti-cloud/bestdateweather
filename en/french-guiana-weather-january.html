Context

Our verdict

French Guiana, French overseas department-region in South America between Brazil and Suriname: 300,000 inhabitants on 84,000 km² (the largest French department in surface area + 1 of the smallest populations). European border with Brazil = France has the longest European border with a South American country via French Guiana. 96% of the territory covered by Amazon jungle = France has the 2nd largest tropical forest in the world after Brazil. Capital Cayenne 60,000 inhabitants. Economy dependent on the Guiana Space Centre at Kourou (CSG, European Ariane launch base since 1968 + Vega + Soyuz, ideal thanks to proximity to the equator 5° latitude vs Cape Canaveral 28° = 17% boost in payload, 50% of European satellites launched from Kourou). August is my favourite month (6.9/10): 23-31°C, 38% rain, this is the ideal "little dry season". French Guiana never tops 6.9/10 (extremely humid equatorial climate year-round). From July to November, it's the relative dry season: 23-32°C, this is the relative tourist high season. From December to January, it's the "little rainy season": 64-67% rain. From March to June, it's the "great rainy season" equatorial monsoon: 75-87% rainfall, the greenest landscapes but roads sometimes flooded. Highlights: Guiana Space Centre (CSG) at Kourou (mandatory free guided tour by reservation, potential observation of an Ariane 5/6 or Vega launch = unique iconic experience = a few launches per year, calendar published in advance), Salvation Islands 15 km off Kourou by boat ("Devil's Island" = former French penal colony 1852-1953 where Captain Alfred Dreyfus was deported 1895-1899 + Henri Charrière author of "Papillon" 1969 = major historical iconic of French Guiana, overwhelming memorial atmosphere with preserved cell ruins, paradisiacal coconut palms contrasting with historical horror), Cayenne capital ("Place des Palmistes" + Cépérou Fort + traditional Central Market + Creole-Amazonian atmosphere), Saint-Laurent du Maroni (Suriname border, former convict transportation camp 1858-1953, "Camp de la Transportation" preserved as museum), Awala-Yalimapo (Atlantic beach on the Suriname border, leatherback sea turtle nesting = the largest turtle in the world 700 kg, March-August, one of the world sites for leatherback observation), Iracoubo (church painted by a Chinese convict Pierre Huguet 1893 = unique French Guianan artistic iconic), Régina and Saül (preserved Amazonian jungle, ecotourism), Kaw Marshes (on the delta of the Approuague river, observation caimans + red ibises), Patawa Camp (Amazonian jungle camp). Activities: jungle expeditions on the Maroni river by traditional Boni dugout canoe (Creole bushinengue people descendants of escaped slaves), bird watching (700 species including 8 toucan species, scarlet macaw), wildlife safari (caimans, sloths, howler monkeys, rare jaguars). Population: unique ethnic mosaic = Creoles 35%, mainland French 12%, Brazilians 10%, Surinamese 6%, Haitians 6%, indigenous Amerindian peoples 5% (Wayampi-Wayana-Teko-Kali'na-Lokono), bushinengue Black Maroons 5%, Hmong refugees Laos 1975 1%, Chinese 1%. Try awara broth (French Guianan national dish based on bitter awara fruit iconic), chicken colombo (Antillean Indian heritage), Antillean blood pudding, fish blaff, French Guianan chaudin, ti'punch (rum + sugar + lemon, the obligatory aperitif of the French DOM-TOM), French Guianan coffee. Cayenne-Félix Eboué airport (CAY).
