Verdict: In Dubai, March and December clearly stand out. August concentrates the least favourable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Dubai is urban extravagance in the heart of the desert: 3.5 million inhabitants (90% expats), Burj Khalifa (828m, the world's tallest building), Palm Jumeirah, air-conditioned malls. March is my favourite month (9.1/10): 19-28°C, 7% rain, sea at 24°C, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. From June to September drop to 2.2-3.5/10 due to extreme heat (26-43°C, sometimes 50°C, max humidity along the Gulf): everything happens in air-conditioned interiors, beaches deserted in the day, hotel prices reduced by 50%. From November to April, it's the high season: 17-32°C, prices x3, 5-star+ hotels fully booked. Highlights: Burj Khalifa (At The Top 124th/148th floor, or 152nd/154th more expensive, book 2 weeks ahead), Dubai Mall (1200 shops, aquarium, musical fountains), Old Dubai (Bastakia historic quarter, abra crossing the Creek for 1 dirham), Palm Jumeirah (Atlantis, monorail), Burj Al Arab (the only "7-star" hotel), Gold Souk and Spice Souk in Deira. Desert excursions: Dune bashing, Bedouin dinner, sandboarding (200-400 AED). Try shawarma, machbous, Medjool dates, Arabic coffee with cardamom. Direct flight Paris in 7h.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
