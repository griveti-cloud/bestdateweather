Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Aswan (Assouan), city in southern Egypt on the first cataract of the Nile: 290,000 inhabitants 900 km south of Cairo. Extreme desert climate of the eastern Sahara. November is my favourite month (8.7/10): 16-29°C, 0% rain, ideal temperatures after the summer heatwave. From November to February, it's the tourist high season: 9-29°C, ideal dry climate. From May to September drop to 3.1-3.3/10 due to extreme heat (24-42°C, sometimes 50°C in June-July, one of the hottest inhabited cities in the world), totally inadvisable for tourism. From March-April and October, these are the in-between seasons: 14-36°C. Highlights: Philae Temple (UNESCO, international rescue 1972-1980 = dismantled stone by stone then rebuilt identically on the neighbouring island of Agilkia to escape the flooding from the Aswan Dam, dedicated to the goddess Isis last ancient Egyptian temple closed in 537 AD), Aswan High Dam (1960-1970, financed by the Soviet USSR after American refusal in 1956 = Suez crisis, 3.6 km long, 111m high, created Lake Nasser 5250 km², the world's 3rd largest artificial lake), Unfinished Obelisk (ancient granite quarry with a 42m obelisk abandoned in the 4th century BC, cracked during cutting — would have been the tallest ever made if completed), Elephantine Island (Nile, archaeological excavations, Nubian village Siou and Koti), Aga Khan III Mausoleum (died 1957, his widow climbed there every day to lay a rose until her own death in 2000), Aswan Souk (one of Egypt's most picturesque, spices, cosmetics, hibiscus karkadé). Major excursions: Abu Simbel 280 km to the south (UNESCO, colossal temples of Ramses II 13th century BC, also rescued 1964-1968 = cut into 30-tonne blocks and reassembled 65m higher to escape Lake Nasser, 4 statues of Ramses II 20m high carved into the cliff, solar alignment 22 February and 22 October illuminating the inner sanctuary, 35 min flight or 3h30 by bus with military convoy), Nile cruise Aswan-Luxor (3-7 nights on a dahabieh or modern ship, descent of 200 km of the Nile, stops at the temples of Kom Ombo and Edfu). Try molokhia, ful medames, kebab and koshary, hibiscus karkadé tea.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
