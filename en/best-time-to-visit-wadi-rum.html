Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Wadi Rum ("Valley of the Moon") is Jordan's desert: 720 km² of Martian landscapes (used for Lawrence of Arabia 1962, The Martian 2015, Star Wars Rogue One and Rise of Skywalker, Dune 2021). October is my favourite month (9.5/10): 15-28°C, 2% rain, ideal desert temperatures. From September to November and March-May are the best periods: 8-31°C, ideal dry climate. From June to August, expect 20-34°C: intense daytime heat (45°C+ possible), but cool nights in the desert (15-20°C). From December to February, expect 4-16°C: pleasant days but freezing nights (sometimes 0°C, snow rare but possible), many camps still operate with heating. Activities: 4×4 tour half-day or full day (Lawrence Spring, Khazali Canyon, Burdah Bridge, red dunes, Nabataean petroglyphs, 30-60€), camel ride 1-3h, hiking on Jebel Khash or Jebel Burdah, climbing (Wadi Rum is a global climbing spot on sandstone). Night in a Bedouin camp = essential experience: classic Bedouin camp (40-80€/night, basic tents, zarb meals cooked in the earth), bubble tents ("Mars camps", transparent domes for stargazing, 100-300€/night). Logistics: Wadi Rum 4h by bus from Amman, 1h from Petra, 1h from Aqaba. Plan 2 days / 1 night minimum. Community tour with a Bedouin family rather than a classic tour operator. Try zarb, mansaf (Jordanian national dish), sage tea.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
