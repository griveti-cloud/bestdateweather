G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Anchorage, largest city in Alaska: 290,000 inhabitants (40% of the state population, which has 730,000 on 1.7 million km² — a density comparable to Siberia). Located at the bottom of the Cook Inlet, surrounded by the Chugach Mountains and the Knik Arm. Air hub, base for exploring wild Alaska. June-July are my favourite months (7.9/10): 10-20°C, 30-37% rain, this is the "endless day" (19h of light at the 21 June solstice, never complete night), hiking, kayaking, whale watching, salmon fishing. From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 7-20°C, hotel prices x3-x4 (Inside Passage cruises, Denali NP). From November to March, expect -10/2°C and 30-44% precipitation often as snow: this is the high season for the Northern Lights (visible 200 nights/year, but Anchorage is less well placed than Fairbanks further north), short polar night (5-6h of light in December). Iditarod (dog sled race, 1600 km Anchorage-Nome, departure early March). Highlights: Anchorage Museum (Alaskan history, indigenous art), Tony Knowles Coastal Trail (18 km of coastal cycle path, moose and beluga observation), Earthquake Park (memorial of the Good Friday earthquake 1964, magnitude 9.2, the 2nd most powerful ever recorded). Excursions: Denali National Park 4h by road (Mt. Denali 6190m, the highest summit in North America, grizzly bears, moose), Kenai Fjords National Park 2h30 away (cruise from Seward, glaciers, humpback whales), Wasilla (Sarah Palin's hometown), Talkeetna (Denali expedition base village). Try wild salmon (king salmon the most prestigious), halibut, reindeer sausage, Alaska beer. Direct flight from Seattle/Portland (3h30).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
