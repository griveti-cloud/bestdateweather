Context

Our verdict

Isle of Skye, the largest of the Inner Hebrides in Scotland: 10,000 inhabitants over 1656 km² north-west of mainland Scotland. Linked by the Skye Bridge since 1995 (before: ferry only). Spectacular Gaelic mountain landscapes ("Eilean a' Cheò" = "Isle of Mist" in Scottish Gaelic), inspiration for many films (Highlander, Prometheus, Macbeth). May is my favourite month (4.4/10): 7-12°C, 53% rain. Skye never tops 4.4/10 (extremely rainy and windy oceanic climate year-round, one of Europe's wettest destinations with 2400 mm/year, vs 600 mm in London). From May to September, it's the tourist high season: 7-15°C, more stable climate but still unpredictable (it can be 12°C and rain in July), long days (18h of daylight at the solstice). From October to April, expect 2-9°C and 53-72% rain: very wet, possible snow, few tourists. Wind: Skye is one of the windiest destinations in the UK, frequent winds 60-100 km/h. Highlights: Old Man of Storr (iconic 50m rock formation in the Trotternish ridge, 4.5 km return trail 2-3h on a moderate climb, one of the most photographed places in Scotland), Quiraing (unique geological landscape of the Trotternish, landslide still moving today, 6.8 km trail, lunar landscape), Fairy Pools ("fairies' pools", succession of crystal-clear waterfalls in the Cuillins, freezing swim possible 8-10°C, 4 km trail), Fairy Glen (landscape of conical hills in geological motion near Uig, strange atmosphere), Cuillins Mountains (Black Cuillins gabbro massif and Red Cuillins granite, one of Scotland's most technical ranges, climbing and hill-walking, Mount Sgurr Alasdair 992m summit), Neist Point Lighthouse (extreme west of Skye, 1909, spectacular cliffs, sunset viewpoint), Dunvegan Castle (Scotland's oldest inhabited castle, seat of Clan MacLeod for 800 years, gardens, Fairy Flag enchanted clan banner), Eilean Donan Castle (on the road to Skye, one of the world's most photographed castles on a bridged islet, Highlander film castle), Talisker Distillery (Skye's oldest distillery 1830, characteristic smoky single malt of the island), Portree (capital of the island, harbour with colourful houses, seafood restaurants). Activities: hiking (the main draw, waterproof gear mandatory, GPS recommended as fog is frequent), landscape photography, orca and whale watching (June-September from Neist Point), sea kayaking. Try haggis (stuffed sheep's stomach), Cullen skink (Scottish fish soup), neeps and tatties (turnip-potato mash), Loch Fyne salmon, Loch Eishort oysters, Talisker single malt. Logistics: Skye 5h by car from Edinburgh or Glasgow, direct train Glasgow-Mallaig 5h then ferry 30 min, or by car via Skye Bridge.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Isle of Skye?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: February (ski score 5.0/10). For hiking/summer: May (6.1/10, 12.0°C).
Can you ski in Isle of Skye in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 4.8/10. Cold temperatures (6°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Isle of Skye in summer?
In May, temperatures average 12.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Isle of Skye?
November is the wettest month with November has the lowest climate score of the year.
