Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Marrakech (the "red city" for its rammed-earth ramparts), Morocco's tourist capital: 950,000 inhabitants, UNESCO Medina, Sahara dunes 8h by car. May is my favourite month (9.3/10): 15-30°C, 12% rain, ideal temperatures. From July to August drop to 3.9/10 due to extreme heatwave (21-39°C, sometimes 47°C, feels like 50°C+): impossible to visit in the middle of the day, locals coming out after 21h. From December to February, expect 6-22°C: mild during the day but cool at night (riads aren't heated), good period for athletes (semi-marathon in January, Marrakech opens its winter golf season). Highlights: Place Jemaa el-Fna (UNESCO intangible heritage, snake charmers, storytellers, monkey handlers, transformed in the evening into a giant open-air restaurant), Souks (spices, leather, carpets, metals), Koutoubia Mosque (12th century, sister of Seville's Giralda), Bahia Palace (19th century, mosaics), Saadian Tombs, Ben Youssef Madrasa, Majorelle Garden (Yves Saint Laurent), Médersa Ben Youssef. Excursions: Atlas day trip (Ourika valley, Imlil, Toubkal ascent 4167m), Essaouira 2h30 away (Atlantic coast, hippie atmosphere), Merzouga/Erg Chebbi desert 8h away (2-3 days by 4×4 with bivouac night). Try tagines, couscous (traditionally on Fridays), pastilla, mint tea.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
