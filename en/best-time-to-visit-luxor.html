Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Luxor (former Thebes) is the "historic capital of Ancient Egypt": 500,000 inhabitants on both banks of the Nile, the world's most impressive pharaonic sites. March is my favourite month (8.8/10): 14-29°C, 1% rain, ideal temperature to visit the temples (heat still bearable). From November to March, it's the high season: 9-29°C, ideal dry climate. From May to September drop to 3.2-3.3/10 due to extreme heatwave (24-42°C, sometimes 50°C in July-August, desert nearby), ground temperature in the Valley of the Kings unbearable after 11h. East bank highlights: Karnak Temple (the largest religious complex ever built, 134 giant columns of the hypostyle hall, sound and light show in the evening), Luxor Temple (linked to Karnak by the Avenue of Sphinxes restored in 2021, magnificent when lit up in the evening). West bank: Valley of the Kings (62 royal tombs including Tutankhamun, ticket for 3 tombs + supplement for Tutankhamun/Seti I/Ramses VI), Valley of the Queens (Nefertari), Hatshepsut Temple (Deir el-Bahari, 3 terraces), Colossi of Memnon. Nile cruise Luxor-Aswan (3-4 nights, one of the classic Egypt experiences, dahabieh sailing boat = premium version). Hot air balloon at sunrise over the Valley of the Kings (100-150€). Safety: Luxor remains among Egypt's safest cities (tourist police everywhere). Direct flight Cairo-Luxor in 1h.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)

Luxor averages 10.9 sunshine hours per day in March. It is the sunniest month with a climate score of 9.5/10.

Is Luxor good in winter?
In winter (December-February), Luxor scores 9.1/10 on average — Decent period conditions for cultural visits.. The best month overall remains March with a score of 9.5/10.
