Bolivia, South American country landlocked between Peru-Brazil-Paraguay-Argentina-Chile: 12 million inhabitants, one of the most diverse countries in South America (Andes 6500m + Amazon + Atacama desert). La Paz is the administrative capital at 3640m (the highest capital in the world) and Sucre the constitutional capital. From August to November are my favourite months (7.5-7.7/10): 1-18°C at altitude, 7-42% rain, this is the ideal dry season (clear sky indispensable for Salar de Uyuni photos). From May to October, it's the tourist high season: -5/17°C at altitude, 7-31% rain, dry and cold climate. From December to March, it's the rainy season: 60-69% rainfall, but the Salar de Uyuni flooded becomes giant mirror (one of the most spectacular landscapes in the world, to see between January and April). Highlights: Salar de Uyuni (10,582 km², the largest salt desert in the world at 3656m altitude, 3-day 4x4 trip from Uyuni with night in salt hotel + Coloured Lagoon + pink flamingos + Green Lagoon + Sol de Mañana geysers 5000m), Lake Titicaca (3812m, shared with Peru, Sun Island legendary Inca origin, Copacabana sanctuary of the Virgin), La Paz (longest urban cable car in the world 30 km, El Alto at 4150m panoramic view of the Andes, Witches Market Mercado de las Brujas), Sucre (UNESCO, constitutional capital, white colonial city), Potosí (UNESCO, former largest city in the world 16th-17th century thanks to the Cerro Rico silver mines, still in operation today in very difficult conditions), Madidi National Park (one of the most biodiverse Amazonian parks in the world). Try salteñas (empanada turnover), api (hot purple corn drink), llajwa (spicy sauce), singani (eau-de-vie).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
