Context

Our verdict

Méribel, French ski resort at the heart of the Trois Vallées: 1700m, 1900 permanent residents, founded in 1938 by Briton Peter Lindsay (traditional Savoyard chalet architecture strictly enforced). Considered "the most traditional resort in the 3 Vallées". July is my favourite month (8.0/10): 10-21°C, 47% rain in storms, alpine hiking, MTB, golf (18 holes, one of Europe's finest at 1700m). From June to September, expect 6-21°C: quiet summer season (few tourists outside ski season). From December to April, expect -8/5°C and 39-66% precipitation often as snow: prime ski season. Trois Vallées pistes: 600 km in total, Méribel = 150 km of its own, ideal central position to swing over to Courchevel or Val Thorens in the same day. Saulire (2700m, natural border with Courchevel). Plattières snowpark (one of the best in the Alps). 1992 Albertville Olympics: Méribel hosted the women's hockey events (Olympic Ice Rink still in use). Crowd: well-off French and British families (Méribel has the largest British community in the French Alps with Val d'Isère). More upmarket than Val Thorens, less ostentatious than Courchevel. Hotels: Le Coucou, Le Mont Vallon, Allodis (4-5*, 200-1500€/night). Try diots aux pommes, beaufort, génépi. Logistics: Méribel 2h from Lyon, Paris-Moûtiers train + 35 min bus.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Méribel?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 9.2/10). For hiking/summer: March (9.2/10, 5.0°C).
Can you ski in Méribel in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.6/10. Cold temperatures (1°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Méribel in summer?
In March, temperatures average 5.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Méribel?
November is the wettest month with November has the lowest climate score of the year.
