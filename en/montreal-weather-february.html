Context

Our verdict

Montreal is the 2nd largest French-speaking city in the world after Paris: island at the confluence of the Saint Lawrence, mix of French/English/multicultural. July is my favourite month (8.7/10): 18-27°C, 39% rain, festivals (Montreal International Jazz Festival early July, Just for Laughs mid-July, Osheaga music early August), terraces everywhere. From December to March, expect -12/4°C and 39-44% precipitation (often snow): a real winter with -20°C and blizzards possible. The underground city (RÉSO, 33 km of tunnels) makes total sense. Quebec Carnival 3h away. September-October are my real sweet spots: 7-22°C, spectacular foliage at Mont-Royal, Mont-Tremblant 1h30 away. Highlights: Old Montreal (Place Jacques-Cartier, Notre-Dame), Mont-Royal (Belvédère Kondiaronk for the view), Jean-Talon Market (North America's largest), Plateau Mont-Royal (characteristic external staircases), Mile End (Jewish bagels). Try bagel at St-Viateur or Fairmount, smoked meat at Schwartz's, poutine, tourtière, maple syrup (sugar shacks in March). Efficient metro.
