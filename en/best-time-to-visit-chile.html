Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Chile, South American country: 19 million inhabitants over 4300 km from north to south (one of the longest countries in the world), from the Atacama, the most arid desert, to Patagonia and Tierra del Fuego. 5 distinct climate zones depending on latitude. Seasons are reversed (southern hemisphere). January-February are my favourite months (8.4/10): 7-19°C on average (Santiago), 7-11% rain, this is the austral summer. From November to March, it's the Chilean tourist high season: 2-19°C, dry and warm climate, ideal for Atacama and the southern lakes. From June to August, it's the austral winter: -5/6°C, skiing in the Andes (Portillo, Valle Nevado, Farellones 2h from Santiago), Patagonia closed and inaccessible. Regions to know: Atacama Desert (San Pedro de Atacama, the world's most arid desert, some areas have never recorded rain, Valley of the Moon, Tatio Geysers at 4300m, Salar de Atacama, star observation at ALMA Observatory), Wine valleys (Casablanca, Maipo, Colchagua = Chilean wines among the world's best, Carmenere iconic varietal), Lake District (Pucón, Frutillar, Puerto Varas, conical Osorno volcano, Llanquihue lake), Chilean Patagonia (Torres del Paine National Park = W trek 4-5 days or O 8-10 days with glaciers, turquoise lakes, guanacos, condors, among the most spectacular landscapes in the world), Easter Island (UNESCO, 887 moai sculpted by the Rapanui, flight from Santiago 5h). Santiago is the capital (already covered), Valparaíso UNESCO port city (already covered). Try empanada de pino, completo (Chilean-version hot dog), pastel de choclo, Chilean ceviche, pisco sour (national cocktail, debate with Peru for the origin), Carmenere wines.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
