The month in detail

What to expect in June

June is peak dry season in Djibouti. The air is warm and humidity more bearable than in the rainy season. Rain is virtually absent (1.0% of days) — ideal for planning without a backup. Not the best window, but acceptable if dates are constrained. January (8.9/10) is significantly better if possible.

Context

Our verdict

Djibouti (Djibouti City), capital of Djibouti on the Gulf of Aden at the strategic entrance of the Red Sea: 600,000 inhabitants. Strategic country in the heart of the Horn of Africa = obligatory passage of 30% of world maritime traffic between Mediterranean and Indian Ocean (Suez Canal + Bab-el-Mandeb strait 100 km away), 5 foreign military bases on 23,200 km² (the highest density of military bases in the world) = USA + France + Italy + Japan + China (1st Chinese military base outside China since 2017). Former French colony "French Somaliland Coast" 1862-1977, independence 1977 under President Hassan Gouled Aptidon. Mixed Afar + Issa Somali population. January is my favourite month (9.0/10): 24-29°C, 13% rain, this is the ideal dry season. February-December stay excellent (8.7-9.0/10). From November to February, it's the relative "fresh" dry season: 23-29°C, sea at 25°C, dry and tempered tropical climate ideal thanks to refreshing trade winds. From June to August drop to 6.5-6.9/10 due to extreme heatwave (29-42°C, sometimes 47°C+ with oppressive coastal humidity = among the most uncomfortable urban climates in the world in summer). Djibouti city highlights: Place du 27 Juin (official central square, tribute to independence 1977), Djibouti Central Market (unique mixed Afar-Issa-Arab-Ethiopian atmosphere), Hamoudi Mosque, Place Ménélik (French colonial heritage), European Quarter (preserved 19th-20th century French colonial architecture), Djibouti Beach / Khor Ambado (urban beach with crystal water), Port of Djibouti (one of the most strategic ports in the world, military logistics + global maritime trade). Essential Djibouti excursions = this is why one visits Djibouti: Lake Assal 1h30 away (the lowest point in Africa -155m below sea level + the saltest lake in the world 35% salinity = 10 times more than the oceans, more than the Dead Sea 30%, spectacular crystal-white lunar landscapes iconic view of Djibouti), Lake Abbé at the Ethiopian border 4h (surreal landscapes with 50m-high iconic limestone chimneys, filming location of the film "Planet of the Apes" 1968 by Franklin Schaffner = cult cinematographic experience, post-apocalyptic atmosphere unique in the world), Beaches of Plage des Sables Blancs + Khor Ambado (urban beaches), Moucha and Maskali Islands 30 min by boat (iconic snorkelling and diving), Tadjourah (historic old town, former sultanate capital), Goda Mountains Day Forest National Park (microclimate altitude forest), swimming with whale sharks (October-February, the Gulf of Tadjourah is one of the world sites for whale shark observation). Safety: Djibouti = one of the safest countries in Africa thanks to massive foreign military presence, low urban crime in Djibouti city. Try sambusa (iconic samosas Horn of Africa), skoudehkaris (chicken with spices), grilled fish, lahoh (Ethio-Somali pancake), Djiboutian coffee (mix of neighbouring Yemen + Ethiopia = exceptional quality), Somali tea with cardamom. Djibouti-Ambouli airport (JIB).
