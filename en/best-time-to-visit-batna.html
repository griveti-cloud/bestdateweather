Batna, town of the High Plateaus 425 km south-east of Algiers in Algeria: 290,000 inhabitants at 1040m altitude. Berber Chaoui capital = the town is at the heart of the Aurès region, historic stronghold of the Chaoui Berbers ("Imazighen"), people who triggered the Algerian War on 1 November 1954 by the first coordinated FLN attacks at Batna and surroundings. Modest town tourist-wise but access base to the Aurès and Timgad UNESCO. September is my favourite month (9.1/10): 16-30°C, 17% rain, ideal temperatures. May-June-October stay excellent (7.5-8.6/10). From April to October, it's the relative tourist high season: 9-34°C, semi-arid altitude climate. From July to August, expect 19-34°C with possible heatwave 40°C+ despite the altitude. From November to March, expect 1-15°C and 11-33% rain: cold semi-arid altitude with snow possible at the neighbouring mountains. Highlights: Timgad 35 km away (UNESCO 1982, one of the best-preserved Roman cities in the world, founded 100 AD by Emperor Trajan as a colony for veterans, nicknamed "Pompeii of Africa", regular grid plan with Trajan's Arch + theatre 3500 places + library + temples + baths, buried under Saharan sand since the 7th century then cleared 1881-1960 = major Algerian tourist signature), Lambèse / Lambaesis 30 km away (other major Roman site, former military camp of the Legio III Augusta 2nd century AD, praetorium = best-preserved Roman building in North Africa), Aurès mountains (Saharan Atlas chains up to 2326m Mt Chélia, wild landscapes, spectacular gorges Tighanimine + Rhoufi, traditional Chaoui villages Menaa + Tkout in stone), Tighanimine Gorges (spectacular 600m deep canyon), Ghoufi Balcony (panorama over Aurès troglodyte villages), Batna city centre (Algerian working-class atmosphere, central market, mosque). Safety: the Aurès region is globally calm, increased France Diplomacy vigilance for Algeria in general. Try chakhchoukha (Chaoui national dish = crumbled bread in sauce signature of the Aurès), Chaoui couscous with dried vegetables, méchoui spit-roast lamb, Aurès honey, Deglet Nour dates, mint tea. Logistics: Batna Mostefa Ben Boulaid airport (BLJ), also accessible by train or by taxi from Constantine (2h) or Algiers (6h).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
