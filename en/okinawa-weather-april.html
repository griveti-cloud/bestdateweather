Context

Our verdict

Okinawa, subtropical archipelago in southern Japan: 160 islands, 1.5 million inhabitants, distinct culture (Ryūkyū kingdom until 1879), American military bases (75% of US bases in Japan are here). October-November are my favourite months (8.0-8.2/10): 21-28°C, 37-45% rain, sea still at 26°C, end of typhoon season. From June to September, it's the hot/humid season: 45-75% rainfall, oppressive humidity (24-30°C), frequent typhoons (about ten per season, can paralyse inter-island ferries for several days). From December to March, expect 15-21°C: mild but often cloudy, sea at 22°C (still swimmable for Europeans). Highlights: Naha (capital, Shuri Castle former Ryūkyū royal palace UNESCO, burned down in 2019, reconstruction underway), Kokusai-dori (shopping street), Cape Manzamo. Islands to explore: Ishigaki (Kabira Bay beach), Iriomote (subtropical jungle), Taketomi (traditional village by bicycle), Miyako (beaches among Japan's most beautiful). Diving world-class spots (Yonaguni with its mysterious underwater ruins). Try sōki soba (pork noodles), gōyā champurū (sautéed bitter cucumber), awamori (rice alcohol). Direct flight from Tokyo (2h30) or Osaka (2h).
