Context

Our verdict

Skiathos, smallest island of the Sporades archipelago in the Aegean Sea: 6000 inhabitants on 49 km², in the west of the archipelago near the continental coast. Renowned for its exceptional fine sand beaches (rare in Greece where pebbles and rocks dominate): the most famous is Koukounaries, regularly ranked among the most beautiful beaches in Europe. Main filming location of the film Mamma Mia! 2008 and Mamma Mia! Here We Go Again 2018 (Skiathos for the main scenes, Vis Croatia for the sequel). July is my favourite month (9.5/10): 25-27°C, 6% rain, sea at 23°C, ideal temperatures. August stays excellent (9.2/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 22-27°C, sea at 21-25°C, hotel prices x2-x3 (British in the majority thanks to direct charter flights from UK). From November to March, expect 13-15°C and 25-38% rain: mild but windy, many hotels close. Unique climate: Skiathos has the particularity of having low temperature variations year-round (never below 13°C even in winter, never more than 27°C even in summer thanks to the constant sea breeze). Highlights / Beaches: Koukounaries ("Pines" in Greek, golden sand beach 1.2 km bordered by a dense pine forest, European Blue Flag, one of the iconic beaches of Greece, accessible by bus or car from Skiathos Town), Lalaria Beach (north coast, access only by boat, white round pebbles polished by the sea, white cliffs, Tripia Petra = natural arch in the cliff), Banana Beach and Little Banana (sand, younger atmosphere), Big Banana Beach (the liveliest), Vromolimnos (water sports), Aselinos and Mandraki (north coast, wilder), Agia Eleni Beach, Megali Aselinos, Krassa ("Naturist Beach" = official nudist beach). Skiathos Town (charming capital with whitewashed alleys, marina, dynamic nightlife with rooftop bars — including Final Step Bar sunset institution). Bourtzi (peninsula in the heart of the harbour with 13th-century Venetian fortress transformed into cultural place). Monastery of Panagia Evangelistria (18th century, the Greek flag was designed and raised for the first time here in 1807 during the war of independence). Excursions: Skopelos 30 min by ferry (neighbouring island, filming location Mamma Mia! notably the church Agios Ioannis sto Kastri perched on a rocky peak), Alonissos (national marine park of the Mediterranean monk seal), Mount Athos (visible in the distance in clear weather, religious access only to Orthodox men). Try kakavia (Greek fish soup), grilled octopus, local feta, ouzo, Retsina resin wine. Skiathos airport (JSI), famous for its spectacular landing above Megali Ammos beach = landing filmed by all Instagram avgeeks, similar to Saint Martin Princess Juliana.
