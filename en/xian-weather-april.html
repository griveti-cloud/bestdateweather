Context

Our verdict

Xi'an (Xian), capital of Shaanxi province in China: 9 million inhabitants, former capital of 13 Chinese dynasties (notably Han and Tang, for 1000 years, until the 9th century). Historic starting point of the Silk Road. Site of the most stunning archaeological discovery of the 20th century: the Terracotta Army. May is my favourite month (8.8/10): 15-28°C, 23% rain, ideal temperatures before the summer heatwave. From September to October, expect 10-26°C: excellent period, foliage early October. From June to August, expect 20-33°C with 26-35% rain: hot, sometimes scorching (39°C+). From December to February, expect -4/10°C and 6-13% rain: dry continental cold, atmospheric pollution often high (Xi'an suffers from the winter smog of northern China). Highlights: Terracotta Army (UNESCO, 35 km east of Xi'an, discovered by chance by farmers in 1974, 8000 life-size soldiers in baked clay buried in 210 BC to guard the tomb of Emperor Qin Shi Huang, first unifying Chinese emperor, 3 pits excavated out of an estimated 600 pits in total, still being excavated), Ming City Walls (14th century, 14 km tour on foot or by bike, one of the only intact Chinese ramparts, height 12m, width 15m), Big Wild Goose Pagoda (Big Wild Goose Pagoda, 64m, Tang dynasty 7th century, built to house the Buddhist sutras brought back from India by Xuanzang), Muslim Quarter (Hui People, descendants of Arab/Persian merchants of the Silk Road settled in Xi'an in the 7th-8th centuries, unique atmosphere, street food, Xi'an Mosque 14th century = the oldest intact mosque in China, atypical Chinese architecture for a mosque), Drum Tower and Bell Tower, Famen Temple 2h away (Buddha relic). Try rou jia mo ("Chinese hamburger" pulled-pork flatbread sandwich), biang biang noodles, lamb skewers from the Muslim quarter, cumin-flavoured beef.
