Context

Our verdict

Singapore is the ultra-modern city-state par excellence: 6 million inhabitants, 100% urban, but 47% green space. Equatorial climate = it's hot and humid ALL year (constant 24-30°C). February is my favourite month (6.9/10): 24-30°C, 68% rain, start of the north-east dry monsoon. From April to May and from November to January, it's monsoon season: 80-93% rainfall in brief but intense showers (often late afternoon). Singapore never tops 6.9/10: too humid a climate to reach excellent. Highlights: Gardens by the Bay (Supertrees, air-conditioned domes), Marina Bay Sands (pool 200m up, paid entry at the top), Sentosa (island park), Universal Studios. Hawker centres for cuisine (Maxwell, Lau Pa Sat, Newton): chicken rice, chili crab, laksa, char kway teow at 4-7$. Districts: Chinatown, Little India, Kampong Glam (Arab). Very expensive outside hawkers: restaurant meal 30-60$, 4-star hotel 200-400$.
