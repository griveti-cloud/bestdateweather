Bergen, second city of Norway: 290,000 inhabitants on the west coast, nicknamed the "Gateway to the Fjords" (starting point for Geirangerfjord, Sognefjord, Hardangerfjord). Also called the "rainiest city in Europe" (200-300 days of rain/year, 2300 mm/year). June is my favourite month (7.1/10): 9-16°C, 53% rain, long days. From June to August, it's the high season: 9-17°C, 53-66% rain (the chance of having a day without rain is low all year). Bergen never tops 7.1/10 (very humid oceanic climate). From September to March, expect -1/10°C and 56-68% precipitation (often snow in winter): Bergen under the rain or snow has its particular charm. From November to March, days at 6-7h of light, but Northern Lights rare at this latitude (60°N, further south than Tromsø). Highlights: Bryggen (UNESCO, 14th-century Hanseatic quarter, colourful wooden houses on the harbour, iconic of Bergen, several fires over the centuries, rebuilt identically), Fish Market (Fisketorget) (on the harbour, since 1276, fresh seafood), Fløibanen Funicular (8 min to Mount Fløyen 320m, panoramic view of the fjords and the town), Ulriksbanen Cable Car (Mount Ulriken 643m, 360° view), Saint Olaf Cathedral (12th century), Hanseatic Museum (life of the Hanseatic merchants). Fjord excursions: Norway in a Nutshell (train + boat + bus combo from Bergen to Flåm/Sognefjord, full day 1500-2000 NOK), Geirangerfjord cruise (UNESCO, 5h by ferry), Hardangerfjord (3rd longest fjord in the world). Try fiskesuppe (fish soup), bacalao, brunost (brown cheese characteristic of Norway). Very expensive (Norway = one of the most expensive countries in the world, meal 30-60€, beer 8-12€).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 22–28°C, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 16°C + tmax ≥ 26°C)
