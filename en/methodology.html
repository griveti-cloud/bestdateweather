The principle in one sentence

‚òÄÔ∏è
BestDateWeather is a probabilistic aggregator: it does not predict the weather, it calculates the likelihood of good conditions for your project at a given date and location, by combining 10 years of climate history with seasonal trend models.

This distinction matters. When you check a 5-day forecast, you get a real prediction from numerical atmospheric models. When you look up a date 4 months from now, you get a statistical estimate based on what has happened on the same dates in previous years, adjusted by current seasonal model trends.

All our weather data comes from Open-Meteo, an open-source API that aggregates ERA5 reanalysis data from the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts (ECMWF), along with operational models from M√©t√©o-France, Germany's DWD, and NOAA.

Why ERA5?

ERA5 is the world's reference reanalysis dataset: it reconstructs the state of the atmosphere hour by hour since 1940 at a 31 km resolution, assimilating all available observations (satellites, radiosondes, surface stations). It is the dataset used by climate researchers to establish norms.

Our analysis window covers the last 10 years (2015‚Äì2024). This is intentional: including older data would introduce a cooling bias that grows with climate change, making estimates less representative of current conditions.

‚ÑπÔ∏è
The World Meteorological Organization (WMO) standard for a "climatological normal" is 30 years. Our 10-year window is a pragmatic choice to reflect the recent climate ‚Äî we therefore use the term "recent trend" rather than "climatology" in the strict sense.

What our tool does not do

We prefer to be clear about limitations rather than downplay them.

‚ö†
BestDateWeather does not replace a professional weather forecast for high-stakes decisions (safety, heavy logistics). For an important wedding or festival, always cross-check with a local meteorological service within 7 days of the event.
  • No microclimates. ERA5 spatial resolution is 31 km ‚Äî local variations (valleys, coastlines, altitude) are not captured. A seaside resort can differ by 5¬∞C from the nearest city.
  • No climate change projection. Our 10-year window partially incorporates recent warming, but does not extrapolate future trends. Projections to 2030 and beyond are outside our scope.
  • No forecasts beyond 7 months. Beyond this limit, even ECMWF seasonal models no longer carry significant predictive skill. We then display a pure historical profile without correction.
  • Extreme events are not foreseeable. Hurricanes, exceptional storms, record heatwaves ‚Äî these rare events are not represented in average statistics.
  • No hourly data beyond D+7. For future dates, we display an indicative hourly daily profile, not hour-by-hour forecasts.

Frequently asked questions

Where does sea temperature data come from?

Sea surface temperature comes from Open-Meteo's Marine API for nearby dates (D+0 to D+7) and from a historical climatological database by coastal city for dates further ahead.

Why are sunrise and sunset times calculated rather than forecast?

Sunrise and sunset are deterministic: they depend solely on latitude, longitude, and date. They are computed by an astronomical algorithm and do not vary from year to year (to within a few seconds).

What is the difference between the "pessimistic" and "optimistic" scenarios?

Scenarios are built from the P10 and P90 percentiles of the historical distribution. The pessimistic scenario corresponds to the 10% least favourable days observed over the period (maximum rain, extreme temperatures, strong winds). The optimistic to the 10% most favourable days.

How do I compare two dates or two destinations?

Use the "12-month view" to compare scores month by month for a destination. To compare two cities, run two separate searches and compare the scores for the same period.

How often is the data updated?

Historical data (climate baseline) is fixed ‚Äî it covers 2015‚Äì2024. Real-time forecasts (D+0 to D+7) are fetched live from the Open-Meteo API at each consultation, with an update latency of 1 hour.
