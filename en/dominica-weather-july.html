Context

Our verdict

Dominica is the "nature island" of the Caribbean: 750 km², 70,000 inhabitants, mountainous (Morne Diablotin 1447m), volcanic, 365 rivers, primary tropical forests. Not to be confused with the Dominican Republic. May is my favourite month (8.6/10): 23-28°C, 41% rain, sea at 27°C. From June to November, it's the cyclonic season: 59-74% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Maria 2017 devastated the island at 90%). From January to April, expect 21-27°C: tourist high season. Highlights: Morne Trois Pitons National Park (UNESCO, volcanic landscapes), Boiling Lake (boiling water lake, 2nd largest in the world, 12h round trip hike, demanding), Trafalgar Falls (twin waterfalls), Emerald Pool (emerald basin in the forest), Champagne Reef (volcanic underwater bubbles, snorkelling), Sari Sari Falls, Middleham Falls. Roseau = capital, market. Carib Territory in the east (reserve of the last Kalinago descendants, prehispanic Amerindian people). Whale watching (sperm whales year-round, one of the rare spots in the world with permanent presence). No mass tourism: no large all-inclusive, eco-lodge hotels and guesthouses. Try mountain chicken (actually giant frog, now protected species), callaloo, sancocho, local Macoucherie rum. Flight via Antigua, Barbados or Saint Lucia + small plane 30 min.
