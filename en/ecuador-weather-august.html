Context

Our verdict

Ecuador, smallest Andean country: 18 million inhabitants on 283,000 km² crossed by the geographic equator (hence its name). 4 distinct regions: Sierra Andean, Costa Pacific, Oriente Amazonian, Galápagos. From July to September are my favourite months (7.6-7.7/10) at altitude: 7-19°C, 32-46% rain, this is the Andean dry season. From June to September, it's the tourist high season for the Sierra: 7-19°C, clear sky. From November to May, it's the rainy season in the Sierra: 79-93% rainfall, green landscapes but often in fog. On the coast, reversed seasons (dry December-May, rainy June-November). Regions to know: Quito (capital 2850m, UNESCO 1978, largest preserved colonial historic centre in Latin America, see dedicated review), Galápagos (UNESCO 1978, archipelago of 19 islands 1000 km from the continent, unique fauna: giant tortoises, marine iguanas, blue-footed boobies, equator penguins; Charles Darwin developed his theory of evolution here in 1835; minimum 8 days cruise recommended, prices 3000-8000€ high season), Cuenca (UNESCO, 3rd city, colonial high coconut trees), Otavalo (2h from Quito, the largest indigenous market in South America on Saturdays), Mitad del Mundo ("Middle of the World", monument at the equator 0°, iconic photo balanced on the line, but the real geographic equator is 240m further by modern GPS), Cotopaxi Volcano (5897m, one of the highest active volcanoes in the world, 2-day ascent), Chimborazo Volcano (6263m, the "closest point to the sun" if measured from the centre of the Earth, equatorial bulge), Baños (Andean "Little Switzerland", Pailón del Diablo waterfall hike), Ecuadorian Amazon (Yasuní National Park = one of the most biodiverse in the world). Try ceviche de camarón (coastal), llapingachos (potato cakes), encebollado (fish soup), fritada, cuy (roast guinea pig in the Sierra).
