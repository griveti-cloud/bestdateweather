FAQ

Bali wins overall. Bali's dry season (May-October) delivers scores of 8.5-9.5/10. The Dominican Republic peaks in January-April (8.0-8.5/10) but faces more hurricane risk June-October. For reliability: Bali's dry season. For winter sun from North America: Dominican Republic.
The Dominican Republic wins for pure beach — Punta Cana's 50km of white sand and turquoise Caribbean water is among the world's best beach setups. Bali offers dramatically more beyond the beach: rice terraces, temples, spa culture.
Dominican Republic is much closer — 9-11 hours vs 16-18 hours to Bali. For Europeans on a 1-2 week holiday: Dominican Republic saves 12+ hours of travel. Bali makes more sense for 2-3 week trips.
The Dominican Republic is in the Atlantic hurricane belt (June-October). Travel insurance is essential June-October. Bali is outside the main typhoon paths. For weather reliability year-round: Bali is safer.
Dominican Republic for beach relaxation — all-inclusive resorts, shorter flight, English spoken. Bali for those wanting culture with their beach — temples, rice terraces, cooking classes, world-class spa. Bali is more experiential; Dominican Republic is easier.
Bali is best in July (7.6/10). Dominican Republic is best in January (8.2/10).
