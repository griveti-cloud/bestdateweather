How we calculate the score

For mountain destinations, we calculate two scores per month — skiing and hiking — and display the higher of the two.

⛷️Ski score DEC → MAY
  • 40% snow cover (slope T° corrected by lapse rate -6.5°C/1000m)
  • 40% winter thermal comfort (-5 to 5°C optimal)
  • 20% sunshine
  • Off-season penalty: ×0.70 adjacent months · ×0.35 outside season
🥾Hiking score JUN → SEP
  • 40% temperature (10–22°C optimal)
  • 40% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 20% sunshine

Context

Our verdict

At Chamonix, the question isn't when, but why. For skiing, March remains my pick: long days already (10h of sunlight), still maximum snow cover on the Grands Montets glacier, transformed snow that forgives mistakes. February is colder (-2/7°C) but great for powder. During Christmas and February school breaks, prices double or triple, lifts have queues, restaurants turn people away. For hiking, July-August open up Mont Blanc and the great routes (Refuges Albert 1er, Couvercle), but it's packed. September stays my favourite in summer: stable weather, refuges still open until the 20th, a third of the crowds. October keeps a mild climate (tmax 15°C, little rain) and remains excellent for self-guided hiking — even though the lifts close and refuges shutter. November marks the real transition: everything closes, weather often bleak, this is for those who love wide empty spaces.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Chamonix?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 9.1/10). For hiking/summer: March (9.1/10, 8.0°C).
Can you ski in Chamonix in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.3/10. Cold temperatures (4°C max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Chamonix in summer?
In March, temperatures average 8.0°C. Best period for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Chamonix?
November is the wettest month with November has the lowest climate score of the year.
