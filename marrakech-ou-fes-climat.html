FAQ

Marrakech is drier but hotter (40°C+ in summer). Fes sits at 500m — slightly cooler in summer (35°C) and genuinely cold in winter nights (5-8°C). Both have excellent spring and autumn weather (22-26°C).
Fes el-Bali wins on historic authenticity — the world's largest medieval Islamic city (UNESCO), the largest car-free urban zone on Earth, essentially unchanged for 800 years. Marrakech's medina is more theatrical and tourist-oriented.
Marrakech is more tourist-oriented and easier to navigate. Fes's medina is famously disorienting (9,000 lanes) — a licensed guide is strongly recommended. Marrakech has better international flight connections. For first-time Morocco: Marrakech is the gateway.
Yes — Marrakech to Fes by train takes 8h or 7-8h direct bus. Classic 10-day Morocco circuit: Marrakech → Atlas Mountains → desert → Fes → Chefchaouen → fly home.
March is excellent for both — 20-24°C, spring light, far fewer tourists than summer. Fes in March has the World Sacred Music Festival (variable date). Marrakech in March has the Marathon. Both cities at their most atmospheric in spring.
Marrakech is best in May (8.8/10). Fes is best in May (9.2/10).
