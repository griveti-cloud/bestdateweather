Context

Our verdict

Rajasthan, the largest Indian state (132,000 sq miles): 80 million inhabitants, land of Maharajas, Thar desert, palaces, fortresses, colorful traditions. February-March are my favorite months (8.9/10): 55-88°F, 3-9% rain, ideal temperatures. From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 48-81°F, ideal dry climate. From April to June, it's the extreme heatwave: 73-104°F (sometimes 120°F in the desert), impossible to visit. From July to September, it's the light monsoon: 41-69% rainfall, green landscapes unusual for the region. Classic itineraries: Golden Triangle Delhi-Agra-Jaipur (5-7 days), Royal Loop Delhi-Jaipur-Pushkar-Udaipur-Jodhpur-Jaisalmer-Bikaner-Delhi (15-21 days). Main cities: Jaipur ("pink"), Udaipur ("white"), Jodhpur ("blue"), Jaisalmer ("golden"), Pushkar (Hindu holy city, camel festival in November), Bikaner (Junagarh Fort, Karni Mata rat temple). National parks: Ranthambore (Bengal tigers, October-June, jeeps booked 90 days ahead), Sariska (less frequented alternative). Festivals: Holi (March, festival of colors), Diwali (October-November), Pushkar Camel Fair (November, 50,000 camels). Try dal baati churma, gatte ki sabzi, ker sangri, Jodhpur lassi. October-November and March-April are the best tourism/weather compromises.
