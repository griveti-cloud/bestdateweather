Bujumbura, economic capital of Burundi in Great Lakes Africa on the shores of Lake Tanganyika: 600,000 inhabitants. Official administrative capital since 2018 = Gitega (62 miles inland), but Bujumbura remains the de facto economic capital. Country among the poorest and most densely populated in Africa (12 million inhabitants on 10,700 sq miles), traumatised by inter-Hutu/Tutsi ethnic violence (civil war 1993-2005 = 300,000 deaths, partial genocide similar to neighbouring Rwanda 1994). Capital modernised since 2010 but still politically unstable. August is my favorite month (9.4/10): 68-82°F, 4% rain, this is the ideal dry season. June-July-September stay excellent (9.0-9.2/10). From May to October, it's the cool dry season: 66-84°F thanks to altitude (Bujumbura at 2,500 ft on the shores of Tanganyika), unique temperate-tropical climate in sub-Saharan Africa. From November to April, it's the rainy season: 25-61% rainfall, but stable temperatures 68-82°F, more verdant landscapes. Permanent climate: Bujumbura never tops 84°F in maximum average and rarely descends below 66°F in minimum average thanks to altitude + influence of the great lake, ideal "Eternal Spring" tropical altitude climate. Highlights: Lake Tanganyika (the 2nd deepest lake in the world after Baikal Russia 4,800 ft deep, the 6th largest African lake, bordering Tanzania-DRC-Zambia, beaches with golden sand unusual for a freshwater lake, refreshing swimming 73-79°F, hippopotamuses and crocodiles in some areas DRC side), Bujumbura beaches = main attraction: Saga Beach, Karera Beach, Carrefour Beach (surprising seaside atmosphere in the heart of Great Lakes Africa), Régina Mundi Cathedral, Central Mosque of Bujumbura, Central Market (East African atmosphere), Living Museum of Bujumbura (Burundian living museum with Drums of Burundi dancers = UNESCO cultural heritage 2014, traditional percussion performance iconic Burundian cultural recognised worldwide), Source of the Nile (Southernmost source of the Nile south of Bujumbura at Rutovu = pyramid marked "1934, southernmost source of the Nile" = theoretical source farthest from the Nile delta in Egypt, iconic geographic experience with classic source-Nile controversy between Burundi-Rwanda-Uganda-Ethiopia), Rusizi National Park (9 miles from Bujumbura, hippopotamuses-crocodiles-vervet monkeys observation half-day safari). Burundi excursions: Gitega 2h away (official administrative capital since 2018, more traditional atmosphere), Kibira National Park (primary altitude forest, wild chimpanzee observation possible), Karera hot springs, Vyanda Reserve (chimpanzees). International excursions: Rwanda Kigali 6h by bus (Akanyaru border), Tanzania Kigoma on Tanganyika east side by weekly ferry MV Liemba (venerable German boat from 1913 crossing the lake), DRC Uvira 30 min west side (border). Safety: fragile political situation after 2015 coup attempt, moderate urban crime, France Diplomacy advises against Bujumbura at night + Tanzania-DRC borders. Try ugali (East African iconic corn porridge), Tanganyika fish (iconic endemic Mukeke), brazier skewers, beans-rice, plantain, Primus Beer (national beer brewed in Bujumbura), Burundian coffee (one of the best Arabica coffees in the world for niche, mainly exported to United States and Japan specialty). Bujumbura International airport (BJM).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
