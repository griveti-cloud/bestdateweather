Context

Our verdict

Fiji is an archipelago of 333 islands in the South Pacific: 900,000 inhabitants, 2 large islands (Viti Levu where the capital Suva is located, Vanua Levu), hundreds of smaller islands (Mamanuca and Yasawa Islands for tourism). June is my favorite month (7.7/10): 63-72°F, 47% rain, austral dry season. From May to October, it's the dry season: 47-60% rainfall, temperatures 61-77°F, sea at 77-79°F. From November to April, it's the wet season: 75-94% rainfall, heavy heat (68-79°F), real cyclone risk (Winston 2016 category 5 killed 44 people). Highlights: Mamanuca Islands (the most accessible from Nadi, day trips by boat), Yasawa Islands (wilder, further away), Coral Coast (south coast of Viti Levu, hotels), Suva (capital), Garden of the Sleeping Giant (botanical garden). Activities: diving (among the world's best, magnificent soft corals), kava ceremony (traditional drink from pepper-tree root, welcomed in villages), surf (Cloudbreak at Tavarua world-renowned). Cuisine Indo-Fijian influence (40% of Fijians of Indian origin, British colonial heritage): kokoda (raw fish in coconut milk), rourou (taro), curry. Direct flight from Sydney/Auckland in 3h, from Los Angeles in 11h.
