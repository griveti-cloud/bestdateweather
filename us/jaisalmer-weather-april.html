Context

Our verdict

Jaisalmer, the "Golden City": 78,000 inhabitants, yellow sandstone citadel in the heart of the Thar desert near the Pakistani border, one of the rare still-inhabited fortresses in the world. November is my favorite month (9.2/10): 63-84°F, 1% rain, ideal temperatures for the desert. From November to February, it's the high season: 48-81°F, ideal climate. From March to June, it's the extreme heatwave: 66-106°F (sometimes 120°F), desert at 122°F, impossible. July is the worst month (2.1/10): 82-100°F with 24% light monsoon, monstrous humidity for the desert. Highlights: Sonar Quila/Golden Fort (one of the only "living forts" in the world, 5000 people still live here, but overcrowding threatens its foundations), Patwon Ki Haveli (5 sumptuously sculpted havelis), Salim Singh Ki Haveli, Nathmal Ki Haveli, Jain Temples (12th-17th centuries in the fort), Gadi Sagar Lake (walking distance from the fort, atmospheric at sunset). Desert excursions: Sam Sand Dunes 26 miles away (the most famous but touristy, camel ride, night in luxury "glamping" tent), Khuri Sand Dunes 31 miles away (quieter, less frequented). Try ker sangri (desert vegetables), bajre ki roti, ghotua ladoo. Logistics: night train from Jodhpur (5h) or Delhi (3 PM), no direct commercial airport. October and March are the best compromises.
