Context

Our verdict

Peloponnese (Πελοπόννησος), large peninsula of southern Greece connected to mainland Greece by the Isthmus of Corinth: 1.15 million inhabitants on 8,320 sq miles. Cradle of ancient Greek civilisation = legendary sites of Olympia + Mycenae + Sparta + Epidaurus + Mistra. Geographically separated from the continent since 1893 when the Corinth Canal was dug (4 miles × 81 ft × 260 ft deep, one of the most spectacular canals in the world, dizzying artificial gorge iconic view of modern Greece). June is my favorite month (9.3/10): 64-84°F, 16% rain, sea at 72°F, ideal temperatures. May-September stay excellent (8.9-9.0/10). From April to October, it's the tourist high season: 50-91°F, ideal Mediterranean climate. From July to August, expect 72-91°F with possible 104°F+ heatwave. From November to March, expect 39-66°F and 33-40% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, snow possible at Mt Taygetos 7,900 ft. Highlights: Olympia (UNESCO 1989, ancient sacred town of Olympia where the ancient Olympic Games were held 776 BC-393 AD every 4 years for 1170 years = universal cultural iconic, Temple of Zeus with columns collapsed by earthquake 522 AD + chryselephantine statue of Zeus = one of the 7 Wonders of the ancient world today disappeared, Ancient Stadium where the modern Olympic flame is lit by the rays of the sun in a parabolic mirror before each Olympics since 1936, palaestra + gymnasium + Phidias workshop), Mycenae (UNESCO 1999, capital of King Agamemnon in Homer's Iliad, one of the oldest fortified cities in Europe 16th-12th BC, Lion Gate 13th BC, Treasury of Atreus dome tomb 13th BC one of the largest pre-Roman structures, "Agamemnon" gold mask found by Schliemann 1876 preserved at Athens National Museum), Epidaurus (UNESCO 1988, sanctuary of Asclepius 4th BC, Ancient Theater of Epidaurus = one of the best preserved ancient theaters in the world, capacity 14,000 places, exceptional acoustics that allow you to hear from the last row a coin falling at the center of the stage, still used for theater in summer during the Athens-Epidaurus Festival), Sparta (the legendary city-state of the Spartans rival of Athens 5th century BC, few visible vestiges today ironically as the Spartans did not build in durable stone, more symbol than site), Mistra (UNESCO 1989, former Byzantine capital of the Despotate of Morea 13th-15th century, impressive ruins on hill with 30 preserved monasteries and palaces, one of the most beautiful Byzantine medieval cities in the world), Monemvasia ("Greek Gibraltar", medieval fortified town on islet connected by narrow strip of land, unique atmosphere in the Mediterranean, one of the postcard images of the Peloponnese), Nafplio (Nafplio, first capital of modern Greece 1828-1834 before Athens, preserved Venetian old town, Palamidi fortress on 710 ft hill), Mani peninsula (the wildest region of Greece, unique-in-the-world stone Maniote tower-houses, austere atmosphere, preserved beaches Vathia + Limeni), Kalamata (already covered), Mistras, Pylos and Methoni (Venetian fortresses), Voidokilia Beach (one of the most beautiful beaches in Europe according to many rankings, supernatural regular arc bay near Pylos), Patras (3rd city of Greece, ferry port to Italy). Itinerary: 7-10 days by car to cover the essentials, base at Nafplio ideal for visiting Mycenae-Epidaurus-Olympia-Sparta-Mistra-Monemvasia. Try Greek moussaka, souvlaki, gyros, IGP Peloponnesian feta, iconic IGP Kalamata olives, IGP Kalamata olive oil, Mantineia AOC moscofilero white wine, Nemea AOC agiorgitiko red wine, ouzo, tsipouro, Mavroudi from the Peloponnese.
