Context

Our verdict

Wroclaw (Breslau), 4th city of Poland in the south-west in Lower Silesia: 670,000 inhabitants. Former capital of Silesia, town with complicated history (Polish in the Middle Ages, Austrian 1526-1741, Prussian-German 1741-1945, Polish since 1945 after population exchange). 70% destroyed in 1945 during the Soviet siege. Nicknamed the "Polish Venice" for its 12 river islands and 130 bridges. European Capital of Culture 2016. June is my favorite month (8.1/10): 57-75°F, 35% rain, ideal temperatures. From June to August are the tourist high season: 57-77°F. From November to March, expect 30/50°F and 30-38% rain: continental cold, snow possible. Highlights: Rynek (Market Square, one of the largest medieval squares in Europe 700 ft × 580 ft, bordered by colorful 13th-19th century burgher houses, 13th-16th century Gothic town hall in the center, lively atmosphere terraces), Saint John the Baptist Cathedral (Ostrów Tumski, on the ecclesiastical island = cradle of Wroclaw, 13th-century Gothic), University of Wroclaw (one of the oldest in Central Europe founded 1702, Aula Leopoldina = exceptional baroque hall, mathematician Steinhaus + 9 Nobel laureates from the university), Panorama Racławicka (circular 360° painting of 370 ft circumference × 49 ft high representing the battle of Racławice 1794, one of the last great panoramic paintings in the world still exhibited), Hala Stulecia (Centennial Hall UNESCO 2006, reinforced concrete dome 1913 by Max Berg, one of the first large reinforced concrete buildings in the world, capacity 6500 people, considered an architectural masterpiece precursor of modernism), Krasnale ("Wroclaw Dwarves", 800+ statuettes of dwarves 30 cm high scattered in the city, to seek as a treasure hunt, tribute to the Pomarańczowa Alternatywa movement which drew dwarves to ridicule the communist regime in the 80s). Quarter of the 4 Religions (4 places of worship of 4 different religions — Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox, Jewish — cohabit in 4 adjacent streets). Excursions: Ksiaz Castle 1h away (3rd largest castle in Poland, basements used as Nazi bunkers), Auschwitz 3h by bus (UNESCO memorial place former extermination camp), Karkonosze National Park (Giant Mountains, skiing). Try pierogi ruskie (potato cheese dumplings), zurek, bigos, makowiec (poppy cake), Tyskie or Lech beer.
