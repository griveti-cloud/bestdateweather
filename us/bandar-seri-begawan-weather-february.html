Context

Our verdict

Bandar Seri Begawan ("BSB"), capital of Brunei (Brunei Darussalam) on the island of Borneo: 100,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 280,000 in urban area. Absolute sultanate ruled by Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah since 1967 (one of the longest reigns in the world, 56 years in 2023, 5th world fortune according to Forbes 28 billion USD personal). Ultra-rich country thanks to oil and gas, one of the strictest countries in the world religiously (full sharia since 2014 = stoning for adultery, death penalty for homosexuality since 2019 = international boycott of Sultan hotels including Beverly Hills Hotel + The Dorchester London). February is my favorite month (7.6/10): 75-84°F, 68% rain, this is the relative dry season. BSB never tops 7.6/10 (very humid equatorial climate year-round). From January to March, it's the relative dry season: 75-84°F. From September to November, it's the most humid season: 88-94% rainfall. Extremely stable climate: BSB never tops 86°F in maximum average and rarely descends below 75°F in minimum average, annual range among the smallest in the world. Highlights: Sultan Omar Ali Saifuddin Mosque (1958, 24-carat gold dome iconic view of BSB, one of the most beautiful mosques in South East Asia, open to non-Muslims outside prayers), Jame'Asr Hassanil Bolkiah Mosque (1992, largest mosque in Brunei with 29 domes in tribute to the 29 Sultans of the dynasty, capacity 5000 worshippers), Istana Nurul Iman (Sultan's Palace, the largest residential palace in the world according to Guinness Book = 1788 rooms + 257 bathrooms + 110 garages = for the Sultan's private collection estimated at 7000 cars including 600 Rolls-Royce, open to the public only 3 days/year for Hari Raya end of Ramadan), Royal Regalia Museum (museum of royal regalia, state diplomatic gifts received by the Sultan, golden royal palanquin for his wedding), Kampong Ayer ("Water Village", stilt quarter on the Brunei river housing 13,000 inhabitants for 1300 years, one of the largest aquatic-villages in the world still inhabited, nicknamed "Venice of the Orient" by Magellan in 1521 during his passage, accessible by traditional water taxi), Tasek Lama Recreational Park (forest park in the city center with proboscis monkeys endemic to Borneo). Essential excursions = this is why one comes to Brunei: Ulu Temburong National Park 1h by boat + 4x4 (Borneo primary jungle park, one of the last intact ecosystems in South East Asia, accessible only by guided expeditions, Canopy Walkway = canopy walkways at 200 ft high iconic, observation orangutans-proboscis monkeys-hornbills-cobras-leeches, exotic iconic experience). Safety: Brunei very safe criminally, but very strict Islamic laws applied even to foreigners (private alcohol forbidden, conservative dress code, public shows of affection forbidden). No alcohol in Brunei (total ban, possibility of bringing 2 liters for private consumption on arrival for non-Muslims). Try ambuyat ("national dish" = sago porridge with spicy fermented durian tempoyak sauce + cacapan, iconic unique Bruneian culinary experience), nasi katok (rice with curry + fried chicken, popular snack), kueh (Malay cakes), local coffee. Brunei International airport (BWN).
