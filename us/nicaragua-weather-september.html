Context

Our verdict

Nicaragua, country in Central America between Honduras and Costa Rica: 6.7 million inhabitants. Capital Managua. Country marked by the Sandinista revolution 1979 (fall of the Somoza dictatorship) then civil war Contra 1981-1990 (rebels armed-financed by Reagan CIA), contested re-election of President Daniel Ortega 2007-today become himself authoritarian (massive demonstrations repressed 2018 = 300 deaths, opponents imprisoned-exiled since). Country among the poorest in Latin America. January-March are my favorite months (6.9/10): 72-93°F, 8-10% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Nicaragua never tops 6.9/10 (hot humid tropical climate year-round). From November to April, it's the dry season: 72-93°F, this is the tourist high season. From May to October, it's the rainy season: 57-88% rainfall. Highlights: Granada (see dedicated review), León ("the other old colonial capital", historic rival of Granada, more university and revolutionary atmosphere = birthplace of poet Rubén Darío + cradle of Sandinism, León Cathedral UNESCO 2011 = the largest cathedral in Central America, panoramic roof ascent iconic), Managua capital (little touristic, ravaged by the 1972 earthquake which destroyed the historic center, chaotic capital rebuilt randomly), Volcán Masaya 30 min from Managua (one of the only active volcanoes in the world where one can look into the crater of the incandescent lava lake = unique iconic experience, access by car to the edge of the Santiago crater, to see at dusk for visual effect), Volcán Concepción and Volcán Maderas (on the Ometepe island in the Lake Nicaragua = the largest volcanic island in freshwater lake in the world, 2 volcanoes in "hourglass", demanding Concepción 5,300 ft hike but exceptional panorama over the largest lake in Central America), San Juan del Sur (south Pacific beach resort, popular surf beach for international surfers, nightlife), Caribbean coast ("Mosquito Coast", little accessible and very different from the rest of the country, indigenous Miskito people, distinct Afro-Caribbean atmosphere, Bluefields), Corn Islands (Big Corn / Little Corn, Caribbean archipelago accessible by plane, paradise turquoise beaches, reggae creole atmosphere, traditional alternative to traditional tourist Caribbean), Indio-Maíz Reserve (preserved jungle Costa Rican border), Solentiname (archipelago of islands in Lake Nicaragua, community of naive primitive Nicaraguan painters). Geological particularity: Nicaragua has 19 volcanoes of which 7 active, crossed by the "Pacific Cordillera" volcanic alignment. Safety: fragile political situation since 2018, potential demonstrations, but crime globally lower than in neighbouring Honduras. Try gallo pinto (rice+red beans, national dish shared with Costa Rica), nacatamales (giant Nicaraguan tamales), vigorón, quesillo (tortilla+cheese+onions), local Toña/Victoria beer, Flor de Caña Nicaraguan rum (one of the best in the world, several international awards). Managua Augusto César Sandino airport (MGA).
