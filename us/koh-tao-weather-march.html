What to expect in March

March is peak dry season in Koh Tao. The air is warm and humidity more bearable than in the rainy season. Rain risk remains low (17.0% of days), leaving good room for outdoor activities. Not the best window, but acceptable if dates are constrained. January (8.6/10) is significantly better if possible.

Context

Our verdict

Koh Tao, small island on Thailand's east coast in the Gulf of Thailand: 8 sq miles, 2000 inhabitants, nicknamed "Turtle Island" (its shape recalls a turtle + sea turtles historically present). One of the world's destinations for learning to dive (PADI Open Water training 9000 baht ≈ $275 all-inclusive, among the cheapest in the world). January-February are my favorite months (8.6/10): 77-81°F, 28-30% rain. From January to August, it's the relative dry season in the Gulf (different climate from the Andaman west coast): 77-84°F, sea at 82°F. From May to August, it's the tourist high season. From September to December, it's the north-east monsoon: 44-84% rainfall, rough sea, many dive centers close or scale back. Highlights: Sairee Beach (the longest at 1 miles, nightlife, restaurants), Mae Haad (port, ferries), Chalok Baan Kao (south, quieter), Shark Bay (snorkelling with blacktip reef sharks and turtles), Tanote Bay (east coast, 4×4 access tricky, snorkelling, deep water solo climbing), John-Suwan Viewpoint (south panorama over 3 clover-leaf bays, 30 min climb), Koh Nang Yuan (3-pronged islet linked by sandbars, one of Thailand's best-known postcard images, 1500 baht private entry). Major dive sites: Chumphon Pinnacle (the top, seasonal bull sharks), Sail Rock (between Koh Tao and Koh Phangan, one of the Gulf's best sites, whale shark possible March-May), Japanese Gardens, Twin Peaks, White Rock. PADI centers: Koh Tao has the world's highest density of dive centers (50+), Big Blue Diving and Crystal Dive among the best known. Crowd: backpackers mostly, 20-35 year olds, international diver training. Excursions: Koh Phangan 1h30 by ferry (Full Moon Party at Haad Rin), Koh Samui 2h (more upmarket island). Try Massaman curry, BBQ seafood, tropical smoothies. Logistics: ferries from Chumphon (2h, the most practical), Surat Thani (4h), Koh Samui (2h).
