What to expect in January

January is peak dry season in Battambang. The air is warm and humidity more bearable than in the rainy season. Rain is virtually absent (10.0% of days) — ideal for planning without a backup. Not the best window, but acceptable if dates are constrained. January (6.9/10) is significantly better if possible.

Context

Our verdict

Battambang, 2nd city of Cambodia: 250,000 inhabitants on the Sangker river at the heart of the Cambodian "rice belt". Often neglected by tourists in favour of Siem Reap (Angkor) or Phnom Penh, it is yet the Cambodian city where the French colonial heritage is best preserved (1920s-30s architecture along the river). January-February are my favorite months (6.9/10): 72-93°F, 10-15% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Battambang never tops 6.9/10 (hot tropical climate). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 72-95°F, dry climate, bearable temperatures in the morning and evening. From March to May, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 77-95°F (sometimes 104°F), impossible to visit in mid-day. From May to October, it's the monsoon: 63-89% rainfall, but milder temperatures (75-93°F). Highlights: Bamboo Train ("Norry", bamboo platforms on rusty railway tracks, propelled by an 8 hp engine, speed 19 miles/h, 4.3 miles ride through rice paddies and villages, 5$/person, unique world experience), Sampov Lighthouse 7 miles away (hill with temple, Killing Caves memorial of the Khmer Rouge massacres 1975-1979), Battambang Bat Cave (near Phnom Sampeau, exit of millions of bats every evening 6pm, 30 min spectacle in continuous black line, free), Wat Banan 16 miles away (11th-century Angkorian temple, mini Angkor prefiguration), Wat Kor Temples (on foot in the city). Asia Lighthouse and French colonial architecture of the city center. Phnom Penh 5h by bus, Siem Reap 4h by bus (Angkor). Try amok (fish curry), beef lok lak, num pang sandwich (French heritage), bia angkor.
