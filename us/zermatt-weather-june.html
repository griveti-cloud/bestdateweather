Context

Our verdict

Zermatt, premium Swiss ski resort at the foot of the Matterhorn (14,700 ft, the most recognised Alpine summit): 5800 inhabitants, car-free village (electric taxis, horse-drawn carriages), accessible only by train from Täsch. July is my favorite month (8.0/10): 48-66°F, 36% rain, alpine hiking in high summer. From June to September, expect 43-66°F: summer hiking season (Five Lakes, Riffelsee with Matterhorn reflection), MTB, climbing. From December to April, expect 19/36°F and 35-42% precipitation often as snow: prime ski season (200 miles of pistes linked Zermatt-Cervinia cross-border to Italy, year-round summer skiing possible at Plateau Rosa 12,700 ft). May and October-November are the off-seasons: little snow and limited hiking, many hotels close for 4-6 weeks. Highlights: Gornergrat (10,100 ft, rack railway since 1898, panorama over 29 peaks above 13,100 ft, Matterhorn/Monte Rosa view), Klein Matterhorn (12,700 ft, Europe's highest cable car station, year-round glacier skiing), Sunnegga-Rothorn (lakes-side panoramic), Five Lakes Walk (5 lakes with Matterhorn reflections), Bahnhofstrasse (main street, palace hotels since 1854). Logistics: Zermatt 3h30 from Geneva by train (change at Visp). Very expensive: restaurant meals $33-$66, ski pass $88/day, hotels $165-$1,100/night. Try raclette, fondue, cervelas. Matterhorn ascent via local guides ($1,650/person, 4 days acclimatisation, advanced mountaineering level).

When is the best time to visit Zermatt?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: April (ski score 9.3/10). For hiking/suiner: April (9.3/10, 6.0°F).
Can you ski in Zermatt in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.7/10. Cold temperatures (-1°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Zermatt in suiner?
In April, temperatures average 6.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
