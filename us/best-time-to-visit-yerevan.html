Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Yerevan, capital of Armenia: 1 million inhabitants at 3,200 ft altitude on the Armenian plateau, spectacular view of Mount Ararat (16,900 ft, sacred summit for Armenians where Noah's Ark allegedly landed according to Genesis, paradoxically located in Turkish territory since 1920 — permanent nostalgia). The world's oldest continuously inhabited capital according to dating (Erebuni fortress 782 BC, that is 30 years before the founding of Rome). Multi-millennial country (1st country in the world to adopt Christianity as state religion in 301 AD). June and September are my favorite months (9.4/10): 61-86°F, 11-16% rain, ideal temperatures. From May and September-October, these are the best seasons: 50-82°F, 11-40% rain. From July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (70-93°F, continental climate). From December to March, expect 25/54°F and 19-35% rain: dry winter cold, snow possible. Highlights: Republic Square (heart of the city, Soviet neo-Armenian architectural ensemble signed Alexander Tamanian 1924 who redesigned Yerevan in concentric radial plan with iconic pink volcanic tuff buildings), Cascade (monumental staircase 572 steps, open-air contemporary sculpture garden with Botero, Lynn Chadwick), Matenadaran ("manuscript depository", one of the world's most important centers of ancient manuscripts, 17,000 manuscripts including 12th-15th century Armenian religious illuminations), Armenian Genocide Memorial ("Tsitsernakaberd", essential to understand Armenian identity, 1.5 million deaths 1915-1923, Turkish denial continues to poison diplomacy), Saint Gregory the Illuminator Cathedral (1997-2001, the largest Armenian cathedral in the world), GUM Market (spices, dried fruits), Yerevan Brandy factory (Ararat brandy, the favorite brandy of Churchill and Stalin). Excursions: Etchmiadzin 20 min away (UNESCO, seat of the Armenian Apostolic Church 301, equivalent of the Vatican), Garni 1h away (Greek-Roman temple 1st century, the only Greco-Roman temple still standing in the former USSR, restored in the 1970s), Geghard (UNESCO, medieval monastery partially carved into the rock), Lake Sevan (6,200 ft altitude, the largest alpine lake in the Caucasus). Try Armenian dolma (stuffed vine leaves), khorovats (traditional lamb BBQ), lavash (UNESCO flatbread), khash (winter beef-feet soup), Ararat brandy, Armenian wines (Areni — 6100 years of viticulture, oldest wine cellar in the world discovered here).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
