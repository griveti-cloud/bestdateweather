Context

Our verdict

Zakynthos (Zante), 3rd largest Greek Ionian island: 40,000 inhabitants over 160 sq miles south-west of Kefalonia. Nicknamed "the flower of the Levant" by the Venetians. iconic image of Greece: Navagio Beach ("shipwreck beach", isolated cove with rusted shipwreck stranded 1980, white limestone cliffs 660 ft, indescribable turquoise water). May is my favorite month (8.9/10): 61-75°F, 19% rain, sea at 70°F. June-July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (70-91°F) and the extreme crowds on Navagio (access restriction since 2018 for landslide risks). September stays excellent (8.8/10). From November to March, expect 50-66°F and 26-46% rain: mild but rainy, many hotels close. Highlights: Navagio Beach (the most photographed beach in Greece, accessible only by boat from Porto Vromi or Saint-Nicolas, swimming since 2022 strongly discouraged due to landslide risks, aerial panoramic platform above, one of the most viral beaches on Instagram), Blue Caves (sea caves at Cape Skinari, water of supernatural blue, accessible by boat), Marathonissi (Turtle Island) (turtle-shaped islet in Laganas Bay, protected zone for the nesting of loggerhead turtles Caretta caretta = protected species, Zakynthos marine reserve), Zakynthos Town (Chora rebuilt after the 1953 earthquake which destroyed 95% of the island, Solomos Square in tribute to the Greek national poet Dionysios Solomos born here in 1798), Cape Keri (wild cliffs south-west, sunset panorama), Porto Limnionas (magnificent cove, cliffs and turquoise water). Loggerhead turtles = nesting May-August on Laganas Bay, observation possible but NEVER walk on the beach at night (eggs destroyed by footsteps), swimming with a turtle forbidden. Try pasteli (sesame-honey), ladotyri (local cheese), retsina (resined wine). Zakynthos airport (ZTH).
