Context

Our verdict

Cairns, in tropical north Queensland, is the access base for the Great Barrier Reef (UNESCO, the largest coral reef in the world, 1,430 miles, visible from space). August-October are my favorite months (8.7/10): 64-84°F, 23-31% rain, dry tropical season. From November to April, it's the wet season: 40-75% rainfall, possible cyclones (historic category 5), Irukandji jellyfish (very dangerous, swimming forbidden or in stinger suits). From May to October, it's the ideal tourist season: clear sky, calm sea, maximum visibility for diving. Highlights: Great Barrier (day-trip boat 150-250$, diving or snorkelling at Hastings Reef, Michaelmas Cay, Agincourt Reef), Daintree Rainforest (UNESCO, the oldest tropical forest in the world, 180 million years), Cape Tribulation (where the forest meets the reef), Kuranda by Skyrail (4.7 miles cable car above the canopy), Atherton Tablelands (plateaus, waterfalls, crater lakes). Cairns Esplanade Lagoon (free artificial pool by the sea, secure swimming year-round). Cairns has no swimmable beaches in town (sea 30 min away). Try barramundi, mud crab, kangaroo. Direct flight from Singapore, stopover Sydney.
