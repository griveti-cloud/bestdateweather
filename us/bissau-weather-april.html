Context

Our verdict

Bissau, capital of Guinea-Bissau on the Atlantic coast of West Africa: 500,000 inhabitants. Former Portuguese colony (1446-1973 = the longest European colonisation in sub-Saharan Africa), independence by bloody liberation war under Amílcar Cabral (pan-African intellectual-revolutionary assassinated 1973). Country among the poorest in Africa, chronic political instability (8 successful coups since 1980). Tourism near non-existent. December-January are my favorite months (8.5-8.7/10): 70-91°F, 0-1% rain, this is the ideal dry season + refreshing Saharan harmattan. From November to May, it's the dry season: 70-93°F, pleasant dry and hot climate. From June to October, it's the West African monsoon: 32-92% rainfall. Bissau highlights: Old town Bissau Velho (decrepit but preserved Portuguese colonial atmosphere, paved alleys, colonial house facades collapsing = unique melancholic atmosphere), Amura Fortress (18th-century Portuguese, today houses the military garrison + Amílcar Cabral's tomb), Notre-Dame Cathedral of la Candeleria, Central Mosque of Bissau, Bandim Central Market (lively West African atmosphere), National Ethnographic Museum, Pidjiguiti Memorial (1959 massacre of striking dockers by Portuguese police = trigger of the liberation war), Praia de Bissau Beach (urban, not very clean). Essential Guinea-Bissau excursions = this is why one comes to Guinea-Bissau: Bijagos Archipelago 1h by boat from Bissau (UNESCO biosphere reserve 1996, archipelago of 88 islands of which 21 inhabited, traditional matrilineal animist peoples unique in Africa = the woman chooses her husband by Bijagos tradition, the islands of Bubaque, Orango, João Vieira, Caravela, Carache are accessible with preserved fauna endemic salt water hippopotamuses + green turtles nesting January-May + rare African manatees + saltwater crocodiles), Bijagos Islands National Park (the largest national park in Guinea-Bissau), Cacheu ("first European city in sub-Saharan Africa" founded 1588 by Portuguese as slave trading post, historic fort, slavery memorial). Safety: Bissau globally calm but urban crime, chronic political instability, very basic tourist infrastructure. Try jollof rice (greasy rice), Caldou (fish in sauce), Guinea-Bissauan feijoada (Portuguese heritage), galinha à cafrial (Portuguese spicy chicken), bagre (catfish), Portuguese coffee, vinho português, Pampa beer (national beer). Bissau Osvaldo Vieira airport (OXB), flights from Lisbon TAP Portugal direct, from Dakar neighbouring Senegal, very limited infrastructure.
