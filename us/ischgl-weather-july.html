Context

Our verdict

Ischgl, Austrian ski resort in the Paznaun Valley (Tyrol): 1600 inhabitants at 4,500 ft, ultra-festive resort nicknamed "the Ibiza of the Alps" (sadly famous as the European epicenter of Covid-19 in March 2020 = thousands of European skiers infected). Linked to Samnaun in Switzerland by lifts (Silvretta Arena = cross-border skiing without customs formalities, duty-free shopping in Switzerland). August is my favorite month (7.7/10): 50-72°F, 58% rain in altitude storms, Paznaun hiking, mountain bike. From June to September, expect 39-72°F: calm summer season. From December to April, expect 18/43°F and 42-64% precipitation often as snow: ski high season, one of the snowiest in Austria. Pistes: 150 miles Silvretta Arena (Ischgl + Samnaun, 1 single ski pass), altitude 1377-9,400 ft, vertical drop 4,900 ft, 45 modern lifts (Ischgl is known for its heated and hooded chairlifts, one of the most modern lift parks in Europe). Idalp = central plateau at 7,500 ft, direct access by cable car from the village (15 min). Top of the Mountain Concert = season opening/closing late November/early May with a free open-air major concert by international artists (Mariah Carey, Kylie Minogue, Bon Jovi, Robbie Williams, Elton John, James Blunt have played). Après-ski: Pacha, Trofana Alm, Niki's Stadl, Schatzi Bar = Austrian party institutions (atmosphere until 22-11 PM, prices x3 vs elsewhere). Audience: Germans and British in the majority, young 25-45, more party than sport. Hotels: Trofana Royal (5*+), Hotel Madlein (5*), Yscla, $220-$1,650/night. Try Kasspatzln, Tiroler Gröstl, Glühwein, Stroh 80. Logistics: Ischgl 1h30 from Innsbruck by car/bus.
