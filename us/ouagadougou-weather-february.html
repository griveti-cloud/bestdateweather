Context

Our verdict

Ouagadougou ("Ouaga"), capital of Burkina Faso in Sahelian Africa: 2.5 million inhabitants in urban area. Former French colonial Upper Volta, renamed Burkina Faso ("land of upright men" in Mooré + Dioula) in 1984 by revolutionary President Thomas Sankara = "African Che Guevara" assassinated in October 1987 by his friend Blaise Compaoré. Cultural capital of West Africa for African cinema (FESPACO = Pan-African Film Festival of Ouagadougou = the largest film festival in Africa since 1969, biennial). October-November-January are my favorite months (6.5-8.2/10): 63-99°F, 0-18% rain, this is the ideal dry season + refreshing Saharan harmattan. From November to February, it's the cool dry season: 63-97°F, dry and tempered climate in the morning (sometimes 54°F in January), permanent harmattan. From March to May, it's the hot dry season: 72-104°F, extreme Sahelian heatwave (sometimes 113°F). From June to September, it's the monsoon: 31-78% rainfall, milder temperatures 73-95°F. Ouagadougou highlights: Place de la Nation and Place de la Révolution (Sankara memorial central squares), Ouagadougou Cathedral, Thomas Sankara Memorial (under construction since 2023, former military base where Sankara was assassinated on 15 October 1987 = pan-African pilgrimage place), Central Market (Rood Wokó, West African atmosphere), Grand Mosque of Ouagadougou, National Museum of Burkina Faso (ethnographic collection masks Mossi-Bobo-Lobi-Senufo iconic), Loaga craftsmen village 15 min away (traditional Mossi bronze workers + wood sculptors). Essential Burkina Faso excursions (currently inadvisable for security): Bobo-Dioulasso 4h away (2nd city, more traditional atmosphere, Grand Mosque in mud brick), Banfora and Karfiguela Falls (west, sugar cane plantations, surprising verdant landscapes in Burkina), Sindou Peaks (spectacular rock formations), Nazinga Reserve (south, savannah elephants), Royal Court of the Mossi at Ouaga (the Mogho Naaba = emperor of the Mossi receives every Friday morning the ceremonial of the "false departure" = millennial Mossi tradition, open to visitors). Safety: inadvisable France Diplomacy for the entirety of the country except increased vigilance Ouaga since 2015 (periodic jihadist attacks against cafés-restaurants-hotels Splendid + Cappuccino 2016 + 18 deaths including 6 French, Aziz Istanbul 2017), 40% of the territory escapes government control, 2 military coups 2022, Wagner presence since 2024, very inadvisable travel. Try tô (West African iconic millet ball), greasy rice, dolo (traditional millet beer), bissap, bissap-ginger juice, beef skewers with chilli. Ouagadougou airport (OUA).
