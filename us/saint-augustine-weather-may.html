Context

Our verdict

Saint Augustine, in Florida: 14,000 inhabitants, founded by the Spanish in 1565 ("San Agustín"), the oldest continuously occupied European-founded city in the USA (42 years before Jamestown 1607 and 55 years before Plymouth 1620). Preserved historic center, unique colonial Spanish atmosphere in North America. May is my favorite month (8.4/10): 70-81°F, 28% rain, end of spring with ideal temperatures. From April to June and October-November are the best seasons: 64-84°F, mild climate. From June to September, expect 75-86°F: heavy and humid heat, real cyclone risk (east coast of Florida exposed), reduced hotel prices. From December to February, expect 52-72°F: mild for Florida, numerous American snowbirds (Canadians and inhabitants of the US north-east flee winter). Highlights: Castillo de San Marcos (the oldest stone fortress in the USA, built 1672-1695 by the Spanish in coquina = soft stone composed of shells, which absorbs cannonballs rather than cracking, intact after 33 sieges, national park), Spanish old town (Saint George Street pedestrian street, reconstituted colonial casas, Plaza de la Constitución the oldest urban square in the USA), Lightner Museum (formerly Hôtel Alcazar 1888, art deco), Flagler College (formerly Ponce de Leon Hotel 1888, Henry Flagler railroad magnate, luxurious Hispano-Moorish architecture, Tiffany stained glass, guided tour), Lighthouse Museum (1874 lighthouse, 219 steps), St. Augustine Distillery, Fountain of Youth Archaeological Park (legend of the fountain of youth sought by Juan Ponce de León 1513, first European landing on the continent). Beaches: St. Augustine Beach and Anastasia State Park (soft sand, dunes). Excursions: Daytona Beach 1h away, Orlando 2h away (Disney parks). Try minorcan clam chowder (local spicy chowder), datil pepper, fried shrimp. 1h30 from Jacksonville, 2h from Orlando.
