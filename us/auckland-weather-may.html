What to expect in May

Spring marks the start of the good season in Auckland. Temperatures rise (18.0°F) and tourists have not yet arrived en masse. Expect rain on 42.0% of days — a light raincoat in the bag is recommended. Not the best window, but acceptable if dates are constrained. January (8.3/10) is significantly better if possible.

Context

Our verdict

Auckland is New Zealand's largest city: 1.7 million inhabitants (1/3 of the country), "City of Sails" (1 sailboat for 4 inhabitants), built on 50 dormant volcanoes. January-February are my favorite months (8.2-8.3/10): 64-73°F, 39-40% rain, austral summer, swimming at the East Coast beaches (Mission Bay, Takapuna). From June to August (austral winter), expect 50-61°F with 42-48% precipitation: mild for the southern hemisphere but damp. Auckland has a "4 seasons in one day" climate typical of New Zealand. Highlights: Sky Tower (1,100 ft, the southern hemisphere's tallest structure, SkyJump for the brave), Auckland Domain and War Memorial Museum, Viaduct Harbour (port redone for the America's Cup), Devonport (12 min by ferry, village charm). Excursions: Waiheke Island 40 min by ferry (wines, beaches, restaurants), Rangitoto Island (young 600-year-old volcano, hike to the summit), Hobbiton 2h30 away (Lord of the Rings set at Matamata), Coromandel Peninsula 2h away (Cathedral Cove, Hot Water Beach to dig your own hot pool at low tide). Try hangi (Maori cuisine cooked in the earth), pavlova, hokey pokey ice cream, NZ wines (Marlborough Sauvignon Blanc). Auckland is generally the starting or ending point for New Zealand road trips.
