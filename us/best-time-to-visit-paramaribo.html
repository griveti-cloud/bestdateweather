Paramaribo, capital of Suriname (former Dutch Suriname, independent since 1975): 240,000 inhabitants on the Suriname river. UNESCO 1980 for its historic center in wood with Dutch colonial 17th-19th century houses. Multi-ethnic country unique in the world: 27% Indo-Surinamese, 22% Maroons (descendants of escaped slaves), 18% Creoles, 14% Javanese, 5% Chinese, etc. Official language Dutch (only country on the continent), but Sranantongo Afro-Creole vehicular language. March is my favorite month (8.0/10): 75-82°F, 57% rain, end of the small dry season. From September to November, it's the great dry season: 77-90°F, 43-70% rain, ideal for visiting. From December to February and May-July, these are the rainy seasons: 67-97% rainfall, but stable temperatures (75-86°F). Paramaribo highlights: Historic center (UNESCO, largest set of wooden colonial architecture in the Caribbean, to discover on foot along Waterkant Avenue), Fort Zeelandia (17th-century Dutch, history museum), Saints Peter and Paul Cathedral (the largest wooden cathedral in Latin America, 1885), Keizerstraat Mosque and Neveh Shalom Synagogue (cohabit side by side, multi-religious symbol of the country unique in the world), Centraal Market (Market Place, 6 distinct ethnic markets: Indian, Creole, Javanese, Chinese, Maroon). Excursions: Brownsberg Nature Park (Amazonian forest 81 miles away, hiking, waterfalls), Galibi (sea turtle reserve at the mouth of the Marowijne), Amazon Forest (90% of Surinamese territory is virgin forest, one of the highest forest cover rates in the world, expeditions to the Saramaccan Maroons). Try pom (Creole cassolette), bami goreng (Javanese heritage), roti curry (Indian), pomtaja, Parbo Beer.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
