Saint George's, capital of Grenada (not to be confused with Granada in Spain): 4000 inhabitants on the south-west coast of the island of Grenada in the Lesser Antilles (southern Caribbean chain). Independent state since 1974 (Commonwealth realm), nicknamed "the spice island" (Grenada produces 20% of world nutmeg, 2nd producer after Indonesia, national flag adorned with a nutmeg). Small island 130 sq miles, 110,000 inhabitants. April is my favorite month (8.5/10): 77-84°F, 37% rain, sea at 82°F, end of the dry season. From January to May, it's the tourist high season: 75-84°F, 37-44% rain, the most pleasant and dry period. From June to November, it's the rainy season: 65-74% rainfall, but stable temperatures (79-86°F), real cyclonic risk (but Grenada is further south than the Antilles "cyclonic corridor" so less hit than its more northern neighbours). Highlights: Carenage (horseshoe-shaped port with red-yellow-blue houses on slope, the postcard-image of the city), Fort George (17th century on promontory, panorama, place of the assassination of Prime Minister Maurice Bishop in 1983, which triggered the American invasion the same year), Central Market (Spice Market, colorful spice stalls: nutmeg, cinnamon, ginger, turmeric, cocoa beans), Saint George Cathedral, National Museum (former French-British fort). Grenada island highlights: Grand Anse Beach (2 miles of white sand, one of the most beautiful beaches in the Caribbean according to many rankings), Annandale Falls (waterfall accessible by car), Concord Falls, Underwater Sculpture Park at Molinière Bay (1st underwater sculpture park in the world 2006, snorkelling/diving), Belmont Estate (traditional cocoa plantation), Grand Etang National Park (tropical forest, crater lake). Try oil down (national dish: meat + dumplings + breadfruit + coconut milk), callaloo soup, nutmeg ice cream, Westerhall rum, Chocolate House (local chocolatier).
