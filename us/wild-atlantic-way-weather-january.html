Context

Our verdict

Wild Atlantic Way, coastal itinerary of Ireland on the Atlantic west coast: 1,555 miles of coastal roads from Inishowen Peninsula (Northern Ireland border) to Kinsale (Cork) passing through 9 counties (Donegal-Sligo-Mayo-Galway-Clare-Limerick-Kerry-Cork) = one of the longest coastal tourist itineraries in the world, created in 2014 by Tourism Ireland as a route of discovery of wild Ireland. May is my favorite month (7.1/10): 45-59°F, 40% rain, ideal temperatures with Irish spring blooming. June-August stay excellent (5.9-6.6/10) despite rains. From May to September, it's the tourist high season: 41-66°F, long Atlantic days (18-7 PM of day at the June solstice), ideal visiting conditions. From November to March, expect 34-48°F and 35-46% rain: mild for the North Atlantic thanks to the Gulf Stream, but windy and rainy, short days (8h day at the December solstice), mystical-melancholic atmosphere. Highlights on the Wild Atlantic Way (north to south): Inishowen Peninsula Donegal (extreme north, Cape Malin the northernmost point of Ireland, spectacular cliffs), Slieve League Donegal ("Cliffs of Slieve League", 2,000 ft = among the highest in Europe after Croaghaun Achill 2,300 ft, higher than Cliffs of Moher but less frequented, exceptional panorama), Glenveagh National Park, Ben Bulben Sligo (Sligo iconic flat-top mountain, place of burial of poet W.B. Yeats at Drumcliff 1948 + his lines "Cast a cold eye on life, on death, horseman pass by"), Mullaghmore Head Beach (giant winter waves 98 ft+ extreme surf reserved for pros), Achill Island Mayo (the largest Irish island, 5 blue flag beaches, Keem Bay iconic view), Croagh Patrick Mayo (sacred mountain of Saint Patrick 2,500 ft, annual pilgrimage "Reek Sunday" last Sunday of July), Connemara National Park Galway (wild "Connemara mountains" landscapes iconic view of romantic Ireland, many songs by Michel Sardou "Les Lacs du Connemara" 1981), Kylemore Abbey (19th-century neo-Gothic abbey at the foot of Connemara, iconic view), Aran Islands (Inis Mór + Inis Meáin + Inis Oírr, preserved traditional Gaelic atmosphere, Dún Aonghasa iconic prehistoric fortification at the edge of the cliff), Cliffs of Moher Clare (UNESCO Geopark 2011, the most visited image of Ireland 1.5 million visitors/year, 700 ft cliffs overlooking the Atlantic over 5 miles iconic view, filming location "Princess Bride" 1987 + "Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince" 2009), Burren (UNESCO Geopark, unique karst landscape in Europe), Dingle Peninsula Kerry ("most beautiful peninsula in the world" according to National Geographic 2011, preserved Gaelic atmosphere, Slea Head the westernmost point of Europe, Blasket Islands), Ring of Kerry ("Ring of Kerry", 110 miles circular road around the Iveragh Peninsula, iconic Ireland tourist), Killarney National Park (lakes of Killarney + 19th-century Muckross House), Mizen Head Cork (the southernmost point of Ireland), Kinsale Cork (picturesque old town + culinary capital of Ireland). Itinerary: 2-3 weeks by car to fully cover, but individual sections 3-5 days possible (Connemara + Cliffs of Moher in 5 days). Try Irish Stew (iconic lamb-potato stew), Boxty potato pancakes, soda bread, fish and chips Atlantic fish, Fish Pie, Galway Bay oysters, fresh draught Guinness, Irish whiskey Jameson + Bushmills + Tullamore + craft. Logistics: Dublin (DUB) airports 4h by car from the departure points + Shannon (SNN) Clare in the heart of the Wild Atlantic Way + Kerry (KIR) extreme south.
