Islamabad, capital of Pakistan at the foot of the Himalayas at 1,800 ft altitude: 1.1 million inhabitants intra-muros, 2.2 million with its twin city Rawalpindi. Planned capital built ex-nihilo in the 1960s (before: capital Karachi on the coast) by the Greek urbanist Constantinos Doxiadis, grid plan with quarters numbered F-G-H-I, one of the greenest and calmest capitals in South Asia. October is my favorite month (9.0/10): 61-82°F, 14% rain, ideal temperatures after monsoon. April stays excellent (8.6/10). From September to November and March-April, these are the best seasons: 46-86°F. From May to June, expect 68-95°F: pre-monsoon heatwave. From July to August, it's the monsoon: 60-61% rainfall, temperatures 75-90°F, greener landscapes. From December to February, expect 41-72°F: relative cold in Pakistan, snow possible in neighbouring mountains. Highlights: Faisal Mosque (Faisal Masjid, one of the largest mosques in the world, capacity 100,000 worshippers inside + 200,000 on the esplanade, financed by King Faisal of Saudi Arabia, modern architecture 1986 by Turkish architect Vedat Dalokay inspired by a Bedouin tent), Daman-e-Koh and Pir Sohawa (panoramic hills with view over the city, mountain restaurants), Rawal Lake (artificial lake for paddleboat rides), Pakistan Monument (Shakarparian Hills, national monument in the form of flower petals, 2007), Lok Virsa Museum (Pakistani ethnological museum), Saidpur Village (preserved pre-Mughal village, traditional restaurants). Essential excursions: Taxila 1h away (UNESCO 1980, one of the oldest universities in the world 600 BC, focus of Gandhara Buddhism 2nd century BC-5th century AD), Murree 1h30 away (mountain station 7,500 ft, temperate summer climate), Galyat (other mountain stations), Lahore 4h by bus or 1h by plane (already covered by BDW), Hunza Valley 6 PM by bus (legendary valley of the Karakoram mountains, K2 7th + Rakaposhi panoramas, centenarian Burusho community), Skardu and Baltoro Glacier (base camp of K2 28,300 ft 2nd highest summit in the world, 12-15 day trek expeditions for confirmed mountaineers). Safety: Islamabad globally safe, but Pakistan in general subject to Islamist terrorism, inadvisable France Diplomacy for most regions, organized travel recommended. Try Pakistani biryani (signature with Lahore), nihari (beef stew cooked all night), karahi (wok curry), naan, samosas, lassi yoghurt, chai (spiced tea), kebab. Islamabad International airport (ISB) opened 2018.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
