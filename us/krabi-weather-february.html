Context

Our verdict

Krabi is the south-west Thai beach province, a more natural alternative to Phuket: Ao Nang (main resort), Railay (limestone peninsula accessible only by boat), Koh Lanta. From November to February (6.9/10): 72-88°F, 31-46% rain, sea at 82°F, the dry high season. Krabi never tops 7/10 (constant hot-humid tropical climate). From May to October, it's the south-west monsoon: 75-92% rainfall, rough sea, many boats halted. From March to April, expect 73-93°F with 42-75% rain: stifling heat. Highlights: Railay Beach (peninsula accessible only by boat from Ao Nang 15 min, 4 beaches: West, East, Phra Nang, Tonsai — climbing mecca on limestone cliffs, more than 700 routes), Phra Nang Cave Beach (one of the world's most beautiful beaches according to many rankings), Ao Nang (main resort, hotels and restaurants), 4 Islands tour (Phra Nang Cave, Koh Tup, Chicken Island, Koh Poda by longtail), Hong Islands tour (spectacular interior lagoon), Emerald Pool and Hot Springs at Khao Phra Bang Khram. Koh Phi Phi 2h by ferry from Krabi. Koh Lanta to the south (quieter island, long beach, diving at Hin Daeng/Hin Muang). Tiger Cave Temple (Wat Tham Sua, 1237 steps for the view). Try tom yum, massaman curry, local seafood. Direct flight Bangkok-Krabi in 1h15.
