Context

Our verdict

Luzon, largest island of the Philippines and the 17th largest island in the world: 50 million inhabitants on 42,500 sq miles = 50% of the total Filipino population. Main island sheltering the capital Manila + Quezon City + spectacular landscapes: Banaue UNESCO terraced rice paddies + Palawan beaches (technically Palawan = other island administratively) + active volcanoes (Pinatubo 1991 explosion 2nd most powerful of the 20th century). March is my favorite month (8.1/10): 72-84°F, 9% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January-February-April-December stay excellent (7.7-7.9/10). From November to May, it's the dry season "tag-init": 66-90°F, this is the tourist high season. From June to October, it's the active cyclone season "tag-ulan": 50-80% rainfall, real typhoon risk (Philippines = country in the world most affected by typhoons, 20+ per year of which 5 major on average, Yolanda/Haiyan 2013 = 6300 deaths in neighbouring Visayas). Luzon highlights: Manila capital (already covered by BDW, 14 million inhabitants in urban area, chaotic-cosmopolitan-polluted megacity), Banaue Rice Terraces (UNESCO 1995, Ifugao terraced rice paddies built 2000 years ago at 4,900 ft altitude by indigenous Cordilleras peoples, nicknamed "8th wonder of the world" by Filipinos, spectacular landscapes covering 3,860 sq miles mountainous, Batad village = the most picturesque accessible 30 min hike from Banaue town, Bangaan, Hapao, in danger according to UNESCO as the rice paddies collapse due to lack of maintenance from peasants leaving for the city), Sagada (Cordillera mountains, Igorot indigenous people, hanging coffins = ancestral funeral tradition unique in the world of coffins hung from cliffs, cultural iconic), Vigan (UNESCO 1999, preserved 17th-19th century Spanish colonial town, one of the only completely preserved Hispanic colonial towns in Asia, unique Hispano-Filipino atmosphere), Bohol Chocolate Hills (technically Bohol = Visayas, but often included Philippines tour from Manila), Mount Pinatubo (active volcano June 1991 eruption = 2nd most powerful of the 20th century after Mt Katmai 1912, turquoise blue crater lake visible today, 4h hike one way from Capas + 4x4), Mt Mayon (perfectly conical volcano 8,100 ft "most beautiful volcanic cone in the world" according to Filipinos, in Albay province south-Luzon), Donsol (whale shark snorkelling with whale sharks November-May, ethical alternative to Oslob), Batanes Islands (extreme north archipelago, improbable Scottish-Irish atmosphere in tropical, lush green hill landscapes, unique Ivatan peoples), Pagsanjan Falls (waterfalls by traditional dugout boat "shooting the rapids" iconic). Safety: Mindanao (other island in the south) inadvisable for Abu Sayyaf Islamist terrorism, but Luzon globally safe, urban crime vigilance Manila. Try adobo (chicken/pork in vinegar-soy sauce, national dish), lechon (roast pig), sinigang (sour tamarind soup), halo-halo (multi-layer icy dessert), San Miguel beer (institution), Tanduay Filipino rum. Manila NAIA (MNL) + Clark Pampanga (CRK) airports.
