Coron, town and island in the north of the Palawan archipelago in the Philippines: 65,000 inhabitants, capital of the northern Palawan district. Coron town is on Busuanga Island, but the touristy "Coron" = Coron Island 30 min by bangka, geological paradise with black limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons and sacred lakes of the Tagbanwa people (one of the oldest peoples of the Philippines, descendants of the first Austronesians). From January to March are my favorite months (6.9/10): 77-90°F, 20-24% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Coron never tops 6.9/10 (hot tropical climate). From November to May, it's the tourist high season: 77-91°F, sea at 82°F, hotel prices x2-x3, more sheltered from the south-west monsoon than El Nido thanks to the north-east position. From June to October, it's the wetter season: 67-93% rainfall. Highlights: Twin Lagoon (2 lagoons connected by a passage to swim under a rock fault, saline water + freshwater mixed creating thermoclinal mirage effect, signature Coron), Kayangan Lake ("purest lake in Asia" according to UNESCO, crystalline water among limestone cliffs, access by 300 steep steps from the dock 15 min, the panorama of the path towards the lake is one of the most famous images of the Philippines), Barracuda Lake (marine lake with halocline = brutal thermocline between cold salt water bottom and warm fresh water top, unique diving in the world for total visibility), Siete Pecados ("7 Sins", 7 islets with snorkelling around corals), Bulog Dos (thin sandbank between 2 seas, postcard image), Beach 91, Banol Beach, Malcapuya Island (preserved white sand). WWII wreck diving: Coron is one of the most famous wreck diving sites in the world. 12 Japanese Second World War wrecks sunk by US aviation on 24 September 1944 during a surprise attack on the Japanese fleet sheltering in the bay of Coron: Akitsushima (light cruiser 370 ft), Irako (refrigerator-cargo 480 ft), Olympia Maru, Okikawa Maru (620 ft, the largest), depths 12-140 ft, advanced technical diving. Maquinit Hot Springs (seaside hot springs, 100-104°F). Mount Tapyas (700 steps, sunset panorama over Coron bay). Excursions: El Nido 8h by ferry (the other Palawan paradise). Try kinilaw, pancit, lechon, balut, sinigang, Filipino mangoes. Logistics: Busuanga/Francisco B. Reyes airport (USU) 30 min by van from Coron Town, direct flights from Manila (1h15).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
