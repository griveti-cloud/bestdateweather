Trieste, port city in the north-east of Italy on the Slovenian border: 200,000 inhabitants at the bottom of the Adriatic, capital of Friuli-Venezia Giulia. Unique atmosphere mix of Italo-Slavic-Austrian (the city only became Italian in 1918 after remaining Austrian for 500 years 1382-1918, main port of the Austro-Hungarian empire for the Mediterranean). Cosmopolitan cultural center (James Joyce, Italo Svevo, Umberto Saba, Rainer Maria Rilke lived here). June-July-August are my favorite months (9.0/10): 64-84°F, 25-30% rain, ideal temperatures. From April to October, it's the tourist high season: 48-84°F. From November to March, expect 36-57°F and 32-45% rain: mild for northern Italy, but Trieste is famous for the bora = violent cold north-east wind that can reach 81 miles/h in winter, climate signature of the city (taut ropes in the streets to hold on in case of bora peak). Highlights: Piazza Unità d'Italia ("Square of Italian Unity", the largest square open to the sea in Europe, 135,000 sq ft surrounded by 19th-century Habsburg palaces, unique atmosphere with maritime opening, splendid night illuminations), Castello di Miramare (5 miles north-west on promontory, white neo-Gothic castle 1860 built for Archduke Maximilian of Austria — brother of Franz Joseph — before he became Emperor of Mexico 1864 and was shot 1867, gardens, signature sea view), Saint Just Cathedral (San Giusto, 14th-century Romanesque on hill, former Roman forum discovered underneath), Citadel (San Giusto Castle), Roman Theater (1st century), Canal Grande (navigable canal in the heart of the city bordered by 18th-century palaces), Trieste Synagogue (one of the largest in Central Europe 1912, important Jewish community before Holocaust), Risiera di San Sabba (former Nazi concentration camp 1943-1945, the only German extermination camp on Italian soil, memorial), Caffè San Marco (literary café 1914, James Joyce wrote here, traditional Viennese atmosphere), Grotta Gigante 15 min away (one of the largest tourist caves accessible in the world 330 ft × 920 ft × 430 ft), Castello di Duino (Rilke wrote the Duino Elegies here 1912). Excursions: Slovenia 30 min away (Lipica — Lipizzan stud farm, spectacular Postojna cave), Aquileia 30 min away (UNESCO, paleochristian Roman mosaics), Venice 1h30 by train. Try jota (Slovenian bean sauerkraut soup), Triestine goulash (Hungarian heritage), caffè (Trieste = Italian coffee capital with world headquarters Illy + Hausbrandt, coffee is ordered here in special terms: capo in B = cappuccino in glass, nero = espresso), prosciutto crudo, Terrano wine (intense local red wine).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
