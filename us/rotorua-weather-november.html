Context

Our verdict

Rotorua is the New Zealand geothermal capital: 75,000 inhabitants, geysers, hot springs, mud pools, permanent sulphur smell. Also the capital of Maori culture in North Island. January-February are my favorite months (8.1-8.3/10): 57-73°F, 39-41% rain, austral summer. From June to August (austral winter), expect 41-55°F and 39-43% rain: cold and wet, but the hot springs come into their own (steam in the cold). Highlights: Te Puia (Pohutu Geyser up to 98 ft, Maori marae, kiwi house), Wai-O-Tapu Thermal Wonderland (Champagne Pool, Lady Knox Geyser at 10:15am every day), Hell's Gate (mud pools), Polynesian Spa (thermal springs lakeside), Whakarewarewa (living Maori village), Hangi and Maori concert (traditional cuisine cooked in the earth + dance). Outdoor activities: Redwoods Forest (canopy walk Redwoods Treewalk, world-renowned mountain biking), Mountain biking (Whakarewarewa Forest, one of the best mountain-bike spots in the world). Wai-O-Tapu 30 min away, Lake Taupo 1h away (the largest lake in NZ, volcanic crater), Tongariro Alpine Crossing 1h30 away (one of the best hikes in the world, 12 miles). Try hangi, kiwifruit (originating here at Te Puke 1h away). Near-permanent sulphur smell: you get used to it.
