Context

Our verdict

Granada, colonial town of Nicaragua on the shores of Lake Nicaragua = the "colonial pearl" of the country: 130,000 inhabitants. One of the oldest Spanish colonial cities in America founded in 1524 by Francisco Hernández de Córdoba ("Nicaraguan Granada" historic rival of León = liberal-revolutionary vs Granada = conservative-aristocratic, bipolar rivalry that structured all of Nicaragua's political history 19th-20th century). Plundered several times by Caribbean pirates English-French-Dutch 17th century (the pirates went up the Río San Juan from the Caribbean Sea), burned 1856 by the American filibuster William Walker. Today preserved and smart town, unique Hispanic-colonial atmosphere. January-February-March are my favorite months (6.9/10): 72-90°F, 8-10% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Granada never tops 6.9/10 (hot humid tropical climate year-round). From November to April, it's the dry season: 72-93°F, this is the tourist high season. From May to October, it's the rainy season: 57-88% rainfall. Highlights: Granada Cathedral (yellow ochre 1583, one of the oldest cathedrals in Central America, multiple reconstructions after destructions 17th-19th century), Parque Central / Plaza de la Independencia (main square, preserved Hispanic atmosphere), Iglesia La Merced (17th century, ascent of the bell tower panoramic 360° on the ochre roofs iconic view of Granada iconic view = viral Instagram photo), Calle La Calzada (main pedestrian street with restaurants-bars-boutique hotels, lively atmosphere in the evening), Iglesia de Xalteva, Iglesia San Francisco (1529, one of the oldest churches in continental America), Convento San Francisco (museum with pre-Columbian stone stelae from Lake Nicaragua = vestiges of the Chorotega-Nahuatl peoples), Casa de los Tres Mundos (17th-century palace transformed into cultural center by poet Ernesto Cardenal), Cementerio de Granada (monumental 19th-century cemetery with tombs of Granadan elites), Lake Nicaragua / Cocibolca (the largest lake in Central America 3,190 sq miles, one of the only freshwater lakes in the world hosting freshwater bull sharks = endemic species evolutionarily adapted to fresh water = unique biological phenomenon = species that goes up the San Juan river towards the lake via Caribbean), Las Isletas (archipelago of 365 volcanic islets near Granada formed during the ancient eruption of Volcán Mombacho, accessible by boat tour from Granada, some islets are private residences of Nicaraguan fortunes), Volcán Mombacho (4,400 ft, national park with altitude cloud forest, canopy + clouds iconic hike). Essential Nicaragua excursions: Ometepe Island 3h by ferry from San Jorge (the largest volcanic island in the world in a freshwater lake, 2 "hourglass" volcanoes Concepción 5,300 ft + Maderas 4,600 ft, demanding Concepción hike but exceptional panorama), Volcán Masaya 30 min away (one of the only active volcanoes in the world where one can look into the incandescent lava lake crater at dusk = unique iconic experience = access by car to the edge of the Santiago crater), León 2h30 away (the other rival old colonial capital, already covered by BDW), San Juan del Sur 2h away (south Pacific beach resort, popular surf, nightlife), Indio-Maíz Reserve (preserved jungle Costa Rican border accessible via Río San Juan river), Solentiname archipelago (school of naive primitive Nicaraguan painting founded by poet-priest Ernesto Cardenal). Try gallo pinto (rice red beans iconic shared with Costa Rica), nacatamales (giant Nicaraguan tamales iconic), vigorón (cabbage + yuca + pork), tres leches (iconic dessert "3 milks" cake), local Toña/Victoria beer, Flor de Caña Nicaraguan rum (one of the best rums in the world, several international awards). Logistics: Granada 45 min from Managua airport (MGA) by taxi.
