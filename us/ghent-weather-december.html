Context

Our verdict

Ghent (Gent in Dutch), 3rd city of Belgium and capital of East Flanders: 265,000 inhabitants. Often eclipsed by more touristic Bruges, Ghent nevertheless offers an equally exceptional medieval atmosphere with on top a real student urban life (75,000 students Ghent University). In the 14th century, Ghent was the 2nd largest city in Northern Europe after Paris (65,000 inhabitants), major center of the cloth industry. June is my favorite month (8.3/10): 55-72°F, 36% rain, ideal temperatures. July-August stay excellent (8.3/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 55-73°F. From November to March, expect 36-52°F and 41-45% rain: humid cold, snow rare, unique atmosphere under winter mist. Highlights: Saint Bavo Cathedral (14th-16th century Gothic, houses the famous Ghent Altarpiece by Hubert and Jan van Eyck 1432, considered one of the absolute masterpieces of early Flemish painting and one of the most stolen and recovered paintings in history — 13 partial thefts and recoveries since 1432 including by the Nazis 1942, the "Just Judges" panel stolen in 1934 never recovered, recent restoration 2012-2020 revealed the original human Bible hidden under the overpaintings, paid access $14), Belfry of Ghent (UNESCO 1999 with the other belfries of Belgium and France, 300 ft 14th-15th century, ascent by lift), Castle of the Counts of Flanders (Gravensteen, 12th-century fortified castle by Philip of Alsace, one of the last intact fortified castles in Europe, keep, panorama from the ramparts), Saint Nicholas Church (13th-century Scaldian Gothic), Korenlei and Graslei (the 2 medieval quays facing each other on the Lys = the iconic view of Ghent, former wheat and grain port warehouses, popular terraces, illuminated in the evening), STAM Stadsmuseum (urban history museum), SMAK Stedelijk Museum voor Actuele Kunst (contemporary art museum), Patershol (picturesque labyrinth medieval quarter), Werregarenstraat ("graffiti street", the only street in Ghent where graffiti is legal, paintings change constantly), Kouter (flower market square since the 18th century, Sunday morning Ghent institution). Excursions: Bruges 30 min by train (UNESCO, already covered by BDW), Antwerp 1h away (Rubens, port, fashion), Brussels 30 min by train. Try waterzooi (Flemish stew with chicken or fish cream), cuberdon (Ghent specialty conical violet candies with strawberries since 1873, to buy at "Cuberdon Noses" Place du Marché Vendredi), Ghent pralines, jenever (juniper eau-de-vie, the ancestor of gin), Belgian Trappist beers (Westvleteren, Chimay, Orval). Major festival: Gentse Feesten ("Ghent Festivities", 10 days late July, 1.5 million visitors, one of the largest free urban festivals in the world, theater, concerts, food).
