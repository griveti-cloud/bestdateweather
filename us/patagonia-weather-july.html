Context

Our verdict

Argentine Patagonia is the southern tip of the South American continent: endless steppes, glaciers (Perito Moreno), mountains (Fitz Roy, Cerro Torre), the end of the world (Ushuaia). January is my favorite month (7.9/10): 46-66°F, 31% rain, austral summer, 17h days, glaciers accessible, treks doable (W and O at Torres del Paine on the Chilean side). December-February are the tourist high season: prices x2-x3, refuges fully booked 6 months ahead for Torres del Paine. From June to August (austral winter), expect 30/39°F with 18-21% rain: intense cold, but this is ski season at Cerro Castor (Ushuaia) or Cerro Catedral (Bariloche). Many hotels and restaurants close out of season. October-November and March-April are the real sweet spots: still acceptable weather, reduced crowds, lower prices. Highlights: El Calafate (base for Perito Moreno, glacier shuttle, Spegazzini sailing), El Chaltén (capital of Argentine trekking, Laguna de los Tres at the foot of Fitz Roy 7 miles, Laguna Torre 6 miles), Ushuaia ("end of the world", Tierra del Fuego national park, Beagle Channel), Puerto Madryn (Valdés Peninsula, whales June-December, penguins, orcas). Chilean side: Torres del Paine (W trek 4-5 days, O trek 7-9 days). Try Argentine asado, cordero patagonico, Malbec wine.
