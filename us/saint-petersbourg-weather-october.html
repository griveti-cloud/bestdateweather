Context

Our verdict

Saint Petersburg, former imperial Russian capital (1712-1918): 5 million inhabitants, founded by Peter the Great in 1703 on the marshes of the Neva, nicknamed the "Venice of the North" for its 800 bridges and 42 islands. UNESCO 1990. June is my favorite month (8.0/10): 54-68°F, 36% rain, this is the season of the White Nights (mid-June to mid-July, sun almost never set, 7 PM of day, night festivals, drawbridges open 1am-5am to let boats pass), tourist peak. From July to August, expect 57-72°F: high season, days still very long. From December to March, expect 19/36°F with 31-46% precipitation often as snow: Saint Petersburg under snow is magnificent (imperial palaces, frozen Neva, but days at 5h of light end of December, seasonal depression common among the inhabitants). From October-November and April, expect 25/48°F: humid and grey transitions. Highlights: Hermitage Museum (one of the largest museums in the world, 3 million objects, Winter Palace of the tsars itself a masterpiece), Cathedral of the Saviour on the Spilled Blood (traditional Russian style, mosaics), Saint Isaac's Cathedral (4th largest cathedral in the world, observatory at the top), Palace Square, Nevsky Prospect (2.8 miles of main avenue), Peter and Paul Fortress (where the Romanovs are buried), Mariinsky (opera and ballet, world prestige). Excursions: Peterhof ("Russian Versailles", spectacular fountains), Tsarskoye Selo / Pushkin (Catherine Palace with its reconstituted Amber Room). Important: Russian visa difficult for Europeans since 2022, mandatory stopovers.
