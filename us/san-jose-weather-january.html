Context

Our verdict

San José, capital of Costa Rica on the central plateau at 3,800 ft altitude: 340,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 2.5 million in urban area (50% of the total Costa Rican population in the "Gran Área Metropolitana"). Modern and dynamic capital but often seen as the transit stop to Costa Rican natural destinations = "one doesn't come to Costa Rica for San José". Yet offers rich cultural life, gastronomy, peaceful Latin atmosphere. February is my favorite month (9.0/10): 61-79°F, 6% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January-March stay excellent (8.6-8.7/10). From December to April, it's the dry season "verano": 55-79°F. From May to November, it's the green season: 73-89% rainfall, mild temperatures 59-77°F thanks to altitude. Permanent climate: San José never tops 81°F in maximum average and rarely descends below 55°F in minimum average, "Eternal Spring" tropical climate ideal thanks to altitude 3,800 ft, one of the most pleasant climate capitals in the world. Highlights: Teatro Nacional (1897 neo-classical model Paris Opera, renowned café under the interior arcades, one of the most beautiful cultural institutions in Central America, financed by Costa Rican coffee barons 19th century), Plaza de la Cultura (central square with Museo del Oro Precolombino underground, unique pre-Columbian gold art collection), Mercado Central (1880, atmospheric traditional market with local "sodas" restaurant stands, spices, tropical flowers, exotic fruits), Museo Nacional de Costa Rica (in former Bellavista fortress, butterfly garden, pre-Columbian hall, mysterious Diquís stone spheres UNESCO), Museo de Arte Costarricense (in former airport, 20th-century Costa Rican art), Museo de los Niños (former prison reconverted into children's museum), Barrio Amón Quarter (historic quarter preserved 19th-20th century colonial and neo-classical architecture, bohemian atmosphere), Escalante Quarter ("San José foodie", concentration of the best cosmopolitan restaurants in the capital), Metropolitan Cathedral (1871 neo-classical), Iglesia La Merced and Iglesia La Soledad, Museo Calderón Guardia, Parque Metropolitano La Sabana (Costa Rican Central Park, 72 hectares with former airport reconverted). Day excursions from San José: Volcán Poás 1h away (one of the most accessible in Costa Rica, active crater accessible by car, sulphurous green lake, sometimes closed for activity), Volcán Irazú 1h30 away (the highest Costa Rican volcano 11,300 ft, exceptional panorama in clear weather over the 2 oceans Atlantic-Pacific simultaneously), La Paz Waterfall Gardens 1h away (waterfalls + zoo), Cartago 30 min away (former colonial capital, Basilica of Our Lady of the Angels = Costa Rican pilgrimage place 2 August), Orosi Valley 1h30 away (old colonial church 1743, coffee plantations), Sarchí 1h away (craftsmanship of traditional painted carts, iconic Costa Rican cultural UNESCO 2014). Try casado (traditional rice beans meat salad platter), gallo pinto (national breakfast), olla de carne, Costa Rican ceviche based on tilapia, local Tarrazú coffee (one of the best in the world), guaro (cane eau-de-vie), batidos (tropical fruit smoothies).
