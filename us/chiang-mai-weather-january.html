Context

Our verdict

Chiang Mai is the cultural capital of northern Thailand: 130,000 inhabitants in the old town, 200 temples, former capital of the Lan Na kingdom (1296-1558). January is my favorite month (9.1/10): 63-82°F, 13% rain, ideal temperature, clear skies. From November to February, it's the high season: 64-86°F, ideal dry climate. From March to May, it's the burning season (agricultural slash-and-burn): catastrophic air quality (AQI 200-500+), heat (75-97°F), inadvisable for those sensitive to respiratory issues. From June to October, it's the monsoon: 60-96% rainfall in afternoon showers, green landscapes. Highlights: Old town surrounded by moats (1 miles square), 30 temples including Wat Phra Singh, Wat Chedi Luang (majestic ruins), Wat Suan Dok, Doi Suthep (mountain temple 9 miles away, 309 steps, panoramic view over Chiang Mai). Night markets: Sunday Walking Street (the most famous), Saturday Walking Street, Night Bazaar (touristy but unmissable). Activities: Thai cooking classes (Thai Farm Cooking School, Asia Scenic), Thai massage schools (Thai Massage School Old Medicine), ethical elephant sanctuaries (Elephant Nature Park, to favour over tourist farms with training), treks in the tribal villages (Karen, Hmong, Akha, Lahu) to the north. Yi Peng Lantern Festival in November (lanterns released into the sky, one of the world's most beautiful festivals). Try khao soi, sai oua, kanom jeen.
