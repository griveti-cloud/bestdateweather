Context

Our verdict

Yogyakarta, cultural capital of Java island in Indonesia: 420,000 inhabitants (3.6 million in the urban area), the only province still officially ruled by a reigning sultan (the Sultanate of Yogyakarta = monarchic exception within the Republic of Indonesia since 1755). Locals call it "Jogja". Historic capital of Javanese culture (batik, dance, gamelan), gateway to Indonesia's 2 largest Buddhist-Hindu temples: Borobudur and Prambanan. From June to September are my favorite months (6.9/10): 70-88°F, 33-54% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Yogyakarta never tops 6.9/10 (hot humid tropical climate). From May to October, it's the dry season: 72-88°F, this is the tourist high season. From November to April, it's the rainy season: 76-95% rainfall, oppressive humidity, but stable temperatures. Highlights: Borobudur Temple (UNESCO 1991, 25 miles to the north-west, the world's largest Buddhist temple, built 9th century Sailendra dynasty, rediscovered and restored by the British in 1814 then the Dutch and UNESCO in the 1970s, 3D mandala in black volcanic stone with 504 Buddha statues over 9 levels, view at sunrise 4am from Punthuk Setumbu observation point, access to the temple itself limited since 2022), Prambanan Temple (UNESCO 1991, 11 miles to the east, the largest Hindu temple complex in Indonesia, 9th century, Sanjaya dynasty, dedicated to the Shiva-Vishnu-Brahma trinity, 8 main temples + 240 smaller shrines, view at sunset), Yogyakarta Kraton (palace of the reigning sultan, traditional Javanese complex, still home to Sultan Hamengkubuwono X), Taman Sari Water Castle ("water castle", former 18th-century royal complex of baths and gardens), Mount Merapi (active volcano 9,600 ft, one of the most active in the world, last major eruption 2010 with 350 deaths, night hike with guide for sunrise 4am = intense experience), Mount Bromo 8h by bus (active volcano in eastern Java, spectacular caldera panorama at sunrise), Malioboro Street (main shopping street, batik, night market, becak cycle-rickshaws), Pasar Beringharjo (traditional central market since 1758). Cultural activities: batik workshops (Yogyakarta = world capital of batik, the craft has been UNESCO-listed since 2009), Ramayana ballet shows at Prambanan in the open air with the temple lit up in the background (May-October, 250 dancers and gamelan orchestra), gamelan, wayang kulit shadow puppet theater (UNESCO 2003). Try gudeg (sweet green-jackfruit curry, iconic Jogja dish), bakpia (mung-bean pastry), nasi goreng, sate ayam. Yogyakarta International airport (YIA) 45 min away since 2020.
