Context

Our verdict

Montenegro (Crna Gora = "Black Mountain"), small Balkan country on the Adriatic: 620,000 inhabitants. Peaceful independence from Serbia 2006 by referendum (the last Yugoslav separation), EU candidate since 2010, uses the euro despite non-EU member. Atmosphere mix of Serbian Orthodox + Venetian Dalmatian + Balkan mountain culture. June is my favorite month (9.1/10): 70-82°F, 29% rain, sea at 72°F, ideal temperatures. September stays excellent (8.3/10). From May to September, it's the tourist high season: 54-88°F. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (75-88°F). From November to March, expect 41-63°F and 34-49% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, snow in the Durmitor mountains 8,300 ft. Coast highlights: Bay of Kotor (Boka Kotorska, Adriatic fjord unique in the Mediterranean, considered one of the most beautiful fjords in the world, UNESCO 1979), Kotor (UNESCO, fortified Venetian medieval old town 12th century at the foot of Mt Lovćen, 1350 stairs to the panoramic San Giovanni fortress 920 ft iconic), Perast (delightful baroque village on the bay edge facing 2 islets including Our Lady of the Rocks = artificial islet built by the Kotor sailors 15th-18th centuries on voluntarily sunken wrecks, iconic church), Budva (15th-century fortified old town + main beach resort of Montenegro, dynamic Russian-Eastern European tourist atmosphere), Sveti Stefan (14th-century medieval fortified peninsula transformed into ultra-exclusive 5*+ Aman hotel-village 1960-2007, iconic view of Montenegro = postcard image of the country, currently limited access), Herceg Novi (at the entrance of the Bay of Kotor, fortification town, tropical palm trees), Petrovac and Bar (calmer beach resorts in the south), Ulcinj (extreme south Albanian border, Albanian-speaking community, 7 miles of beach Velika Plaža = the longest beach in Montenegro). Mountain sites: Mt Lovćen (national park, mausoleum of poet-prince Petar Petrović-Njegoš 19th century at 5,400 ft), Cetinje (former royal and religious capital of Montenegro pre-1918, Cetinje monastery 15th century), Durmitor National Park (UNESCO 1980, mountains 8,300 ft, glacial Black Lake Crno jezero, Tara gorges = one of the deepest gorges in Europe 4,300 ft, Tara River rafting), Žabljak (winter ski resort). Excursions: Dubrovnik 30 min away (Croatia, simple border), Mostar 3h away (Bosnia-Herzegovina), Tirana 4h away (Albania). Try kacamak (Montenegrin polenta), pršut (iconic Montenegrin dried ham), kajmak (Balkan fermented butter), riblja čorba (fish soup), Vranac iconic Montenegrin red wine (one of the great little-known Balkan wines), rakija (eau-de-vie). Tivat (TIV) and Podgorica (TGD) airports.
