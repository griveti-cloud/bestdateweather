Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Niamey, capital of Niger on the Niger river: 1.3 million inhabitants. Poorest Sahelian country in the world according to UN HDI human ranking, extreme arid climate (75% of the territory in the Sahara), former French colony (1922-1960), among the least touristic countries in the world (1500 visitors/year approximately). Military coup in July 2023 by General Tchiani (overthrowing democratically elected President Mohamed Bazoum), expulsion of French and American forces end 2023, rapprochement with Putin's Russia and Erdogan's Turkey, temporary ECOWAS embargo. January-December are my favorite months (8.5-8.7/10): 61-91°F, 0% rain, this is the ideal dry season + refreshing Saharan harmattan. From November to February, it's the cool dry season: 61-97°F, ideal dry desert climate. From March to June, it's the Sahelian pre-monsoon heatwave: 66-106°F (sometimes 117°F+ = among the highest temperatures in West Africa, extreme climate). From July to September, it's the short Sahelian rainy season: 15-53% rainfall. Niamey highlights: Niamey Central Market (Grand Marché, traditional Sahelian atmosphere), Boubou-Hama National Museum (one of the best museums in Sahelian Africa, Hausa-Songhai-Tuareg mask collection, Niger dinosaurs "Sahel saurus" + "Nigersaurus" iconic paleontological), Central Mosque of Niamey, Notre-Dame Cathedral of Niamey, Kennedy Bridge (on the Niger river), Banizoumbou Quarter, Wadata craftsmen village. Essential Niger excursions (very inadvisable for security currently): Aïr Massif (UNESCO 1991, Saharan mountains 6,600 ft highest point Mount Bagzane, prehistoric Tassili-Aïr rock paintings 8000 BC, traditional Tuareg peoples, spectacular desert landscapes, inadvisable for security jihadist zone since 2014), Ténéré Desert (the most iconic Saharan desert after the central Sahara, Bilma dunes 330 ft+ high, Tree of Ténéré = legendary solitary tree in the most isolated desert in the world 250 miles from any other tree, hit by a drunk truck 1973, replaced by a metal monument today), Agadez (UNESCO 2013, Tuareg capital of north-Niger, iconic 15th-century mud mosque), W National Park (UNESCO 1996 cross-border Niger-Benin-Burkina Faso, West African savannah, elephants-lions-buffaloes, inadvisable for security). Safety: inadvisable France Diplomacy for the entirety of the country = unstable post-coup situation, active jihadist terrorism (Boko Haram south-east, AQMI/ISGS north-west), Western kidnappings possible, formally inadvisable travel. Try djerma (millet ball), Niger greasy rice, fonio, dolo, bissap-ginger juice, spiced Arabic coffee, ceremonial Tuareg tea 3 glasses iconic cultural ("1st bitter as life, 2nd sweet as love, 3rd suave as death" according to tradition). Niamey Diori Hamani airport (NIM).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
