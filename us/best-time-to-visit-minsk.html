Minsk, capital of Belarus: 2 million inhabitants. Former Soviet republic 1922-1991, ruled with an iron fist since 1994 by Alexander Lukashenko ("last dictatorship in Europe"), rigged elections 2020 having triggered massive demonstrations severely repressed. Unwavering ally of Putin's Russia, logistical support to the Russian invasion of Ukraine 2022. June is my favorite month (8.4/10): 55-72°F, 29% rain, ideal temperatures. July-August stay excellent (8.2-8.4/10). From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 54-75°F. From November to March, expect 21/37°F and 31-46% rain: harsh continental Soviet cold, abundant snow. Highlights: Independence Square (Niezaliežnasci Square, one of the largest squares in Europe 7 hectares, monumental Stalinist urbanism iconic), Independence Avenue (Niezaliežnasci Avenue, 9 miles one of the longest main avenues in Europe, magnificently preserved Stalinist architecture 1944-1956 = Minsk was destroyed at 80% during the Second World War then rebuilt in grandiose Stalinist architecture), Victory Square (Pieramohi Square, monument to the Soviet dead of the Great Patriotic War), National Library of Belarus (modern architecture in the form of a polygonal diamond 2006, 240 ft high, panorama from the roof accessible to visitors), Museum of the Great Patriotic War (opened 2014 by Lukashenko and Putin together, one of the largest post-Soviet military museums, Russo-Soviet perspective on the Second World War), Trinity Suburb old town (Trojeckaje Pradmiescie, the only old quarter preserved in Minsk, 19th-century colored houses on the Svislatch river, picturesque atmosphere rare in this rebuilt city), Holy Spirit Cathedral (17th-century Polish Baroque Orthodox cathedral), Saints Simon and Helena Church ("Red Church", 1910 neo-Romanesque red bricks), Komarovka Quarter (Kamarouski Rynak, traditional central market). Belarus excursions: Mir Castle 62 miles away (UNESCO 2000, 16th-century Gothic-Renaissance castle, one of the best preserved in Central Europe), Niasvij/Nieswiez (UNESCO 2005, castle of the Radziwill magnates 16th-19th century), Brest (heroic Second World War fortress, Polish border), Białowieża Forest (UNESCO shared with Poland, last primary forest in Europe, last European bison in the wild). Safety: Belarus political situation makes any tourist visit inadvisable since 2020 (international sanctions, direct flights from Europe canceled, international payments blocked, political activists imprisoned, Western travelers risk arbitrary detention), France Diplomacy formally advises against. Try draniki (potato pancakes, Belarusian national dish), machanka (pork sauce), kvas (fermented Slavic drink), Belarusian vodka Belaja Vieza, local pierogi. Minsk National airport (MSQ) under EU sanctions since 2021.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
