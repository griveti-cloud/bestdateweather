Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Hydra (Ύδρα), Greek island in the Saronic Gulf 43 miles south of Athens: 2000 permanent inhabitants over 19 sq miles. No motorised vehicles allowed on the island since 1968 (royal decree to preserve the unique character of the village): only means of transport = mules and donkeys for luggage transport, walking paths, water-taxis to the distant beaches. Smart-bohemian atmosphere, refuge of artists and intellectuals (Leonard Cohen had his house here 1960-2007 where he wrote several albums). June is my favorite month (9.5/10): 72-86°F, 9% rain, sea at 72°F, ideal temperatures. September stays excellent (9.3/10). July-August stay good (6.9-8.8/10) despite the heat (77-91°F) and the crowds (Athenians on weekends, Italians, Americans). From November to March, expect 46-66°F and 22-26% rain: mild, many establishments close, island almost deserted (unique charm). Highlights: Hydra Port (horseshoe-shaped, one of the most beautiful Aegean ports, 18th-19th century mansion-houses in stone of the Hydriot shipowners who made fortune in maritime trade then contributed to the Greek War of Independence in 1821, unique atmosphere with donkeys waiting to transport luggage to the hotels), Mansions Tombazi, Voulgaris, Tsamados, Boudouris (some 18th-century palaces open to the public), Cathedral of the Dormition (17th-century with iconic white marble bell tower), Hydra Historical Museum (the island's role in the Greek War of Independence), Lazaros Koundouriotis House (18th-century Hydriot shipowner museum), Leonard Cohen's House (private, view from outside, near the port). Beaches: Hydra has no sandy beach at the village (flat rocks with direct access to deep water), for real beaches take a water-taxi: Vlychos (sandy, 30 min on foot or 5 min by boat), Bisti (the most beautiful, turquoise water, access by boat only), Agios Nikolaos (extreme west, isolated), Mandraki (north-east, former shipyard converted into a beach). Paths: Hydra is a hiker's paradise (mountainous island, paths from the village to the Profitis Ilias and Eleni monasteries 2h away), cultural route to the fishing village of Kamini (15 min on foot). Saronic excursions: Spetses, Aegina, Poros by ferry 30 min to 2h. Try amygdalota (almond cakes iconic of Hydra), saganaki, local sea urchin (fresh urchins), ouzo. Logistics: Hydra accessible only by hydrofoil from Piraeus Athens in 1h30 (Hellenic Seaways $33/person, no direct flight, no bridge), Saronic ferry 3h.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
