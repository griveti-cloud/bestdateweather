Context

Our verdict

San Antonio, in Texas: 1.5 million inhabitants (7th largest city in the USA), founded by the Spanish in 1718, the most historic "Hispanic" city in the USA after Saint Augustine. Place of the Battle of the Alamo (23 February-6 March 1836, the founding event of Texan identity, 187 Texan defenders killed by Santa Anna's Mexican troops). October is my favorite month (8.7/10): 66-84°F, 25% rain, ideal temperatures after the summer heatwave. From April to June and October-November are the best seasons: 55-86°F. From July-August drop to 5.8-5.9/10 due to the extreme heat (77-97°F, sometimes 104°F+), oppressive humidity typical of Texas. From December to February, expect 43-70°F: mild for the south, but brutal variations possible (norther winds that can drop the temperature 68°F in a few hours). Highlights: The Alamo (Spanish Franciscan mission 1718 transformed into fortress 1835, restored as monument since 1905 by the Daughters of the Republic of Texas, 2.5 million visitors/year, one of the most emotionally charged sites in American history, free), San Antonio Missions National Historical Park (UNESCO 2015, 4 Spanish missions + the Alamo, 7 miles to cycle along the San Antonio River: Concepción, San José, San Juan, Espada), River Walk ("Paseo del Río", 15 miles of developed promenade along the river 1 level below the street, restaurants, bars, gondolas, opened 1939-41 as part of the New Deal, one of the most successful urban projects in the USA), Tower of the Americas (740 ft, panoramic view), Pearl District (former brewery converted into trendy gastronomic zone), Mercado (Market Square, the largest Mexican market outside Mexico). Fiesta San Antonio (10 days late April, annual festival since 1891 celebrating Hispanic heritage). Excursions: Hill Country to the north-west (hilly landscapes, Texan wines, lavender fields, Fredericksburg town founded by Germans), Austin 1h30 away. Try Tex-Mex (origin of the genre), Texan barbecue, breakfast tacos, margaritas (the frozen "Texan margarita" allegedly invented in San Antonio in 1971).
