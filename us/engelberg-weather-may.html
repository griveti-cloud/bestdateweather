Context

Our verdict

Engelberg, Swiss ski resort in the canton of Obwald in central Switzerland: 4400 inhabitants at 3,300 ft altitude at the foot of Mount Titlis (10,600 ft). 1h from Lucerne and 1h30 from Zurich = the most accessible ski resort from the Swiss capitals for weekends. July-August are my favorite months (7.3-7.4/10): 55-70°F, 70-78% rain, ideal temperatures for alpine hiking. From June to September, it's the summer high season: 43-70°F, hiking, mountain bike, paragliding. From December to March, expect 25/43°F and 47-78% precipitation often as snow: ski high season. Pistes: 51 miles Engelberg-Titlis (modest vs 375 miles Portes du Soleil or 3 Valleys, but excellent snow quality thanks to altitude and the Titlis glacier which guarantees snow year-round), 2 sides: Brunni north side (family, sunny in the afternoon) and Titlis-Jochpass south side (more demanding, access to the 9,800 ft glacier). Legendary pistes: Laub (legendary off-piste descent 4,300 ft vertical between Stand 7,900 ft and Engelberg 3,400 ft, one of the longest groomed off-pistes in Europe, attracts world freeriders), Steinberg (glacial descent), Jochpass-Engstlenalp (long 4.3 miles family piste). Off-piste: Galtiberg, Sulz, Steinberg back, accessible by cable car from Stand 7,900 ft, freeriders from around the world come to Engelberg for the famous "Engelberg Powder", one of the deepest powders in Europe in good season (3 ft+ after snowfalls). Mount Titlis (cable car "Titlis Rotair" from Engelberg-Trubsee-Stand-Titlis 9,900 ft summit, 30 min journey, the cable car of the last section Stand-Titlis rotates 360° in 5 min = unique in the world, panorama over 100 alpine summits, panoramic restaurant at the summit, Cliff Walk = suspension bridge 330 ft above the void, Glacier Cave = cave sculpted in the glacier, Ice Flyer = chairlift over the glacier). Excursions: Lucerne 1h by train (already covered by BDW), Mount Pilatus (other panoramic summit near Lucerne with the steepest cogwheel train in the world 48%), Zurich 1h30 away, Mount Rigi. Audience: Swiss Germans in the majority, many British (Engelberg = one of the British favorite resorts historically), Asians in groups via Titlis cable car (Engelberg is one of the alpine resorts most visited by Asian Chinese tourists, especially in summer for the Cliff Walk photo), few French. Hotels: Kempinski Palace Engelberg (5*+ luxury opened 2021), Hotel Bellevue Terminus (4*), Hotel Schweizerhof, $220-$1,650/night. Try rösti, fondue moitié-moitié, raclette, Älplermagronen (potato pasta gratin), bündner fleisch (dried meat). Logistics: Engelberg accessible by direct train from Lucerne 1h (Zentralbahn picturesque line), 1h30 from Zurich airport.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Engelberg?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 9.0/10). For hiking/suiner: March (9.0/10, 6.0°F).
Can you ski in Engelberg in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.5/10. Cold temperatures (3°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Engelberg in suiner?
In March, temperatures average 6.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Engelberg?
July is the wettest month with July has the lowest climate score of the year.
