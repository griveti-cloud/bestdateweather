Context

Our verdict

Dhaka, capital of Bangladesh on the Ganges-Brahmaputra delta: 10 million inhabitants intra-muros, 22 million in urban area = one of the most densely populated megacities in the world (44,000 inhabitants/km² in the heart, denser than Manhattan). Bangladesh = one of the poorest and most densely populated countries in the world (170 million on the size of Iowa), but in rapid economic growth thanks to textile (2nd world exporter after China, main supplier H&M, Zara, Primark). Very little touristic. February is my favorite month (9.0/10): 61-81°F, 8% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January-November-December stay excellent (8.7-9.0/10). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 55-88°F, dry climate and cool in the morning (sometimes 57°F in January, surprising for tropical Asia). From April to May, expect 75-91°F: pre-monsoon heatwave + potential cyclones (Bangladesh = most cyclonic country in the world with Bay of Bengal). From June to October, it's the monsoon: 46-91% rainfall (Bangladesh = country in the world most at risk of flooding, 50% of the territory flooded each year in monsoon, Cyclone Bhola 1970 killed 500,000 people = deadliest natural catastrophe of the 20th century). Highlights: Old Dhaka (Mughal-chaotic-oriental atmosphere), Lalbagh Fort (17th-century Mughal fortress unfinished by Aurangzeb after the death of his daughter Pari Bibi, tomb in the center, gardens), Ahsan Manzil ("Pink Palace", 19th-century pink stucco palace on the banks of the Buriganga, former residence of the Bangladeshi Nawab), Star Mosque (Tara Masjid, 18th century with mosaic decoration of blue stars on white façade), Baitul Mukarram Mosque (national mosque, 7th largest in the world, capacity 40,000 worshippers), Sadarghat (river port on the Buriganga, the largest passenger river port in the world, ferries to all of Bangladesh, extraordinary atmosphere with crowd of boats), Bangladesh Parliament (Jatiya Sangsad Bhaban, designed by American architect Louis Kahn 1962-1982, considered one of the masterpieces of modern 20th-century architecture), Bangladesh National Museum, Hindu Street (Shankhari Bazaar, Hindu quarter with traditional workshops). Bangladesh excursions: Sundarbans 6h away (UNESCO 1997, the largest mangrove forest in the world, Indian border, houses the Bengal tiger Royal Bengal Tiger 100 individuals, accessible only by 2-3 day boat), Cox's Bazar 10h by bus or 1h flight ("the longest natural beach in the world" 75 miles of continuous sand, main beach resort of the country but important pollution), Rohingya Camp at Cox's Bazar (1 million Muslim Rohingya refugees fleeing the genocide in Burma since 2017, the largest refugee camp in the world, limited access). Safety: Dhaka = chaotic city with extreme density, high crime, frequent political demonstrations, air pollution among the worst in the world. Try Dhaka biryani (different from Indian biryani, more strong in mustard), bhuna khichuri (rice with lentils), hilsa (national fish, the most prized in Bangladesh), pitha (fermented rice cakes), shorshe ilish (hilsa in mustard sauce iconic Bengal), Bengal tea. Dhaka Hazrat Shahjalal airport (DAC).
