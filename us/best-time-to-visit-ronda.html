Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Ronda is one of the most spectacular pueblos blancos of Andalusia: town perched at 2,500 ft, crossed by the Tajo canyon (390 ft deep), Puente Nuevo built in 1793. June is my favorite month (9.8/10): 63-82°F, 7% rain, ideal temperatures before the heat, golden light on the Arab walls. From July to August stay excellent (8.7-8.8/10) with 70-91°F: dry heat bearable thanks to the altitude (50°F cooler than in Seville). From December to February, expect 43-59°F and 22-28% rain: mild during the day, cool in the evening, ideal for visiting without crowds. Highlights: Puente Nuevo (plunging view over the Tajo, iconic photo), Plaza de Toros (1785, one of Spain's oldest, ronda bullfighting school), Casa del Rey Moro and its hanging gardens, Baños Árabes (13th-century Arab baths), Mirador de Aldehuela. Hemingway and Orson Welles stayed here regularly (the Plaza de Toros is mentioned in For Whom the Bell Tolls, the author buried in Sun Valley). Camino de los Molinos trail to descend into the Tajo. Setenil de las Bodegas (troglodyte village) 30 min away, Grazalema and the sierra 1h away for hiking. Try rabo de toro (oxtail), Ronda wine. May and September-October are the real sweet spots tourism/weather.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
