Context

Our verdict

Cambodia (Kingdom of Cambodia), country in South-East Asia between Vietnam, Laos, Thailand: 16.5 million inhabitants. Constitutional kingdom, King Norodom Sihamoni since 2004. Country marked by the genocide of the Khmer Rouge 1975-1979 (Pol Pot, 2 million deaths or 25% of the population, one of the worst genocides of the 20th century, intellectual elite systematically murdered), reconstruction since 1993. Capital Phnom Penh, main attraction Angkor Wat. January is my favorite month (6.9/10): 72-88°F, 12% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Cambodia never tops 6.9/10 (hot tropical climate). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 72-91°F, dry climate and cool in the morning (sometimes 64°F in December, surprisingly cool for tropical Asia). From March to May, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 77-97°F (sometimes 108°F+), impossible to visit Angkor at midday without risk of heatstroke. From May to October, it's the monsoon: 65-92% rainfall, but milder temperatures (75-91°F) and the greenest landscapes. Highlights: Angkor (UNESCO 1992, the largest religious complex in the world 150 sq miles, 1000 Khmer temples 9th-15th century, capital of the Khmer empire 9-15M inhabitants at its 12th-century apogee = the largest pre-industrial city in history, rediscovered by Henri Mouhot 1860 even if never forgotten by the locals, already covered by BDW Siem Reap), Angkor Wat (the iconic temple, 530 ft × 710 ft, the largest religious monument in the world, dedicated to Vishnu then Buddha, on the Cambodian national flag = the only time a monument features on a flag in the world), Angkor Thom (fortified city 3 sq miles, Bayon temple with 216 giant faces), Ta Prohm ("Tomb Raider Temple" 2001, giant kapok roots invading the temple), Banteay Srei ("woman's fortress", the most delicate pink sandstone temple). Phnom Penh capital (1.7 million inhabitants, urban Cambodian atmosphere, Royal Palace + Silver Pagoda, S-21 Genocide Museum = former Tuol Sleng prison of the Khmer Rouge where 17,000 people were tortured, poignant testimony, Killing Fields Choeung Ek 9 miles away where 9000 bodies were exhumed, memorial with stupa containing 8000 skulls), Battambang 4h away (already covered by BDW), Sihanoukville and Koh Rong Islands (Cambodian beaches, Sihanoukville in full Chinese boom-bust 2017-2020 discouraging, Koh Rong remains paradisiacal), Kampot (charming French colonial town in the south, famous for its IGP black pepper), Kep ("crab capital" and flower), Mondulkiri and Ratanakiri (wild provinces in the north-east, ethical elephant sanctuaries). Try amok (fish curry in banana leaf, national dish), beef lok lak, num pang sandwich (French heritage), Cambodian pho, prahok (iconic fermented fish paste), bia angkor.
