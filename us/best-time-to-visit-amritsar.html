Amritsar, holy city of Sikhism in north-western India in the state of Punjab on the Pakistani border: 1.1 million inhabitants. Spiritual center of Sikhism with its Golden Temple, one of the most visited religious sites in the world (100,000 pilgrims per day, 30 million per year). Memory of the Jallianwala Bagh massacre 1919 (379 dead by British troops, trigger of the Indian independence movement) and of the Partition 1947 (creation of Pakistan, border 19 miles from the city, the most violent partition in history). March is my favorite month (8.8/10): 57-79°F, 18% rain, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 43-79°F, dry and cool climate. From June to September, it's the monsoon: 24-58% rainfall, temperatures remaining high (75-102°F) with oppressive humidity. From April to June, expect 66-102°F: extreme northern Indian heatwave, sometimes 113°F, impossible to visit in midday. Highlights: Golden Temple (Harmandir Sahib, the "Sanctuary of God", central Sikh temple, founded 1577 by Guru Ram Das 4th Sikh guru, covered with 750 kg of gold by Maharaja Ranjit Singh 1830, located in the middle of a sacred pool "Amrit Sarovar" = "the pool of nectar" which gave its name to the city Amritsar, accessible 24h/24 to all religions = Sikh principle of universal welcome, exceptional spiritual atmosphere at sunrise 4am with continuous recitation of the Guru Granth Sahib Sikh holy book, crowd of pilgrims immersed in the sacred pool), Langar (free communal kitchen inside the Golden Temple, 100,000 meals served daily to all religions = Sikh principle of equality, overwhelming human experience, possibility of helping in the kitchen to serve), Jallianwala Bagh (memorial of the 1919 massacre where Brigadier Reginald Dyer ordered firing without warning on 5000 unarmed people gathered in an enclosed garden, 10 minutes of fire, well where some jumped to escape the bullets still visible, memorial), Partition Museum (Town Hall, 2017, first world museum dedicated to the partition of India 1947, survivor testimonies), Akal Takht (Sikh temple seat of religious authority), Wagah Border Ceremony 19 miles away at the Pakistani border (daily flag-lowering ceremony at 5pm, Indian and Pakistani military rivalling in exaggerated choreographed steps, crowd of 25,000 people on the Indian side and 5000 on the Pakistani side, unique atmosphere between military circus and nationalist expression, free, arrive 1h before). Try amritsari kulcha (Punjabi flatbread stuffed with potato), chole bhature, butter chicken (iconic of Punjab), Punjabi lassi, parantha, sweet prasad of the Golden Temple. Logistics: Amritsar airport (ATQ) with direct flights from New Delhi 1h, direct Delhi-Amritsar train 6h.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
