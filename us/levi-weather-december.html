Context

Our verdict

Levi, the main ski resort of Finnish Lapland 155 miles north of the Arctic Circle: 1100 permanent inhabitants (10,000 in tourist season). Largest ski resort in Finland with 43 pistes and 27 lifts, but above all iconic destination for Northern Lights observation (Aurora Borealis), Father Christmas at neighbouring Rovaniemi + Nordic winter activities (dog sledding, snowmobile, cross-country skiing, Finnish sauna, etc.). July is my favorite month (7.8/10): 52-66°F, 42% rain, ideal temperatures in Nordic summer with midnight sun (June-July the sun never sets under the Arctic Circle 24h/24 of day). June stays excellent (7.5/10). From June to August, it's the paradoxical summer high season: 46-66°F, Lapland hiking, mountain bike, fishing (Levi has a 9-hole golf course above the Arctic Circle open even at midnight thanks to the midnight sun). From December to March, expect 7/28°F and 30-40% precipitation often as snow: this is the ski + Northern Lights observation high season, glacial temperatures (sometimes -40°F peak January-February, very cold clothing mandatory). From November to January: polar night ("Kaamos" = sun never rises 1-2h max twilight, optimal conditions for the auroras). Pistes: 27 miles Levi Resort (modest vs Alps: 375 miles 3 Valleys), altitude 200-1,700 ft (very low compared to Alps 1000-11,500 ft, but high latitude compensates for the snow), long ski season November-May, snow guaranteed thanks to permanent cold climate. iconic pistes: Levi Slope (Alpine Ski World Cup giant slalom descent every November, traditionally the first stage of the FIS circuit), Front Slope (main side). iconic Nordic winter activities: dog sledding ("husky safari", iconic Lapland experience, ride or full day, $110-$330/person, drive 6-dog huskies yourself), snowmobile ("snowmobile safari"), cross-country skiing (Levi = paradise of langlauf 145 miles of trails), snowshoeing, fat bike (wide-tyre bike on snow), reindeer safari in the company of indigenous Sami breeders (traditional Scandinavian Lapp people), Northern Lights safari (night outing 9pm-1am by snowshoe or snowmobile in search of the Northern Lights far from urban lights, optimal conditions required: clear sky + solar activity = Kp +3 minimum level, no guarantee but 70-80% chance per stay of 5+ days in season December-March). Finnish sauna = unique Finnish cultural institution in the world (UNESCO inscription 2020), to practice authentically with ritual: sauna 194°F → ice/pure snow bath → sauna → ice bath → relaxation, mandatory iconic experience in Finland. Hotels: Hotel Levi Panorama (4*+ panoramic), Hotel K5 Levi, Glass Igloos Levin Iglut (iconic of Levi = transparent glass igloo rooms to observe the auroras from your bed, $440-$1,100/night in high season), Aurora Cabins. Excursions: Rovaniemi 2h by bus ("Santa Claus Village" = official Father Christmas village where all children's letters from the world addressed to Father Christmas are received, biggest tourist attraction in Lapland, already covered by BDW), Saariselkä 2h away (other ski resort + auroras further north). Try reindeer (iconic Lapland reindeer pan with berry sauce), salmon soup, korvapuusti (Finnish cinnamon buns), cloudberry (Arctic blackberry local berry), lakka liqueur, glögi (spiced Finnish mulled wine), salmon brine. Kittilä airport (KTT) 15 min away, direct flights Helsinki + European charters in season.

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Levi?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: April (ski score 8.6/10). For hiking/suiner: April (8.6/10, 2.0°F).
Can you ski in Levi in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 6.6/10. Cold temperatures (-7°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Levi in suiner?
In April, temperatures average 2.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Levi?
October is the wettest month with October has the lowest climate score of the year.
