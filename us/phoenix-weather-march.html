Context

Our verdict

Phoenix is the opposite of the European calendar. From November to April, the climate becomes ideal: 43-88°F, permanent blue sky, 1-13% rain. Europeans land for golf, Camelback Mountain hikes and the botanical gardens in bloom. April stays my favorite month (9.4/10): saguaros in bloom, still bearable temperatures before the heat, festivals in the desert. From June to September, the heat exceeds 104°F every day and represents real danger: every year, careless hikers lose their lives here. Phoenicians live in air-conditioning, go out before 7am or after 9pm, and flee to altitude (Flagstaff, Sedona). If you come anyway during this season, plan 4L of water per hike and forget hikes after 9am. March also works well (8.8/10) with the MLB spring training that animates the whole city. Sedona, 2h by road, stays 50°F cooler in summer.
