Context

Our verdict

Tbilisi, capital of Georgia: 1.1 million inhabitants at 1,200 ft altitude in a valley of the Mtkvari (Kura) river at the foot of the ancient Caucasus. Founded in the 5th century by King Vakhtang I who allegedly discovered the local hot springs during a hunting trip (the pheasant killed fell into a hot-water spring and was instantly cooked — origin of the name "Tbilisi" = "warm"). Georgian capital with multiple civilisations: Georgian, Persian, Ottoman, Russian, Soviet. June is my favorite month (9.0/10): 64-82°F, 32% rain, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. From May to June and September, these are the best seasons: 55-82°F, 16-44% rain. August drops to 8.7/10 due to the heat (70-90°F), July 6.9/10. From December to March, expect 32-55°F and 17-35% rain: continental winter cold, snow possible. Highlights: Old town (Old Tbilisi) (unique atmosphere, characteristic carved-wood balconies, twisting alleys, lift from Rike Park), Narikala Fortress (4th century, iconic panorama over the city, accessible by 1.4 miles cable car from Rike Park), Metekhi Church (13th century, on a cliff above the river, equestrian statue of King Vakhtang I), Sameba Cathedral (Holy Trinity, 2002-2004, the largest Orthodox cathedral in the Caucasus), Abanotubani Sulphur Baths ("baths quarter", brick architecture with domes, traditional Georgian experience with kissi masseur $33-$55), Bridge of Peace (controversial contemporary modern footbridge 2010), Rustaveli Avenue (Georgian Champs-Élysées, opera, parliament, grand luxury hotels), Sololaki Quarter (19th-century bourgeois buildings undergoing gentrification), Dezerter Bazaar Market (spices, cheeses, fruits, wines). Excursions: Mtskheta 20 min away (UNESCO, former capital until the 5th century, Jvari monastery and Svetitskhoveli cathedral), Kazbegi/Stepantsminda 3h away (Mount Kazbek 16,600 ft, Tsminda Sameba church 7,100 ft iconic photo of Georgia on the high Caucasus balcony), Sighnaghi 2h away ("city of love", Kakheti wine region, qvevri amphora-buried wines 8000-year UNESCO method). Try khinkali (Georgian dumplings), khachapuri (cheese bread, particularly adjarian boat-shaped with fried egg), satsivi (chicken with walnut sauce), tkemali (plum sauce), Georgian wines (saperavi red, rkatsiteli white), tchatcha (marc eau-de-vie).
