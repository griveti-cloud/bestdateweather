Cotswolds, rural region of central-western England: 200,000 inhabitants spread across a succession of villages classified AONB ("Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty" since 1966), covering 6 counties (Gloucestershire, Oxfordshire, Wiltshire, Worcestershire, Warwickshire, Bath and North East Somerset). The postcard image of the classic English countryside: golden "Cotswold stone" houses with tiled or thatched roofs, flowery cottage gardens, traditional pubs, sheep in the meadows. July is my favorite month (7.8/10): 55-68°F, 42% rain, mild temperatures ideal for hiking. From May to September, it's the tourist high season: 46-68°F, unpredictable English climate (can be freezing even in July), long days (15h of daylight in June), gardens in full bloom June-July. From November to March, expect 34-50°F and 36-45% rain: damp cold, few tourists, unique charm of the English winter countryside (smoking chimneys, pubs with wood fires). iconic villages to visit: Bibury (often called "the most beautiful village in England" by William Morris in the 19th century, Arlington Row = former 14th-century wool granary converted into cottages, on a 19th-century British banknote, viral Instagram photo), Bourton-on-the-Water ("Venice of the Cotswolds" for its Windrush river crossing the center with stone bridges, model village in the gardens), Stow-on-the-Wold (old market village, the imposing church of Saint Edward with door between 2 thousand-year-old yews — likely inspiration for Tolkien's "Door of Durin"), Castle Combe ("most beautiful village in England" according to others, preserved 14th century, filming location of several films including War Horse Spielberg 2011), Lower and Upper Slaughter ("slaughter" = comes from old English "slo" = wet place, no massacre, two villages on the edge of a river), Snowshill, Painswick, Chipping Campden, Burford, Broadway. Castles and gardens: Blenheim Palace at Woodstock (UNESCO, 18th-century baroque palace, birthplace of Winston Churchill 1874), Hidcote Manor Garden and Sudeley Castle. Cotswold Way (long-distance trail 100 miles Chipping Campden-Bath, 7-10 day hike). Excursions: Bath 1h away, Oxford 30 min-1h away, Stratford-upon-Avon (Shakespeare's birthplace). Try traditional cream tea (scones + clotted cream + jam Cornish or Devonshire = regional war over the order of cream then jam or vice versa), local pub ales (Hook Norton, Donnington's), warm pies. Logistics: no central station in the Cotswolds, better to rent a car from London, Bristol or Birmingham.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
