Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Nouméa is the capital of New Caledonia (French overseas territory of the South Pacific): 100,000 inhabitants, "Nice of the Pacific" atmosphere, multicultural community (Europeans, Kanak Melanesians, Polynesians, Asians). November is my favorite month (8.5/10): 70-81°F, 43% rain, end of the austral spring, sea at 75°F. From May to November is the relative dry season: 33-47% rainfall, temperatures 63-79°F. From December to April, it's the wet season: 50-70% rainfall, heavy heat (75-84°F), cyclone risk (Niran 2021). Highlights: Anse Vata (main urban beach, lively), Baie des Citrons (other urban beach), Tjibaou Cultural Center (Renzo Piano, dedicated to Kanak culture), Nouméa Market (Port Moselle, in the morning). Excursions: Île aux Canards (marked underwater path, accessible by 5-min shuttle), Ilot Maître (resort on islet), Amédée Lighthouse (day trip). The Caledonian lagoon is the largest closed lagoon in the world (UNESCO, 9,270 sq miles): diving and snorkelling on the 2nd largest barrier reef in the world. Loyalty Islands 1h by plane (Lifou, Maré, Ouvéa for the most beautiful beach). Grande Terre (main island): Yaté for nature, Rivière Bleue, Mont Panié. Try bougna (traditional Kanak dish), bichique. Direct flight Paris-Nouméa via Tokyo in 24h (Aircalin). Expensive (French Pacific island), but very beautiful destination.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
