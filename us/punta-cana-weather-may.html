Context

Our verdict

Punta Cana is the all-inclusive Caribbean beach resort par excellence: 37 miles of coconut beaches in the east of the Dominican Republic. March is my favorite month (8.2/10): 73-81°F, 52% rain, sea at 79°F. From June to November, it's hurricane season: 64-78% rainfall, hurricane risk (main Caribbean route), but off the main path in Punta Cana (Atlantic east side). From December to April, expect 73-84°F: North-American and Canadian high season, hotel prices x2-x3. Highlights: Beaches Bávaro, Cabeza de Toro, El Cortecito (the liveliest), Macao (wilder). Hoyo Azul (turquoise cenote at Scape Park), Saona and Catalina (catamaran excursions to postcard islets), Isla Saona (tourist peak, departures from Bayahibe). ATV/buggy excursions in the countryside, diving on the east coast. Bavaro and Higüey are the tourist zones. Las Terrenas (Samaná Peninsula) 2h away offers a more European, less all-inclusive atmosphere (substantial French community). Santo Domingo 2h (UNESCO, the first European city in the Americas 1496). Try mangú (plantain), pollo guisado, sancocho, mama juana (rum-wine-spices). All-inclusive culture very dominant: little incentive to leave the resorts.
