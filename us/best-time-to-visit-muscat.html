Muscat, capital of the Sultanate of Oman on the Gulf of Oman: 1.4 million inhabitants in the urban area. Reputed to be one of the most discreet and preserved Arab capitals (strict architecture respecting traditional heritage: no skyscrapers, mandatory white-beige paint on all buildings, no gaudy advertising panels). Absolutist sultanate but peaceful and welcoming (no religious conflicts, openness to non-Muslims, crime among the lowest in the world). March is my favorite month (9.2/10): 72-84°F, 6% rain, this is the ideal dry season. November stays excellent (9.1/10). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 63-84°F, ideal dry and temperate climate. From May to September, it's the extreme heatwave: 77-102°F (sometimes 117°F+), impossible to visit in the middle of the day, oppressive coastal humidity. From April to October drop to 3.4-6.9/10 due to the heat. Highlights: Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque (Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque, 2001, one of the world's largest mosques, capacity 20,000 worshippers, houses the world's 2nd largest hand-woven carpet 230 ft × 200 ft + 46 ft-tall Swarovski chandelier, open to non-Muslims Saturday-Thursday 8h-11h, strict dress code for women: abaya + headscarf provided at the entrance), Royal Opera House Muscat (opera house inaugurated 2011, traditional Omani architecture in white and marble, capacity 1100 spectators, classical and contemporary programming, one of the smartest opera houses in the Arab world), Mutrah Souk (former traditional Omani souk, one of the oldest in the Gulf, frankincense (Oman = world capital of frankincense since Antiquity, the incense route ran from Salalah to the Mediterranean), spices, traditional silver jewellery khanjar, fabrics, perfumes), Mutrah Corniche (2 miles seaside promenade, one of Oman's most beautiful with traditional atmosphere), Portuguese forts Al-Jalali and Al-Mirani (16th century, defended the royal port, accessible only from outside), Al Alam Palace (ceremonial palace of the Sultan, modern typically Omani architecture, no visit access but exterior photos), Bait Al Zubair Museum (museum of traditional Omani art: khanjar collection, costumes, jewellery), Old Muscat district (historic old town, guarded by fortified walls, almost deserted in the evening but traditional), National Museum (Oman's largest museum opened 2016). Essential Oman excursions: Wadi Shab 2h away ("turf valley", turquoise canyon with natural pools + iconic hidden cave, 1h hike to get there, swimming required to reach the cave through a tunnel, one of the natural wonders of Oman), Wadi Bani Khalid 3h away (another canyon with turquoise pools, more accessible), Wahiba Sands Desert 3h away (huge orange dunes 330 ft, night in a Bedouin desert camp under the stars, dune-bashing 4×4), Nizwa 1h30 away (former Omani capital 17th century, iconic circular fort, traditional Friday souk with goat-camel auctions), Jebel Akhdar 2h away ("Green Mountain" 6,600 ft, rose plantations for AOC rose water, cool temperatures), Jebel Shams 3h away ("Mountain of the Sun" 9,900 ft, Oman's highest point, the "Grand Canyon of Arabia" with panoramic Balcony Walk hike). Try shuwa (lamb cooked for 24h in an underground oven in banana leaves, iconic Omani festive dish), majboos (spiced biryani-like Arab rice), Omani halwa (iconic spiced sweet), Arabic coffee with dates (Omani welcome ritual), kahwa (cardamom coffee).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
