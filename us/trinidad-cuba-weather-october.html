Context

Our verdict

Trinidad, colonial town in central Cuba in Sancti Spíritus province: 75,000 inhabitants 3.7 miles from the Caribbean coast. Founded in 1514 by Diego Velázquez, it's one of the oldest towns in the Americas. UNESCO 1988 with the neighbouring Valle de los Ingenios for the best-preserved colonial ensemble in Cuba (frozen in time after the decline of sugar at the end of the 19th century). January-February are my favorite months (8.9/10): 68-84°F, 14-16% rain, this is the ideal Caribbean dry season. From November to April, it's the tourist high season: 68-88°F, 9-30% rain. From March-April drop to 6.9/10 due to increasing heat. From May to October, it's the rain and cyclone season: 47-62% rainfall. Highlights: Plaza Mayor (main square, intact 17th-19th century colonial ensemble), Iglesia Parroquial de la Santísima Trinidad (19th-century neoclassical), Museo Romántico (Casa Brunet, 19th-century palace furnished from origin), Museo Municipal (Palacio Cantero, 360° panorama from the bell tower), Casa de la Música (staircase in front of the church, salsa/son live music every evening in the open air, the Cuban soul), Casa de la Trova (traditional music), Plaza Carrillo, Casitas Quarter (colorful colonial houses). Excursions: Valle de los Ingenios (UNESCO, valley of the former sugar plantations with 75 ruined 18th-19th century refineries, Manaca-Iznaga Tower 150 ft built by a planter to watch his slaves), Playa Ancón 7 miles away (iconic beach of Trinidad, turquoise water), Topes de Collantes 30 min away (mountainous national park 2,600 ft altitude, hike Salto del Caburní waterfall), Cienfuegos 50 miles away (UNESCO, French town founded 1819, baroque). Logistics: Trinidad 4h by car from Havana (Carretera Central road), casa particular (with locals) recommended over hotels ($16-$33/night, more local and traditional experience). Try ropa vieja (shredded meat), congrí (black rice with beans), canchánchara (iconic Trinidad honey-lemon-rum cocktail), Cubita coffee.
