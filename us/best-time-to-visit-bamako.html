Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Bamako, capital of Mali on the Niger river: 2.8 million inhabitants, one of the largest cities in francophone West Africa. Sahelian country among the poorest in Africa, fragile political situation since 2012 (Tuareg rebellion + jihadist occupation 2012, French intervention Operation Serval 2013, French withdrawal 2022 after several military coups 2020 and 2021, Russian Wagner presence since 2022, inadvisable France Diplomacy). Cultural capital of West Africa for Mandingo music. January-December are my favorite months (8.5/10): 64-91°F, 0-1% rain, this is the ideal dry season + refreshing harmattan. From November to February, it's the cool dry season: 63-93°F, dry and tempered climate, Saharan harmattan. From March to May, it's the hot dry season: 75-102°F, pre-monsoon heatwave. From June to September, it's the monsoon: 40-74% rainfall, but milder temperatures 73-95°F. Bamako highlights: Bamako Central Market (Rose Market, lively West African atmosphere), National Museum of Mali (one of the best museums in West Africa, Dogon-Bambara-Senufo-Bozo ritual masks, ancient Malian art), Bridge of the Martyrs on the Niger river, Bamako Mosque, Tower of Africa and Independence Monument, Hippodrome Quarter (lively Mandingo music club atmosphere). Essential Mali excursions (currently inadvisable for security): Dogon Country (UNESCO 1989, Bandiagara cliff 125 miles long with traditional Dogon villages inhabited since the 14th century, mud architecture unique in the world, iconic ritual masks, 4-7 day hike but unstable insecurity zone), Timbuktu (UNESCO 1988, legendary Sahelian African city, 14th-16th century mud mosques, former cultural capital of the 14th-century Mali Empire with Sankoré University one of the oldest in the world, ancient manuscripts threatened since the 2012 jihadist occupation, 4000+ manuscripts saved in Bamako, inadvisable for security), Djenné (UNESCO 1988, the largest mud mosque in the world rebuilt every year during the Crepissage festival, Djenné Monday market one of the most colorful in Africa), Mopti ("Venice of Mali", river town at the Niger-Bani confluence), Ségou (historic capital of the 17th-19th century Bambara Empire, annual festival on the Niger). Safety: inadvisable France Diplomacy for the entirety of the country except Bamako zone under increased vigilance since 2012, periodic jihadist attacks, Western kidnappings possible, very inadvisable travel. Try tô (millet ball), Malian greasy rice, fonio (West African iconic cereal), Niger river captain (grilled fish), bissap, dolo (traditional millet beer), Touba coffee (spiced). Bamako-Sénou airport (BKO).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
