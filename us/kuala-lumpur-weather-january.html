Context

Our verdict

Kuala Lumpur is Malaysia's capital, a cosmopolitan city (Malays, Chinese, Indians), with its Petronas Twin Towers (1,500 ft). January-February are my favorite months (5.9-6.0/10): 75-90°F, 64-77% rain, this is the north-east dry season, more bearable humidity. From March to May and October to November, these are the monsoons: 76-96% rainfall in afternoon tropical showers. KL never tops 6/10: equatorial climate too hot-humid. Highlights: Petronas Twin Towers (skybridge on the 41st floor and observatory on the 86th, booking required), Batu Caves (272 steps, giant gilded statue of Murugan), KL Tower (360° view), Chinatown Petaling Street, Little India Brickfields. Hawker culture but less developed than in Singapore or Penang. Try nasi lemak (national dish), char kway teow, satay, durian (June-August season, very strong smell). KL is cheaper than Singapore: meals 4-8 RM ($1-$2) at hawkers, 4-star hotel $55-$110.
