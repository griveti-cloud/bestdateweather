Context

Our verdict

Tanzania, country in East Africa: 60 million inhabitants, official capital Dodoma (but Dar es Salaam remains the economic capital). The most geographically diverse country in East Africa: Kilimanjaro (19,300 ft, Africa's highest peak), Serengeti and Ngorongoro (UNESCO, the world's most famous safari parks), Indian coast with Zanzibar, Lake Tanganyika (Africa's deepest). August is my favorite month (9.6/10): 61-82°F, 0% rain, this is the ideal dry season, wildlife visible. From June to October, it's the safari high season: 59-88°F, 0-6% rain, blue sky, wildlife concentrated around water points (maximum visibility). From July-August, it's the Great Migration (1.5 million wildebeest cross from the Serengeti to the Masai Mara in Kenya, spectacular Mara River crossings with crocodiles). From March to May, it's the long rainy season: 14-47% rainfall, safari prices halved or thirded but difficult access (tracks), many lodges close. From November-December, it's the short rainy season: 17-57% rainfall. Regions to know: Serengeti National Park (UNESCO, 5,690 sq miles, plains as far as the eye can see, Great Migration, abundant predators), Ngorongoro Crater (UNESCO, the world's largest intact caldera 11 miles in diameter, complete closed-circuit ecosystem, record animal density), Tarangire (elephants, ancient baobabs), Lake Manyara (tree-climbing lions), Kilimanjaro National Park (UNESCO, 6-9 day ascent via 7 different routes, overall success rate 60%, up to 90% on the 8-day Lemosho route, altitude sickness the main challenge), Zanzibar (UNESCO Stone Town), Selous Game Reserve (UNESCO, Africa's largest hunting reserve but recently transformed into Nyerere National Park), Mafia Island (whale shark diving), Mwanza and Lake Victoria (Africa's largest lake). Try ugali, nyama choma, mishkaki, pilau, ndizi (banana), kahawa (Tanzanian coffee), Kilimanjaro Lager, Konyagi gin.
