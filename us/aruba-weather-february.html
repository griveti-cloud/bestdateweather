Context

Our verdict

Aruba is part of the ABC islands (Aruba-Bonaire-Curaçao): 20 miles long, 110,000 inhabitants, autonomous Dutch dependency, outside the hurricane zone (southern Caribbean). February-March are my favorite months (8.6/10): 77-84°F, 34-38% rain, steady trade winds, sea at 81°F. From September to December, expect 79-90°F with 28-65% rainfall: only period when showers are frequent (but often brief). From December to April, it's the American tourist high season: hotel prices x2-x3. The whole year is viable since it's outside the hurricane zone: a major advantage vs other Caribbean islands. Highlights: Eagle Beach (regularly ranked Caribbean beach #2-3 after Grace Bay), Palm Beach (livelier, hotels along the shore), Arikok National Park (40% of the island, desert landscapes with cacti, rock paintings, Natural Pool, Conchi), Oranjestad (colorful Dutch capital), Baby Beach (calm lagoon in the south), California Lighthouse, Alto Vista Chapel. Activities: kitesurf and windsurf at Fisherman's Hut (steady trade winds), diving (SS Antilla wreck), 4×4 in Arikok. Currency: AWG (florin) but USD accepted everywhere, papiamento (local creole) + Dutch + English + Spanish spoken. Try keshi yena, pastechi, balashi (local beer). Direct flight from Amsterdam (8h) or via Curaçao.
