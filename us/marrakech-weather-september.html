Context

Our verdict

Marrakech (the "red city" for its rammed-earth ramparts), Morocco's tourist capital: 950,000 inhabitants, UNESCO Medina, Sahara dunes 8h by car. May is my favorite month (9.3/10): 59-86°F, 12% rain, ideal temperatures. From July to August drop to 3.9/10 due to extreme heatwave (70-102°F, sometimes 117°F, feels like 122°F+): impossible to visit in the middle of the day, locals coming out after 9 PM. From December to February, expect 43-72°F: mild during the day but cool at night (riads aren't heated), good period for athletes (semi-marathon in January, Marrakech opens its winter golf season). Highlights: Place Jemaa el-Fna (UNESCO intangible heritage, snake charmers, storytellers, monkey handlers, transformed in the evening into a giant open-air restaurant), Souks (spices, leather, carpets, metals), Koutoubia Mosque (12th century, sister of Seville's Giralda), Bahia Palace (19th century, mosaics), Saadian Tombs, Ben Youssef Madrasa, Majorelle Garden (Yves Saint Laurent), Médersa Ben Youssef. Excursions: Atlas day trip (Ourika valley, Imlil, Toubkal ascent 13,700 ft), Essaouira 2h30 away (Atlantic coast, hippie atmosphere), Merzouga/Erg Chebbi desert 8h away (2-3 days by 4×4 with bivouac night). Try tagines, couscous (traditionally on Fridays), pastilla, mint tea.
