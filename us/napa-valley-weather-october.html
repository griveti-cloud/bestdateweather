Context

Our verdict

Napa Valley, in California: the most prestigious wine region in the USA, 50 miles north of San Francisco, 3 parallel valleys (Napa, Sonoma, Russian River), 400+ wineries. June is my favorite month (9.7/10): 55-86°F, 2% rain, ideal temperatures before the summer heatwave. From May to October, it's the tourist high season: 50-91°F, 0-11% rain. September-October = harvest season ("crush"), maximum activity in the wineries, but also fire season increasingly frequent since 2017 (major Atlas Fire 2017, Glass Fire 2020 burned entire wineries). From November to March, expect 41-63°F and 11-35% rain: quieter period, reduced hotel prices, tastings without crowds, green landscapes. Highlights: Wineries to visit: Castello di Amorosa (replica of an Italian castle, kitsch but photogenic), Robert Mondavi (modern Napa pioneer, died 2008), Opus One (Mondavi-Rothschild joint venture, prestige), Far Niente, Domaine Carneros (sparkling), Sterling Vineyards (cable car to reach the winery), Beringer (the oldest, 1876). Cabernet Sauvignon = the king grape of Napa (60% of production), Chardonnay and Pinot Noir to the south (cooler Carneros). Booking mandatory for tastings (50-200$/person, sometimes more for cult wines Screaming Eagle, Harlan, Scarecrow). Activities: Napa Valley Wine Train (gastronomic train 3-6h, 200-500$/person), hot air balloon at sunrise ($440), spa at Calistoga (geothermal springs, mud baths). Yountville = gastronomic village (French Laundry by Thomas Keller, 3*** Michelin restaurant among the most prestigious in the USA, 425$ menu). 1h30 from San Francisco.
