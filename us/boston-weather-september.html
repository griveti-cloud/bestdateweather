Context

Our verdict

Boston is the most European of the major American cities: cobblestones, red bricks, Harvard university in Cambridge. August stays my favorite month (8.8/10): 64-82°F, 34% rain, parks in full swing, Cape Cod beaches accessible. July is also excellent (8.6/10) with the 4th of July (fireworks over the Charles River). September-October are my real sweet spots: 48-73°F, spectacular New England foliage (peak mid-October, drive Vermont route from Boston). From December to March, expect 23/46°F: dry cold, frequent snow (blizzards possible), Boston Marathon on the 3rd Monday of April (Patriots' Day). Highlights: Freedom Trail (2.5 miles, 16 historic sites of the American Revolution), Faneuil Hall and Quincy Market, Beacon Hill (historic district with gas lamps), Harvard and MIT in Cambridge (campus visit), Fenway Park (the oldest active baseball stadium, 1912). Districts: North End (Italian cuisine, Mike's Pastry for cannoli), Back Bay, South End. Try clam chowder, lobster roll (at Neptune Oyster), Boston cream pie. Cape Cod and the islands (Martha's Vineyard, Nantucket) 2h away in summer.
