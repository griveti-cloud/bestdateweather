Context

Our verdict

Saalbach, Austrian ski resort in the Salzburg Land (Pinzgau region): 3200 inhabitants at 3,300 ft altitude in the heart of the Skicircus Saalbach Hinterglemm Leogang Fieberbrunn area = 170 miles of pistes linked on a single ski pass (one of the largest in Austria). Nicknamed "Home of Lässig" ("home of the cool") for its festive and welcoming atmosphere. August is my favorite month (7.8/10): 54-73°F, 53% rain, ideal temperatures for alpine hiking. June-July stay excellent (7.6/10). From June to September, it's the summer high season: 41-73°F, Salzburger Alpen hiking, mountain bike (Saalbach = Austrian mountain-bike capital with 125 miles of trails, World Cup VTT Descente every year), golf, paragliding. From December to April, expect 21/39°F and 42-65% precipitation often as snow: ski high season. Pistes: 170 miles Skicircus (Saalbach + Hinterglemm + Leogang + Fieberbrunn = 4 connected resorts, 70 lifts, 3rd largest connected area in Austria after Ski Amadé and Skiwelt Wilder Kaiser-Brixental), altitude 800-6,900 ft, vertical drop 4,300 ft, 170 miles of which 65 miles blue (family), 81 miles red (intermediate), 22 miles black (expert). iconic pistes: Schattberg West (3.7 miles descent, one of the longest), Zwölferkogel (the World Cup "Trophy" descent sometimes used for the Alpine Ski World Cup slalom), Wildalmgipfel (panorama 6,900 ft summit of the area). Off-piste: Saalbach has no world-renowned off-piste (unlike Engelberg or Verbier), but offers free riding accessible to intermediates between Schattberg and Limbergalm. Snowpark Nightpark Hinterglemm = one of the best in Austria, illuminated for night skiing. Legendary après-ski: Saalbach rivals Ischgl and Mayrhofen for the title of après-ski capital of Austria: Hinterhag Alm (institution), Goaßstall (on the pistes), Bauer's Schi Alm, King Bar, Bla Bla Bar, festive atmosphere until 22-11 PM in the Almen (altitude bars) then transfers to the village bars (Hannerl Hütte, Le Petit Sept, Ortbar) until 4-5am. Audience: Germans in the majority (60% of winter clientele), many Dutch, British, Hungarians, Czechs, few French. Hotels: Hotel Alpin Juwel (4*+), Hotel Bauer, Wellness-Resort Romantikhotel Die Hubertus, $165-$770/night in high season. Try Tiroler Gröstl, Käsespätzle, Kaiserschmarrn (the Saalbach version is served at Bauer's Schi Alm), Apfelstrudel, Glühwein, Stroh 80 (strong Austrian eau-de-vie). Logistics: Saalbach 1h30 from Salzburg airport (SZG) by car/bus, 2h30 from Munich airport (MUC), Salzburg-Saalfelden train + 30-min bus.
