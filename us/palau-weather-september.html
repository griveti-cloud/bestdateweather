Context

Our verdict

Palau is an archipelago of 340 islands in the north-west Pacific: 18,000 inhabitants, capital Ngerulmud (the least populated in the world). Independent state in free association with the USA since 1994. March is my favorite month (6.4/10): 77-82°F, 68% rain, relative dry season. Palau never tops 6.5/10: constant humid equatorial climate. From February to April, it's the relative dry season: 68-82% rainfall. From May to November, it's the wet season: 86-97% rainfall, heavy heat (77-84°F). No cyclonic season (Palau is outside the zone). Palau is world-renowned for diving: Rock Islands Southern Lagoon (UNESCO, marine landscape of 250 mushroom-shaped limestone islands covered with jungle), Blue Corner (one of the 5 best diving sites in the world, grey sharks in school), German Channel (manta rays), Jellyfish Lake (lake where one can swim among millions of non-stinging jellyfish). Activities: diving (700-1500$ for 4-7 days), kayaking in Rock Islands (day excursion 100-200$). Historic sites: WWII wrecks (Palau was a major battleground, Battle of Peleliu in 1944). Try coconut crab, fish stew, taro. Mandatory flight via Manila, Taipei or Tokyo. High total cost: $4,400-$6,600 for 8 days all-inclusive diving.
