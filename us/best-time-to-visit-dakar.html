Dakar, capital of Senegal: 1.1 million inhabitants (3 million in urban area), at the western tip of the African continent (westernmost point of continental Africa = Cap-Vert peninsula). West African hub (seat of several pan-African institutions, regional air hub). Considered one of the most stable and democratic capitals in West Africa (Senegal has never had a coup since independence 1960). January-December are my favorite months (9.1/10): 68-82°F, 1-2% rain, this is the dry season with refreshing trade winds from the Atlantic. From November to May, it's the tourist high season: 66-86°F, 0-1% rain, ideal dry and cool climate. From June to October, it's the rainy season ("hivernage"): 22-49% rainfall, but brief afternoon showers, stable temperatures 73-84°F. Highlights: Gorée Island (UNESCO 1978, 20 min by ferry from Dakar, former center of transatlantic slave trade 15th-19th century, House of Slaves with its "Door of No Return" poignant memorial — although historians debate the actual numerical importance of Gorée in the trade, the site remains symbolically major, visited by Obama 2013, Mandela, Pope Francis), Sandaga Market (the largest in Dakar), Plateau (administrative center and historic colonial), African Memory Cathedral (1936), Mosque of the Divinity (modern architecture 1973), African Renaissance Monument (160 ft statue inaugurated 2010 by Wade for the 50th anniversary of independence, one of the most controversial in Africa), Museum of Black Civilisations (2018), Soumbédioune Beach (fish market), Pink Lake / Lake Retba 19 miles away (natural pink-fuchsia water due to algae + salt, former arrival of the Paris-Dakar Rally 1979-2008). Excursions: Saint-Louis 4h north (UNESCO 2000, former French colonial capital), Saly and Petite Côte in the south (beach resorts), Casamance in the south (green region, totally different from the north, accessible by boat or plane). Try thieboudienne ("rice with fish" national dish), yassa with chicken, mafé (peanut sauce), bissap (hibiscus drink).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
