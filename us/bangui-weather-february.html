Context

Our verdict

Bangui, capital of the Central African Republic on the Ubangi river bordering the Democratic Republic of the Congo: 700,000 inhabitants. Country among the poorest and most unstable in sub-Saharan Africa = former French colony "Ubangi-Shari" 1903-1960, independence 1960, delirious dictatorship of Emperor Bokassa 1966-1979 (self-proclaimed emperor in 1976 during a coronation costing 22 million USD inspired by Napoleon, accused of cannibalism by opponents, overthrown by French operation "Barracuda" 1979), recurring civil wars 2003-2007 + 2012-2014 + chronic crises. Russian Wagner presence since 2018 against French influence. December is my favorite month (8.2/10): 70-88°F, 17% rain, this is the relative dry season. January-November stay excellent (8.0-8.1/10). From December to February, it's the dry season: 66-93°F, dry and hot climate with Saharan harmattan. From March to November, it's the equatorial humid season: 38-67% rainfall. Bangui highlights: Bangui Central Market (lively West African atmosphere), Notre-Dame Cathedral of Bangui (1937 French colonial heritage), Central Mosque of Bangui, KM5 Quarter ("Kilometer 5", traditional Muslim quarter become epicenter of inter-community violence 2013-2014, memorial atmosphere), Boganda National Museum (modest museum, Central African heritage), Boali Rapids 59 miles away (spectacular waterfall 160 ft high on the Mbali river, one of the most beautiful waterfalls in Central Africa, modest tourist iconic of CAR). Essential CAR excursions (inadvisable for security): Dzanga-Sangha National Park in the south-west (equatorial forest reserve bordering Cameroon-Congo, houses one of the largest populations of western lowland gorillas in the world + forest elephants + traditional Aka pygmy people, very difficult access), Manovo-Gounda St Floris National Park (UNESCO 1988, one of the largest national parks in Africa 6,720 sq miles, seriously devastated by massive poaching 1990-2010 = 80% of animals disappeared, in danger UNESCO 1997). Safety: absolutely inadvisable France Diplomacy for the entirety of CAR, extreme urban crime in Bangui, rival armed militias control 80% of the territory, formally inadvisable travel except experienced humanitarian missions with escort. Try ngounza (cassava leaf sauce), gozo (fermented cassava stick), Ubangi fish broth, mafé skewers, Central African Robusta coffee, local Mocaf beer. Bangui M'Poko airport (BGF).
