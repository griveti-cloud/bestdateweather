Context

Our verdict

Nantes, historic capital of the Duchy of Brittany on the Loire in France: 320,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 670,000 in the urban area. Former Breton capital (1532 reunion with France), today the chief town of Loire-Atlantique department in Pays-de-la-Loire region = sensitive Breton identity issue (Nantes residents often campaign for Loire-Atlantique to return to historic Brittany region). Dynamic French cultural capital = birthplace of Jules Verne 1828, European green capital 2013, dynamic university with 60,000 students. July is my favorite month (8.9/10): 57-77°F, 19% rain, ideal temperatures. June-August stay excellent (8.7-8.8/10). From May to September, it's the tourist high season: 48-77°F. From November to March, expect 34-55°F and 28-44% rain: mild for Atlantic-oceanic France (rare frost thanks to Atlantic proximity), ideal for visiting without crowds. Highlights: Castle of the Dukes of Brittany (13th-15th century, former residence of the Dukes of Brittany including Anne of Brittany born here in 1477 = duchess-queen, twice queen of France, houses the modern Nantes History Museum 2007 in 32 thematic rooms, iconic tourist attraction), Saint-Pierre-Saint-Paul Cathedral (15th-19th century flamboyant gothic, one of France's longest cathedrals 340 ft, monumental tomb of François II last Duke of Brittany signed Michel Colombe 1502 = masterpiece of French Renaissance), Passage Pommeraye (1843 shopping arcade on 3 levels with monumental staircase iconic of 19th-century Nantes, one of Europe's most beautiful shopping galleries, classified historic monument), Place Royale (18th-century neoclassical square with iconic fountain), Place du Bouffay (preserved medieval atmosphere), Quai de la Fosse (former slave-trading port of Nantes 18th century = Nantes was the leading French slave-trading port with 450,000 slaves deported to the West Indies, abolition of slavery memorial 2012 on the quay), Memorial to the Abolition of Slavery (work 2012 by Krzysztof Wodiczko + Julian Bonder = moving underground memorial under the quay with 2000 glass plaques naming the 1710 Nantes slave-trading expeditions 18th-19th century), Les Machines de l'Île = unique iconic tourist attraction worldwide (theme park-museum inspired by Jules Verne + Leonardo da Vinci, giant animal mechanical machines: Great Elephant 39 ft tall 8 tons rideable iconic + Carrousel des Mondes Marins ocean-themed carousel + Galerie des Machines, created 2007 by La Machine company), Jardin des Plantes (19th-century botanical park), Bouffay district (Nantes nightlife), Île de Versailles (Japanese garden on an islet in the Erdre). Excursions: Vannes and Carnac 1h30 away (Brittany, already covered by BDW), Saint-Malo 1h45 away (already covered), La Baule 1h away (iconic beach resort of the French Atlantic coast), Pornic and Le Croisic (Jade coast), Pays de la Loire châteaux Angers 1h, Saumur 2h, Tours 2h30. Voyage à Nantes = annual summer contemporary art trail (July-August), green line traced on the ground links the works scattered through the city, iconic Nantes tourist experience. Try beurre blanc nantais (iconic sauce invented in Nantes 1890), pike with beurre blanc, Petit-Beurre LU (biscuit created in Nantes by Lefèvre-Utile 1886), Muscadet AOC (local iconic white wine with Grenoble sole), curé nantais (cheese), Berlingot nantais (iconic sweet), Folle Blanche Cognac. Nantes-Atlantique airport (NTE).
