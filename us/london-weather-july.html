What to expect in July

Summer in London brings pleasant temperatures (22.0°F) and long sunny days (11.9h of sunshine). Expect rain on 34.0% of days — a light raincoat in the bag is recommended. A good balance between weather and crowds, even if August (8.3/10) remains theoretically better.

Context

Our verdict

London works almost all year round despite its reputation: July-August are my favorite months (8.2-8.3/10), 57-72°F, 28-34% rain, parks in full swing (Hyde Park, Hampstead Heath), Wimbledon early July, BBC Proms. From December to February, expect 36-50°F and 37-40% rain, days closing at 4 PM. Paradoxically the best museum season (free, half the visitors): British Museum, Tate Modern, V&A, National Gallery. Christmas markets at Hyde Park Winter Wonderland. May-June and September are the real sweet spots: weather often mild, tourist crowds less dense than in midsummer. The Soho pubs, Borough Market, Albert Hall concerts, West End musicals stay viable all year round. Brexit reminder: passport now required, no ID card.
