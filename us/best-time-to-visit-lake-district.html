Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Lake District, mountainous region in north-west England in Cumbria: 40,000 inhabitants over 890 sq miles, national park since 1951, UNESCO 2017 for its cultural landscape. Region of the English Romantic poets: William Wordsworth was born here (1770) and lived here, along with Samuel Taylor Coleridge and Robert Southey. Beatrix Potter lived here and created Peter Rabbit. England's highest peak: Scafell Pike (3,200 ft). 16 main lakes including Windermere (the longest lake in England 11 miles). May-July are my favorite months (7.0/10): 41-59°F, 47-62% rain. The Lake District never tops 7.0/10 (extremely rainy climate = one of the wettest regions in the UK, 3000-4000 mm/year in the steep valleys vs 600 mm in London, the European region with the highest precipitation at altitude). From June to August, expect 46-59°F: tourist high season (British school holidays), hikers, lively pubs, but unpredictable weather. From November to March, expect 32-43°F and 56-64% rain: damp cold, possible snow on the fells, many pubs and farms stay open for winter hikers. Highlights: Lake Windermere (steamboat cruises from Bowness-on-Windermere), Lake Ullswater (the 2nd longest, the most picturesque according to Wordsworth who wrote his poem Daffodils after seeing it), Lake Derwent Water (around Keswick, ferries between 7 jetties on the lake), Lake Coniston Water, Lake Buttermere (4.3 miles loop walk), Wastwater (the deepest and wildest). Villages: Ambleside, Grasmere (Wordsworth's Dove Cottage his home 1799-1808), Hawkshead (Beatrix Potter Gallery), Keswick (the main town in the north of the park), Bowness-on-Windermere. iconic hikes: Scafell Pike (3,200 ft, England's highest peak, 5-6h return ascent, dangerous weather), Helvellyn (3,100 ft via Striding Edge, the famously vertiginous ridge), Catbells (the family hike par excellence, 3h from Keswick). Hill Top Farm (Beatrix Potter's house at Near Sawrey, kept as it was). Safety: British mountains underestimated, weather changing in 30 min, frequent thick fog, several deaths each year of poorly equipped hikers. Ordnance Survey maps + compass mandatory on the fells. Try Cumberland sausage (regional spiral sausage), Kendal mint cake (energy bar for hikers since 1869, taken by Hillary to the summit of Everest in 1953), sticky toffee pudding (locally claimed origin), Cumbrian ales (Jennings Brewery, Hesket Newmarket). Logistics: train from London Euston to Oxenholme (3h) then branch line to Windermere or Penrith.

How we calculate the score

For mountain destinations, we calculate two scores per month — skiing and hiking — and display the higher of the two.

⛷️Ski score DEC → MAY
  • 40% snow cover (slope T° corrected by lapse rate -3.6°F/1000ft)
  • 40% winter thermal comfort (23–41°F optimal)
  • 20% sunshine
  • Off-season penalty: ×0.70 adjacent months · ×0.35 outside season
🥾Hiking score JUN → SEP
  • 40% temperature (50–72°F optimal)
  • 40% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 20% sunshine

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Lake District?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: January (ski score 7.6/10). For hiking/suiner: January (7.6/10, 3.0°F).
Can you ski in Lake District in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.2/10. Cold temperatures (3°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Lake District in suiner?
In January, temperatures average 3.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Lake District?
October is the wettest month with October has the lowest climate score of the year.
