Context

Our verdict

Lake Garda ("Lago di Garda"), the largest lake in Italy: 140 sq miles, 98 miles circumference, in the shape of an inverted Y between Veneto/Lombardy/Trentino. More accessible and less jet-set than Lake Como, more family-friendly and sporty. June-August are my favorite months (8.6-8.8/10): 57-84°F, 36-37% rain, swimming in the lake (water at 75°F in midsummer), windsurf and kitesurf (steady wind Pelér from the north in the morning and Ora from the south in the afternoon). From November to March, expect 37-57°F and 24-36% rain: mild for northern Italy, Limone lemon trees still green, skiing possible 30 min away (Monte Baldo, Madonna di Campiglio, Folgaria). Regions: South (Sirmione, Desenzano, Peschiera) flat, warmer, livelier, easy access from Verona/Milan; North (Riva del Garda, Limone sul Garda) mountainous, alpine, watersports, narrower lake (fjord effect). Highlights: Sirmione (Scaliger fortified peninsula, hot springs, Grottoes of Catullus = Roman ruins), Riva del Garda (Trentino side), Limone sul Garda (lemon groves, its limonaie = stone greenhouses), Malcesine (cable car to Monte Baldo 5,800 ft), Bardolino (red wines), Desenzano (lively port). Gardaland (the largest theme park in Italy, at Castelnuovo del Garda). Activities: sailing, windsurf, kitesurf at Torbole, Monte Baldo hiking, lake-route cycling. Try tortelli di zucca (pumpkin ravioli), risotto al tastasal (sausage), bigoli, Bardolino/Lugana wine, lake olive oil.
