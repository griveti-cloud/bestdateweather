Context

Our verdict

Sapa, mountain station in northern Vietnam: 9000 inhabitants at 4,900 ft altitude in the Tonkin Alps, base for the famous terraced rice paddies of Mường Hoa and Mount Fansipan (10,300 ft, the "Roof of Indochina"). Significant minority population (Black Hmong, Red Dzao, Tay), ethnic markets. April is my favorite month (7.6/10): 59-72°F, 59% rain, clear skies, mild mountain temperatures. From March to May, it's the tourist high season: 54-73°F, often clear skies, paddies at the fluorescent-green growth stage. From September to November is the 2nd high season: 54-72°F, clear skies, golden pre-harvest paddies (the famous photos with yellow gradients). From June to August, it's the rainy season: 78-95% rainfall, frequent fog hiding the view, but the lushest landscapes. From December to February, expect 46-63°F: cold for Vietnam, rare possibility of snow at the summit of Fansipan. Highlights: Mường Hoa Valley (spectacular terraced rice paddies on 5-10 levels, hike Cat Cat → Lao Chai → Ta Van 7 miles on foot), Mount Fansipan (cable car opened 2016, 20,600 ft of cable, 15 min from Sapa, vs 2-3 days of trekking previously), Bac Ha Market (on Sundays, 2h from Sapa, one of the most colorful in northern Vietnam, flower-Hmong minorities). Trek with a local guide (usually a young Hmong woman) in the villages: Cat Cat (Black Hmong), Ta Phin (Red Dzao), Lao Chai (Black Hmong), Ta Van (Giay). Homestay possible ($11-$28/night with meals). Try thắng cố (traditional Hmong soup made from organs), cá hồi (trout), fresh altitude vegetables, local rice alcohol (rượu cần). Logistics: Sapa 6h by bus from Hanoi via the motorway, or overnight train to Lao Cai + 1h bus.
