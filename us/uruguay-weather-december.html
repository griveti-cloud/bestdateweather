Context

Our verdict

Uruguay, the smallest Spanish-speaking South American country: 3.5 million inhabitants on 68,000 sq miles, squeezed between Argentina and Brazil. Considered one of the most stable, democratic and progressive countries in Latin America (first country to legalise gay marriage and cannabis). Temperate climate without extremes (reversed seasons of the southern hemisphere). January is my favorite month (9.0/10): 68-79°F, 27% rain, this is the austral summer, beaches of Punta del Este and José Ignacio in full effervescence with Argentinians and Brazilians on holiday (prices x3-x5 on the coast in January-February). From December to March, it's the tourist high season: 64-79°F, 26-36% rain. From April to May and September-November are the best shoulder seasons: 52-72°F, mild climate. From June to August, it's the austral winter: 48-59°F, 25-29% rain, few tourists, but Montevideo stays pleasant. Regions to know: Montevideo (capital, see dedicated review), Punta del Este ("South American Saint-Tropez", smart beach resort since the 60s, casinos, peninsula between Atlantic and Rio de la Plata, Brava and Mansa beaches, Casapueblo by Carlos Paez Vilaró Uruguayan architect, prices x3-x5 in January-February when the Argentinians migrate en masse), José Ignacio (30 min from Punta del Este, more discreet and smarter, haunt of magnates and celebrities, La Mansa beach), Colonia del Sacramento (UNESCO, 17th-century Portuguese colonial town on the Rio de la Plata, accessible in 1h by ferry from Buenos Aires Argentina), Cabo Polonio (isolated village without official electricity or road, accessible only by 4x4 on the beach, bohemian/hippie atmosphere, sea lion colonies), Punta del Diablo (bohemian surf fishing village), Carmelo (Uruguayan vineyards, Tannat iconic grape). Try chivito ("national sandwich" beef-ham-cheese-egg-bacon), parrillada (Uruguayan asado), dulce de leche, mate (national shared drink, more consumed than in Argentina), Tannat wine (emblem Uruguayan grape).
