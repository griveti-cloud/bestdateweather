Context

Our verdict

Abuja, capital of Nigeria in the center of the country: 800,000 inhabitants intra-muros, 3 million in urban area. Planned capital built ex-nihilo in the 1980s-1991 (previous capital = Lagos south coast) for geopolitical reasons (neutral central position between the 3 majority ethnicities Yoruba south-west + Igbo south-east + Hausa north, more accessible, more defendable). Crescent urban plan inspired by Brasilia + Canberra, calmer and cleaner atmosphere than chaotic Lagos. Official capital since 1991. November is my favorite month (8.2/10): 66-90°F, 9% rain, this is the ideal dry season + refreshing Saharan harmattan. January-February-March-October-December stay excellent (6.1-7.3/10). From November to February, it's the dry season: 66-93°F, pleasant dry and hot climate. From March to May, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 73-97°F. From June to September, it's the West African monsoon: 47-78% rainfall. Highlights: Aso Rock ("Rock of Aso", 1,300 ft granite monolith dominating Abuja, iconic view of the capital, houses the presidential residence Aso Villa at its foot), Abuja National Mosque (1984, one of the largest mosques in sub-Saharan Africa, iconic golden dome and 4 minarets), Our Lady of Assumption National Cathedral ("Our Lady Queen of Nigeria", large modern Catholic cathedral 2005), Eagle Square (official central square where official state ceremonies take place), Millennium Park (the largest public park in Abuja, opened in 2003 by Queen Elizabeth II), Jabi Lake (recreational urban lake), Zuma Rock 1h away (other 2,400 ft granite monolith iconic of Abuja landscapes, more impressive than Aso Rock), Wuse Market, Ushafa Pottery Village (traditional Yoruba-Igbo potter craftsman village). Nigeria excursions: Lagos 1h flight or 8h by bus (economic capital 21 million inhabitants in urban area = the largest sub-Saharan African megacity, already covered by BDW), Yankari National Park 5h away (the most accessible park in Nigeria, elephants + lions + thermal Wikki Warm Springs), Calabar in the south-east (renowned for its annual December carnival, one of the largest carnivals in Africa, festive atmosphere). Safety: Abuja globally safer than Lagos but increased France Diplomacy vigilance: Boko Haram Islamist terrorism active in the north-east of the country (Borno + Yobe + Adamawa states near Lake Chad), tourist kidnappings possible, moderate urban crime in Abuja, attacks in Abuja in 2014 + 2022 targeting shopping centers and bus stations. Try jollof rice (West African iconic, eternal culinary dispute Nigeria vs Ghana vs Senegal for the best version), egusi soup (melon seed soup), suya (spicy chilli + peanut skewers iconic of the Hausa), pounded yam, pepper soup, palm wine, Star Lager + Gulder beer (Nigerian beers). Abuja Nnamdi Azikiwe airport (ABV).
