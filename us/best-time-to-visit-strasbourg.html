Strasbourg, capital of Alsace and seat of the European Parliament: 280,000 inhabitants, Franco-German cultural mix (annexed 4 times in 75 years between 1870 and 1945). July is my favorite month (8.7/10): 61-79°F, 38% rain, terraces in Petite-France, cruises on the Ill. August excellent (8.6/10). From November to March, expect 32-48°F and 36-43% rain or snow: continental cold, but this is the tourist apogee with the Christmas Market (Christkindelsmärik) from the last Friday in November to 24 December = the oldest in France (1570) and one of the largest in Europe. Hotel prices x3 during this period. Highlights: Notre-Dame Cathedral (470 ft, the world's tallest in the 15th century, astronomical clock, 332-step platform for the view), Petite-France (UNESCO, half-timbered houses on the canals), Palais Rohan, European quarter (Parliament, Council of Europe). Try choucroute (at Chez Yvonne or Maison Kammerzell), tarte flambée ("flammkueche"), bredele at the Christmas markets, Alsace wine (Riesling, Gewurztraminer). Alsace Wine Route 30 min away, Colmar 30 min by train.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
