What to expect in August

Context

Our verdict

Dili, capital of East Timor (Timor-Leste) on the north coast of the island of Timor in South East Asia: 280,000 inhabitants. Youngest country in Asia = independence on 20 May 2002 after 24 years of bloody Indonesian occupation 1975-1999 (200,000 deaths or 1/3 of the Timorese population by massacres-famine-violence) then UN administration 1999-2002. Catholic country at 96% (heritage of Portuguese colonisation 1515-1975) = only Catholic country in Asia with the Philippines, official languages Tetum + Portuguese. August is my favorite month (8.9/10): 70-88°F, 4% rain, this is the ideal dry season. June-July-September stay excellent (8.7-8.9/10). From May to November, it's the dry season "bailoro": 66-90°F, sea at 81°F, dry and cool tropical climate thanks to south-east trade winds, this is the relative tourist high season. From December to April, it's the wet season "roromata": 33-72% rainfall, but stable temperatures 75-90°F. Dili highlights: Cristo Rei de Dili (Christ-King of Dili, 89 ft high Christ statue on panoramic hill 260 ft overlooking Dili Bay, copy inspired by Christ the Redeemer Rio de Janeiro but offered by Indonesia 1996 during the occupation as a controversial "reconciliation" symbol, accessible by iconic 580 steps, exceptional panorama over Dili + north coast), Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception (1989 opened by John Paul II during historic 1989 visit = encouragement to independence in the context of Indonesian occupation), Tais Market ("tais" = traditional Timorese textile iconic cultural, hand-woven fabric with geometric patterns unique to each village, UNESCO intangible heritage classification 2021), Resistance Museum (museum of Timorese resistance against Indonesian occupation 1975-1999, overwhelming memorial atmosphere, photos of massacre victims), Santa Cruz Cemetery ("Santa Cruz cemetery" site of the Santa Cruz massacre of 12 November 1991 = 250+ young Timorese massacred by the Indonesian army during the funeral of independence activist, filmed by British journalist Max Stahl = video that alerted the whole world to the Timorese genocide and triggered international solidarity = major memorial place), Statue of Nicolau Lobato (independence hero assassinated 1978), Areia Branca Beach ("white beach" urban beach of Dili with golden sand, peaceful local atmosphere). Timor excursions: Atauro Island 25 min by boat from Dili ("most biodiverse island in the world" according to Conservation International 2016 with 643 fish species discovered at the foot of the beaches = top world for diving and snorkelling, traditional insular atmosphere), Maubisse 3h away in the central mountains 4,600 ft (cool temperate climate, Timor-Leste coffee = one of the most prestigious Arabica coffees in Asia, plantations to visit), Mount Ramelau (highest point 9,800 ft, 4h round trip hike for sunrise at the summit), Baucau 2nd city (preserved Portuguese colonial atmosphere), Jaco Island (extreme east, sacred uninhabited island by Tetum tradition, paradise beach accessible 30 min by boat from Tutuala). Activities: diving and snorkelling (Atauro top world), coffee tour (Timor-Leste = one of the world's coffees sought by chef roasters), Mount Ramelau hiking, humpback whale watching (October-December, migration route). Try ikan saboko (iconic grilled fish in banana leaf), batar daan (corn cooked with beans), Timorese feijoada (Portuguese heritage), Timor-Leste coffee, imported Portuguese vinho ("tradition"), local Tiger Beer. Dili Presidente Nicolau Lobato airport (DIL), flights from Bali Denpasar (1h30) + Darwin Australia (1h) + Singapore (3h30).
