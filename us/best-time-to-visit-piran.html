Piran, port town of Slovenia on the Adriatic coast: 4000 inhabitants at the extreme south-west of the country (Slovenia has only 29 miles of coast). Former Venetian possession 1283-1797, preserved Venetian atmosphere with near-intact 13th-18th century architecture ("Slovenian pearl of the Adriatic"), nicknamed "little Venice" despite its modest size. Birthplace of the baroque composer Giuseppe Tartini (1692-1770, virtuoso Devil's Trill Sonata). July-August are my favorite months (9.1-9.2/10): 66-82°F, 22-27% rain, sea at 75°F, ideal temperatures. June stays excellent (9.1/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 57-82°F, large crowds (Slovenes and Croats on weekend, Italians thanks to Trieste proximity 30 min, Germans, Austrians, hotel prices x2-x3). From November to March, expect 37-59°F and 30-44% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, many establishments close, unique off-season atmosphere. Highlights: Tartini Square (Tartinijev Trg, marble main square dedicated to Giuseppe Tartini with his statue 1896, Venetian atmosphere with 19th-century town hall and 18th-century Tartini palace), Saint George's Cathedral (Sv Jurij, 14th-17th century Venetian baroque perched on 230 ft hill, signature panorama over Piran and the Adriatic at sunset), Punta of Piran (extreme tip of the peninsula where the sea meets the sea, lighthouse and church Saint Mary of Salute 1637), 1st of May Square (Trg 1 Maja, former main medieval square before Tartini replaced it, 18th-century cistern in the center), Venetian House (Casa Veneziana, 14th-century medieval house with inscription "LASA PUR DIR" = "let them speak" engraved by a Venetian to his mistress to silence the rumours), Marine Museum (Sergej Mašera Pomorski Muzej, 17th-century palace, maritime history of Piran), Medieval ramparts (30 min walk from the center, 360° panorama over Piran and the bay), Piran beaches (essentially rocky with concrete ramps, no sand as in most of the northern Adriatic, but crystalline water). Excursions: Sečovlje Salt Pans 5 miles away (nature park traditional salt pans still in operation since the 13th-century Middle Ages, "Piran salt" harvest technique preserved by craftsmen, therapeutic spa with local brines Lepa Vida), Portoroz 2.5 miles away ("Port of Roses", more lively modern beach resort, casinos, equipped beaches), Strunjan Nature Reserve (the highest flysch cliffs of the Adriatic 260 ft), Trieste Italy 30 min away (already covered by BDW), Croatian Istrian coast Pula/Rovinj 1-2h away, Postojna Cave 1h away, Lipica 30 min away (Lipizzan white horse stud farm since 1580), Ljubljana 1h30 away. Try Adriatic seafood (Piran is one of the rare Slovenian towns for traditional seafood), brodet (Croatian-Slovenian fish stew), Piran olive oil (the best in Slovenia), Piran salt (gourmet gifts), Refošk (local Istrian red wine), Malvazija (white wine). Logistics: Piran 1h30 from Ljubljana by car, 30 min from Trieste, 2h30 from Venice.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
