Context

Our verdict

Malabo, capital of Equatorial Guinea on the island of Bioko in the Gulf of Guinea: 250,000 inhabitants. Geographical particularity unique in the world = the capital is on an island (Bioko) 155 miles from the African continent where the rest of the country is located, situation inherited from Spanish colonisation where Bioko = former Fernando Pó important island for cocoa + slavery 16th-19th century. Only Spanish-speaking country in sub-Saharan Africa = former Spanish colony 1778-1968 (with Western Sahara), official languages Spanish + French + Portuguese + Bantu African languages. Ultra-rich country thanks to offshore oil since 1995 (highest African GDP per capita) but massive corruption under President Teodoro Obiang Nguema (in power since 1979 = longest reign in the world after Queen Elizabeth II, family dictatorship, international controversy). January is my favorite month (8.6/10): 77-82°F, 65% rain, this is the relative dry season. December-February stay excellent (7.6-8.4/10). Malabo never tops 8.6/10 (extremely humid equatorial climate year-round). From December to March, it's the relative dry season: 77-84°F, refreshing Saharan harmattan in January-February. From April to November, it's the equatorial humid season: 80-95% rainfall (Malabo is one of the most watered African capitals with 2000 mm/year), Pico Basile volcano visible 9,900 ft highest point Bioko. Malabo highlights: Place de la Indépendance (central square with preserved 19th-20th century Spanish colonial architecture), Saint Elizabeth Cathedral (1916 Spanish colonial heritage neo-Gothic), Presidential Palace of Malabo (President Obiang's residence), Central Market of Malabo, Arena Blanca Beach ("white sand beach", contrasting with typical Bioko volcanic black sand), Pico Basile 9,900 ft (dormant volcano, highest point Bioko + Equatorial Guinea, 2-day hike for exceptional panorama over the island + Gulf of Guinea + island of São Tomé visible), Lake Biao (volcanic crater lake in the south of Bioko), Arena Negra Beach (volcanic black sand beach iconic of Bioko). Equatorial Guinea excursions: Bata 1h flight (economic capital on the continent, more traditional sub-Saharan African atmosphere), Monte Alén National Park on the continent (preserved equatorial jungle with gorillas + chimpanzees + forest elephants + drills + mandrills, very difficult access), Bata beaches (continental Atlantic). Safety: Malabo and Equatorial Guinea = among the most difficult African countries to access for tourism (very restrictive visa, multiple authorisations required, journalism forbidden, photography of official buildings strictly forbidden prison sentences), moderate urban crime but ambient political repression, inadvisable travel except oil business. Try succotash (Africanised Spanish fricassee), chicken in moambé (neighbouring Congo heritage), sopa de pescado (fish soup Spanish heritage), yam fufu, malanga, Equato-Guinean Robusta coffee, imported Spanish wines. Malabo Saint-Isabel airport (SSG).
