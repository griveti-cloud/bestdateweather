Vaduz, capital of Liechtenstein (Principality of Liechtenstein): 5500 inhabitants. 4th smallest country in Europe after Vatican, Monaco, San Marino = 62 sq miles, 40,000 inhabitants in total, monarchical constitutional principality ruled by the House of Liechtenstein since 1719 (one of the oldest dynasties in Europe still reigning, originating from Lower Austria). Alpine country enclaved between Switzerland and Austria, no own currency (uses Swiss franc), no airport (the closest: Zurich), only accessible by car-train-bus from Switzerland. Historic tax haven (eased since 2009). June-August are my favorite months (7.7/10): 57-75°F, 56-63% rain, ideal temperatures for alpine hiking. From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 52-75°F. From December to March, expect 27/52°F and 45-50% precipitation often as snow: skiing possible at Malbun (5,200 ft, modest 14 miles of pistes only). Highlights: Vaduz Castle (Schloss Vaduz, perched on cliff 390 ft above the town, official residence of the reigning Prince Hans-Adam II and his family, 12th-17th century castle, closed to the public as private residence but visible from outside, one of the last castles in Europe still really inhabited by a royal family), Center of Vaduz ("Städtle", pedestrian street with town hall, LGT Bank, restaurants, duty-free boutiques, smart small town atmosphere), Liechtenstein Art Museum (Kunstmuseum Liechtenstein, opened 2000, discrete black-grey cubic architecture, modern and contemporary art collection including works lent by the princes of Liechtenstein — their private collection is one of the most important in the world), National Museum of Liechtenstein (history of the principality), National Treasure (Schatzkammer, opened 2015, princely regalia + diplomatic gifts + unique works of art from the princely collection on rotating display), Saint Florin Cathedral (1873 neo-Gothic), Red House (Rotes Haus, iconic medieval house with red façade), Tourist Office (where you can have your passport stamped for 3 CHF = collector souvenir). Activities: Liechtenstein Trail (hiking trail crossing the whole country over 47 miles in 5 stages, one of the unique tourist particularities of Liechtenstein), Malbun 30 min away (only ski resort in the country, modest but spectacular landscapes), Triesenberg (Walser mountain village with unique dialect, hikes). Excursions: Zurich 1h away (Switzerland), Innsbruck 1h30 away (Austria, already covered by BDW). Try Käsknöpfle (iconic alpine cheese gnocchi), Ribel (corn semolina), Rebstöcke wines from Liechtenstein (microscopic production, princely AOC), Vaduzer Eistee (local iced tea), kirsch cherry eau-de-vie. Logistics: Vaduz accessible by bus from Sargans (Switzerland, 30 min from Zurich by train + 25 min bus), no direct rail connection.

How we calculate the score

For mountain destinations, we calculate two scores per month — skiing and hiking — and display the higher of the two.

⛷️Ski score DEC → MAY
  • 40% snow cover (slope T° corrected by lapse rate -3.6°F/1000ft)
  • 40% winter thermal comfort (23–41°F optimal)
  • 20% sunshine
  • Off-season penalty: ×0.70 adjacent months · ×0.35 outside season
🥾Hiking score JUN → SEP
  • 40% temperature (50–72°F optimal)
  • 40% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 20% sunshine

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Vaduz?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: January (ski score 8.5/10). For hiking/suiner: January (8.5/10, 6.0°F).
Can you ski in Vaduz in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.9/10. Cold temperatures (6°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Vaduz in suiner?
In January, temperatures average 6.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Vaduz?
November is the wettest month with November has the lowest climate score of the year.
