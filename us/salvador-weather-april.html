Context

Our verdict

Salvador (de Bahia) is the African soul of Brazil: 3 million inhabitants, Brazil's 1st capital (until 1763), Afro-Brazilian heritage (capoeira, candomblé, bossa nova). October is my favorite month (8.2/10): 75-82°F, 54% rain, end of the wet season. From May to August (austral winter), expect 72-79°F and 71-80% rain: often grey skies, rough sea, but bearable temperatures and reduced prices. From December to March (austral summer), expect 77-84°F: tropical heat, sea at 82°F, but also Salvador Carnival (the world's largest in terms of people, 6 days in February, Olodum/Ilê Aiyê Afro blocos in the streets, prices x4). Highlights: Pelourinho (UNESCO, colorful colonial old town, Largo do Pelourinho), Igreja de São Francisco (rococo covered in gold, one of Latin America's most beautiful), Elevador Lacerda (240 ft lift between upper and lower town), Mercado Modelo (Afro-Brazilian crafts). Beaches: Porto da Barra (urban), Ondina, Stella Maris (further away). Itacaré and Morro de São Paulo 2-4h away for wild beaches. Safety: Pelourinho touristy by day but demands vigilance at night, avoid visible bags, don't venture into the favelas without a guide. Try moqueca de peixe, acarajé (fried black-eyed pea balls), feijoada, caipirinha. Capoeira and candomblé in regular public performances.
