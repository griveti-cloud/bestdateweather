Context

Our verdict

Grandvalira, largest ski area of Andorra and the Pyrenees: 130 miles of pistes connecting 6 resorts in the eastern Pyrenees, on the Franco-Spanish border. Andorra = principality co-ruled by the French president and the Catalan bishop of Urgell, one of the last vestiges of the European feudal system. July is my favorite month (7.7/10): 48-70°F, 36% rain, ideal temperatures for alpine hiking. From June to September, it's the summer high season: 39-70°F, Pyrenees hiking, mountain biking, paragliding. From December to April, expect 19/39°F and 39-49% precipitation often snow: this is the ski high season. Pistes: 130 miles Grandvalira (130 miles, one of the largest ski areas in the Pyrenees), 6 connected sectors: Pas de la Casa (French gateway, 6,900 ft, the highest), Grau Roig, Soldeu (the smartest), El Tarter, Canillo, Encamp, altitude 1710-8,700 ft, drop 3,100 ft, 130 pistes, 67 lifts including 4 gondolas. Legendary pistes: Avet (Alpine Skiing World Cup descent giant slalom, one of the most difficult on the circuit), Slalom at Soldeu. Assets: prices much cheaper than the Alps (lift passes $55-$66/day vs $77-$99 in France/Switzerland, hotels 30-50% cheaper), Andorran duty-free = alcohol and tobacco very cheap, snow conditions guaranteed thanks to high altitude and excellent artificial snow making. Public: Spanish (Catalans and Madrilenians) in the majority, French from the south (Toulousains, Montpelliérains, Bordelais), numerous British thanks to Barcelona/Toulouse flights + bus, few Italians or Germans. Pas de la Casa = the French border sector, very international festive atmosphere, low prices, cheap accommodation. Soldeu = the smart sector with 4-5* hotels (Hermitage 5* overlooking the village). Andorra principality sites: Andorra-la-Vella capital (highest European capital in altitude 3,400 ft, old town Casa de la Vall = Andorran parliament 16th century), Caldea (the largest thermal spa in South-Western Europe, 64,600 sq ft of thermal water at 158°F, iconic of Andorra, spectacular night illuminations), Madriu-Perafita-Claror Valley (UNESCO 2004, preserved Pyrenean cultural landscape, 9% of Andorra's territory), Meritxell Sanctuary (patroness of Andorra, basilica rebuilt 1976 by Ricardo Bofill after fire). Try escudella (Andorran stew, national dish), trinxat (potato cabbage gratin), cunillo (rabbit), embotits (Pyrenean charcuterie), Vins du Pirineu (rare Andorran appellation, altitude vineyards 3,900 ft+). Logistics: Grandvalira 3h by car from Barcelona, Toulouse, or by shuttle from airport (no airport in Andorra).

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Grandvalira?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 9.2/10). For hiking/suiner: March (9.2/10, 1.0°F).
Can you ski in Grandvalira in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.8/10. Cold temperatures (0°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Grandvalira in suiner?
In March, temperatures average 1.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Grandvalira?
November is the wettest month with November has the lowest climate score of the year.
