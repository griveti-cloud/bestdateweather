Context

Our verdict

Zurich, Switzerland's largest city: 430,000 inhabitants, 1.5 million in the urban area, the country's economic and financial capital (worldwide HQ of UBS, Credit Suisse until the 2023 merger, Swiss Re, Zurich Insurance), regularly ranked among the cities with the highest standard of living in the world. On the shore of Lake Zurich with views of the Alps on clear days. August is my favorite month (8.3/10): 59-77°F, 40% rain, ideal temperatures. June-July stay excellent (8.1-8.2/10). From April to September, it's the tourist high season: 39-77°F. From November to March, expect 28/46°F and 41-46% rain: winter cold, possible snow (Zurich gets 30-50 cm/year cumulatively), frequent fog over the lake ("Hochnebel" thermal inversion), Christmas market on Bahnhofstrasse. Highlights: Bahnhofstrasse (one of the world's most expensive shopping avenues, 1 miles, Swiss banks, luxury watches, jewellers), Old town Altstadt (medieval alleys on both banks of the Limmat, in the city center, very well preserved), Grossmünster (12th-century Romanesque cathedral with 2 towers, site of the Zurich Protestant Reformation by Huldrych Zwingli in 1519, panoramic view from one tower), Fraumünster (9th-century convent, stained glass by Marc Chagall 1970), Saint Peter (Europe's largest clock face, 29 ft diameter), Kunsthaus Zurich (one of Switzerland's most important art museums, David Chipperfield extension 2021), Landesmuseum (Swiss national museum), Lake Zurich and Quai promenade (in summer swimming in the lake from the historic Badis = public baths, Seebad Enge the most famous), Niederdorf district ("Dörfli", medieval nightlife quarter), Kreis 5 and Zurich-West (former industrial districts converted to trendy, gastronomy, art, Frau Gerolds Garten = cult urban garden, Viaduktbögen under viaduct arches), Uetliberg (2,900 ft, Zurich's home mountain, train from HB in 25 min, panorama over the city and lake to the Alps). Excursions: Lucerne 1h away, Rhine Falls 45 min away (Europe's most powerful at Schaffhausen), Lake Constance 1h away, Stein am Rhein. Try Zürcher Geschnetzeltes (sliced veal in cream-mushroom sauce, iconic Zurich dish), rösti, half-and-half fondue, raclette, Birchermüesli (invented in Zurich by Dr Bircher-Benner 1900), Sprüngli Swiss chocolate (no S, the most prestigious in Zurich). Zurich airport (ZRH), one of Europe's most modern, 12 min by train from the center.
