Ipoh, capital of Perak state in north-western peninsular Malaysia: 660,000 inhabitants in urban area, 3rd largest Malaysian city after Kuala Lumpur and George Town Penang. Former tin mining center 19th-20th century when Malaysia was the world's leading tin producer, preserved British colonial architectural heritage + unique Sino-Malay atmosphere thanks to massive 19th-century Chinese immigration for the mines. Town in tourist renaissance 2010-2025 thanks to iconic street food + traditional café. February is my favorite month (6.9/10): 72-90°F, 19% rain, this is the relative dry season. Ipoh never tops 6.9/10 (humid hot equatorial climate year-round). From January to March-June-July-December, these are the best seasons: 72-91°F, moderate humid tropical climate. From April to May-October-November, it's the wet season: 36-43% rainfall. Highlights: Ipoh Old Town ("Old Town", preserved 19th-20th century British colonial architecture with Sino-Malay atmosphere, peranakan colorful shophouse façades, "Mural Art Trail" mural art since 2014 by Lithuanian artist Ernest Zacharevic), Concubine Lane (Lorong Panglima, picturesque alley peranakan shophouses converted into trendy boutiques-cafés-restaurants since 2015), Birch Memorial Clock Tower (1909, tribute to British resident J.W.W. Birch assassinated by Malay nationalists 1875), Ipoh Railway Station (1917 iconic Mughal-British architecture, nicknamed "Taj Mahal of Ipoh"), Town Hall (1916), Sam Poh Tong Temple (Buddhist-cave temple sculpted in a limestone cliff, one of the most impressive in Malaysia, Buddha statues + sacred turtle gardens), Kek Lok Tong (other limestone-cave temple with landscaped ponds), Perak Cave Temple (Perak Tong, cave-temple with 600 steps to the panoramic 330 ft summit), Lost World of Tambun (amusement park with natural hot springs + tigers in sanctuary), Kellie's Castle 30 min away (unfinished Scottish castle 1915 of William Kellie Smith in the Malaysian jungle = unique Gothic-tropical atmosphere in the world), Cameron Highlands 2h away (highlands at 4,900 ft, Boh Tea tea plantations + preserved British colonial residences, temperate climate 59-77°F). Ipoh cuisine = major iconic of the city: Ipoh white coffee ("Ipoh kopi putih" Malaysian-style coffee roasted with margarine then sweetened with condensed milk, iconic culinary emblem of Ipoh, to taste at the legendary café Sin Yoon Loong or Nam Heong 1958), chicken rice Ipoh (different from Hainan, famous for "nga choi kai" bean sprouts), Hor fun (flat noodles in clear broth iconic of Ipoh), dim sum Ipoh (one of the best outside Hong Kong according to local Chinese), caramel custard Sino-Malay, tau fu fa (sweetened soya milk). Excursions: Cameron Highlands 2h away (highly recommended, fresh tropical alternative), Kuala Lumpur 2h by train (already covered), Penang 2h away (already covered), Pangkor Island 1h30 away (beach island). Logistics: Ipoh Sultan Azlan Shah airport (IPH) modest, modern direct ETS Kuala Lumpur-Ipoh train 2h.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
