Context

Our verdict

Poznan (Posen), 5th city of Poland in Greater Poland: 540,000 inhabitants. Cradle of the Polish state = it is here that Mieszko I, the first historical sovereign of Poland, was baptised in 966 thus founding the Polish nation. Commercial economic capital (MTP Poznań fair since 1925, one of the largest in Central Europe). Student city (140,000 students out of 540,000 inhabitants). June is my favorite month (8.1/10): 57-75°F, 35% rain, ideal temperatures. From June to August are the tourist high season: 54-77°F. From November to March, expect 30/48°F and 26-41% rain: continental cold, snow possible. Highlights: Stary Rynek (Old Market Square, one of the most beautiful squares in Poland, 4th largest after Wroclaw / Krakow / Łódź, background of colorful Renaissance bourgeois houses, lively student atmosphere), Town Hall (Renaissance 1551-1560 by architect Giovanni Battista Quadro, iconic view of the square with its arcades + 200 ft tower + clock with 2 mechanical billy goats coming out at noon hitting their heads "Koziołki Poznańskie" since the 16th century, legend of the 2 billy goats fighting during the governor's banquet, saving the kitchen on fire), Poznan Cathedral (Ostrów Tumski, on the cathedral island, place of Mieszko I's baptism 966, tombs of the first Polish sovereigns Mieszko I died 992 and Boleslaw I died 1025), Imperial Castle (neo-Roman palace 1905-1910 built by German Emperor William II, occupied by Hitler as Bunker residence in 1939-1945, controversy over its conservation), Saint Martin Croissants ("rogale świętomarcińskie", IGP UE 2008, Poznan speciality = croissants with almond paste and white poppy, tasted on 11 November Saint Martin festival patron of the city, 250 tons consumed each year), Croissant Museum (rare and amusing), Lake Malta (artificial lake for World Championship water sports, urban beach), Stary Browar ("Old Brewery", international award-winning commercial complex 2008 in a former brewery). Excursions: Gniezno 50 min away (1st capital of Poland, Saint Adalbert tomb-cathedral), Biskupin 1h30 away (archaeological site of a prehistoric Polish city 700 BC, life-size reconstruction of stilts on the lake), Berlin 2h45 by train, Wroclaw 2h by train. Try rogale świętomarcińskie (St. Martin croissants), pyzy (potato dumplings), kaczka po poznańsku (Poznan-style duck with red cabbage), Lech beer (brewed in Poznan).
