Context

Our verdict

Melbourne, Australia's cultural capital: 5 million inhabitants, sporting city (Australian Open in January), world-class coffee culture, alleys covered in street art. February is my favorite month (8.9/10): 59-77°F, 21% rain, austral summer. From June to August (austral winter), expect 45-57°F with 37-42% rain: cold by Australian standards, short days (9h of sunshine), but also the ski season at Mount Buller 3h away. December-March are the tourist high season. Melbourne is famous for its "4 seasons in one day": bring a jacket even in midsummer. Highlights: Federation Square, Hosier Lane (the most famous street art), Queen Victoria Market (since 1878), Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG, 100,000 capacity), St Kilda (urban beach, penguins at the Pier at dusk). Trendy districts: Fitzroy (Brunswick St), Brunswick (Sydney Rd), Carlton (Lygon St Italian quarter). Great Ocean Road 1h30 away (12 Apostles, 155 miles panoramic road), Yarra Valley 1h away (wines, Domaine Chandon), Mornington Peninsula 1h30 away (hot springs, wines). Try flat white in the laneway cafes (Brother Baba Budan, Patricia, Seven Seeds), Vietnamese pho in Richmond (Victoria St). Melbourne and Sydney = friendly historic rivalry.
