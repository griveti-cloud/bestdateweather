Context

Our verdict

Senegal, country in West Africa on the Atlantic coast: 18 million inhabitants. Former French colony 1659-1960 (one of the oldest French colonies in Africa), historic gateway for the transatlantic slave trade. Country among the most democratically stable in francophone West Africa (never had a military coup, regular peaceful political alternations). Capital Dakar, official language French, vehicular language Wolof. November is my favorite month (6.8/10): 70-86°F, 4% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Senegal never tops 6.8/10 (hot sub-Saharan tropical climate). From November to May, it's the dry season: 63-88°F, pleasant dry and hot climate, trade winds refresh the Atlantic coast, harmattan Saharan wind December-February = relative cold and dusty. From June to October, it's the rainy season (West African monsoon "hivernage"): 28-50% rainfall, milder temperatures 73-90°F, greener landscapes but roads sometimes impassable inland. Highlights: Dakar (capital, already covered by BDW, Atlantic peninsula, cosmopolitan West African urban atmosphere), Gorée Island 20 min by ferry from Dakar (UNESCO 1978, one of the most poignant memorial sites in the world on the slave trade, House of Slaves with "Door of No Return" symbolic, overwhelming atmosphere, African-American and Obamesque pilgrimage), Saint-Louis du Sénégal 4h away (UNESCO 2000, former capital of French West Africa 17th-1902, preserved colonial architecture, unique atmosphere on island connected by Faidherbe Bridge 1,700 ft 19th-century Eiffel), Shell Island near Saint-Louis (millions of oyster shells accumulated by 200 years of traditional fishing), Pink Lake (Lake Retba) 1h from Dakar (pink salt lake thanks to halophilic algae Dunaliella salina, historic arrival point of the Paris-Dakar Rally 1979-2008 before moving to South America, hand-harvested salt extraction by women), Lompoul Desert 3h from Dakar (mini Saharan desert 7 sq miles with 160 ft dunes, Bedouin-Moroccan camps, "affordable" desert experience), Petite Côte (Saly, Mbour classic tourist beach resorts), Casamance in the south (more humid tropical forest region, Diola people, Cap Skirring beach resorts), Niokolo-Koba National Park in the south-east (UNESCO 1981 but in danger, last Senegalese savannah with lions and hippopotamuses, inadvisable for insecurity), Bandia Reserve 1h30 from Dakar (well-managed private reserve to observe giraffes, zebras, rhinoceros, secure alternative to Niokolo). Try thieboudienne (rice with fish, Senegalese national dish inscribed UNESCO intangible heritage 2021), yassa (chicken with onion lemon), mafé (peanut sauce), bissap (hibiscus juice), fresh ginger, spicy Touba coffee. Dakar Blaise Diagne airport (DSS) opened 125,365 miles from Dakar.
