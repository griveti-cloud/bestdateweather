Context

Our verdict

Lech am Arlberg, ultra-smart Austrian ski resort: 1500 inhabitants at 4,800 ft, skier quotas limited to 14,000 per day (one of the only resorts in the world with a voluntary quota to preserve the experience). Linked to Zürs-St. Anton by Ski Arlberg (190 miles). July is my favorite month (5.3/10): 48-66°F, 65% rain in altitude storms. Lech remains above all a winter resort. From December to April, expect 18/41°F and 42-68% precipitation often as snow: ski high season, one of the snowiest in Europe (10m/year, Arlberg = the "cradle of snow"). Pistes: 190 miles Ski Arlberg with Zürs-St. Anton-Stuben, 87 lifts. White Ring = 14 miles loop circuit to ski (Lech-Zürs-Zug-Lech), world record of skiers on annual circuit. Off-piste: Mehlsack, Madloch, Roggspitze (with guide). Legendary pistes: Schindlergrat, Auenfeldjet (cable car between Lech and Warth for 210 miles of pistes total). Audience: ultra-smart (Hotels Aurelio, Almhof Schneider, Krone, 5*+, $550-$3,300/night), European and Eastern aristocracy, well-off Dutch and Belgians. The Dutch royal family has been coming for decades (Princess Friso fatal accident in 2012). Wellness: all the 5* have their iconic spas (expensive = $110+/session). Try Käsespätzle, Wiener Schnitzel. Logistics: Lech 2h from Zurich, 1h30 from Innsbruck.

It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 8.9/10). For hiking/suiner: March (8.9/10, 5.0°F).
Can you ski in Lech in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 8.3/10. Cold temperatures (0°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
