Context

Our verdict

Normandy is the west of France: 11,600 sq miles, 3.3 million inhabitants, 375 miles of coastline, cliffs, D-Day landing beaches (1944), camembert and calvados. July-August are my favorite months (8.3-8.4/10): 57-73°F, 33% rain, mild temperatures. From June to September, it's the high season: 55-72°F, but capricious weather (Normandy rainy but often sunny, rapid alternation). From November to March, expect 41-55°F and 41-45% rain: humid oceanic, sky often grey, many seaside resorts dormant. Highlights: Mont-Saint-Michel (UNESCO, one of France's most-visited sites, the strongest tides in Europe ±49 ft, view at night or at high tide, mandatory parking 1.6 miles away + shuttle or walk), D-Day Landing Beaches (Omaha Beach, Utah Beach, Juno, Gold, Sword, Pointe du Hoc — American and British cemeteries, Caen Memorial), Bayeux (11th-century Tapestry telling the conquest of England by William the Conqueror in 1066, UNESCO, 230 ft long), Etretat (Aval-Manneporte-Amont cliffs, painted by Monet, oversaturated in season), Honfleur (picturesque port, painted by Monet and Boudin), Deauville-Trouville (smart 19th-century seaside resorts, American Film Festival in September), Rouen (Gothic cathedral painted 30 times by Monet, place of Joan of Arc's execution), Giverny (Claude Monet's house and gardens, view from April to October). Channel Islands (Jersey, Guernsey) by ferry from neighbouring Saint-Malo. Try camembert, livarot, Pont-l'Évêque (3 AOC cheeses), tarte normande, andouille de Vire, calvados, cider, teurgoule. May-June and September = best compromises.
