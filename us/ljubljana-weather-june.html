Context

Our verdict

Ljubljana, Slovenian capital: 290,000 inhabitants, one of Europe's smallest and most charming capitals ("the green one" — European Green Capital 2016, car-free pedestrian center since 2008). July is my favorite month (9.0/10): 61-82°F, 35% rain, terraces along the Ljubljanica, festivals (Ljubljana Summer Festival July-August). From June to September, it's the high season: 54-82°F, mild continental climate. From December to March, expect 25/54°F and 29-39% rain/snow: dry cold, skiing at Krvavec or Vogel 1h away, charming Christmas market (Festive December) by the river. Highlights: Old town (on foot, around Mestni trg main square), Triple Bridge (Tromostovje, iconic by Plečnik 1932, 3 bridges fanning out at the heart of the city), Dragon Bridge (Zmajski most, 4 guardian dragons, iconic of Ljubljana), Ljubljana Castle (on the hill, accessible by funicular or 15 min walk, panoramic view of the Alps), Central Market (Plečnik, neoclassical riverside), Metelkova (alternative artistic squat, former military barracks, underground nightlife). Excursions: Lake Bled 50 min away ("the Slovenian postcard image", island church, perched castle), Lake Bohinj 1h30 away (wilder, Triglav National Park), Postojna Caves 50 min away (15 miles of galleries, underground train, the most visited in Europe), Predjama Castle (13th-century castle embedded in a cliff). Adriatic coast 1h30 away (Piran medieval Venetian town). Try potica (rolled cake), idrijski žlikrofi (ravioli), kranjska klobasa (sausage), Refošk wine.
