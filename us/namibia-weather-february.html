Context

Our verdict

Namibia, country in southern Africa: 2.5 million inhabitants on 318,500 sq miles (density among the lowest in the world), capital Windhoek. The most arid country in sub-Saharan Africa, former German then South African colony, independence 1990 (the youngest country in Africa). Unique biodiversity: Namib Desert (the oldest in the world, 80 million years) and Kalahari. September is my favorite month (9.5/10): 55-84°F, 2% rain, this is the end of the dry season, wildlife visible at watering holes. From May to October, it's the tourist high season: 46-88°F, 0-6% rain, dry climate ideal for safari and desert. From June-July, expect 46-72°F: glacial nights at altitude (sometimes below 32°F in the Kalahari), ideal for wildlife observation in the morning. From December to March, it's the relative rainy season: 25-33% rainfall, but it's the "green season" with re-greened landscapes and animal births. Regions to know: Sossusvlei (orange dunes of the Namib among the highest in the world — Big Daddy 1,100 ft, Dune 45 photogenic, Deadvlei "dead valley" with black fossilised acacia trees on white and orange background = the most iconic landscape of Namibia), Skeleton Coast (Skeleton Coast, 310 miles of desert + misty ocean, boat wrecks and whale skeletons, desert lions, Cape Cross sea lions 100,000 individuals), Etosha National Park (the largest national park in Namibia, 8,840 sq miles with a central salt "pan" that attracts wildlife in the dry season: elephants, lions, black and white rhinos, oryx, springboks), Damaraland (red rocky landscapes, San rock engravings of Twyfelfontein UNESCO, desert elephants), Fish River Canyon (the 2nd largest canyon in the world after the Grand Canyon, 99 miles long, 17 miles wide, 1,800 ft deep, 5-day hike), Walvis Bay and Swakopmund (Atlantic coast, kayaking with seals and pink flamingos, German atmosphere colonial architecture). Try biltong (dried meat), kapana (street-food grilled meat), oryx steak, Windhoek lager (German heritage).
