Context

Our verdict

The Lofoten Islands, Norwegian archipelago beyond the Arctic Circle: 7 main islands connected by bridges, 24,000 inhabitants, the most spectacular landscapes of Norway (3,300 ft mountains plunging into the sea, colorful fishing villages). July is my favorite month (7.5/10): 52-61°F, 44% rain, this is the "midnight sun" (from 28 May to 14 July, no sunset for 7 weeks), hikes, kayaking, fishing. From June to August, expect 46-61°F: summer high season (few tourists outside cruises), Reine and Hamnøy are the most photographed villages (packed in July). From September to March, expect 23/45°F and 47-61% precipitation often as snow: this is the Northern Lights season (Lofoten = one of the best spots in the world, visible as soon as it's dark AND sky clear, which is rare in winter Lofoten), cod fishing (skrei) in March (the gold of the Norwegian sea, traditional fishing continued for 1000 years). From December to January, it's the polar night (no sun above the horizon for 4 weeks, but magnificent blue light). Highlights: Reine (the most famous village, iconic photo from Reinebringen 1,500 ft, 1500 wooden steps renovated 2018), Hamnøy and its red rorbuer (fishermen's cabins on stilts), Henningsvær ("Venice of the Lofoten", bridge since 1981), Kvalvika Beach (1h hike, white sand), Ramberg (the most accessible beach). Activities: kayaking between the islands, climbing, fishing, Munkebu/Reinebringen hike. Try bacalao, fish soup, brunost (brown goat cheese). Logistics: Oslo-Bodø flight + Bodø-Moskenes ferry 4h or direct Oslo-Leknes/Svolvær flight.
