Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Playa del Carmen is the alternative beach resort to Cancun (1h to the south): human-scale town, European atmosphere, bohemian-chic, base for exploring the Riviera Maya. March is my favorite month (8.9/10): 75-82°F, 27% rain, sea at 77°F, end of the American high season. From June to October, it's hurricane season: 46-72% rain, hurricane risk, possible sargassum. From December to February, expect 72-81°F: European and American high season, hotel prices x2-x3. Highlights: 5th Avenue (Quinta Avenida) pedestrianised over 20 blocks (boutiques, restaurants, bars), Playa Mamitas (animation), Playa Punta Esmeralda (cenote in the sea, quieter). Excursions from Playa: Tulum (45 min by collectivo or ADO bus), Cenotes to explore (Gran Cenote, Cenote Azul, Dos Ojos, Cenote Calavera), Isla Cozumel by ferry 45 min from the port (snorkelling on the Mesoamerican reef, the world's 2nd largest after Australia), Sian Ka'an (UNESCO reserve), Xcaret. Akumal 30 min away to swim with turtles. Try tacos al pastor, ceviche, mezcal. Become very digital nomad: coworkings, café scene, significant French community. Tulum bookings 4-6 months ahead in high season.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
