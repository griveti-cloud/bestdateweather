Context

Our verdict

Sao Tome and Principe, micro-state insular in the Gulf of Guinea off equatorial Africa: 220,000 inhabitants on 2 main islands (Sao Tome 330 sq miles, Principe 55 sq miles) on the geographic equator. Former Portuguese colony 1471-1975 = first European colony to practice large-scale plantation sugar cultivation with African slaves 16th century = model that will then be exported to Brazil and Caribbean. Country among the poorest in Africa but one of the most politically peaceful. June is my favorite month (8.2/10): 72-82°F, 8% rain, this is the ideal dry season ("gravana" = dry season in local creole). From June to August, it's the dry season: 72-84°F, this is the tourist high season (Portuguese and Angolan clientele mainly, few French), turquoise waters, no rain. From September to May, it's the wet season: 19-46% rainfall, but stable temperatures 72-86°F. Unique equatorial climate: Sao Tome is on the geographic equator but receives the cold Benguela current rising from Antarctica = more moderate temperatures than expected on the equator (rare days above 86°F unlike Manaus or Singapore also on the equator). Highlights: São Tomé City capital (60,000 inhabitants, preserved Portuguese colonial atmosphere, limestone cobblestones, independence square), São Tomé Cathedral (15th-century Portuguese Gothic, one of the oldest Christian churches in sub-Saharan Africa), Fortress São Sebastião (16th-century Portuguese, houses the National Museum), Roça Agostinho Neto (former 1900 Portuguese cocoa plantation, plantation hospital reconverted, memorial atmosphere), Roça São João dos Angolares (cocoa plantation reconverted into boutique hotel with gourmet restaurant by chef João Carlos Silva = Sao Tomean culinary ambassador), Pico Cão Grande ("Great Dog Peak", 2,200 ft volcanic needle iconic view of Sao Tome rising from the jungle, extremely difficult ascent reserved for professional mountaineers), Praia Jalé Beach (south-east, sea turtle nesting), Lagoa Azul Beach (north, turquoise waters), Sete Ondas Beach ("7 Waves"), São Nicolau Waterfall (waterfall in the jungle), Obo Natural Park (primary equatorial jungle covering 30% of the island, observation of 130 endemic orchid species, endemic Sao Tome pigeon birds). Principe Island 30 min by plane (smaller wilder and more preserved island, declared UNESCO biosphere reserve 2012, Banana beach one of the most beautiful in Africa, Roça Belo Monte luxury boutique hotel on former plantation). Cocoa: Sao Tome is one of the world origins of fine chocolate (former large Portuguese producer, today premium niche for French chocolatiers like Bonnat since 1884 or Pralus). Try calulu (fish with cassava leaves), Sao Tomean feijoada (Portuguese heritage), arroz doce (Portuguese rice pudding), Sao Tome coffee (Robusta cultivated at altitude, little known but excellent), local Rosema beer, artisanal chocolate. São Tomé airport (TMS) with weekly flights from Lisbon (6h30) and Libreville (1h).
