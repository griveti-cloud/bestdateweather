Context

Our verdict

Rarotonga is the main island of the Cook Islands: 26 sq miles, 14,000 inhabitants, central mountain (Te Manga 2,100 ft), 20 miles loop road around the island. November is my favorite month (8.2/10): 70-72°F, 38% rain, dry season. From May to October is the relative dry season (36-56% rainfall). From November to April, it's the wet season (51-66%). Highlights: Beaches Muri Beach (the most famous, calm lagoon, kayak to the motus), Aro'a Beach (snorkelling), Black Rock Beach (sunset), Cross-Island Track (6h hike across the island, Te Rua Manga "the Needle" as the highest point), Punanga Nui Market in Avarua (Saturday morning, food/crafts market), Avana Lagoon (departure for Aitutaki by boat). Activities: island bus tour (circular bus system "clockwise/anticlockwise", 1 every 30 min), scooter rental (60 NZ$/day) or car, diving and snorkelling. Cultural Village (Cook Maori culture show). Marae (sacred ancestral sites). Accommodation: no big all-inclusive Maldives-style resort, rather bungalows and family hotels at 100-300 NZ$/night. Try ika mata, taro, local pineapple. Auckland-Rarotonga flight 4h, affordable prices. Rarotonga = main low-cost alternative to Tahiti.
