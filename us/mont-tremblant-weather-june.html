Context

Our verdict

Mont-Tremblant, Quebec ski resort in the Laurentides: 9000 inhabitants at 660 ft altitude (Mont-Tremblant summit reaches 2,900 ft), 81 miles north of Montreal. One of the most famous resorts in eastern Canada and North America, renowned for its pedestrian village rebuilt in 1991 (Intrawest) with very successful pseudo-European architecture. July is my favorite month (8.2/10): 61-77°F, 49% rain, but Mont-Tremblant is above all a winter destination. From June to September, expect 41-77°F: active summer season (36-hole golf Le Géant and Le Diable, hiking, mountain bike, swimming Tremblant lake). From December to March, expect 5/36°F and 41-47% precipitation often as snow (4-5m/season): this is ski high season. Pistes: 102 pistes, 14 lifts, vertical drop 2,100 ft (modest compared to the Alps but excellent for North America), 4 sides (South main, North, Soleil, Edge). Characteristic conditions: extreme dry cold (-13°F in January-February, frozen breath on the face within minutes), fresh snow nearly guaranteed (severe continental climate), icy patches typical. Audience: Quebecers in the majority, Northeast Americans (Boston, NY), Europeans seeking a North American experience. Tremblant Pedestrian Village: 50+ restaurants and shops, lively nightlife, resurrected European village atmosphere. Hotels: Fairmont Tremblant (5*), Hôtel Quintessence (5*+ on the lakeside), Westin, 150-800 CAD/night. Try poutine, maple syrup, tourtière, Quebec microbrewery beer (La Diable). Logistics: Mont-Tremblant 1h30 from Montreal by car (highway 15 north then 117), Mont-Tremblant International airport (YTM, seasonal flights).
