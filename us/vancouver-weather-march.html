Context

Our verdict

Vancouver is the most accessible city for Europeans on the Pacific coast of Canada: Pacific on one side, mountains (North Shore Mountains) on the other. July is my favorite month (9.1/10): 59-72°F, 15% rain, finally clear skies, swimming at Kits Beach and English Bay, hiking at Grouse Mountain. From November to March, expect 34-48°F and 55-65% rainfall: city reputed to be the rainiest in Canada (1200mm/year), but also when Whistler (2h away) opens its ski season. May-June and September are the best compromises. Highlights: Stanley Park (1000 acres, larger than Central Park, totem poles), Granville Island (public market, microbreweries), Capilano Suspension Bridge (230 ft above the canyon), Gastown (Steam Clock, the first quarter), Grouse Mountain (cable car for the view). Districts: Yaletown (luxury), Kitsilano (hippie/yoga), Commercial Drive (Italian). Try sushi (Vancouver = the North American sushi capital), salmon, ramen at Marutama, Korean BBQ in Coquitlam. Vancouver Island (Victoria) 1h30 by ferry, Whistler 2h by car.
