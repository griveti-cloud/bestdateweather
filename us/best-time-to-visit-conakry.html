Conakry, capital of Guinea on the Atlantic coast of West Africa: 2 million inhabitants in urban area. Former French colony (1898-1958), first colony in Africa to say "no" to the De Gaulle 1958 referendum and obtain independence immediately (under Sékou Touré, Marxist dictator 1958-1984), country rich in bauxite (1st world producer) but among the poorest in Africa. Military coup in September 2021 by Colonel Mamady Doumbouya (overthrowing authoritarian President Alpha Condé). February-January-March are my favorite months (9.2-9.3/10): 75-86°F, 0-2% rain, this is the ideal dry season + refreshing Saharan harmattan. From November to May, it's the tourist high season: 75-86°F, pleasant dry and hot climate, refreshing Atlantic trade winds. From June to October, it's the intense West African monsoon: 37-99% rainfall (Conakry = one of the most watered African capitals in monsoon, July-August = near-continuous torrential rains, minimal infrastructure flood risk). Highlights: Madina Market (the largest market in West Africa according to locals, chaotic-extraordinary atmosphere), Niger Market, Faisal Grand Mosque (one of the largest mosques in West Africa, capacity 12,000 worshippers, financed by King Faisal of Saudi Arabia 1980), Saint Mary Cathedral (French colonial heritage), Palace of the People (Soviet brutalist architecture, Sékou Touré heritage 1960-70), National Museum of Guinea (Sandervalia, Baga-Soussou-Malinké ritual masks), Kaloum Quarter (main urban peninsula), Conakry Lighthouse, Loos Islands 30 min by boat ("Los Islands", archipelago of 3 preserved islands with wild Atlantic beaches, former Portuguese then British colony before retrocession to France 1904, paradisiacal atmosphere rare in West Africa). Essential Guinea excursions: Mount Nimba (UNESCO 1981, Liberia-Ivory Coast border, exceptional primate biodiversity, rich iron, hiking), Fouta Djallon ("water tower of West Africa", mountain massif where the Niger-Senegal-Gambia rivers are born, spectacular landscapes, traditional Fulani people, Dalaba-Mali-Pita hiking 6h from Conakry), Kindia 2h away ("bride's waterfall", chimpanzees in park), Kankan 12h by bus (Upper Guinea, former pre-colonial Mandingo commercial center), Boké (Atlantic coast, bauxite). Safety: fragile political situation since the 2021 coup, increased France Diplomacy vigilance, high urban crime in Conakry (frequent armed robberies). Try Guinean greasy rice (rice with red palm oil, iconic national dish), fouti (peanut sauce), mafé skewers, fried plantain, bissap, ginger juice, dolo, Guinean Robusta coffee. Conakry Gbessia airport (CKY).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
