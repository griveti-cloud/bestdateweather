Context

Our verdict

Panama, Central American country between Costa Rica and Colombia linking North and South America: 4.4 million inhabitants. Capital Panama City. Country unique in the world thanks to the Panama Canal = 50 miles linking the Atlantic Ocean to the Pacific Ocean, one of the world's engineering masterpieces (built 1904-1914 by the USA after the French Lesseps failure 1881-1889 = 22,000 dead, plus the Panama Affair French political scandal), widened 2007-2016 for Neo-Panamax ships, one of the world's most strategic trade routes (5% of global maritime trade). January is my favorite month (6.9/10): 75-86°F, 32% rain, this is the ideal dry season. Panama never tops 6.9/10 (humid hot equatorial tropical climate year-round). From December to April, it's the dry season "verano": 75-90°F, sea at 81°F, this is the tourist high season. From May to November, it's the green season "invierno": 62-98% rainfall, stable temperatures 75-86°F, greener landscapes. Highlights: Panama Canal (the country's #1 attraction, visits possible at the Miraflores Locks on the Pacific side 30 min from Panama City with modern observation center and museum, watching a Neo-Panamax cargo cross the lock in 8 min, alternative Agua Clara locks on the Caribbean side or intermediate Pedro Miguel locks, partial or full canal cruise possible), Casco Viejo ("old town" UNESCO 1997, old 17th-18th century colonial quarter partly restored as the trendy "Panama trendy" district since 2010, Plaza de la Independencia, Iglesia San José with gilded altar 17th century), Panama City Skyline (modern 2000-2020 skyscrapers nicknamed "Latin America's Dubai", 50+ buildings 660 ft+, one of Latin America's most impressive skylines), Biomuseo (Frank Gehry architecture 2014, biodiversity museum), Panama Viejo (UNESCO, remains of the first Panama founded 1519 and destroyed by Henry Morgan in 1671, ruins in the heart of the modern city), Albrook Mall (one of Latin America's largest shopping centers), Cinta Costera (panoramic seaside promenade). Essential excursions: Bocas del Toro 1h by plane or 12h by bus (Caribbean archipelago of 9 coral islands in the north-west, Afro-Creole English-speaking Caribbean atmosphere, turquoise beaches, surf-snorkelling-diving, the most touristic of Panamanian sites outside the canal), San Blas / Guna Yala 1h by car + lancha (autonomous archipelago of 365 paradisiacal islets on the Caribbean coast administered by the Guna Yala indigenous people = matriarchal people preserved from modern changes, access limited to tourists to preserve the culture, stay in thatched huts on tiny islets with white sand + coconut trees + turquoise sea = unique traditional Caribbean experience worldwide, high price 100-200 USD/day full board but unforgettable), Boquete 6h by bus (Highlands mountains at 3,900 ft, Volcán Barú 11,400 ft, Geisha coffee plantations = among the world's best coffees, Panamanian Cup of Excellence, hiking), El Valle de Antón 2h away (another mountain area), Pearl Islands southern Pacific, Coiba National Park (UNESCO marine, diving). Mayan sites: Panama has no Mayan sites (geographic boundary of the Maya world = Costa Rica-Panama). Try sancocho (chicken+yuca soup, national dish), ropa vieja (shredded beef), patacones (fried plantain), ceviche, Geisha coffee (Boquete origin), seco (local eau-de-vie), Atlas/Balboa/Panama beers. Panama Tocumen airport (PTY) Copa Airlines hub for Latin America.
