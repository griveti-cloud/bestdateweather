Context

Our verdict

Nouakchott ("place of the winds" in Berber), capital of Mauritania on the Atlantic coast of the Sahara: 1.4 million inhabitants. Planned capital built ex-nihilo in 1957-1960 (Mauritania independent 1960, no previous capital, simple fishing village before 1957). Border country between Arab North Africa and sub-Saharan Africa, hybrid Berber + Arab + African cultural identity, official languages Arabic + French + African languages (Pulaar, Soninke, Wolof). Country among the most arid in the world = 90% of the territory in the Sahara. January is my favorite month (9.3/10): 57-86°F, 0% rain, this is the ideal dry season. December-November stay excellent (7.7-8.7/10). From November to March, it's the cool dry season: 57-90°F, ideal dry and tempered desert climate thanks to Atlantic breezes ("Maritime Trade Winds" which considerably refresh the Mauritanian Atlantic coast unlike the inland Sahara). From April to June, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 66-99°F, trade winds persist. From July to September, it's the short Sahelian rainy season (only extreme south): 5-13% rainfall. Nouakchott highlights: Nouakchott Beach (wild Atlantic, strong currents for swimming, but beautiful for the walk), Nouakchott Fishing Port (one of the largest traditional fishing ports in Africa, extraordinary atmosphere with thousands of colorful pirogues at sunset, fishermen returning with fresh fish iconic view of Mauritania), Capitale Market and Traditional Market (traditional Sahelian atmosphere), Saudi Mosque ("Saudi Mosque", one of the largest mosques in Mauritania financed by Saudi Arabia), National Museum of Nouakchott (modest Mauritanian archaeological collection), Saint Joseph Cathedral (modest church for Catholic minority). Essential Mauritania excursions = this is why one visits Mauritania: Banc d'Arguin National Park in the north (UNESCO 1989, one of the largest coastal wetlands in the world, border between Sahara and Atlantic, millions of palearctic migratory birds + pelicans + pink flamingos + dolphins, Imragen people fishermen in collaboration with iconic dolphins, traditional lance navigation), Chinguetti (UNESCO 1996, former 13th-century Saharan caravan holy city, one of the 7 holy cities of Islam according to Mauritanian tradition, medieval manuscript libraries, iconic Saharan dune landscape "Mauritanian desert"), Ouadane (UNESCO 1996, other former abandoned caravan city), Desert Train / SNIM Train ("Iron Train" of the National Industrial and Mining Company, the longest train in the world 1.6 miles long with 200+ wagons transporting iron ore Zouérat-Nouadhibou 435 miles across the Mauritanian Sahara, "legendary" trip possible hooked to the wagons free of charge = world iconic experience for extreme adventurers, 12-6 PM journey in dust), Atar and Adrar (oasis region), Diawling Beach in the south Senegalese border. Safety: Nouakchott globally safer since 2010 (AQMI jihadist terrorism reduced following Mauritanian military operation), but increased France Diplomacy vigilance for the Saharan north and east bordering Mali. Try méchoui (iconic Mauritanian roast mutton), Mauritanian thieboudienne (rice with fish), Mauritanian couscous, Atlantic grilled fish, Mauritanian Adrar dates, ceremonial Tuareg tea 3 glasses iconic ("1st bitter, 2nd sweet, 3rd suave"). Nouakchott Oumtounsy airport (NKC).
