Context

Our verdict

Munich (München), capital of Bavaria: 1.5 million inhabitants, Germany's most prosperous city (BMW, Siemens), "Northern Italy" atmosphere (biergartens, baroque art). July-August are my favorite months (8.1/10): 57-75°F, 46-48% rain in afternoon storms, biergartens packed, festivals. Oktoberfest (mid-September to early October, 16 days, 6 million visitors, 7 million liters of beer) = absolute tourist peak, prices x5, hotels fully booked 6 months ahead, unique atmosphere. From November to March, expect 28/46°F and 38-45% precipitation often as snow: dry cold, Christkindlmarkt Christmas market (Marienplatz, more than 30 Christmas markets in Munich) from 25 November to 24 December, skiing possible 1h30 by bus (Zugspitze 9,700 ft, Garmisch-Partenkirchen). Highlights: Marienplatz (main square, Glockenspiel at 11h and 12h every day — 32 animated figures), Frauenkirche (iconic gothic cathedral with its 2 green domes), Residenz (Wittelsbach palace for 700 years), Hofbräuhaus (the world's most famous brewery since 1589), Englischer Garten (Europe's largest urban park, larger than Central Park, permanent surf wave on the Eisbach, biergartens, Chinesischer Turm), Viktualienmarkt (permanent central market), BMW Welt and Museum. Excursions: Neuschwanstein Castle 2h away (Ludwig II, the castle that inspired Disney, book ahead), Dachau 30 min away (first Nazi concentration camp, memorial, free), Salzburg 1h30, Innsbruck 2h. Try Wiener Schnitzel, Weisswurst (white sausage, traditionally eaten before noon), Brezen (pretzel), Schweinshaxe (pork knuckle), beer (Augustiner, Hacker-Pschorr, Spaten).
