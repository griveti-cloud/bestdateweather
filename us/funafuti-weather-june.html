Context

Our verdict

Funafuti, capital of Tuvalu in the central Pacific: 6300 inhabitants on the main atoll of Tuvalu (main island + 5 motus of 10 sq miles total). Tuvalu = 4th smallest country in the world by surface area after Vatican-Monaco-Nauru, 11,000 inhabitants in total on 9 atolls, independence from the United Kingdom 1978, Commonwealth member. Country at the highest world risk of disappearance by sea level rise: average altitude 7 ft, highest point 15 ft, IPCC forecast = uninhabitable by 2050-2100, active international negotiations to relocate the population to Australia/New Zealand (first agreement with Australia signed 2023 for 280 climate visas per year for Tuvaluans). Tourism near non-existent (1500 tourists/year, among the least visited countries in the world). September is my favorite month (7.7/10): 79-82°F, 73% rain, this is the relative dry season. October-July stay good (6.8-7.6/10). From April to October, it's the dry season "trade wind" trade winds: 81-84°F, sea at 82°F, relatively little rain. From November to March, it's the cyclonic season "cyclone season": 78-84% rainfall, low but existing tropical cyclone risk (cyclone Pam 2015 devastated several motus). Highlights: Atoll of Funafuti (central lagoon of 15 miles × 11 miles bordered by 33 motus including the main island, lagoon among the most beautiful in the Pacific with shallow turquoise waters), Funafuti Beach (powdery white sand opening directly onto the lagoon or the ocean depending on side), Funafuti Conservation Area (13 sq miles marine reserve protected since 1996, 6 uninhabited motus, diving and snorkelling with exceptional living reefs), Government Building (modest government palace), Cathedral of Our Lady of the Islands, Vaitupu Marae (traditional Polynesian ceremonial place), Tuvalu National Library and Archives, WWII Memorial (Tuvalu = WWII theater, American forces occupied Funafuti 1942-1945 as an air base to push back the Japanese in the Pacific). Activities: diving and snorkelling in the lagoon (exceptional living reefs, manta rays, reef sharks), traditional fishing with Tuvaluan fishermen, atoll tour by boat to visit the uninhabited motus (paradise white sand), cycling all around Funafuti island (7 miles, single road), marine bird watching (frigates, terns). Cultural particularities: Tuvalu.tv domain (the country earns 5 million USD/year by leasing its ".tv" internet domain name highly demanded by television channels = strategic source of income for the micro-state), Te Ika Tuvalu ("the Tuvaluan fishermen", decorative Tuvaluan currency not used daily, the Australian dollar is the official currency), Polynesian (Tuvaluan language related to Samoan-Tongan), Fete Te Maeva ("independence day" 1 October, iconic traditional Tuvaluan dances = "fatele" = polyrhythmic chants with percussion on wooden box). Try palu sami (raw fish in coconut milk), pulaka (local starch close to taro), umu (cooking in underground stone oven Polynesian), grilled fish, fresh coconut milk. Logistics: Funafuti International airport (FUN), unique Fiji Airways flight 3x weekly from Suva Fiji 2h30, one of the most difficult airports in the world to access, runway used as public sports field when no plane (rugby, football, children's games — withdrawn when the plane arrives). Cost: Tuvalu extremely expensive (200-300 USD/day minimum), few accommodations (Vaiaku Lagi Hotel the only international hotel, 2 guesthouses).
