Context

Our verdict

The Canary Islands, Spanish archipelago off Morocco: 7 main islands, 2.2 million inhabitants, the most stable subtropical climate in Europe ("eternal spring"). July is my favorite month (9.7/10): 68-82°F, 0% rain. Year-round is workable: 57-84°F on average, sea at 66-75°F, steady trade winds, outside the cyclone zone. From July to October, it's full high season: 66-84°F, 0-7% rain. From December to March, expect 57-72°F: winter high season (Northern Europeans wintering, snowbirds), prices x2 in the resorts. 7 islands to know: Tenerife (the largest, Teide 12,200 ft highest point of Spain), Gran Canaria (Maspalomas dunes), Lanzarote (black volcanic landscapes, Timanfaya, César Manrique), Fuerteventura (the most beautiful beaches, kite/surf), La Palma ("isla bonita", hiking), La Gomera (laurel forest, silbo whistling), El Hierro (the smallest, diving). Highlights: Teide National Park (UNESCO, martian landscapes, Pico del Teide ascent by cable car + special permit for the summit), Maspalomas (dunes by the sea), Timanfaya (still-burning volcano, geothermal heat demonstrations), Garajonay (UNESCO, prehistoric laurel forest). Cuisine: papas arrugadas con mojo (spicy red or green), gofio, vieja (local fish), sancocho. Direct flights from Paris to Tenerife/Gran Canaria/Lanzarote in 4h30.
