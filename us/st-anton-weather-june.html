Context

Our verdict

St. Anton am Arlberg, Austrian ski resort: 2500 inhabitants at 4,300 ft, considered the cradle of modern alpine skiing (Hannes Schneider founded the first modern ski school here in 1907, the "Arlberg" technique that revolutionised teaching). Linked to Lech-Zürs-Stuben via Ski Arlberg (190 miles of linked pistes, the largest area in Austria). August is my favorite month (7.5/10): 54-70°F, 66% rain in mountain storms. From December to April, expect 21/45°F and 44-69% precipitation often as snow: prime ski season, one of the snowiest in Austria (8m/year), legendary après-ski (Krazy Kanguruh, MooserWirt = world-renowned bars, atmosphere until 10 PM after the slopes). Legendary pistes: Stuben Albona (north-facing shaded slope, well-preserved snow), Valluga (9,200 ft, accessible by cable car for skiers with a guide only, legendary freeride). Off-piste: Backside, Maroitäli, Schindlerkar (guide mandatory). St. Christoph above St. Anton = near-deserted hamlet with Hospiz Hotel (historic 5*). Crowd: technical and athletic skiers (the saying goes "a red piste at St. Anton = a black anywhere else"), Brits, Germans. Less chic than Lech or Verbier, more party-oriented. Logistics: St. Anton 1h30 from Innsbruck by train, 2h from Zürich. Hotels $165-$550/night, ski pass $77/day. Try Tiroler Gröstl, Käsespätzle, Kaiserschmarrn, schnaps.
