Context

Our verdict

Komodo, island in the Lesser Sunda Islands archipelago in Indonesia: 150 sq miles, 2000 inhabitants, famous for its Komodo dragons (Varanus komodoensis = the largest lizard in the world, up to 10 ft long and 90 kg, endemic to the islands of Komodo, Rinca, Padar, west Flores). Komodo National Park = UNESCO 1991, one of the 7 New Natural Wonders of the World 2012. July is my favorite month (9.0/10): 77-84°F, 15% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From June to September, it's the dry season: 75-88°F, this is the tourist high season, the most golden landscapes (burnt savannah), the calmest sea. From December to March, it's the rainy season: 75-83% rainfall, sometimes rough sea, greener landscapes. Highlights / Main park islands: Komodo (the main island), Rinca (the other dragon island, smaller, more accessible from Labuan Bajo), Padar Island (island without dragons but with the most famous panorama: 3 differently colored beaches — one pink, one white, one black — seen from the summit at 30 min walk, one of the most viral photos in Indonesia), Pink Beach (one of the only pink beaches in the world thanks to red coral fragments mixed with the white sand, snorkelling), Manta Point (snorkelling with manta rays, almost guaranteed), Kanawa, Kelor, Sebayur = snorkelling islands around Labuan Bajo. Visiting the dragons: guided tours mandatory on Komodo and Rinca (with rangers armed with forked sticks), dragons in near-total freedom, attacks on humans rare but real (1 death per year on average among the local villagers who live near the dragons), to observe but NEVER to approach within 33 ft. Diving: Komodo National Park is one of the world-renowned diving sites (top 5 globally according to several rankings), 1000 fish species, manta rays, white and black tip reef sharks, dolphins, whales, strong currents (reserved for advanced divers), cult sites: Batu Bolong, Crystal Rock, Castle Rock, Manta Point, Cauldron. Liveaboard 3-7 days = the ideal way to visit the park ($33-$330/day depending on boat category, from Labuan Bajo). Try nasi goreng, ikan bakar (grilled fish), sambal terasi. Logistics: Labuan Bajo airport (LBJ) on Flores with direct flights from Bali Denpasar 1h, then private boat or 1-3 day tour for the park ($165-$330/day for a private boat for 4-6 people).
