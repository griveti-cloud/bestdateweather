Context

Our verdict

Jasna, in the Slovak Low Tatras, is the largest ski resort in the country: 31 miles of pistes, Chopok summit at 6,600 ft. Rates among the lowest in Central Europe (day pass $38-$55). August is my favorite month (7.3/10): 45-61°F, 49% rain, good for hiking in the Low Tatras national park. From December to March, expect 10/32°F: ski season. Reliable snow thanks to altitude and continental climate. The resort belongs to the Tatry Mountain Resorts group (which also owns Vysoké Tatry nearby). From November and April, it's the slow shoulder season. Bratislava 4h by car, direct flight Poprad-Tatry from Krakow or Vienna. Ideal for a low-cost ski weekend from Western Europe. Local cuisine (bryndzové halušky: sheep cheese gnocchi) to discover in the koliba (traditional chalets).

When is the best time to visit Jasná?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: April (ski score 8.7/10). For hiking/suiner: April (8.7/10, 5.0°F).
Can you ski in Jasná in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.0/10. Cold temperatures (-6°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.

In April, temperatures average 5.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
