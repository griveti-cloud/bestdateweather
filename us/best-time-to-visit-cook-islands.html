The Cook Islands are a New Zealand archipelago of 15 islands in free association: 17,000 inhabitants, capital Avarua on Rarotonga (the main island). Independent state but New Zealand citizenship. November is my favorite month (8.2/10): 70-72°F, 38% rain, dry season. From May to October, it's the dry season: 36-56% rainfall, temperatures 68-77°F, steady trade winds. From November to April, it's the wet season: 51-66% rainfall, heavy heat, cyclone risk (Pat 2010). Highlights: Rarotonga (main island, 20 miles circumference, Cross-Island Track 6h hike), Aitutaki 50 min by plane (spectacular turquoise lagoon with 15 motus, considered one of the most beautiful in the South Pacific), Atiu (limestone caves, local coffee), Mangaia (the oldest island in the Pacific). Activities: diving and snorkelling, kayaking in the Aitutaki lagoon, hiking on Rarotonga, seabird observation. Punanga Nui Market in Avarua on Saturday. Cook Maori community (Cook Islands Maori language). Cuisine: ika mata (raw fish in coconut milk, Cook version), poké (taro), grilled fish. Direct flight from Auckland in 4h (Air New Zealand) or Los Angeles. Very good value for money vs French Polynesia: half-price, more relaxed atmosphere. Currency: NZ Dollar.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
