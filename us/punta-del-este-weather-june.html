Context

Our verdict

Punta del Este, smart beach resort of Uruguay: 10,000 permanent inhabitants (population x10-x20 in high season from late December to mid-February). "Saint-Tropez of South America", summer haunt of Argentinian, Brazilian and Uruguayan elites (wealthy Argentinians have their secondary residences here, it's one of the most expensive beach destinations in Latin America). January is my favorite month (9.0/10): 66-79°F, 25% rain, this is the peak of the austral summer. From December to February, it's full high season: hotel prices x5-x8 (Conrad Hotel & Casino 800-3000$/night), full restaurants, packed beaches. From March to May, it's the ideal shoulder season (sea still warm, prices divided by 3, crowds disappeared, it's one of the best moments). From June to October, it's the austral winter: 46-70°F, many establishments close, near-deserted town (5000 permanent inhabitants on site). Highlights: La Mano de Punta del Este ("Los Dedos", the most famous sculpture in Uruguay, 5 giant fingers emerging from the sand of Playa Brava, created 1982 by Mario Irarrázabal), Casapueblo (at Punta Ballena 9 miles, hotel-museum-residence created by the artist Carlos Páez Vilaró 1958, Mediterranean architecture in white lime cubist built intuitively without plans, "Ceremonia del Sol" ritual = poetic reading at sunset on the terrace), Punta del Este Lighthouse (1860, on the point between the 2 oceans "Playa Brava" Atlantic with strong waves and "Playa Mansa" calm Río de la Plata), Casa Pueblo. Beaches: La Brava (Atlantic ocean side, waves, surfers), La Mansa (Río de la Plata side, calm, families), José Ignacio 30 min to the north (the smarter and more discreet "hidden gem", frequented by Shakira and other celebrities). Nightlife: Conrad Hotel & Casino, Ovo Beach Lounge. Excursions: Isla de Lobos by boat (250,000 sea lion island), La Barra smart hippie village, Punta Ballena. Try chivito, asado, Tannat wines, dulce de leche, calamares fritos.
