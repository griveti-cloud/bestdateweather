Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Oman (Sultanate of Oman), country in the Arabian peninsula at the far south-east: 5 million inhabitants over 119,500 sq miles. One of the most stable and preserved Arab countries = sultanic monarchy under Sultan Qaboos bin Said 1970-2020 (peaceful modernisation of the country without revolution), now his cousin Haitham bin Tariq since 2020. Oman keeps a preserved cultural identity vs Dubai-Doha modernised to excess, traditional Arab atmosphere with banco-stone architecture + women in black abaya + men in iconic white dishdasha. March is my favorite month (9.2/10): 72-90°F, 4% rain, this is the ideal dry season. February-November stay excellent (8.6-9.0/10). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 61-90°F, sea at 73-81°F, ideal dry and temperate climate. From May to September drop to 3.7-6.0/10 due to extreme heatwave (82-108°F, sometimes 122°F+ with oppressive coastal humidity on the Muscat side). The southern Dhofar region (Salalah) paradoxically benefits from the Khareef monsoon June-September = humidity from the Indian Ocean transforms the Dhofari desert into a green tropical landscape = unique on the Arabian peninsula, iconic view of Oman, attracts millions of Gulf tourists in summer to escape the heatwave. Highlights: Muscat capital (already covered by BDW = preserved Arab atmosphere + Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque iconic), Nizwa 1h30 away (former capital of the sultanate 17th century, Nizwa fort 17th century with huge iconic copper cannon, traditional date market + goats Friday morning), Bahla (UNESCO 1987, preserved oasis with banco fort), Jabrin Castle 17th century (one of Oman's most beautiful palaces), Wadi Shab 2h away (spectacular wadi with iconic turquoise natural pools visual of Oman, hiking + swimming), Wadi Bani Khalid (another major wadi), Wahiba Sands Desert 3h away ("Sharqiya Sands", desert with spectacular red sand dunes, night in a Bedouin camp with dinner-stargazing + 4×4 dune-bashing iconic), Sur (east coast fishing port, traditional wooden "dhow" boat building, Omani maritime heritage), Ras Al Jinz Beach (protected reserve for green turtle laying May-October, one of the world's sites for observation, iconic night experience), Salalah in the south Dhofar (already covered), Musandam Peninsula at the extreme north ("Norway of Arabia", spectacular Arab fjords, dolphins, accessible via Khasab from Emirates Fujairah 1h by car), Jebel Akhdar ("Green Mountain", massif at 6,600 ft, spectacular panoramas, iconic cultivated terraces of Damascena roses producing Omani rose water, exclusive Anantara + Alila 5*+ hotels), Jebel Shams (9,900 ft Oman's highest point, "Grand Canyon of Oman" Wadi Ghul = spectacular canyon 3,300 ft deep). Activities: Sea of Oman diving top globally with Daymaniyat Islands in Muscat (protected marine reserve, seasonal whale sharks), desert safari Wahiba Sands, Musandam dhow cruise 1-2 days iconic, Jebel Shams hiking. Try shuwa (lamb cooked 24h in a pit oven, Omani culinary iconic for Eid Al Adha), majboos (spiced rice), Omani halwa (iconic traditional confectionery), Omani Khalas dates, Omani coffee with cardamom + saffron + rose water, halwa with pomegranate. Logistics: Muscat airport (MCT), accessible directly from Europe via Oman Air or Emirates Doha-Dubai. Audience: Europeans in the majority (Germans + British + French), more traditional and preserved atmosphere than Dubai-Abu Dhabi, nicknamed "Norway of the Gulf" for its grandiose natural landscapes and respectful, sustainable tourism.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
