Context

Our verdict

Guatemala, country in Central America between Mexico and Honduras-Salvador: 17.6 million inhabitants. The most populated country in Central America, the cradle of classical Maya civilisation (Tikal 2nd-9th century), 60% Maya indigenous population = the highest Maya concentration in the world. Capital Guatemala City. Country marked by the civil war 1960-1996 (200,000 deaths including Ixil genocide 1982 under dictator Efraín Ríos Montt = convicted for genocide 2013, freed for senile dementia died 2018), high crime but exceptional landscapes. March is my favorite month (9.2/10): 57-81°F, 15% rain, this is the ideal dry season thanks to altitude. February stays excellent (8.9/10). From November to April, it's the dry season "verano": 55-82°F, temperate climate on the high plateaus thanks to altitude (Guatemala City = 4,900 ft, Antigua = 5,000 ft, Lake Atitlán = 5,100 ft), this is the tourist high season. From May to October, it's the rainy season: 60-84% rainfall, but stable temperatures 59-79°F, greener landscapes (the most visually beautiful season). Highlights: Antigua Guatemala (UNESCO 1979, former capital of the Kingdom of Guatemala = Spanish viceroyalty of Central America 1543-1773, one of the best-preserved Spanish colonial cities in the world, ruins of baroque churches and monasteries destroyed by earthquakes but preserved as is, unique atmosphere paved-bougainvillea-volcanoes in the background = Volcán de Agua 12,300 ft + Volcán de Fuego 12,300 ft + Volcán Acatenango 13,000 ft, 2-day Acatenango hike bivouac panoramic on Fuego in permanent eruption), Tikal (UNESCO 1979, one of the largest Maya sites in the world, capital of a great classical Maya kingdom 2nd-9th century, 6 sq miles of pyramids in the middle of the Petén jungle, iconic Templo I Gran Jaguar 150 ft + Templo IV 210 ft, exceptional experience early access 6am for howler monkeys and birds, mystical atmosphere unique in the world), Lago Atitlán ("the most beautiful lake in the world" according to Aldous Huxley 1934, alpine volcanic lake surrounded by 3 ideal cone volcanoes + 12 indigenous Maya tzutujil-cakchikel villages preserved on its shores accessible only by lancha, Panajachel = main tourist village, San Pedro La Laguna = backpackers, Santa Cruz = more traditional, San Juan La Laguna = Maya tzutujil textile craftsmanship), Chichicastenango (iconic Maya market Thursdays-Sundays, one of the most colorful and spiritual in Latin America, unique atmosphere Maya-Catholic syncretism with shaman rituals in front of the church on the steps), Quiriguá (UNESCO 1981, Maya site with sculpted stelae the highest in the Maya world 26 ft), Semuc Champey (iconic natural turquoise waterfalls in the jungle of Alta Verapaz, swimming in superimposed natural pools 6 levels), Pacaya Volcano (active volcano accessible 1h from Guatemala City, 4h round trip hike to approach incandescent lava flows), Livingston (on the Caribbean coast, unique Garifuna Afro-Caribbean community, accessible only by boat). Safety: Guatemala high crime in Guatemala City (frequent armed robberies, gangs), but Antigua and Lake Atitlán are safe for tourists. Try pepián (iconic spicy stew), kak'ik (Maya turkey soup), Guatemalan tamales, fiambre (iconic cold salad 1 November All Saints), Antigua/Cobán/Huehuetenango coffee (among the best coffees in the world), Zacapa Centenario rum (one of the great world premium rums, awarded). Guatemala City La Aurora airport (GUA), Flores Mundo Maya airport (FRS) for Tikal.
