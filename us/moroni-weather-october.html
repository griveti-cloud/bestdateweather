Context

Our verdict

Moroni, capital of the Union of the Comoros in the Indian Ocean north-west of Madagascar: 65,000 inhabitants on the island of Grande-Comore (Ngazidja). Archipelagic country of 4 islands of which 3 are independent (Grande-Comore, Anjouan, Mohéli) and 1 is French (Mayotte) which refused independence by referendum in 1974, a permanently tense diplomatic issue with France. Africa's poorest island country, chronic political instability (20+ coup attempts since 1975), little tourism. Population a cultural blend Arab-Bantu-Malagasy, Sunni Muslim religion 99%. October is my favorite month (8.2/10): 73-84°F, 47% rain, this is the end of the dry season. May-September stay excellent (7.8-8.1/10). From May to October, it's the dry season "Kashkazi": 70-84°F, sea at 77°F, this is the tourist high season. From November to April, it's the cyclonic season "Kusi": 50-84% rainfall, real cyclone risk but the Comoros are less affected than neighbouring Madagascar. Highlights: Medina of Moroni (traditional old town with narrow paved alleys, preserved Arab-Swahili atmosphere, mosques, traditional cloths), Friday Mosque (Badjanani, 17th century, one of the oldest mosques in the Comoros, iconic white façade), Volga (main square by the port), Central Market (spices, tropical fruits, ylang-ylang = the Comoros' iconic flower, producing 80% of the world's ylang-ylang essential oil used in French perfumes Chanel N°5/Guerlain), Presidential Palace Beit-Salam (exterior visit), Iconi 6 miles away (former pre-colonial historic capital, sultanic ruins, spectacular cliff), Itsandra Beach and Galawa Beach in the north (white-sand beaches with bougainvilleas, little frequented by international tourism). Karthala volcano (7,700 ft, one of the world's largest active volcanoes, last eruption 2007, demanding 7-9h ascent from Boboni village, 360° panorama over the archipelago + Madagascar visible on clear days, summit caldera 2 miles in diameter), Trou du Prophète ("Bangwa Kuni", natural pool in the volcanic lava, swimming), Salt Lake (volcanic crater turned into a salt-water lake). Inter-island excursions: Anjouan 1h by plane or ferry (greener island, ylang-ylang plantations, round island "perfume island"), Mohéli (the smallest and most preserved, marine park with green turtle laying + dolphins + humpback whales July-October), Mayotte 30 min by plane (French territory, already covered by BDW, sensitive political subject on the Comorian side). Try vanilla lobster (Comorian iconic blending Arab spices + Malagasy vanilla), coconut chicken, Comorian samosa (Indian heritage), ladu (coconut-almond cake), local coffee with cardamom (Arab heritage), Indian chai tea. Logistics: Moroni Prince Said Ibrahim airport (HAH), flights via Nairobi/Addis Ababa 12h, from Mayotte 30 min, very basic tourist infrastructure.
