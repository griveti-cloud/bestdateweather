Context

Our verdict

Tromsø, Norwegian town beyond the Arctic Circle: 80,000 inhabitants 215 miles north of the Arctic Circle, the largest town at this latitude in the world. Nicknamed the "Paris of the North" in the 19th century for its refinement compared to the polar explorers (Amundsen, Nansen were based here). July is my favorite month (7.1/10): 50-63°F, 47% rain, this is the midnight sun (from 18 May to 27 July, no sunset for 2 months), hiking, fishing, whale watching (orcas in November-January in the neighbouring fjords). From June to August, expect 45-63°F: summer high season. From November to March, expect 18/30°F and 50-55% precipitation often as snow: this is the tourist high season for the Northern Lights (Tromsø is on the "aurora belt", visible 200 nights/year when sky clear), polar night from 27 November to 15 January (no sun above the horizon, but magnificent blue light 4-5h per day). Hotel prices x3 in winter high season. Highlights: Arctic Cathedral (Ishavskatedralen, 1965, iconic modern architecture visible from the town), Fjellheisen Cable Car (Storsteinen 1,400 ft, panorama over the town and the islands, 4-min ascent), Polaria (polar museum, seals), Polar Museum (history of Amundsen explorations, expeditions to the pole), Tromsø Bibliothek (modern architecture). Activities: Northern Lights chase by bus or minivan (3-5h, $110-$220), husky dog sledding (2h initiation or 1-7 day expedition), whale safari (from Skjervøy in November-January, orcas and humpback whales), night in a Sami cabin, snowshoeing, skiing at Tromsø Alpinpark. Try bidos (reindeer stew, Sami dish), salmon, whale (controversial). Direct flight Oslo in 2h.
