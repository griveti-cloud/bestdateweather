Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

The Maldives are the ultimate paradise archipelago: 1192 coral islands across 26 atolls in the Indian Ocean, of which 200 are inhabited and 150 turned into private resorts. The world's flattest country (max altitude 8 ft). From January to April (6.9/10): 79-84°F, 35-54% rain, sea at 82°F, this is the high dry season, the north-east dry monsoon period. The Maldives never go above 7/10 (constant equatorial climate). From May to November, it's the south-west wet monsoon: 46-76% rainfall in brief but intense showers, sometimes rough sea. From December to March, it's peak tourist season: hotel prices x2-x3, strong demand (European school holidays). Logistics: Direct flight Paris-Malé in 10h. From Malé, transfer to the resort: seaplane for distant atolls (200-500$/person return, scenic flight in itself), speedboat for nearby atolls (50-100$), domestic flight + speedboat for the furthest atolls. Private resorts: 1 island = 1 resort, prices from $330/night (basic) to $5,500+/night (Soneva Jani, Cheval Blanc Randheli, One&Only Reethi Rah). Overwater bungalows are the iconic, diving and snorkelling among the world's best (manta rays, whale sharks, turtles). Malé (capital) at 200,000 inhabitants, possible stopover. Maafushi = local island accessible by public ferry from Malé (budget alternative). Try mas huni (local breakfast), garudhiya (fish soup), roshi.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
