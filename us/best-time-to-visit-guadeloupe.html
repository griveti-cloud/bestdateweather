Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Guadeloupe, French overseas department in the Lesser Antilles: 660 sq miles, 380,000 inhabitants, butterfly-shaped archipelago (mountainous Basse-Terre to the west + flatter Grande-Terre to the east). January is my favorite month (8.3/10): 73-82°F, 50% rain, sea at 81°F, this is the carême (dry season). From December to April, it's the tourist high season: 73-84°F, 50-59% rainfall in brief showers, hotel prices x2-x3 ( From July to October, it's the active hurricane season ("hivernage"): 65-83% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Maria 2017 caused major damage), but stable temperatures (75-88°F). May-June and November are the best shoulder seasons: reduced prices, moderate crowds. Basse-Terre highlights (volcano, jungle, hiking): La Soufrière (4,800 ft, 1h30 hike to summit, active sulphur, fumaroles), Carbet Falls, Grande Anse Beach in Deshaies, Plage de la Perle, Cousteau Reserve (diving and snorkelling). Grande-Terre highlights (beaches, more touristy): Pointe-à-Pitre (capital, St-Antoine market), Sainte-Anne (Caravelle beach), Saint-François (marina, golf), Pointe des Châteaux (far east, limestone cliffs). Neighbouring islands: Marie-Galante ("the island of 100 mills"), Les Saintes (Terre-de-Haut, world's most beautiful bay according to UNESCO), La Désirade. Try bokit, accras, colombo, ti-punch, coconut sorbet, Damoiseau and Bologne rums.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
