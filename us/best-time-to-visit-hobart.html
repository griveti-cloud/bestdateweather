Hobart, capital of Tasmania: 250,000 inhabitants, the most southern city in Australia, cool oceanic climate, Mount Wellington (4,200 ft) overlooking the city. January is my favorite month (8.5/10): 55-72°F, 26% rain, austral summer. From June to August (austral winter), expect 39-55°F: cold with sometimes snow on Mount Wellington (rare in town). March-April are my real sweet spots: 52-68°F, reduced tourist crowds, foliage in Tasmania. Highlights: MONA (Museum of Old and New Art, one of the most avant-garde contemporary art museums in the world, access by catamaran ferry from the center), Mount Wellington (road or Pinnacle Track on foot), Salamanca Market (Saturday morning, historic market on Salamanca Place), Battery Point (19th-century historic district). Tasmania excursions: Cradle Mountain and its Lake St Clair National Park (Overland Track 6 days), Bay of Fires (east coast, beaches with spectacular orange rocks), Wineglass Bay (Freycinet National Park), Port Arthur (former UNESCO penal colony). Try Tasmanian salmon, oysters, scallops, Tasmanian whisky (Sullivans Cove). Direct flight Sydney/Melbourne in 1h30. Tasmania is becoming a major food&drink destination.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
