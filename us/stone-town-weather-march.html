Context

Our verdict

Stone Town, historic capital of the Zanzibar archipelago in Tanzania on Unguja island: UNESCO 2000 for its stone city center, architectural blend unique in the world Swahili-Arab-Indian-European. Historic heart of the slave and spice trade between Africa, Arabia and India from the 10th to the 19th century. August is my favorite month (8.2/10): 73-82°F, 55% rain, this is the ideal dry season, sea at 79°F. From June to October, it's the tourist high season: 73-84°F, dry climate, kuzi wind (SE monsoon). From March to May, it's the long rainy season ("masika"): 64-93% rainfall, many hotels close, ferries to the mainland reduced. From November-December, it's the short rainy season ("vuli"): 67-77% rainfall, but brief showers. Highlights: Old Fort (17th-century Portuguese converted by the Omanis), House of Wonders (former 19th-century sultan's palace, the 1st electricity and 1st lift in East Africa, closed for renovation since 2015 partial collapse), Sultan's Palace (Beit el-Sahel), Aga Khan Mosque, Christ Church Cathedral (Anglican, built on the former slave market, altar on the very site of the whipping post, basement memorial of the underground rooms where slaves were crammed), Slave Market Memorial (poignant), Forodhani Gardens (night market every evening, skewers, pweza wa nazi octopus in coconut, mishkaki, Zanzibar sweet soup), Birthplace of Freddie Mercury (the Queen singer was born Farrokh Bulsara in Stone Town in 1946, plaque on the family building), Spice Tour on the plantations 30 min away (Zanzibar remains the world's largest exporter of cloves, plantations of cardamom, vanilla, cinnamon). East coast beaches 1h away: Paje, Jambiani (kitesurf at Paje, big tides). North coast beaches: Nungwi, Kendwa (no tide effect). Try Zanzibar pizza (unique street food), urojo soup ("Zanzibar mix"), Swahili biryani.
