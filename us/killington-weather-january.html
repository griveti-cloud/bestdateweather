Context

Our verdict

Killington, in Vermont, is the largest ski resort in the American East: 6 interconnected mountains, 155 pistes. Particularly long season (October to May in some years) thanks to an advanced snow cannon system. July is my favorite month (7.7/10): 52-68°F, 48% rain, good for the hike to Killington Peak and Camel's Hump. From December to March, expect 3/28°F: ski season. The resort hosts the women's Alpine Ski World Cup in late November (Killington Cup giant slalom), 1st American stage. From April to May and October-November, it's the slow shoulder season, except for the start of the World Cup season. Vermont in autumn (foliage, mid-September to mid-October) is another reason to come: maple colors. Boston 3h by road, NYC 4h30. More accessible to New Yorkers than the Colorado Rockies.

When is the best time to visit Killington?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: March (ski score 8.7/10). For hiking/suiner: March (8.7/10, -2.0°F).
Can you ski in Killington in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.3/10. Cold temperatures (-7°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Killington in suiner?
In March, temperatures average -2.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
