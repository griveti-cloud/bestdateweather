Context

Our verdict

Penang (Pulau Pinang), island in north-western Malaysia in the Andaman Sea: 1.8 million inhabitants for the province (island + Seberang Perai mainland strip), Malaysia's culinary capital by many rankings ("food paradise of Asia" according to Lonely Planet and CNN Travel). Capital Georgetown = UNESCO 2008 for its preserved British colonial, straits-born Chinese and Indian mixed architecture. From January to February are my favorite months (6.9/10): 75-88°F, 55-62% rain, this is the relatively dry season. Penang never tops 6.9/10 (humid equatorial tropical climate year-round). From November to March, it's the tourist high season: 75-88°F. From April to October, it's the wetter season: 66-97% rainfall. Georgetown highlights: Street Art (Georgetown is famous for its murals since 2012 when Lithuanian artist Ernest Zacharevic created several works that went viral "Children on Bicycle", "Boy on Motorbike", "Girl on Swing" — culture trail signposted on Armenian Street, Cannon Street), Cheong Fatt Tze Mansion ("Blue Mansion", 1880 mansion of Chinese magnate "the Rockefeller of the East", unique Sino-colonial architecture, restored 1990, hotel + guided tours), Pinang Peranakan Mansion (Peranakan mansion = straits-born community of mixed Chinese-Malay origin 19th-20th century), Khoo Kongsi (19th-century clan-temple, one of the most impressive outside China), Fort Cornwallis (18th-century British fortlet, founded by Francis Light who colonised Penang for the British Crown in 1786), Eastern & Oriental Hotel ("E&O Hotel", 1885 colonial hotel where Somerset Maugham and Rudyard Kipling stayed, view over the Andaman Sea), Kek Lok Si Temple (Malaysia's largest Buddhist temple, 120 ft Kuan Yin statue, lit up for Chinese New Year), Penang Hill (Bukit Bendera, accessible by Asia's steepest funicular at 27°, panorama Georgetown + strait + Sumatra on a clear day). Penang cuisine = the main reason to come to Penang: char kway teow (flat noodles wok-fried, iconic dish, Sister Char Kway Teow institution since 1976), Penang laksa (assam laksa with tamarind, sardine-based, the spiciest of Malaysian laksas), Hokkien mee (prawn noodle soup), cendol (iced dessert), nasi kandar (Indo-Malay rice with curries), roti canai (Indian flatbread), Penang rojak (fruit salad). Hawker centers = the queen format: Gurney Drive, New Lane, Chulia Street, Lebuh Kimberley. Excursions: Langkawi 2h by ferry, Kuala Lumpur 4h by bus. Penang International airport (PEN).
