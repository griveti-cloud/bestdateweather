Context

Our verdict

Nusa Penida, volcanic island south-east of Bali in Indonesia: 77 sq miles, 50,000 inhabitants. The largest of the 3 Nusa islands (with Lembongan and Ceningan). Recent tourist discovery (2017-2018 thanks to the explosion of Instagram = viral photos of Kelingking Beach in the shape of a Tyrannosaurus-Rex head). Spectacular cliffs, turquoise waters, preserved beaches. From July to August are my favorite months (8.4/10): 70-79°F, 42-44% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From May to October, it's the dry season: 72-81°F, sea at 79-81°F, this is the tourist high season, hotel prices x2-x3. From November to April, it's the rainy season: 72-94% rainfall, sometimes rough sea for the crossing from Bali, possible mists on the cliffs. Highlights: Kelingking Beach ("T-Rex Beach", Nusa Penida's iconic view, dinosaur-head-shaped cliff plunging into a white-sand beach 660 ft below, the most-visited photo-stop panorama, descent possible but steep 30 min via ropes and improvised ladders, dangerous swimming with currents), Angel's Billabong (natural pool in the rock, swimming at low tide only), Broken Beach (Pasih Uug, geological natural arch with turquoise sea in a round cavity), Crystal Bay (crescent-shaped bay, one of the most beautiful beaches, snorkelling with mola mola = seasonal sunfish July-October), Atuh Beach (east side, limestone cliffs, preserved beaches with "Diamond Beach" rocks), Diamond Beach (next to Atuh, recent access via a staircase built in 2018), Peguyangan Waterfall ("Blue Waterfall", 700 descending steps along a cliff to reach a sacred Hindu temple + seaside pools, with turquoise waters). Diving: Nusa Penida is one of Asia's major diving sites: manta rays at Manta Point year-round (gentle, 3-16 ft wingspan, visibility 10-82 ft), mola mola (giant sunfish 1-10 ft, seasonal July-October, Nusa Penida's iconic for divers), Crystal Bay and Toyapakeh main sites. Typical visit: 1-day west tour (Kelingking + Angel's Billabong + Broken Beach + Crystal Bay) or east tour (Atuh + Diamond + Peguyangan), no time to do everything in 1 day so an overnight stay is recommended. Activities: snorkelling, diving, scooter hire (mediocre road condition, rough terrain, difficult driving), cliff swing (panoramic swings above the void). Logistics: fast ferry from Sanur Bali in 30 min (200,000-300,000 IDR ≈ $13-$20), often rough return in the afternoon.
