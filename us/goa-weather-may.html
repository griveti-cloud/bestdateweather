The month in detail

What to expect in May

Spring marks the start of the good season in Goa. Temperatures rise (32.0°F) and tourists have not yet arrived en masse. Expect rain on 34.0% of days — a light raincoat in the bag is recommended. Not the best window, but acceptable if dates are constrained. January (6.9/10) is significantly better if possible.

Context

Our verdict

Goa, former Portuguese colony (1510-1961): India's smallest state (1,430 sq miles), 2 million inhabitants, beaches, Portuguese colonial heritage, fusion cuisine, persistent hippie atmosphere. From November to February (6.9/10): 68-90°F, 0-17% rain, sea at 82°F, this is the tourist high season. Goa never tops 7/10 (climate too hot-humid to reach excellent). From June to September, it's the monsoon: 87-99% rainfall, deserted beaches, many hotels and restaurants close, but also the greenest landscapes and rivers in spate. From October to May, stable dry climate with high season December-January (Europeans wintering), hotel prices x3-x4. North Goa vs South Goa: North (Anjuna, Vagator, Baga, Calangute, Arambol) more festive, Anjuna hippie flea market on Wednesdays, backpacker atmosphere; South (Palolem, Agonda, Patnem) quieter, wild beaches, yoga retreats. Highlights: Old Goa (UNESCO, Basilica of Bom Jesus with relics of Saint Francis Xavier, Sé Cathedral), Panaji/Panjim (capital, Latin Fontainhas quarter), Dudhsagar Falls (1,000 ft waterfall, 4×4 + train tour), Spice Plantations. Try vindaloo, xacuti, fish curry rice (local national dish), pão (Portuguese bread), feni (cashew alcohol).
