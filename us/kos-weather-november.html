Context

Our verdict

Kos, Greek island in the Dodecanese off the Turkish coast: 33,000 inhabitants over 110 sq miles, birthplace of Hippocrates (ancient doctor, creator of the oath) in the 4th century BC. Renowned for its beaches, its Greco-Roman heritage and its European charter atmosphere (British in the majority). June is my favorite month (9.8/10): 72-81°F, 4% rain, sea at 73°F. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (75-86°F, sometimes meltemi wind) and the crowds (British in the majority, hotel prices x3, congested beaches). September-October stay excellent (8.6-9.4/10). From November to March, expect 52-68°F and 28-42% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, many hotels close. Highlights: Asclepieion of Kos (sanctuary of Asclepius 4th century BC on 3 terraces, place of Hippocrates' medical teaching, ancient school of medicine founded here, 2.5 miles from the port), Old Town of Kos (city center, 14th-century Knights of Saint John castle, ancient agora, Hippocrates' Tree = 700-year-old plane tree said to have seen Hippocrates teaching under its shade, more legend than fact), Embros Therma (sulphurous hot springs 122°F flowing into the cold sea, free natural swimming, access by 10-min path), Beaches: Tigaki (7 miles), Paradise Beach (Bubble Beach, volcanic gas bubbles emerge from the sand), Kefalos (Camel Beach), Mastichari (kitesurf), Nikolas at Plakas (one of the most beautiful beaches of Kos), Lambi (urban), Zia (altitude village for the sunset). Excursions: Bodrum (Turkey) 1h by ferry (former Halicarnassus, Mausoleum of Halicarnassus one of the 7 ancient Wonders), Nisyros (active volcano, neighbouring island), Kalymnos (climbing), Pserimos. Try krasotyri (local cheese), Greek baklava, Kos ouzo. Kos airport (KGS) with charters from Europe.
