The month in detail

What to expect in March

March is peak dry season in Luang Prabang. The air is warm and humidity more bearable than in the rainy season. Rain risk remains low (16.0% of days), leaving good room for outdoor activities. Not the best window, but acceptable if dates are constrained. December (9.2/10) is significantly better if possible.

Context

Our verdict

Luang Prabang is the former royal capital of Laos, UNESCO listed: 33 temples, French colonial architecture, the Mekong river. December is my favorite month (9.2/10): 63-82°F, 11% rain, clear skies, golden light on the gilded roofs. November to February are the high season: 61-88°F, little rain. From May to October, it's the monsoon: 35-90% rainfall, but green landscapes. From April to May, it's stifling heat (75-95°F) and agricultural pollution (slash-and-burn). Essential sites: Tak bat ceremony at dawn (procession of 200 monks in saffron robes collecting offerings, around 5:30-6am, very silent, do not disturb), Mount Phousi (300 steps, 360° view at sunset), Wat Xieng Thong (the most beautiful temple, 1560), Royal Palace Museum. Kuang Si Falls 19 miles away (turquoise water, swimming possible, bear sanctuary). Night market textile/handicrafts. Mekong cruise to the Pak Ou caves. Direct flight from Bangkok or Hanoi.
