Context

Our verdict

Cebu, capital of the Visayas region in the Philippines: 1 million inhabitants (3 million in the urban area), the Philippines' 2nd largest city after Manila, former Spanish colonial capital (Magellan arrived here in 1521 before being killed a few weeks later in nearby Mactan). Base for exploring the most beautiful Philippine islands (Bohol, Siquijor, Camotes, Malapascua). January is my favorite month (6.9/10): 75-82°F, 70% rain. Cebu never tops 6.9/10 (humid equatorial tropical climate). From December to May, it's the relatively driest season: 75-86°F, 50-70% rain, this is the tourist high season. From June to November, it's the wetter season: 83-94% rainfall, but stable temperatures (77-86°F), real cyclone risk (the Visayas were devastated by Yolanda/Haiyan in November 2013, 6300 deaths). Cebu City highlights: Magellan's Cross (cross erected by Magellan in 1521, first Christian celebration in the Philippines), Basilica del Santo Niño (the oldest church in the Philippines, 16th century), Fort San Pedro (Spanish 17th century), Taoist Temple, Tops Lookout (panoramic view). Cebu excursions: Kawasan Falls in the south (adventurous canyoneering), Moalboal (sardine run, diving), Oslob (whale shark watching, ethical controversy as whale sharks are fed to stay on site), Bantayan Island in the north. Neighbouring islands by ferry: Bohol (Chocolate Hills, tarsiers, Alona Beach at Panglao), Siquijor (mystical island of the white witches, turquoise beaches), Camotes Islands, Malapascua (thresher shark diving). Try Cebu lechon (the best roasted suckling pig in the Philippines according to Anthony Bourdain), kinilaw (ceviche), puso (rice cooked in woven leaves). Direct flight Cebu from Doha/Singapore (compulsory stopover from Europe).
