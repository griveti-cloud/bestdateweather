Founder · BestDateWeather

Palawan, the largest province of the Philippines: an elongated 280 miles island at the western tip of the archipelago, 800,000 inhabitants. Regularly ranked "most beautiful island in the world" by travel magazines (Travel + Leisure, Condé Nast Traveler). Capital: Puerto Princesa. Turquoise waters, limestone cliffs, hidden lagoons, exceptional marine biodiversity. March is my favorite month (8.1/10): 72-82°F, 55% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From January to April, it's the dry season: 72-84°F, sea at 81°F. From May to October, it's the south-west monsoon (Habagat): 71-95% rainfall, rough sea on the west (El Nido difficult to access), Coron on the north-east side more sheltered, many hotels close. Cyclone season June-November, but Palawan is less affected than the Visayas (Cebu) or Luzon (Manila). Highlights: Puerto Princesa Underground River (UNESCO, one of the 7 New Natural Wonders of the World 2012, navigable underground river of 5 miles one of the world's longest, 50 miles north of Puerto Princesa, access by boat from Sabang after a 2h drive, gravity-defying cave entrance bordered with limestone), El Nido in the north (see dedicated review), Coron in the north (see dedicated review), Port Barton (bohemian alternative to El Nido, quieter, less developed), Honda Bay (1-day excursion from Puerto Princesa with island hopping Cowrie/Luli/Pandan), Sabang Beach (gateway to the Underground River), Nacpan Beach in El Nido (2.5 miles of white sand, one of the world's most beautiful beaches according to Condé Nast). Tubbataha Reefs excursions (UNESCO, marine reserve park among the world's largest, accessible only by liveaboard March-June, diving only, among the best diving spots worldwide). Activities: island hopping (Palawan's main activity, 3-7 islands per day in outrigger bangka, BBQ lunch on a deserted beach, snorkelling), diving at Coron (WWII Japanese wrecks), kayaking in the mangrove at El Nido, climbing on the karst peaks. Try kinilaw (Filipino ceviche), tamilok (mangrove worm, a local delicacy heavily contested by tourists), pancit, balut (fertilised duck egg), pinakbet, Filipino mangoes (the world's best according to Filipinos). Logistics: main airport Puerto Princesa (PPS), secondary airports El Nido (ENI) and Busuanga/Coron (USU), access from Manila in 1h15-1h30 direct flight.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
