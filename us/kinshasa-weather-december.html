Context

Our verdict

Kinshasa, capital of the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC, "Congo-Kinshasa") on the Congo River: 17 million inhabitants in urban area = 2nd largest sub-Saharan African megacity after Lagos Nigeria, projection 35 million by 2050 = future largest city in Africa. Chaotic and frenetic capital ("Kin la Belle" marketing nickname vs reality "Kin la Poubelle" resigned local nickname), facing Brazzaville on the other side of the Congo River. Former Belgian colony "Belgian Congo" 1908-1960 (Congolese genocide under Leopold II 1885-1908 = 10-15 million deaths for rubber exploitation, one of the worst colonial crimes of the 19th century), independence 1960, Mobutu dictatorship 1965-1997, Congo wars 1996-2003 = the deadliest war since WWII with 5.4 million deaths from disease + famine + direct violence. June is my favorite month (9.1/10): 64-84°F, 1% rain, this is the ideal dry season. May-August stay excellent (8.0-8.7/10). From May to September, it's the dry season "cacimbo": 64-86°F, dry and cool tropical climate. From October to April, it's the equatorial humid season: 50-65% rainfall. Kinshasa highlights: Boulevard du 30 Juin (main artery of Kinshasa, tribute to the date of independence 1960), Stade des Martyrs (80,000 places, one of the largest stadiums in Africa), Central Market of Kinshasa ("Marché Central", extraordinary-chaotic-extreme atmosphere), Academy of Fine Arts ("Kinshasa school of painting" = Congolese painting school recognised worldwide, artists Chéri Samba + Moke + Bodys Isek Kingelez), National Museum of the DRC (African ethnographic collection, iconic ritual masks Lega-Kuba-Songye-Pende-Yaka), Symphonie des Mille Et Une Nuits (amusement park), Quay of the Congo River (river atmosphere facing Brazzaville visible 2.5 miles away), Lola ya Bonobo 30 min away ("Bonobo Paradise", sanctuary of the bonobos = great ape species endemic only to the DRC, more genetically close to humans than chimpanzees but much more peaceful), Kinshasa Cathedral (Notre-Dame du Congo). Essential DRC excursions = this is why the DRC is visited despite the dangers: Virunga National Park (UNESCO 1979 the oldest national park in Africa 1925, houses mountain gorillas Mount Mikeno + the Nyiragongo volcano 11,400 ft with permanent lava lake one of the most active in the world, 5h hike to the summit for bivouac above the crater, but Rwandan border very unstable zone and current M23 conflict = inadvisable), Garamba National Park (UNESCO 1980 in danger, last northern white rhinoceros before near-total extinction by poaching), Lola ya Bonobo, Brazzaville 15 min by ferry (unique world iconic, see Brazzaville). Safety: increased France Diplomacy vigilance for Kinshasa, extreme urban crime (frequent armed robberies), current M23 + ADF + other armed groups wars in Kivu (east of the DRC Rwanda-Uganda border) = the 2nd largest humanitarian crisis in the world after Sudan, very inadvisable travel except professional escort, do not attempt Virunga without experienced organization. Try moambé (iconic Congolese culinary, chicken or fish in red palm nut sauce), saka saka (cassava leaves), pondu, mikate (Congolese beignets), thomson (fish), cassava fufu, Primus + Skol Congolese beers. Kinshasa N'Djili airport (FIH).
