Kampala, capital of Uganda: 1.7 million inhabitants in urban area on 7 hills ("7 hills of Kampala"), 22 miles north of Lake Victoria, the largest lake in Africa. Temperate equatorial altitude climate (3,900 ft) year-round. January is my favorite month (8.4/10): 64-81°F, 43% rain, end of the 1st dry season. From June to August and December-February, these are the 2 dry seasons: 63-81°F, 40-64% rain, the most pleasant periods. From March to May and September-November, these are the 2 rainy seasons: 67-88% rainfall, but brief afternoon showers then clear sky. Year-round, stable temperatures (63-82°F thanks to altitude). Highlights: Tombs of the Buganda Kings at Kasubi (UNESCO 2001, burial place of the last 4 Buganda kings 15th-20th century, wood and thatch structures, partially destroyed by fire in 2010 reconstruction in progress), Cathedral of Uganda Martyrs Namugongo (9 miles away, place of execution of the 22 Ugandan martyrs Christianised in 1885-1887 under King Mwanga II, basilica pilgrimage 3 June Martyrs Day), Saint Paul Namirembe Cathedral (the oldest Anglican cathedral in Uganda 1919), Gaddafi National Mosque (financed by Muammar Gaddafi 2007, 360° panorama from the minaret), Owino Market (one of the largest markets in East Africa), Old Taxi Park Quarter (absolute and tourist chaos of matatus), Independence Monument. Excursions: Lake Victoria 30 min away, Source of the Nile at Jinja (the place where the Nile officially leaves Lake Victoria, 62 miles east, Class V whitewater rafting on the Nile rapids = among the best in the world), Bwindi Impenetrable Forest (UNESCO, 9h away, mountain gorillas, one of the 2 last areas with Rwanda and DRC). Safety: Kampala is globally safe, vigilance in the rural areas of the north (former pacified LRA areas) and DRC border. Try matoke (cooked plantain, national dish), luwombo (dish in banana leaf), rolex (omelette in chapati), waragi (local gin).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)

Frequently asked questions

What is the best time to visit Kampala?
January is the best month with 81°F highs, 64°F lows, 11.9h of sunshine and 43.0% rainy days. The months of January & February offer comparable conditions.
How many hours of sunshine does Kampala get in January?
Kampala averages 11.9 sunshine hours per day in January. It is the sunniest month with a climate score of 8.7/10.
What is the rainiest month in Kampala?
April is the most unfavourable month with April has the lowest climate score of the year and the lowest climate score of the year.
What are the temperatures at night in Kampala in January?
In January, overnight lows reach 64°F, while afternoon highs average 81°F.
Is Kampala good in winter?
In winter (December-February), Kampala scores 8.5/10 on average — Acceptable conditions for cultural visits.. The best month overall remains January with a score of 8.7/10.
When should you avoid Kampala?
April is the most difficult month to visit Kampala, with April has the lowest climate score of the year. Climate conditions are at their lowest of the year during this period.
