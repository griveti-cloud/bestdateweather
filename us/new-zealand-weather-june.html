Context

Our verdict

New Zealand is one of the most beautiful countries in the world: 5 million inhabitants for 103,500 sq miles (metropolitan France makes 213,100 sq miles), 30 times more sheep than inhabitants. January-February are my favorite months (8.1-8.2/10): 59-68°F average national, austral summer, long days, Great Walks (Milford Track, Routeburn Track, Tongariro Alpine Crossing) accessible. From June to August (austral winter), expect 39-55°F: cold with snow at altitude, this is ski season (Queenstown, Wanaka, Mt Ruapehu). March-April and October-November are the best compromises. "4 seasons in one day" climate: plan a warm/waterproof jacket even in midsummer. Top sites: Milford Sound (Fjordland), Aoraki/Mount Cook (12,200 ft, highest summit), Lake Tekapo (Church of the Good Shepherd, Dark Sky starry sky), Franz Josef and Fox glaciers, Rotorua/Taupo (geysers), Wellington, Auckland, Bay of Islands, Hobbiton, Wanaka. Logistics: plan 3-4 weeks minimum to see the essentials (2 islands), road trip in motorhome or car (left-hand), reasonable distances. Domestic flights: Air NZ, Jetstar. Direct flight Paris-Auckland in 24-26h (via Singapore or Doha). Reversed seasons southern hemisphere.
