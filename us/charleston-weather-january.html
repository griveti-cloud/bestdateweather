Context

Our verdict

Charleston, in South Carolina: 150,000 inhabitants, English colonial city (1670), preserved historic center, major role in slavery and the Civil War. Regularly ranked among the most beautiful cities in the USA. May is my favorite month (8.7/10): 68-81°F, 38% rain, end of spring with azaleas and magnolias in full bloom. April-June are the tourist high season: 61-84°F, 24-54% rain. From June to September, it's the cyclone season (US east coast): 38-64% rainfall, oppressive humidity (Charleston can hit records of feels-like heat). From December to February, expect 43-63°F and 28-32% rain: mild for the south, low tourist footfall. October-November are my real sweet spots: 52-77°F, clear skies after the cyclone season. Highlights: Old town (200 colonial houses pre-1860 preserved, more than any other US city), Battery Park and Rainbow Row (colorful houses, iconic photo), Old Slave Mart Museum (former slave market, memorial), Fort Sumter (first shot of the Civil War in 1861, ferry from Liberty Square), Magnolia Plantation and Drayton Hall (former plantations 30 min away, sensitive but educational topic), Boone Hall Plantation (centuries-old oak avenue used in The Patriot and Forrest Gump). Try shrimp and grits (Lowcountry dish), she-crab soup, fried green tomatoes, sweet tea. 1h from Hilton Head Island. Direct flight from NY/DC/Boston.
