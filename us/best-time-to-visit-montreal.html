Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Montreal is the 2nd largest French-speaking city in the world after Paris: island at the confluence of the Saint Lawrence, mix of French/English/multicultural. July is my favorite month (8.7/10): 64-81°F, 39% rain, festivals (Montreal International Jazz Festival early July, Just for Laughs mid-July, Osheaga music early August), terraces everywhere. From December to March, expect 10/39°F and 39-44% precipitation (often snow): a real winter with -4°F and blizzards possible. The underground city (RÉSO, 21 miles of tunnels) makes total sense. Quebec Carnival 3h away. September-October are my real sweet spots: 45-72°F, spectacular foliage at Mont-Royal, Mont-Tremblant 1h30 away. Highlights: Old Montreal (Place Jacques-Cartier, Notre-Dame), Mont-Royal (Belvédère Kondiaronk for the view), Jean-Talon Market (North America's largest), Plateau Mont-Royal (characteristic external staircases), Mile End (Jewish bagels). Try bagel at St-Viateur or Fairmount, smoked meat at Schwartz's, poutine, tourtière, maple syrup (sugar shacks in March). Efficient metro.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
