Context

Our verdict

Belize (formerly British Honduras), small English-speaking country in Central America between Mexico and Guatemala on the Caribbean coast: 410,000 inhabitants. Only English-speaking country in Central America (British colonial heritage 1862-1981), former pre-Columbian Maya territory. Renowned worldwide for the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef (2nd largest coral reef in the world after the Australian, 185 miles long off Belize) and the Great Blue Hole. February is my favorite month (8.1/10): 68-82°F, 57% rain, this is the ideal dry season. January stays excellent (8.0/10). From December to April, it's the tourist high season: 68-90°F, sea at 79°F, hotel prices x2-x3. From June to October, it's the active cyclonic season: 79-84% rainfall, real hurricane risk (Belize on the Caribbean "Hurricane Alley", Hurricane Hattie 1961 = 300 deaths devastating). Highlights: Great Blue Hole (circular blue hole 980 ft diameter 410 ft deep off Ambergris Caye, karstic submerged formation at the end of the last glaciation, popularised by Jacques-Yves Cousteau who declared it "one of the 10 best diving sites in the world" in 1971, diving reserved for advanced divers due to depth 130 ft+, lunar décor with underwater stalactites + reef sharks, world iconic aerial photo, boat-diving tour 250-350 USD/day from Ambergris Caye), Ambergris Caye (the largest island in Belize, main tourist destination, town San Pedro "La Isla Bonita" inspiration of Madonna's 1986 song, diving and snorkelling on the reef + Hol Chan Marine Reserve), Caye Caulker (the other famous island, calmer and bohemian, local motto "Go Slow", reggae-backpacker atmosphere), Tobacco Caye and South Water Caye (more remote islands), Belize Barrier Reef (UNESCO 1996, in danger 2009-2018 then saved by protection measures), Caracol (largest Maya archaeological site in Belize 3 sq miles, classical 2nd-9th century, Caana pyramid 140 ft artificial highest point of Belize, difficult access in the jungle), Xunantunich ("the maiden of stone", more accessible Maya site with El Castillo pyramid 130 ft), Lamanai (Maya site in jungle accessible by boat), Altun Ha (Maya site), Cayo District (interior region mountain pine ridge, jungle, waterfalls, ATM Cave caves famous for Maya skeleton crystals = remains of human sacrifices preserved in the cave covered with crystalline calcite, unique world adventure experience), Hopkins and Placencia (Garifuna coastal villages, unique Afro-Caribbean culture). Garifuna = unique people in Belize-Honduras-Guatemala = descendants of shipwrecked Africans mixed with indigenous Caribs in the 17th century, Garifuna language UNESCO intangible heritage 2008. Diving: Belize is one of the top world destinations with Great Blue Hole + Mesoamerican Reef + Lighthouse Reef + Turneffe Atoll (one of the only coral atolls outside the Indian-Pacific Ocean). Activities: diving, snorkelling, kayaking, jungle trekking, ATM Cave (Actun Tunichil Muknal, one of the most extreme tourist adventures in the world = partially submerged Maya underwater cave to swim through, in the company of sacrificed skeletons), cave tubing. Try rice and beans (iconic national dish with coconut milk), conch ceviche, fry jacks, cohune cabbage, hudut (Garifuna plantain mash), Belikin beer (national beer brewed locally), local One Barrel rum. Belize Goldson airport (BZE).
