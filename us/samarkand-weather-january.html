Context

Our verdict

Samarkand, legendary city of the Silk Road in Uzbekistan: 540,000 inhabitants. UNESCO 2001 for its historic center ("Samarkand - Crossroads of Cultures"), capital of Tamerlane Timur the Lame's empire 1370-1405. Islamic architectural heritage among the most spectacular in the world (turquoise blue mosaics and gilded geometric Timurid iconic). May and September are my favorite months (9.2-9.3/10): 57-86°F, 3-25% rain, ideal temperatures before/after the summer heatwave. From April to May and September-October, these are the best seasons: 46-86°F, ideal dry climate. From June to August, it's the heatwave: 66-97°F, 1-5% rain (arid continental climate, Kyzyl Kum desert nearby). From November to March, expect 30/61°F and 17-39% rain: continental cold, snow possible. Highlights: Registan Square ("the sandy place", one of the most spectacular squares in the world, ensemble of 3 madrasas of the 15th-17th centuries: Ulugh Beg 1417, Sher-Dor 1636 "of the iconic tiger lions" postcard image of Uzbekistan, Tilya-Kori 1660 "covered in gold" the most richly decorated), Shah-i-Zinda Necropolis (12th-15th century funeral avenue, one of the most beautiful Islamic architectural ensembles in the world, mausoleums with blue mosaics, place of pilgrimage), Gour Emir Mausoleum ("Tomb of the emir", burial place of Tamerlane + his sons, typical Samarkand turquoise blue dome 120 ft), Bibi-Khanym Mosque (1399-1404, formerly the largest mosque in the world built by Tamerlane in honour of his favorite wife), Ulugh Beg Observatory (1429, 130 ft sextant discovered in 1908, Tamerlane's grandson calculated the length of the astronomical year with modern precision). Excursions: Bukhara 4h by train (UNESCO, see dedicated review), Khiva 11h away (UNESCO, see dedicated review), Tashkent capital (already covered). Try Uzbek plov (rice pilaf with mutton, national dish), shashlik, manty, samsa (fried samosa), Karakum sauerkraut, Chinese green tea.
