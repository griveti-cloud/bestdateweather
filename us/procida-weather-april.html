Context

Our verdict

Procida, the smallest of the three islands of the Gulf of Naples (Procida, Ischia, Capri): 2 sq miles, 10,000 inhabitants, fishing island preserved from mass tourism, typical pastel houses of Marina Corricella. Italian Capital of Culture 2022 (the first time an island has received this title). July is my favorite month (9.4/10): 73-84°F, 8% rain, sea at 75°F, ideal temperatures. August stays excellent (9.1/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 68-84°F, but Procida stays much less frequented than Capri or Ischia (Italian tourists in the majority, Neapolitan weekenders, few foreigners), hotel prices x2-x3. From November to March, expect 46-59°F and 30-48% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, many hotels close, island almost deserted (4000 permanent inhabitants). Highlights: Marina Corricella (iconic fishing port, pink-yellow-blue pastel houses, one of the most famous postcard images of Italy, popularised by The Talented Mr Ripley 1999 and Il Postino 1994), Terra Murata (medieval citadel at the highest point 300 ft, panoramic view), Abbey of San Michele Arcangelo, Marina Grande (arrival port for ferries from Naples), Chiaiolella Beach (the most famous, volcanic black sand), Pozzo Vecchio Beach ("Postino Beach" where Massimo Troisi shot the film). Excursions: Vivara (islet linked by a bridge, nature reserve), Ischia 25 min by ferry (hot springs). Try spaghetti alle vongole, lemon cake (Procidan lemons, smaller than Amalfi), insalata di limoni (yes, salad of peeled Procidan lemons). Logistics: Procida 1h from Naples by ferry (departures from Beverello port), 25 min by hydrofoil. No car needed (island walkable in 1h).
