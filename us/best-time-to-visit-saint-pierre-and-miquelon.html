Saint Pierre and Miquelon, French overseas community in the North Atlantic off Canadian Newfoundland: 6000 inhabitants on 93 sq miles spread over 8 islands of which 2 main inhabited (Saint-Pierre 2 sq miles + Miquelon-Langlade 83 sq miles). Only French territory in North America = vestige of the French Empire of Canada lost by the Treaty of Paris 1763, kept after the other colonies for the fishing rights in the Newfoundland Grand Banks region. Unique atmosphere of Brittany-Normandy of the North Atlantic with preserved 19th-century French colonial architecture. During the American Prohibition 1920-1933, Saint-Pierre was the alcohol smuggling base to USA = legendary local enrichment (Al Capone allegedly came here). August is my favorite month (7.5/10): 54-64°F, 32% rain, ideal temperatures (North Atlantic summer). July stays excellent (7.4/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 43-66°F, mild Atlantic temperatures, sea at 54-59°F (very cold), typical Newfoundland fogs frequent. From December to April, expect 14/36°F and 38-49% precipitation often as snow: North Atlantic winter cold, snowy landscapes, difficult access (flights often canceled for fog). Highlights: Saint-Pierre town (6000 inhabitants, the only town of the archipelago, French Normandy 19th-century atmosphere with colorful multi-colored wooden houses typical Newfoundland style, Place du Général-de-Gaulle town center, Saint-Pierre Cathedral 19th-century neo-Romanesque), Arche Museum (historical museum with memory of Prohibition + great fishing), Île aux Marins (5 min by boat, former fishing village abandoned 1965 and preserved as open-air museum = unique memorial atmosphere, accessible May-October), Galantry Lighthouse (signal for ships, panorama), Pointe au Cheval and Pointe du Diamant (wild Atlantic rocky coast), Saint-Pierre Cemetery (unique memorial atmosphere with tombs of sailors of all nationalities who died at sea in the region), Miquelon-Langlade (neighbouring island accessible by 1h ferry, wild and preserved atmosphere, semi-wild horses in the Grande Barachois lagoon, observation grey seals + dolphins, wild hike 16 miles), Cap aux Morts (Miquelon north cape with lighthouse). Activities: whale and seal watching (July-September, humpback whales + minke whales + dolphins + grey seals in the strait), big game fishing (cod + halibut + tuna, Grand Banks heritage), wild hiking, sea kayaking. Population: 100% French-speaking, French Hexagone atmosphere in miniature in the heart of the North Atlantic, preserved Norman-Breton accent. No special currency = uses euro since 2002, very high import prices. Try Saint-Pierraise tourtière (signature local fish pie), traditional salted cod (Grand Banks heritage), American lobster (July-August season), Saint-Pierraise blood pudding, imported French cheeses, French wines, Saint-Pierraise beer. Logistics: Saint-Pierre Pointe-Blanche airport (FSP), Air Saint-Pierre flights from Halifax Canada (1h30) or Montreal (1h45) + seasonal Paris CDG flights (5h30 charter Air France summer), passage often complicated by Atlantic fogs.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
