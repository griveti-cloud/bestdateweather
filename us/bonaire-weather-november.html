Context

Our verdict

Bonaire is the smallest and calmest of the ABC islands: 110 sq miles, 20,000 inhabitants, declared "Marine Park" in 1979 (one of the first in the world). Outside the cyclonic zone. March is my favorite month (8.6/10): 77-82°F, 39% rain, sea at 79°F. From September to December, expect 79-88°F with 31-72% rainfall. From January to August, stable dry climate. Year-round is workable as outside hurricane zone. Bonaire is above all a diving destination: 86 sites accessible directly from the beach (drive-in diving with roadside markers), full marine reserve, exceptional biodiversity (more than 350 species of fish), among the best spots in the world for "shore diving" (without boat). Highlights: Washington-Slagbaai National Park (40% of the island in the north, desert landscapes, highest point Brandaris 790 ft, 4×4 necessary), Kralendijk (colorful capital, village size), Lac Bay (protected lagoon, kitesurf and windsurf, constant winds), Pekelmeer Sanctuary (pink flamingos, largest nesting site in the southern Caribbean), Salt pans (colored salt marshes pink/blue/white), Klein Bonaire (uninhabited islet accessible by water taxi). Try goat stew, fish soup. Dutch + Papiamento + English. Much less touristic than Aruba or Curaçao, dive resort atmosphere. Direct flight from Amsterdam or via Curaçao.
