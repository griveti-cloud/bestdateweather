Context

Our verdict

Novosibirsk, 3rd city of Russia and capital of Siberia: 1.6 million inhabitants 2,050 miles east of Moscow on the Ob river, on the Trans-Siberian line. Young city (founded in 1893 during the construction of the Trans-Siberian), nicknamed "Scientific Capital of Siberia" thanks to Akademgorodok ("City of Academicians" 1957, Soviet scientific complex with 35 research institutes). June-July are my favorite months (8.3-8.5/10): 54-75°F, 32-39% rain, this is the short continental summer. From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 43-75°F, 31-39% rain, mild climate. From December to February, expect -2/14°F and 25-41% precipitation often as snow: extreme Siberian continental winter (sometimes -40°F, blizzard). Highlights: Opera and Ballet Theater (1945, the largest opera theater in Russia, larger than the Bolshoi in Moscow or the Mariinsky in Saint Petersburg, 1700 seats, characteristic dome), Ascension Cathedral, Lenin Square (with statue of Lenin and bronze of Stalin), Metro Bridge (the longest metro bridge in the world 7,000 ft, connects the 2 banks of the Ob), Akademgorodok (19 miles to the south, Novosibirsk State University NSU, research institutes in mathematics, nuclear physics, geology, archaeology), Trans-Siberian and Railway Museum, Novosibirsk Zoo (one of the largest in Russia). Excursions: Tomsk 4h away (former 19th-century historic capital with unique wooden architecture), Lake Karakol in the Altai 8h away (paradisiacal altitude lake), Belokurikha (Soviet thermal station), Sheregesh ski 5h away in the Kuznetsk Alatau mountains (the main ski resort in western Siberia). Logistics: Novosibirsk 4h by plane from Moscow, 50h by train (the 3rd important stop on the Moscow-Vladivostok Trans-Siberian after Yekaterinburg), Tolmachovo airport. Try Siberian pelmeni (probable origin of Siberia), pirogues, Siberian ukha (Ob fish soup), borscht, vareniki, omul (endemic fish of Lake Baikal), vodka, kvass.
