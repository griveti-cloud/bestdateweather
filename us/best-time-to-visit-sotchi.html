Sochi, Russian seaside resort on the Black Sea coast in Krasnodar Krai: 410,000 inhabitants. Unique humid subtropical climate in Russia (the only one in the country), nicknamed the "Russian Riviera". Hosted the 2014 Winter Olympics (the most expensive in history estimated at 50 billion $, controversy over massive corruption and working conditions), 2018 Football World Cup, F1 until 2021 (canceled following the invasion of Ukraine). August is my favorite month (9.0/10): 72-82°F, 26% rain, sea at 77°F, ideal temperatures. From June to September, it's the seaside tourist high season: 64-82°F, warm sea, beaches packed with Russians (Sochi has been the main Russian summer resort since the Soviet era). From December to February, expect 37-54°F and 43-49% rain: mild winter for Russia (sometimes 59°F in January), but skiing on the neighbouring heights at Krasnaya Polyana (22 miles, 1500-7,500 ft, Rosa Khutor complex of the 2014 Olympics, one of the most modern Russian ski resorts). Sochi-city highlights: Seaside promenade, Riviera Park (since 1898, botanical garden, magnolia avenue), Cathedral of Saint Michael the Archangel (1874-1891, neo-Byzantine), Mount Akhun (2,200 ft, 1936 panoramic tower), Dendrarium (alpine botanical garden), Fisht Olympic Stadium (2018 World Cup), Sochi Park (Russian theme park). Excursions: Krasnaya Polyana 35 min away (skiing, summer hiking), Lago-Naki Plateau, Verticulina Falls, Caucasus hiking. Audience: Sochi remains an essentially Russian destination (Europeans become rare since 2022 sanctions, EU-Sochi direct flights almost non-existent), nostalgic post-Soviet atmosphere still present. Try Georgian-Russian shashlik (close cultural heritage), khachapuri (Georgian cheese bread popular in Sochi), pelmeni, blini, vodka.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
