Context

Our verdict

Hemsedal, Norwegian ski resort in Buskerud in south-eastern Norway: 2300 inhabitants at 700-4,900 ft altitude. One of the main ski resorts in Norway with Trysil and Geilo, paradise of Scandinavian alpine skiing (Norway is the country that invented modern skiing, but paradoxically mainly cross-country not alpine, Hemsedal is an exception with more rugged relief). Renowned for its legendary après-ski ("Scandinavian Aspen") with dynamic nightlife. June-July are my favorite months (7.2-7.5/10): 43-59°F, 44-56% rain, mild temperatures for hiking, long days (6 PM of day at the June solstice, midnight sun possible at higher altitudes). Hemsedal never tops 7.5/10 (cold Scandinavian alpine climate). From June to August, it's the summer high season: 32-59°F, hiking, mountain bike, 9-hole golf course. From December to April, expect 12/30°F and 31-49% precipitation often as snow: ski high season. Exceptionally long ski season: Hemsedal generally has skiable snow from late November until late April (5 months) thanks to the maintained cold climate and altitude. Pistes: 33 miles Hemsedal Skisenter (modest vs Alps: 375 miles 3 Valleys or Portes du Soleil), 56 pistes of which 35% intermediate, 30% expert, altitude 700-4,900 ft, vertical drop 2,600 ft, 21 lifts including 4 detachable chairlifts. iconic pistes: Reidarløypa (the longest red piste 4 miles), Tinden (summit at 4,800 ft, panorama over Hallingdal valley). Off-piste: Hemsedal has 29 sq miles of accessible off-piste with its Hemsedal Adventure Skiing program, skin hiking to reach preserved places. Snowpark: one of the best in Scandinavia. Norwegian particularity: the culture of "Nordic ski touring" Telemark style is still very present in Hemsedal (you'll see many skiers with free heels and flexible knee turns Norwegian iconic, technique invented in neighbouring Telemark province in the 19th century). Legendary après-ski: Hemsedal Skisenter pub, Garasjen, Stadium Bar (surprisingly active night clubs for such a remote resort). Audience: Norwegians on Oslo weekends (3h by car), Swedes (Stockholm 5h by car thanks to the bridge connecting the 2 capitals), Danes thanks to charter flights, few British or Western Europeans. Hotels: Skarsnuten Hotel (4* on the mountain), Skogstad Hotel (3*+ village center 1900), Quality Hotel Hemsedal, $165-$550/night. Try reindeer, elk, fårikål (mutton cabbage, Norwegian national dish), brunost (typical caramelised brown cheese), aquavit (Norwegian eau-de-vie), Norwegian craft beers Lervig/Nogne. Logistics: Hemsedal 3h by car from Oslo, Oslo Gardermoen airport (OSL) then Hemsedal Express bus or car, station relatively far from the main cities (preserved charm but difficulty of access).

Questions

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Hemsedal?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: April (ski score 9.3/10). For hiking/suiner: April (9.3/10, 2.0°F).
Can you ski in Hemsedal in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 7.2/10. Cold temperatures (-5°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Hemsedal in suiner?
In April, temperatures average 2.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.
What is the wettest month in Hemsedal?
October is the wettest month with October has the lowest climate score of the year.
