The month in detail

What to expect in May

Spring marks the start of the good season in Brussels. Temperatures rise (18.0°F) and tourists have not yet arrived en masse. Expect rain on 37.0% of days — a light raincoat in the bag is recommended. A good balance between weather and crowds, even if July (8.3/10) remains theoretically better.

Context

Our verdict

Brussels, Belgian capital and capital of the European Union: 1.2 million inhabitants in urban area, bilingual French/Dutch city, cultural mosaic (1/3 of residents are foreigners). July-August are my favorite months (8.3/10): 57-73°F, 35-36% rain, terraces, Brussels-les-Bains (urban beach on the canal mid-July to mid-August). From November to February, expect 36-50°F and 42-45% rain: mild for Northern Europe, Plaisirs d'Hiver Christmas market mid-November to early January (Grand-Place, Place Sainte-Catherine, Marché aux Poissons). From April to June, expect 41-72°F: spring often rainy but pleasant. Highlights: Grand-Place (UNESCO, one of the most beautiful squares in the world, 15th-century Town Hall, 17th-century Guild Houses), Manneken Pis (60 cm, always disappoints by its smallness), Atomium (330 ft, opened for Expo 1958, lift in the central sphere), Saint Michael and Saint Gudula Cathedral, Sablon Quarter (antique dealers, chocolatiers), European Quarter (European Parliament visitable), Mont des Arts. Museums: Magritte (the most surreal), Royal Museums of Fine Arts, Comic Strip Museum (Tintin, Smurfs, Astérix), Horta Museum (Art Nouveau). Try moules-frites (at Léon since 1893), waterzooi, carbonade flamande, fries (Maison Antoine), Liège waffles, chocolate (Pierre Marcolini, Neuhaus, Godiva), Trappist beer (Westvleteren, Chimay, Orval).
