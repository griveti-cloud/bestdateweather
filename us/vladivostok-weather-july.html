Context

Our verdict

Vladivostok, capital of the Russian Far East on the Sea of Japan: 600,000 inhabitants 5,780 miles from Moscow (the eastern terminus of the Trans-Siberian the longest continuous train journey in the world 6 days/7 nights), 62 miles from the Chinese border and 435 miles from Tokyo. Unique "Russian San Francisco" atmosphere (hills, spectacular bay, suspension bridges, Asian-Russian cultural mix). City closed to foreigners until 1992 (Pacific Fleet military base). July is my favorite month (7.7/10): 64-70°F, 31% rain, end of the cool oceanic season. Vladivostok never tops 7.7/10 (oceanic climate with persistent fogs). From June to September, it's the tourist season: 54-73°F, 29-49% precipitation, mild summer climate. From December to February, expect 7/25°F and 6-11% rain: extreme dry winter cold with glacial Siberian winds, sea sometimes taken by ice (rare). Highlights: Russky Bridge (Russky Bridge, 2012, the longest cable-stayed bridge in the world 3,600 ft main span, one of the symbols of Russian modernisation), Zolotoy Bridge ("Golden Bridge", 2012, on Golden Horn Bay), Russky Island (FEFU university campus 2012, APEC Summit 2012 sites — Vladivostok was previously forbidden to foreigners and the 2012 APEC summit justified massive infrastructure investments), Svetlanskaya Street (main historic center artery, 19th-20th century buildings), Tokarevsky Lighthouse (1904, on peninsula, place of burial of local wild white cats), Funicular (1959, the only one in Russia outside Moscow-Saint Petersburg), S-56 Submarine Museum (WWII submarine transformed into museum), Arseniev Museum. Excursions: Russky Island Aquarium (one of the largest aquariums in the world 2016), Shamora Beach 30 min away (the main urban beach), Land of the Leopard Reserve (62 miles away, last world population of Amur leopard 100 individuals). Logistics: Vladivostok 8h by plane from Moscow, direct flight from Seoul/Tokyo/Beijing. The Trans-Siberian arrives at Vladivostok after 5,780 miles from Moscow (among the most legendary train journeys in the world). Try pelmeni, ukha (fish soup), raw fish and sushis (strong Japanese and Korean influence), Sakhalin salmon, vodka.
