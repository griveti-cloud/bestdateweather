Context

Our verdict

Kazan, capital of the Republic of Tatarstan in Russia: 1.3 million inhabitants 495 miles east of Moscow on the bank of the Volga. Multicultural city unique in Russia: 50% Tatar Muslims + 45% Russian Orthodox cohabit in harmony for 5 centuries (model of religious integration highlighted by the authorities). Historic capital of the Khanate of Kazan (1438-1552 conquered by Ivan the Terrible), 3rd city of Russia for its economic importance. July is my favorite month (8.7/10): 61-77°F, 29% rain, this is the short continental summer. From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 55-77°F, 22-29% rain, pleasant climate. From December to February, expect 9/23°F and 36-38% rain often as snow (3 ft+/season): rigorous continental winter but fairy-tale charm with the snow-covered Kremlin, mixed Russian-Tatar Christmas markets unique. Highlights: Kazan Kremlin (UNESCO 2000, fortified ensemble unique in the world mixing Orthodox and Muslim architecture, 16 hectares), Kul Sharif Mosque (within the Kremlin enclosure, 2005, modern reconstitution of the mosque destroyed by Ivan the Terrible in 1552, 4 minarets of 190 ft, one of the largest in Europe), Annunciation Cathedral (16th century, next to the mosque, symbol of the cohabitation), Söyembikä Tower (17th century, leaning tower 190 ft of the Söyembikä dynasty), Bauman Street (pedestrian artery with historic buildings, restaurants, boutiques), Lake Kaban (legend of the khan's treasure swallowed in its waters), Old Tatar Sloboda Quarter (preserved traditional Tatar architecture), Orthodox Cathedral of Saint Peter and Saint Paul (1726). Excursions: Sviyazhsk (UNESCO 2017, monastic island founded by Ivan the Terrible 1551 as a base to conquer Kazan, accessible by boat or road), Bolghar (UNESCO 2014, ancient capital of the Volga Bulgars 9th-15th century). Logistics: Kazan 1h by plane from Moscow, 11h by night train (Sapsan rapid or traditional train), 2 daily flights. Try chak-chak (Tatar honey-fried dessert), echpochmak (triangular meat-potato turnover), peremyach, kystyby, Tatar tea with milk.
