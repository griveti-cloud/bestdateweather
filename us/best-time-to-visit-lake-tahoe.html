Gilles' view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Lake Tahoe is North America's largest alpine lake (at 6,200 ft altitude, 1,600 ft deep, crystal-clear water). 2 distinct seasons: winter skiing (Heavenly, Squaw Valley/Palisades, Northstar, Kirkwood, one of North America's largest ski areas) and summer paddleboard and hiking. July is my favorite month (9.5/10): 59-81°F, 7% rain, lake at 64-68°F (cool but accessible). From December to March, expect 27/41°F with 32-39% precipitation (often snow): this is ski high season with sometimes record snowfall (the Sierra Nevada can take 5-33 ft over a winter). The roads can close during a storm. From April to May, it's the difficult transition: skiing winding down, summer not yet started, many restaurants in the off-season. South Lake Tahoe (Nevada side) for the casinos, North Lake (California side) for the calm. Tahoe Rim Trail (165 miles) for hikers.
