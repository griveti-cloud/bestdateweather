Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Cape Cod is the arm-shaped peninsula south-east of Boston, Massachusetts: 65 miles long, 350,000 permanent inhabitants (multiplied by 3 in summer), Victorian shingle-clad houses, Atlantic beaches, historic lighthouses. July is my favorite month (8.8/10): 68-79°F, 31% rain, sea at 70°F (still fresh), ferries to the islands. August stays excellent (8.7/10). From June to September, it's the American tourist high season: hotel prices x3-x4, bookings 6 months ahead for the charming inns (Chatham Bars Inn, Wequassett). From December to March, expect 28/45°F and 35-39% precipitation often as snow: 60% of restaurants and hotels close, but calm winter atmosphere. Highlights: Provincetown (P-Town, at the northern tip, LGBT atmosphere since the 70s, ferries to Boston), Cape Cod National Seashore (37 miles of wild Atlantic beaches), Cape Cod Rail Trail (25 miles of cycle path on a former railway line), Lighthouses (Highland Light, Nauset Light, Chatham Light). Islands to discover by ferry: Martha's Vineyard (45 min from Falmouth, former retreat of the Kennedys and Obamas, smart Edgartown), Nantucket (1h15 or 30-min plane from Hyannis, preserved 19th-century whaling atmosphere). Whale watching from Provincetown (late May to October). Try lobster roll (at Mac's Seafood), clam chowder, fried clams (Whole Belly at Kream 'N' Kone), Wellfleet oysters. 2h from Boston by car.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
