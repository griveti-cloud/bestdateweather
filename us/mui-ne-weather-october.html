Context

Our verdict

Mui Ne, fishing village and kitesurf destination on Vietnam's south-east coast: 25,000 inhabitants 125 miles north-east of Saigon in Binh Thuan province. Atypical in Vietnam for its red and white sand dunes at the heart of near-desert landscapes (one of Vietnam's only desert zones). From January to April are my favorite months (6.9/10): 72-90°F, 2-18% rain. Mui Ne never tops 6.9/10 (hot tropical climate, landscapes more than nature). From November to March, it's the kitesurf tourist high season (steady north-east winds 18-25 knots, kitesurf paradise in Asia), 72-86°F. From May to October, it's the monsoon + heat season: 27-77% rainfall, more humid, less consistent kitesurf. Highlights: White Sand Dunes (Bau Trang, 19 miles from Mui Ne, surprising Saharan landscape, view at sunrise 5am, quad or jeep rental), Red Sand Dunes (Doi Hong, closer to the village 3 miles, view at sunset), Fairy Stream (Suoi Tien, stream flowing between red and white rock formations, walk barefoot in 30 cm of water, 30 min return), Mui Ne fishing port (traditional round basket boats, fish market in the morning), Ham Tien (main beach village, hotels), Cham Towers Po Shanu (11th century, vestige of the Cham empire that ruled central Vietnam 2nd-15th century). Activities: kitesurf (one of the world's kite destinations with its sheltered Cap Rouge bay, Cap Diem, steady thermal winds Nov-March, international schools C2Sky, Surfpoint), windsurf, surf (modest waves), jeep or quad hire for the dunes, basket-boat ride. Audience: Russians historically in the majority (up to 50% pre-Covid, some restaurants with menus exclusively in Russian), European kitesurfers, growing European numbers. Excursions: Da Lat 4h by bus (mountains), Saigon 5h away, Phan Thiet 30 min away (provincial capital). Try nuoc mam (fish sauce — Phan Thiet is the world capital of nuoc mam), seafood, banh xeo (pancake), pho. No airport on site, transfers from Saigon (Tan Son Nhat SGN) in 5h.
