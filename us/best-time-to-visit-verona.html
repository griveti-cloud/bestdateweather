Verona, city in Italian Veneto: 260,000 inhabitants at the foot of the pre-Alps, 62 miles from Venice. UNESCO 1999 for its exceptionally preserved Roman and medieval old town. World-famous for Romeo and Juliet (Shakespeare's play, around 1597, set in Verona, but the "Juliet's house" is a 20th-century tourist invention, the Capulet/Montague families are fictional). June is my favorite month (9.0/10): 64-82°F, 36% rain, ideal temperatures before the heatwave. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to heavy heat (68-86°F, suffocating humidity on the plain). From September to October, expect 52-77°F: excellent period, harvests. From November to March, expect 32-55°F and 20-33% rain: damp cold, fog. Highlights: Verona Arena ("Arena" 1st century, the 3rd largest Roman amphitheater after the Colosseum and Capua's, very well preserved, venue of the Verona Opera Festival open-air in June-August, one of the world's largest opera festivals, capacity 15,000 spectators with exceptional natural acoustics), Casa di Giulietta ("Juliet's House", medieval house bought by the city in the 19th century, balcony added in 1936 for tourism, Juliet statue whose right breast fans touch for luck, walls covered in love notes-graffiti), Piazza delle Erbe (ancient Roman square, market since Antiquity, medieval atmosphere), Piazza dei Signori with statue of Dante (who lived in Verona in exile), Castelvecchio (14th-century fortress with crenellated medieval bridge, museum), Basilica San Zeno (12th century, one of the masterpieces of Lombard Romanesque art), Santa Anastasia Church and Duomo, Lamberti Tower (280 ft panoramic view), Castel San Pietro (sunset panorama over the city). Excursions: Lake Garda 30 min away (Sirmione = Catullus's Roman peninsula, Bardolino = wine), Mantua (UNESCO, Renaissance Gonzaga city), Venice 1h by train. Try risotto all'amarone, pastissada de caval (Amarone-braised beef), Verona pandoro (Christmas cake born here), Valpolicella and Amarone wines (special vinification by drying the grapes). Verona-Villafranca airport (VRN).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
