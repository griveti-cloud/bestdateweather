What to expect in March

Spring marks the start of the good season in Naxos. Temperatures rise (17.0°F) and tourists have not yet arrived en masse. Expect rain on 26.0% of days — a light raincoat in the bag is recommended. A good balance between weather and crowds, even if June (9.8/10) remains theoretically better.

Context

Our verdict

Naxos, the largest of the Greek Cyclades: 22,000 inhabitants over 170 sq miles in the heart of the Cyclades. More traditional and less touristy than neighbouring Mykonos or Santorini, greener (interior mountains, Mount Zas 3,300 ft highest point of the Cyclades), beaches among the most beautiful in the Aegean (golden sand). June is my favorite month (9.8/10): 70-82°F, 6% rain, sea at 72°F, meltemi wind that cools things down. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (75-86°F) and the meltemi wind (north wind at 50-43 miles/h in peak, ferries often canceled, rough sea for swimming). September stays excellent (9.3/10). From November to March, expect 50-63°F and 22-41% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, many hotels close, island almost deserted (guardian of Cycladic tradition). Highlights: Portara (monumental marble gate of the unfinished temple of Apollo 6th century BC on Palatia islet linked to the port by a dyke, one of the most famous postcard images of Naxos, view at sunset), Chora of Naxos (old town inside the 13th-century Venetian Kastro, labyrinthine alleys, white houses), Filoti and Apiranthos (altitude villages in the mountains, traditional stone architecture, white marble everywhere — Naxos is the ancient "marble mountain" of the Cyclades), Renowned beaches: Agios Prokopios (one of the most beautiful beaches in Greece according to several rankings), Agia Anna, Plaka (the longest 2.5 miles, wild), Mikri Vigla (kitesurf, windsurf), Alyko (cedars unique in Greece). Excursions: Lesser Cyclades (Koufonisia, Schinoussa, Iraklia = trio of preserved islands south of Naxos, 1h ferry), Mykonos and Santorini 1h30-2h by ferry. Try kitron (lemon liqueur unique in Greece), graviera (cheese), Naxos potatoes (the most renowned in Greece). Naxos airport (JNX, domestic flights) or ferry from Piraeus 5h.
