Jaipur, capital of Rajasthan, is the "Pink City": 3 million inhabitants, fortified old town painted pink in 1876 for the Prince of Wales's visit. A major stop on the Golden Triangle Delhi-Agra-Jaipur. February-March are my favorite months (8.9/10): 55-88°F, 3-9% rain, ideal temperatures, golden light on the walls. From November to February, expect 48-81°F: tourist high season, clear sky. From April to June, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 73-104°F (sometimes 113°F), impossible to visit during the day. From July to September, it's the monsoon: 41-69% rainfall, but more bearable temperatures (75-91°F). Highlights: City Palace (royal palace still inhabited, museum), Hawa Mahal ("palace of the winds", 953 pink-stone openings, iconic photo), Jantar Mantar (UNESCO, 18th-century astronomical observatory with giant instruments), Amber Fort 7 miles away (16th-century hill fortress, climb on elephant-back or by jeep, mirror palace), Nahargarh Fort (panoramic view over the city at sunset), Jal Mahal (lake palace). Bazaars: Johari Bazaar (jewellery), Bapu Bazaar (textiles), semi-precious stones everywhere. Try dal baati churma, laal maas (spicy goat curry), kachori. October-November and March are the best crowd/weather compromises.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
