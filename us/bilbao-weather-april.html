Context

Our verdict

Bilbao, Spain's 4th city and capital of the Basque Country: 350,000 inhabitants, industrial city converted into a cultural capital since the opening of the Guggenheim Museum in 1997 (Frank Gehry architecture, the "Bilbao effect" = global urban catalyst). August is my favorite month (8.6/10): 63-77°F, 30% rain, mild temperatures ("verde" for its wetter climate than Mediterranean Spain). July stays excellent (8.5/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 59-77°F, festivals (Aste Nagusia mid-August, patron saint festival, 9 days of partying, cow run, txosnas Basque tents with sidra and pintxos). From November to February, expect 43-59°F and 47-55% rain: mild for Spain, humid oceanic climate (the Basque Country gets 1200 mm/year). Highlights: Guggenheim Museum Bilbao (Frank Gehry 1997, deconstructivist titanium architecture, "Puppy" giant floral sculpture by Jeff Koons at the entrance, one of Spain's most visited museums), Casco Viejo (old town, Las Siete Calles = 7 medieval streets, Santiago Cathedral, Plaza Nueva with its arcades), Mercado de la Ribera (Europe's largest covered market, Art Deco), Funicular de Artxanda (3 min to Mont Artxanda 740 ft, panoramic view over Bilbao), Vizcaya Bridge at Portugalete (UNESCO, the world's first transporter bridge 1893). Basque Country excursions: San Sebastián 1h away (Donostia, La Concha beach), Guernica 30 min away (bombed by the German air force in 1937, immortalised by Picasso), San Juan de Gaztelugatxe 35 min away (islet with hermitage and 241 steps, Game of Thrones "Dragonstone"). Try pintxos ("Basque tapas" on a slice of bread for $2-$3/piece, bar-hopping in Casco Viejo), bacalao al pil pil, txuleta (1kg rib steak), txakoli (local sparkling white wine poured from above), Basque cider.
