Cuenca, 3rd city of Ecuador in the Andean sierra: 330,000 inhabitants at 8,400 ft altitude, UNESCO listed 1999 for its well-preserved Spanish colonial historic center. Considered the most European city in Ecuador (more than Quito), renowned for its quality of life (one of the favorite destinations of American and Canadian retirees in Latin America, expat community of 5000+ people). August is my favorite month (7.3/10): 46-66°F, 66% rain. Cuenca has a constant climate year-round (stable temperatures 46-68°F thanks to altitude, little seasonal variation), main variations on rainfall. From August to January, it's the relative "dry" season: 46-68°F, 66-83% rain, sun in the morning/afternoon storms typical of the equatorial Andean sierra. From February to July, it's the wetter season: 46-66°F, 73-93% rainfall. Highlights: Centro Histórico (UNESCO, maze of paved alleys, 16th-18th century Spanish colonial architecture), New Cathedral ("Catedral Nueva", Inmaculada Concepción 1885-1967, blue and white domes iconic of Cuenca, panoramic view from the terraces), Old Cathedral (1557, become museum), Plaza Calderón (main square between the 2 cathedrals), Mercado 10 de Agosto (central market, top floor = food court with hornado specialty of Cuenca), Río Tomebamba (river that crosses the city, El Barranco path along with colorful houses suspended on the cliff), Barrio Las Herrerías (blacksmiths' quarter), Museo del Banco Central Pumapungo (Inca-Cañari ruins, ethnobotanical garden), Museo de las Conceptas. Panama Hat industry: Cuenca is in reality the true world capital of the Panama Hat (the famous toquilla straw hat, despite its name is manufactured in Ecuador since the 16th century, exported via the Panama Canal in the 19th century = origin of the misleading name), visits to the Homero Ortega and K. Dorfzaun factories. Excursions: Cajas National Park 30 min away (Andean paramo, 270 glacial lakes, hiking altitude 3000-14,800 ft, llamas), Ingapirca 2h away (the most important Inca ruins in Ecuador, but modest compared to Peru), Cordillera Real. Try cuy (roast guinea pig, traditional Andean dish), hornado (roast pork à la Cuenca, the most famous in Ecuador), locro de papas (potato soup), morocho (white maize drink).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
