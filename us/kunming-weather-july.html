Context

Our verdict

Kunming, capital of Yunnan province in south-western China at 6,200 ft altitude: 8 million inhabitants. "City of Eternal Spring" (春城 "Chūnchéng") = one of the only Chinese cities with temperate climate year-round thanks to tropical altitude, one of the most climatically pleasant Chinese destinations, cultural capital of Yunnan ethnic minorities = 26 different ethnicities out of 56 officially recognised in China. April is my favorite month (8.5/10): 54-72°F, 12% rain, ideal temperatures. March-May stay excellent (8.0-8.4/10). From March to May and September-November, these are the best seasons: 46-73°F, ideal temperate climate. From June to August, it's the wet season "monsoon": 50-67% rainfall, but stable temperatures 61-77°F thanks to altitude. From December to February, expect 39-63°F: mild for China (vs Beijing 14°F in January), little rain. Permanent climate: Kunming never tops 77°F in maximum average and rarely descends below 39°F in minimum average thanks to its altitude 6,200 ft, ideal "Eternal Spring" tropical climate strongly contrasting with Beijing (14°F / +95°F) or Shanghai (34°F / 91°F). Kunming highlights: Lake Dianchi (the 6th largest freshwater lake in China 120 sq miles, on the edge of the city), Western Hills (Xishan, panoramic hills over Lake Dianchi with Dragon Gate Long Men Taoist temple sculpted in the cliff), Stone Forest (Shilin) 62 miles away (UNESCO 2007, karst park with 98 ft-high limestone pillars forming a unique-in-the-world "stone forest", iconic view of Yunnan), Green Lake Park (Cuihu, urban park with overwintering Siberian seagulls November-March iconic of Kunming), Yunnan Provincial Museum (museum of Yunnan ethnic minorities, traditional Yi-Bai-Naxi-Hani-Dai costumes), Yuantong Temple (8th-century Buddhist temple, one of the oldest in Yunnan), Cuihu Lake, Bird and Flower Market (picturesque traditional market). Essential Yunnan excursions = this is why one comes to Kunming: Dali 4h by train (UNESCO 2013 Tentative, former Bai kingdom capital 7th-13th century, preserved old town, iconic 3 pagodas, spectacular Lake Erhai), Lijiang 6h away (UNESCO 1997, perfectly preserved medieval Naxi old town, iconic tourist of Yunnan, Yulong Snow mountain 18,400 ft visible), Shangri-La 8h away ("Zhongdian" renamed by Chinese marketing 2001 after the novel "Lost Horizon" James Hilton 1933, Tibetan atmosphere at 10,500 ft altitude, iconic Songzanlin monastery), Tiger Leaping Gorge (Hutiaoxia, one of the deepest gorges in the world 12,800 ft deep, 2-3 day iconic trekking hike in China), Yuanyang Rice Terraces (UNESCO 2013, Hani terraced rice paddies over 16,000 hectares, iconic photography at sunrise), Xishuangbanna in the south (tropical region bordering Laos-Myanmar, Theravada Buddhist Dai people, preserved jungle), Jiuxiang Caves. Try guoqiao mixian ("crossing-the-bridge noodles", iconic noodle soup of Yunnan), Yunnan ham (prestige Chinese ham equivalent of Italian Parma ham), Pu'er tea (fermented Pu'er tea from Yunnan, the most prestigious of Chinese teas, ages like wine), Yunnan tea, edible flowers (Yunnan iconic).
