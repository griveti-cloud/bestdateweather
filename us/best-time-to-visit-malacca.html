Malacca (Melaka), historic city in south-west Malaysia: 460,000 inhabitants on the Strait of Malacca (one of the world's most strategic maritime trade routes for 2000 years). UNESCO 2008 (with Penang) for its triple colonial heritage: Portuguese (1511-1641), Dutch (1641-1824), British (1824-1957). Cradle of the Peranakan people (straits-born Chinese-Malay), unique Sino-Portuguese atmosphere. February is my favorite month (6.9/10): 75-86°F, 43% rain. Malacca never tops 6.9/10 (humid equatorial tropical climate). From January to March, it's the relatively drier season: 75-88°F. From April to November, it's the wetter season: 71-92% rainfall year-round. Highlights: Stadthuys (Dutch "Town Hall" 1650, the oldest surviving Dutch building in Asia, painted signature brick red, houses the Melaka History Museum), Christ Church (1753, one of the oldest active Anglican churches in South-East Asia, painted red), Dutch Square (red ensemble with Queen Victoria fountain 1904 + clock tower), A Famosa ("The Famous One", Portuguese fortress 1511, largely demolished by the British 1806, only the Porta de Santiago gate survives = the signature visual of Malacca, one of the oldest European fortifications in Asia), Saint Paul's Church (on the hill, Portuguese remains 1521, temporary tomb of Saint Francis Xavier Jesuit missionary before transfer to Goa), Cheng Hoon Teng Temple (the oldest Chinese temple in Malaysia, founded 1645), Kampung Kling Mosque (syncretic Malay-Sumatran 18th-century architecture), Jonker Walk district (Jonker Street, Sino-colonial atmosphere with 2-storey shophouses, weekend night market on Friday-Saturday-Sunday evenings, antiques, street food, cult local karaoke), Babas and Nyonya Heritage Museum (Peranakan museum in a 19th-century shophouse, to understand this unique syncretic culture), Maritime Museum (full-scale replica of the Portuguese galleon Flor de la Mar wrecked near Malacca in 1511 with a legendary treasure never recovered), Trishaw Tour (ridiculously decorated cycle-rickshaws — Hello Kitty, flowers, flashing lights — to cross the city, openly kitsch but a local experience). Try Nyonya cuisine = the Chinese-Malay blend signature of Malacca: Nyonya laksa, chicken rice ball (Hainan chicken served with rice balls, Malacca specialty), satay celup (skewers to dip in a communal peanut soup), cendol (Jonker 88 institution), devil's curry (spicy Portuguese curry), gula melaka (local palm sugar), Portuguese settlement also worth seeing (descendants of the 16th-century Portuguese soldiers still present with the Kristang Creole dialect). Kuala Lumpur airport (KUL) 2h by bus (Malacca 93 miles south-east of KL).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
