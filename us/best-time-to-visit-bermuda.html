Founder · BestDateWeather

Bermuda is a British overseas territory in the North Atlantic (620 miles from North Carolina, closer to Halifax than to Miami): 138 islands, 64,000 inhabitants. May is my favorite month (8.3/10): 70-72°F, 35% rain, sea at 70°F (cooler than the Caribbean), end of spring. From June to August, expect 75-82°F: pleasant summer, sea at 79°F. From September to November, it's the hurricane season: 49-54% rainfall, real risk (Bermuda outside the main Caribbean trajectory but can be affected). From December to April, expect 64-72°F and 34-49% rain: too cool for swimming (sea at 64-68°F), but hotels at third-price, golf in full activity. Highlights: Hamilton (capital, colorful Front Street), Royal Naval Dockyard (West End, cruise port, National Museum of Bermuda), St. George's (UNESCO, former capital founded 1612, oldest continuous English colony in America), Crystal Caves (spectacular limestone caves), Horseshoe Bay Beach (pink sand, iconic of Bermuda), Tobacco Bay (snorkelling). Pink-sand beaches: characteristic of Bermuda (foraminifera). Diving: 300+ wrecks in Bermudian waters ("world capital of wreck diving"). Bermuda Triangle = folklore subject, no tourist reality. Try fish chowder (national dish), rum swizzle, dark 'n' stormy. Outside the Caribbean zone = currency BMD (1:1 parity with USD). Very expensive: tax haven/financial.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
