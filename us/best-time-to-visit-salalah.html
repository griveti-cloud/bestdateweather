Salalah, 2nd city of the Sultanate of Oman in the Dhofar province in the extreme south, 620 miles south of Muscat near the Yemeni border: 200,000 inhabitants. Unique climate in the Middle East thanks to the Khareef = Indian Ocean monsoon that transforms the usually arid region into a lush green jungle from June to September, making Salalah the "Arab Switzerland" for Gulf inhabitants fleeing the heatwave in Riyadh-Doha-Dubai. January-February are my favorite months (8.6/10): 64-81°F, 2-4% rain, this is the ideal dry season. From November to March, it's the traditional dry season: 64-86°F, classic temperate Arab climate. From June to September, it's the Khareef = paradoxical tourist high season: 75-88°F, 27-41% rain (rare in the Middle East), constant fogs ("fog of Khareef"), spectacular temporarily verdant landscapes (rare worldwide phenomenon: land becomes jungle, waterfalls appear in the wadis, grass covers the usually desert mountains), Khareef Festival July-August very popular for Gulf families, hotel prices x4-x5. From April to May and October, expect 73-91°F: hot but no monsoon, few tourists. Highlights: Salalah Beach (Al-Mughsayl, golden sand beach 7 miles, palm trees, calmer than the Muscat beaches), Marneef Cave and Al-Mughsayl Blowholes 31 miles away (natural geysers where the sea is projected 66 ft high through holes in the limestone rock, especially in June-September when the sea is rough), Taqa Fortifications (19th century), Ruins of Al-Baleed (UNESCO, former city of Zafar = incense trading port 9th-16th century, archaeological vestiges on the seafront), Land of Frankincense Museum ("Land of Incense", modern museum on the Frankincense Route = the Dhofar is one of the only regions in the world where the frankincense tree Boswellia sacra grows, which produces the olibanum incense resin used since Antiquity by Egyptians, Romans, Hebrews, Magi of the Gospels, Omani incense was more precious than gold at the time of Jesus), Wadi Darbat 19 miles away (oasis with lake and waterfall in Khareef season June-September, improbable tropical landscapes in the Middle East), Tomb of the Prophet Job (Nabi Ayyub, place of Muslim and Christian pilgrimage according to Quranic tradition, on hill with panorama), Sumhuram 22 miles away (UNESCO, ancient port of Ubar = "Atlantis of the sands" mentioned by Pliny the Elder, ruins 2nd century BC), Banana and coconut plantations around Salalah (unique in Arabia thanks to the Khareef climate), Wadi Hinna and Ayn Hamran (natural springs in the Dhofar mountains). Excursions: Empty Quarter 4h away (Rub al-Khali, "Empty Quarter" = the largest continuous sand desert in the world 251,000 sq miles, Saudi Arabian border, dunes 820 ft high, organized 4x4 expeditions 2-3 days), Mirbat 1h30 away (historic 19th-century whaling port, former capital of the Omani Mahra state), Dragon's Blood Trees around Mughsayl (iconic endemic tree). Try harees (iconic Arab spicy porridge), shuwa, Omani halwa, Arabic coffee with local Salalah Mabsali dates, fermented camel milk. Salalah airport (SLL) with domestic flights from Muscat (1h30) and international Doha, Dubai in Khareef season.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
