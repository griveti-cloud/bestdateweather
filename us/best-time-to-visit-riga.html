Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Riga, Latvian capital: 615,000 inhabitants on the Baltic, the largest Baltic-state city. UNESCO medieval old town + 800 Art Nouveau buildings (jugendstil, the world's largest concentration, notably along Alberta Street). June is my favorite month (8.4/10): 54-72°F, 41% rain, 17h days in June (almost a white night at the solstice). From June to August, it's the tourist high season: 57-72°F. From November to March, expect 27/43°F and 35-48% precipitation often as snow: Christmas market on Doma laukums (the public Christmas tree was invented in Riga in 1510, Latvians defend the origin against claims from Tallinn), days down to 7h of light end of December. Highlights: Old town (UNESCO, Doma laukums cathedral square, Blackheads House medieval merchant guild rebuilt after WWII destruction), Riga Lutheran Cathedral (13th century, one of the largest in the Baltic states), Saint Peter's Church (400 ft, lift to the view), Swedish Bastion (iconic yellow medieval gate), Three Brothers (3 adjacent medieval houses, the oldest in Riga), Art Nouveau quarter (Alberta Street = the most beautiful, buildings by Mikhail Eisenstein, father of the filmmaker), Riga Central Market (5 former WWI zeppelin hangars, one of Europe's largest markets), Freedom Monument (1935, guarded 24/7). Maskavas district (former Jewish/Russian quarter, more local). Excursions: Jūrmala 25 min by train (Baltic seaside resort, long beach), Sigulda 1h away ("Switzerland of Latvia", Gauja Valley, Turaida Castle). Try Riga sprats (smoked sardines in a tin), pīrāgi (savoury pastry), Black Balsam (45° bitter liqueur made from 24 plants, Latvian iconic).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
