Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

N'Djamena ("the city where we rested" in Arabic), capital of Chad in the central Sahel on the Chari river Cameroonian border: 1.4 million inhabitants. Country among the poorest and largest in Sahelian Africa = 1,109,700 sq miles (2.4 times metropolitan France), 18 million inhabitants scattered. Country landlocked between Sudan + Libya + Niger + Nigeria + Cameroon + Central African Republic = at the heart of regional instability (Boko Haram + Sudan crisis + Libya chaos + Central African Republic war). Authoritarian regime of the Déby family = Idriss Déby 1990-2021 (died in combat against rebels April 2021) then Mahamat Déby son 2021-today, strong French military presence (Operation Barkhane 2014-2022, Adji Kossei base). January is my favorite month (8.8/10): 57-88°F, 0% rain, this is the ideal dry season + refreshing Saharan harmattan. December-November stay excellent (7.9-8.4/10). From November to February, it's the cool dry season: 57-93°F, dry and tempered climate in the morning (sometimes 54°F in January night, surprising for Sahel), permanent Saharan harmattan. From March to May, it's the pre-monsoon heatwave: 66-108°F (sometimes 113°F+ extreme Sahelian heatwave). From June to September, it's the short Sahelian rainy season "hivernage": 7-43% rainfall. N'Djamena highlights: N'Djamena Central Market (traditional Sahelian atmosphere), Notre-Dame Cathedral of N'Djamena (1962 French colonial heritage), Faisal Grand Mosque, National Museum of Chad (notable archaeological collection with the Toumaï skull = Sahelanthropus tchadensis 7 million years discovered in Chad 2002 = oldest known human ancestor rivalling Lucy from Ethiopia), Dembé Market, Place de la Nation (official central square), Bridge over the Chari river connecting to Kousseri Cameroon. Essential Chad excursions = this is why one visits Chad (but security difficult): Zakouma National Park in the south (one of the most beautiful parks in Central Africa, large elephant migration in dry season, lions, buffaloes, nocturnal predators), Ennedi Massif in the north-east (UNESCO 2016, spectacular Saharan landscapes with colossal sandstone arches "Aloba Arch" 390 ft high iconic, prehistoric rock paintings 7000 BC, very difficult access), Tibesti in the north (Saharan volcanic massif 11,200 ft Emi Koussi highest point of central Sahara, lunar-volcanic landscapes, preserved nomadic Toubou peoples, near-impossible access for insecurity), Lake Chad 125 miles west (4th largest lake in Africa historically, but reduced by 90% since 1960 by drought + irrigation = major Sahel ecological catastrophe, traditional Kanouri fisherman peoples). Safety: inadvisable France Diplomacy for the entirety of Chad except N'Djamena under increased vigilance, active jihadist terrorism (Boko Haram in the west Nigeria border, AQMI in the north Libya border), moderate urban crime in N'Djamena, very inadvisable travel except professional escorts or military-humanitarian missions. Try daraba (millet porridge), boule (iconic Sahelian millet ball), mafé skewers (beef in peanut sauce), grilled Chari fish (captain), ceremonial Tuareg tea 3 glasses ("1st bitter as life, 2nd sweet as love, 3rd suave as death"). N'Djamena Hassan Djamous airport (NDJ).

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
