Context

Our verdict

Frankfurt am Main, financial capital of Germany and the eurozone: 760,000 inhabitants in an urban area of 5.5 million, nicknamed "Mainhattan" for its skyscraper skyline rare in Europe (Germany has only 17 buildings > 490 ft, of which 14 are in Frankfurt). HQ of the European Central Bank, the Bundesbank, the German stock exchange Deutsche Börse, the trade fair giant Messe Frankfurt. July is my favorite month (8.8/10): 61-79°F, 34% rain, ideal temperatures. From June to August are the tourist high season: 57-79°F. From November to March, expect 32-48°F and 39-44% rain: damp cold, Römerberg Christmas market among Germany's most beautiful. Highlights: Römerberg (medieval square with Römer 13th-century city hall and half-timbered houses, partially rebuilt after 1945), Saint Bartholomew's Cathedral (Kaiserdom, place of election of the Holy Roman Emperors 1356-1792 and of their coronation 1562-1792, 13th-century gothic), Goethe House (birthplace of Wolfgang von Goethe 1749, rebuilt after WWII bombings), Main Tower (650 ft, the only public panoramic platform on the Frankfurt skyscrapers, café), Museumsufer ("Museum Bank", 13 museums on the south bank of the Main: Städel Museum = one of Germany's most important art museums, Liebieghaus, Schirn Kunsthalle, Frankfurt Filmmuseum), Sachsenhausen district (south bank, Apfelweinwirtschaften = "cider taverns", a local institution for 250 years), Palmengarten (1871 botanical garden, one of Germany's 3 largest), Kleinmarkthalle (covered market), Banking quarter (Bankenviertel around Taunusanlage, skyscrapers: Commerzbank Tower 850 ft the tallest, Messeturm 840 ft, Skyper). Major fairs: Buchmesse (the world's most important book fair, October), IAA (auto), Frankfurter Musikmesse, Lighting + Building. Excursions: Heidelberg 1h away, Rhine Valley 1h away (UNESCO Loreley), Wiesbaden 30 min away (spa town). Try local Apfelwein cider ("Ebbelwoi" in Hessian dialect, drunk from the traditional ribbed Bembel jug), Frankfurter Würstchen (long sausage similar to a frankfurter), Grüne Soße (green sauce with 7 herbs, traditional dish served with hard-boiled egg and potato), Handkäse mit Musik (sour cheese with raw onions, gives you wind). Frankfurt airport (FRA) is Germany's largest, Lufthansa hub.
