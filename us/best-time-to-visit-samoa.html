Samoa (formerly Western Samoa) is an independent state in the South Pacific: 200,000 inhabitants, capital Apia on Upolu, 10 islands. Not to be confused with American Samoa (US territory). June-August are my favorite months (6.9/10): 75-82°F, 73-76% rain, relative dry season. Humid equatorial climate = Samoa never tops 7/10. From May to October, it's the relative dry season: 73-86% rainfall. From November to April, it's the wet season: 88-95% rainfall, heavy heat, cyclone risk (Evan 2012, Gita 2018). Highlights: To Sua Ocean Trench (natural pool in a collapsed cliff, the iconic image of Samoa), Lalomanu Beach (beach), Robert Louis Stevenson Museum (the Treasure Island author lived and is buried here at Vailima), Lake Lanoto'o crater, Cape Mulinu'u (the "first point in the world" to see the sun after the date line change in 2011). Islands to explore: Upolu (the main one with Apia), Savai'i (the largest, lava fields, Alofaaga blowholes). Cultural villages: fa'a Samoa = traditional way of life still very alive, Sunday a sacred day (everything closed). Cuisine: palusami (taro and coconut milk), oka (raw fish), umu (cooking in the earth). Flight from Auckland (4h) or Brisbane. Far less touristy than Fiji/Tahiti, preserved atmosphere.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
