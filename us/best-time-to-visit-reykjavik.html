When to visit Reykjavik?

The decision rests on several criteria. Climate score, tourist crowds and feasible activities.

Verdict: In Reykjavik, July and August clearly stand out. December concentrates the least favorable conditions.
G
Gilles's view
Founder · BestDateWeather

Reykjavik lives two opposing worlds. July and August are the only truly viable months: 48-55°F, midnight sun that doesn't really set, southern fjords accessible, hikes until midnight. July remains my pick for landscapes: the F roads (highlands) open around the 15th, giving access to Landmannalaugar and Þórsmörk. Tourist peak at the same time: Golden Circle tours overflowing, 4×4 rentals climbing to $275/day. From October to March, conditions become severe: days closing at 4 PM, 50 miles/h winds, northern roads cut by snow. This is northern lights season: December-February, with their 6 PM nights, are the best months for aurora hunters, provided you accept near-total darkness. May-June offer a decent transition: increasing sunlight, prices still reasonable, but capricious weather.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
