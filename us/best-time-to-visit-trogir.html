Trogir, medieval town of central Dalmatia in Croatia: 13,000 inhabitants on islet connected to the continent by 2 bridges, 19 miles west of Split. UNESCO 1997 for its exceptionally preserved historic center (one of the best-preserved medieval fortified cities in the Adriatic). Venetian atmosphere (Venice controlled Trogir 1420-1797). June is my favorite month (9.4/10): 66-81°F, 14% rain, sea at 72°F, ideal temperatures. May and September stay excellent (8.4-9.0/10). From June to September, it's the tourist high season: 55-84°F, large crowds (cruise passengers stopping over from Split port, hotel prices x2-x3). July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heat (70-86°F). From November to March, expect 41-59°F and 30-49% rain: mild for the Mediterranean, many establishments close. Highlights: Saint Lawrence Cathedral (Sv. Lovro, Gothic 13th-16th century, architectural jewel with the Radovan Portal = Romanesque portal 1240 sculpted by Croatian master Radovan, one of the most important Romanesque works in the Balkans, monumental biblical figures and zodiac signs, panorama tower 150 ft), John Paul II Square (main square, former agora, Venetian atmosphere with 15th-century town hall), Trogir Loggia (15th-century Venetian with clock), Cipiko Palace (15th-century Venetian Renaissance, dwelling of the great Cipiko family who governed Trogir), Kamerlengo Fortress (15th-century Venetian quadrangular, panorama over the bay), Saint Mark Tower (15th century), Small Loggia, Saint Sebastian Church (15th century, delicate facade), Saint Nicholas Benedictine Convent (12th century with relief of Kairos = Greek god of luck). Riva Quarter (picturesque seafront with yachts, restaurants on the water, lively atmosphere in the evening). Trogir-Čiovo Bridge and Čiovo Island (on the other side of the canal, coastal beaches, fishing villages). Beaches: Pantan Beach 5 min away (rare sand in Croatia), Okrug Gornji Beach on Čiovo (the longest beach in the area 1.2 miles, popular), Slatine Beach. Excursions: Split 30 min by bus (UNESCO, larger city with Diocletian Palace 3rd century, already covered by BDW), Hvar and Brač islands 1h by ferry from Split (already covered), Krka National Park 1h away (waterfalls, swimming allowed), Plitvice National Park 4h away (UNESCO, already covered), Šibenik 1h away (UNESCO, exceptional 15th-16th century Saint James Cathedral). Try pasticada (Dalmatian beef), peka (meat/fish cooked under bell in embers), grilled fresh fish (Adriatic), pršut prosciutto, Pag cheese, Plavac mali wine (Croatian grape variety). Logistics: Split airport (SPU) 3 miles from Trogir = the closest airport to a UNESCO site in Europe, taxi transfers 5-10 min.

How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)
