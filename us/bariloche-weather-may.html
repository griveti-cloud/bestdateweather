Context

Our verdict

Bariloche is the "little Argentine Switzerland": northern Patagonia, lakes and araucaria forests, chalet architecture. January is my favorite month (9.3/10): 50-75°F, 11% rain, 14h days, austral summer ideal for the Circuito Chico (40 miles panoramic road), Cerro Catedral (summer lift, view over Nahuel Huapi), Refugio Frey (hike to the glacial lake). From June to August (austral winter), it's the ski season on Cerro Catedral (the largest resort in South America, 75 miles of pistes): temperatures at 34-43°F at the bottom, snow guaranteed from July to mid-September. From April to November, expect 34-57°F and 26-53% rain: humid Patagonian climate, but the autumn-colored forests (May) stay photogenic. Bariloche is famous for its chocolate: Calle Mitre concentrates the chocolatiers (Mamuschka, Rapanui, Fenoglio). El Bolsón 2h away for the Argentine hippies. Cerro Tronador (active volcano) for a day hike.

Frequently asked questions

When is the best time to visit Bariloche?
It depends on the activity. For skiing: July (ski score 8.7/10). For hiking/suiner: December (9.3/10, 20.0°F).
Can you ski in Bariloche in winter?
Yes, it's peak ski season. Average ski score December–February: 1.0/10. Cold temperatures (24°F max in January) and frequent snowfall.
Is it warm in Bariloche in suiner?
In December, temperatures average 20.0°F. Ideal for hiking and outdoor activities.

May is the wettest month with May has the lowest climate score of the year.
