What to expect in May

Spring marks the start of the good season in Tel Aviv. Temperatures rise (26.0°F) and tourists have not yet arrived en masse. Rain is virtually absent (6.0% of days) — ideal for planning without a backup. This is one of the best times of year to visit Tel Aviv.

Context

Our verdict

Tel Aviv is one of the most secular and most 24/7 cities in the Middle East. UNESCO White City with 4000 1930s Bauhaus buildings, urban beaches, global tech scene. June is my favorite month (9.6/10): 70-84°F, 2% rain, sea at 73°F. From July to September, expect 73-88°F with heavy humidity (the hamsin/sirocco can push the feels-like to 104°F). Tel Avivians live in air conditioning, head out to the seafront in the evening. From December to March, expect 50-68°F and 25-39% rain: humid, sometimes storms, but mild winters. The Carmel and Levinsky markets for Israeli cuisine (sabich, hummus, sambousek). Yafo (Jaffa, old Arab city) to the south for the port and antique dealers. The security context evolves by period: to be checked (notably 2023-2025). Jerusalem 1h by train, Dead Sea 1h30 away, Negev desert 2h away. The nightlife scene is one of the most active in the Mediterranean basin.
