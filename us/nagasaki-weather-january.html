Context

Our verdict

Nagasaki, the 2nd city to suffer an atomic bombing on 9 August 1945 (74,000 deaths), keeps a singular history in Japan: the only port open to Europeans (Dutch and Portuguese) during the Edo isolation (1639-1854), Christian influence unique in Japan. October is my favorite month (8.2/10): 64-73°F, 27% rain, ideal temperatures before winter. From June to mid-July, it's the tsuyu. July-August drop to 6.9/10 due to the heatwave. From December to February, expect 41-54°F and 28-35% rain: mild thanks to the maritime effect, sometimes clear skies. Highlights: Atomic Bomb Museum (memorial to the bombing), Peace Park (peace statue), Hypocenter Park, Glover Garden (house of Scottish merchant Thomas Glover 19th century, panoramic view over the port), Oura Cathedral (UNESCO, oldest wooden church in Japan, 1864), Dejima (artificial island where the Dutch were confined), Shinchi Chinatown, Mount Inasa (panoramic night view, among the 3 best in Japan). Excursions: Hashima/Gunkanjima by boat (abandoned coal-mining island, UNESCO, James Bond Skyfall), Hirado (former Portuguese base). Lantern Festival in Chinese February (15 days, more than 15,000 lanterns). Try Nagasaki champon (Sino-Japanese fusion noodles), castella (cake of Portuguese origin), turkish-rice. 2h by Shinkansen from Fukuoka.
