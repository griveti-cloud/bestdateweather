How we calculate the score

The score combines 4 weighted criteria using 10 years of real climate data (ERA5).

  • 40% temperature (sweet spot 72-82°F, extreme heat penalty)
  • 35% rain (frequency + daily intensity)
  • 25% sunshine (hours per day)
  • Humidity penalty (dew point ≥ 61°F + tmax ≥ 79°F)

Context

Our verdict

Paris shows its best face from May to September. Terraces overflow, the sun stretches past 12h a day. June stays my favorite month (8.3/10): 57-73°F, 13h of light, and the city hasn't yet been emptied by holidays. Roland-Garros, the Fête de la Musique, evenings stretching until 10 PM on the quays — this is Paris when everything is happening before the summer exodus. July-August are hot and heavy (heatwaves arrive more and more often), but Parisians flee and the city empties in a strange and beautiful way. From December to February, the sky is grey and leaden, just 3h of sun, days closing at 5 PM. This is the season for Christmas markets, museums without crowds, and Marais cafés. To each their own Paris.
