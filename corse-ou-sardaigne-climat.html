FAQ

Sardinia edges Corsica marginally — slightly warmer, a little more sunshine in peak summer (July: Sardinia 9.8/10, Corsica 9.5/10). Both are among Europe's best Mediterranean islands. Choose based on other factors.
Sardinia wins — the Costa Smeralda's turquoise water and white sand (La Pelosa, Cala Brandinchi) is regularly rated among Europe's finest. Corsica has excellent beaches too (Palombaggia, Rondinara) but Sardinia's variety wins.
Corsica wins clearly — the GR20 is considered Europe's most beautiful long-distance trail. Corsica's mountains rise to 2,706m with dramatic alpine scenery. Sardinia has excellent hiking too but nothing matching the GR20.
Corsica is generally cheaper than Sardinia's Costa Smeralda resort area, which is one of Italy's most expensive. Sardinia's interior and less touristy south/west are affordable. Similar cost overall.
Both are excellent in September — crowds thin, prices drop 25-40%, sea stays at 25-26°C. Corsica: GR20 at its best. Sardinia: beaches peaceful, Costa Smeralda accessible. September is arguably better than August peak for both.
Corsica is best in July (8.6/10). Sardinia is best in June (8.8/10).
