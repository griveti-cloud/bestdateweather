FAQ

Tenerife wins on weather — warmer (annual average 22°C vs Madeira's 20°C), drier and more sunshine in the south. Madeira has more cloud and rain due to its volcanic terrain. For guaranteed sun: Tenerife's south. For green dramatic scenery: Madeira.
Tenerife wins in February — 20-22°C in the south, very low rain, and the famous Santa Cruz Carnival (one of the world's largest). Madeira in February is cooler (17°C) with more rain but hosts the Funchal Carnival. Both are excellent European winter escapes.
Madeira wins — the levada trail network through UNESCO laurisilva forest is unique in the world. Pico Arieiro to Pico Ruivo is one of Europe's great mountain walks. Tenerife has excellent hiking too (Teide National Park) but Madeira's levadas are uniquely Madeiran.
Tenerife wins — Playa de las Teresitas (imported Saharan sand), El Médano (long natural beach, windsurfing), south coast resorts. Madeira has very limited beach access — volcanic island with mostly rocky coastlines.
Both broadly similar. Tenerife has many budget package options (all-inclusive from €600/week from UK). Madeira focuses on independent tourism with quintas (manor houses) and boutique hotels at excellent value in a stunning setting.
Tenerife is best in August (9.0/10). Madeira is best in August (9.1/10).
